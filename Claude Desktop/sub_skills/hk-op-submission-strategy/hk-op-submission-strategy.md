# HK OP Submission Strategy: The Road to OP
Practical strategy for managing the Occupation Permit path in Hong Kong, from completion evidence and statutory forms to BD inspection readiness and closeout.

---

## 1. Scope and Intent
Use this skill when the project team needs to:
- Plan the OP pathway and filing sequence.
- Coordinate statutory forms (BA12, BA13, BA14) with actual site status.
- Prepare for BD OP inspection and comments cycle.
- Assemble and quality-check the "full set" of record drawings and supporting test reports.

Core principle: OP success is mostly a records-and-sequence discipline problem, not only a construction completion problem.

---

## 2. OP Form Workflow (BA12 / BA13 / BA14)
Treat the forms as a linked control chain:

1. **BA14 readiness gate (works completion evidence):**
   - Confirm works package completion status against approved/accepted submissions.
   - Close material non-conformities and unresolved statutory comments.
   - Lock revision index for record drawing issue.

2. **BA12 application package (OP application stage):**
   - Submit OP application with structured document index.
   - Cross-reference record drawings, test certificates, supervision records, and outstanding concession status.
   - Ensure all claimed compliances can be demonstrated physically on site.

3. **BA13 outcome management (OP issuance / partial OP path):**
   - Track BD comments, rectification actions, and resubmission timing.
   - Maintain traceability between comments, corrective actions, and revised evidence.

Always verify current BD circulars and latest form notes before submission.

---

## 3. OP Inspection Strategy with BD
Before BD walk-through, run a formal pre-inspection audit:

- **Physical compliance walk:** MOE, fire doors, compartmentation interfaces, accessibility features, signage, and critical dimensions.
- **Document-to-site match test:** pick random items from submitted records and confirm exact site traceability.
- **Defect triage:** classify issues as critical / major / cosmetic, with closure owner and deadline.
- **Comment rehearsal:** prepare concise response notes for likely BD queries.

Inspection-day protocol:
- Single point coordinator (usually AP side) to control information flow.
- Immediate capture of BD remarks, location, and required evidence.
- Same-day action register issuance after inspection.

---

## 4. Full Set Coordination (Record Drawings + Test Reports)
Create one controlled submission matrix with unique IDs:

- **Record drawings:** architectural, structural, fire services interfaces, barrier-free provisions, and approved minor amendments.
- **Certificates/test reports:** fire service tests, lift/escalator commissioning records, drainage tests, material certificates where required.
- **Version control:** no mixed revision sets; all references must point to current accepted revision.
- **Evidence mapping:** each statutory requirement linked to a specific drawing/test item.

Minimum quality checks:
- Drawing title block consistency.
- Grid/level references aligned across disciplines.
- Test report dates and location references match actual installed works.

---

## 5. Grey Area A: Partial OP (Podium/Retail First, Tower Ongoing)
When podium/retail can operate while tower works continue:

### Strategic requirements
- Clear physical and operational separation between occupied and construction zones.
- Independent life-safety logic for occupied portion (means of escape, fire separation, access control).
- Construction risk controls preventing interface hazards to occupied users.

### BA13 partial OP approach
- Define exact boundary of the partial OP area in drawings and site marking.
- Demonstrate that partial area is fully code-compliant as a standalone operational zone.
- Provide management plan for ongoing tower works (logistics, dust/noise control, emergency segregation).

### Common failure points
- Shared escape routes still dependent on unfinished tower areas.
- Incomplete fire compartment interfaces at podium-tower junction.
- Building operation procedures not aligned with temporary construction conditions.

---

## 6. Grey Area B: Minor Deviations Before OP Walk-Through
Minor mismatches to latest approved plans must be regularized before inspection where they affect compliance perception.

Deviation triage:
1. **No compliance impact:** document clearly in as-built records and close.
2. **Potential compliance impact but regularizable:** file minor amendment / update submission path early; do not wait until inspection week.
3. **Material compliance impact:** correct physically or secure acceptance before OP inspection.

Practical rule:
- If a BD surveyor can observe the deviation during OP walk-through and question compliance, treat it as pre-OP action item, not post-OP housekeeping.

---

## 7. Grey Area C: Site Records for BD Audit
AP-side records must be audit-ready:

- **Site Supervision Account:** complete, signed, and logically continuous.
- **Site Diary:** daily entries consistent with major milestones, inspections, and non-conformance closure.
- **Inspection evidence:** dated photos, marked plans, test witness notes, and cross-references.
- **Change log:** who approved what, when, and under which submission reference.

Red flags to eliminate:
- Missing dates/signatures.
- Retroactive bulk entries with no supporting evidence.
- Inconsistency between diary events and submitted completion dates.

---

## 8. Suggested 8-Week OP Readiness Program
1. **W-8 to W-6:** gap audit (site vs approved drawings), deviation register freeze.
2. **W-6 to W-4:** close critical deviations, finalize draft record set and test report index.
3. **W-4 to W-3:** internal mock OP inspection and rectification sprint.
4. **W-3 to W-2:** finalize BA12 package and supporting compliance matrix.
5. **W-2 to W-1:** leadership review, sign-off checks, and inspection rehearsal.
6. **W-0 onward:** BD inspection response cycle with 24-hour action tracking.

---

## 9. Output Templates (Recommended)
- OP readiness dashboard (traffic-light by discipline).
- Deviation register with regulatory impact column.
- Comment-response tracker (BD comment, action, evidence, closure date).
- Record set transmittal index with immutable revision log.

---

*Reference baseline: Buildings Ordinance Cap. 123, current BD circular/form guidance, approved plans and accepted amendments, project-specific consent conditions, and discipline test/commissioning requirements.*
