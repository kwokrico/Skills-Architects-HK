# HK Cashflow & Debt Recovery
Execution playbook for architect-side billing discipline, receivables control, and practical debt recovery in the Hong Kong developer market.

---

## 1. Core Position
- Treat invoicing as a project control function, not an admin afterthought.
- Link each invoice to a pre-agreed contractual milestone and evidence package.
- Escalate aged debt early; delay reduces leverage and collection probability.
- Protect relationship and legal position at the same time: commercial tone, forensic records.

---

## 2. Milestone-Linked Billing in HK Practice
Typical architecture fee schedules in Hong Kong are tied to authority or delivery milestones.

Common examples:
- `10%` on BD Approval.
- `5%` on Occupation Permit (OP).

Other common triggers used in appointments:
- Contract signing / mobilization retainer.
- Concept design sign-off.
- General Building Plan (GBP) submission and approval.
- Tender documentation issue.
- Contract award / construction commencement.
- Practical completion / handover.
- Defects liability period closeout.

Control points:
1. Define milestone language precisely in appointment documents.
2. Define what documentary evidence triggers invoice entitlement.
3. Confirm whether partial milestones permit pro-rata billing.
4. Track authority timeline risk and protect downstream payment timing.
5. Define payer entity and tax invoice details early (SPV vs parent developer vs JV vehicle).

## 3. Invoicing Execution Standard
Each invoice package should include:
1. Invoice with milestone reference and contract clause reference.
2. Supporting milestone evidence (submission receipts, approval letters, minutes).
3. Statement of account (current + overdue + aging bucket).
4. Payment due date and accepted payment method.

Operational discipline:
- Issue invoice within 1-2 business days of milestone achievement.
- Keep a centralized receivables tracker by client/entity/project.
- Reconcile received payments weekly against invoice register.
- Assign a single debt owner for each account (PM or commercial lead) to avoid responsibility gaps.

Recommended data fields in receivables tracker:
- Client group, contracting entity, project code, invoice number.
- Invoice date, due date, aging bucket, amount invoiced, amount paid, balance.
- Trigger milestone, evidence file location, AP contact, decision-maker contact.
- Last follow-up date, next action date, dispute status, suspension-rights status.

## 4. Aged Debt Control Framework
Suggested aging bands:
- `0-30 days`: normal follow-up cadence.
- `31-60 days`: controlled escalation with formal reminder.
- `61-90 days`: management-level intervention and payment plan request.
- `90+ days`: pre-legal strategy and suspension-rights review.

Key metrics:
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO).
- Overdue amount as percentage of billed-to-date.
- Top 5 debtor concentration by value.
- Collection effectiveness index (cash collected vs opening overdue).
- Weighted average days overdue for invoices above a materiality threshold.

Minimum follow-up cadence:
- `0-30`: 1 formal reminder + 1 call before due date or immediately after.
- `31-60`: weekly calls and written recap after each call.
- `61-90`: director-level escalation every 7-10 days.
- `90+`: formal notice timetable with legal review checkpoints.

## 5. HK Developer Market Recovery Tactics
Practical recovery sequence:
1. Confirm debt validity (PO, contract milestone, approval evidence, invoice receipt).
2. Make direct commercial follow-up with project lead and accounts payable.
3. Escalate to commercial decision-maker (development director/CFO level).
4. Offer structured settlement only with clear timetable and default trigger.
5. Reserve rights under appointment and applicable contract mechanisms.

Tactical principles:
- Keep communications factual, dated, and evidence-backed.
- Separate relationship tone from legal position: be professional but unambiguous.
- Avoid informal concessions without documented approval.
- Escalate horizontally and vertically: project team + AP + leadership chain.
- Keep internal script discipline so all team members present the same position.

Common debtor explanations and response posture:
- "Waiting for internal approval": request named approver and decision date.
- "Need revised invoice format": issue corrected version same day, preserve original due date position where contract allows.
- "Milestone not achieved": provide objective evidence pack and request written dispute particulars.
- "Cashflow tight this month": move to formal payment plan with dated installments and default consequences.

## 6. Contractual Safeguards for Cashflow
- Include entitlement to interest on late payment where commercially feasible.
- Include suspension/remobilization rights for prolonged non-payment.
- Require clear notice periods for payment default actions.
- Avoid front-loading effort where fee schedule is back-loaded without protections.
- Align consultant sub-appointments with upstream client payment profile where possible.

High-value clause checklist before signing:
1. Payment due period clearly stated (for example, 14/30 days from invoice date).
2. No "pay when paid" exposure in consultant agreements unless explicitly priced.
3. Clear right to suspend services for persistent non-payment.
4. Right to recover remobilization cost after suspension.
5. Defined dispute-notice window (late disputes deemed accepted where enforceable).

## 7. Escalation Playbook (Action by Age)
### Stage A: `15-30 days overdue`
- Written reminder with invoice copy and milestone evidence attached.
- Confirm AP has logged invoice and no documentary blockers exist.
- Schedule payment date call; document commitment in writing.

### Stage B: `31-60 days overdue`
- Send formal notice to project lead + AP + commercial manager.
- Request settlement date within 5 business days.
- Pause non-critical out-of-scope requests until arrears are regularized.

### Stage C: `61-90 days overdue`
- Director-to-director call and written recap with amount, history, and ask.
- Offer conditional payment plan only if first installment date is immediate.
- Prepare suspension notice draft and legal review pack.

### Stage D: `90+ days overdue`
- Trigger formal default notice per contract.
- Execute suspension or reduced-service protocol (if contractually supported).
- Prepare recovery bundle for legal counsel: contract, invoices, evidence, correspondence chronology.

---

## 8. Red Flags
- Repeated "processing delay" explanations without payment dates.
- Milestone disputes raised only after invoice due date.
- Significant debt concentration in a single developer group.
- No clear contractual trigger wording for major billing events.
- Team instructed to continue out-of-scope work despite unpaid certified stages.
- Invoice acceptance by project team but rejection by AP without written basis.
- Frequent contracting-entity changes across related companies.

---

## 9. Communication Templates (Short Form)
### Friendly reminder (early stage)
"Please note Invoice [X] for [milestone] is now due. Attached are the invoice and milestone evidence. Kindly confirm payment date."

### Formal escalation (mid stage)
"Invoice [X] remains unpaid at [N] days overdue. Please provide settlement date by [date]. Failing this, we will need to implement the contractual escalation process."

### Payment plan confirmation
"We confirm your proposed payment plan: [dates/amounts]. This arrangement is accepted on the basis that any missed installment triggers immediate escalation."

---

## 10. Practical Output Checklist
- Milestone-trigger matrix prepared and contract-aligned.
- Invoice evidence checklist applied on every billing event.
- Weekly aging report maintained and escalation path defined.
- Aged debt recovery script and timeline standardized.
- Suspension/interest rights reviewed before major cashflow stress.
- Named owner assigned to each overdue invoice above threshold.
- Monthly debtor-risk review completed for top exposure accounts.
