# HK Certificate of Compliance (CC) & Consent to Assign
Execution playbook for navigating Lands Department completion conditions, obtaining Certificate of Compliance (or Consent to Assign), and coordinating Green Area handover in Hong Kong private land grant developments.

---

## 1. Core Position
- Treat CC/Consent to Assign as a multi-party closeout process, not a single filing event.
- Build backward from intended sale/assignment milestones and legal completion deadlines.
- Lock handover evidence quality early; incomplete records are a common source of delay.
- Coordinate legal, surveying, engineering, and authority interfaces under one document control workflow.

---

## 2. What CC / Consent to Assign Means
- `Certificate of Compliance (CC)`: Lands Department confirmation that key lease/grant obligations for assignment have been satisfied.
- `Consent to Assign`: Permission to assign where a full CC is not yet issued but assignment can proceed subject to conditions.
- Usually relevant to private land grants where lease conditions impose completion, infrastructure, or handover obligations before free assignment.
- Commercial impact: without CC/consent, assignment and disposal strategy can be delayed or constrained.

---

## 3. Typical Trigger Conditions in Private Grants
Common conditions that must be closed out or substantially satisfied:
1. Building completion and compliance with approved plans.
2. Lease-condition works completion (including external works and access interfaces).
3. Government, institution, or community obligations tied to the grant.
4. Green Area formation and handover to relevant departments where required.
5. Required certificates, as-built records, and maintenance period obligations.

Control principle:
- Build a condition-by-condition closeout matrix from the grant and modification letters; assign owner, evidence, status, and dependency.

---

## 4. Green Area Handover Framework
Where the grant requires Green Area works and surrender/handover:
- Confirm exact Green Area boundary, scope, and technical standards in grant documents.
- Map receiving authority by asset type (for example roads, slopes, landscaping, drainage interfaces).
- Plan early coordination with departments commonly involved:
  - `HyD` for public roads/footpaths and highway-related assets.
  - `LCSD` for landscape/open space elements where applicable.
  - Other works departments depending on asset nature and location.

Key handover evidence package:
1. As-built drawings and survey records.
2. Test reports and commissioning records.
3. Completion photos with location references.
4. Defect rectification log and closeout confirmation.
5. Maintenance responsibility statement and handover minutes.

---

## 5. End-to-End Process (Practical Sequence)
1. Review grant conditions and extract all CC/assignment-critical obligations.
2. Build closeout register with responsible party and target date.
3. Pre-consult LandsD and receiving departments on handover expectation and evidence format.
4. Complete works and compile evidence pack by condition.
5. Run internal readiness check before formal submission.
6. Submit CC/consent package and manage authority comments with tracked responses.
7. Complete outstanding handover actions and secure confirmation records.
8. Close conditions, issue final confirmations, and align with legal assignment timeline.

---

## 6. Submission & Document Control Standard
Minimum recommended package structure:
- Section A: Grant-condition tracker and status summary.
- Section B: Drawings and approvals set.
- Section C: Completion records and test/inspection evidence.
- Section D: Green Area handover records (department-wise).
- Section E: Correspondence log, meeting minutes, and action tracker.

Discipline rules:
- One source of truth register for condition status.
- Version-controlled evidence index with file references.
- Written confirmation for every verbal authority direction.
- Weekly closeout meeting with action owners until CC/consent milestone is achieved.

---

## 7. Risk Hotspots
- Late discovery of unclosed lease obligations.
- Mismatch between built condition and approved / accepted scope.
- Fragmented ownership across consultant, contractor, and legal teams.
- Green Area handover scope disagreements at late stage.
- Missing test records or non-traceable as-built documentation.
- Assuming CC timing without confirmed authority feedback cycle.

---

## 8. Mitigation Checklist
- Grant-condition matrix prepared and approved by project leadership.
- Green Area boundary and receiving authority map confirmed early.
- Handover evidence template agreed before completion stage.
- Department interface log maintained with dated decisions.
- Closeout dry-run conducted before formal CC/consent submission.
- Legal and commercial teams aligned on fallback plan if only Consent to Assign is available initially.

---

## 9. Practical Output Checklist
- CC / Consent to Assign strategy note issued.
- Condition-by-condition closeout register live and owner-assigned.
- Green Area handover package completed and traceable.
- Department comments tracker closed or actioned with timetable.
- Assignment critical path integrated with legal/commercial milestones.
- Residual obligations clearly listed for post-consent governance.

---

*Project teams should align this framework with the specific land grant conditions, Lands Department correspondence, and project legal advice for transaction-critical decisions.*
