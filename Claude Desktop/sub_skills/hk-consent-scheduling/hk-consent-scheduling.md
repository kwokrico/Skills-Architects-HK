# HK Consent & Scheduling
Covers the statutory procedures for obtaining Consent to Commence Works under s.14 of the Buildings Ordinance, Electronic Submission Hub (ESH) protocols, and realistic architectural programming for Hong Kong development cycles.

---

## 1. Statutory Timelines (Buildings Department)
Under the Buildings Ordinance, the BD operates on strict "deemed-to-be-approved" or "deemed-to-be-granted" timelines.

| Submission Type | Statutory Period | Reference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Plan Approval (GBP/Structural)** | 60 Days | BO s.15(1) |
| **Major Amendments / Re-submissions** | 30 / 60 Days | Subject to complexity |
| **Consent to Commence Works (Form BA8)** | 28 Days | BO s.14(2) |
| **Occupation Permit (Form BA13)** | 14 Days | BO s.21 |
| **Minor Works Notification (Class II/III)** | 7 Days (Before) | B(MW) Reg. |

---

## 2. Consent to Commence Works (s.14 BO)
Consent is the legal "green light" to start physical works on-site after plans are approved.

### 2.1 Pre-requisites for Consent
* **Approved Plans:** Corresponding plans (Foundation, Superstructure, etc.) must be approved.
* **AP/RSE/RGE Appointment:** Valid appointments must be in place.
* **Site Supervision Plan (SSP):** Submitted and accepted by BD.
* **Contractor Appointment:** Registered General Building Contractor (RGBC) or Specialist Contractor (RSC) must be appointed.
* **Land Conditions:** Necessary Hoarding/Scaffolding permits (Form BA14/14A) and land-grant compliance.

### 2.2 Concurrent Approval and Consent (PNAP ADM-19)
To fast-track programs, an AP may apply for plan approval and consent *simultaneously*.
* **Benefit:** Shaves ~28 days off the critical path.
* **Risk:** If plans are disapproved, the consent application is automatically rejected.
* **Common Use:** Standard foundation works or simple A&A.

---

## 3. Fast-Track Processing (PNAP APP-97)
For "straightforward" cases, the BD offers an expedited 30-day approval cycle (instead of 60).

| Eligibility | Requirements |
| :--- | :--- |
| **One-stop Service** | Warehouses, small-scale industrial, or single-block residential. |
| **Pre-submission Enquiry (PSE)** | Highly recommended to resolve major GFA/Height/Fire Safety issues before formal filing. |
| **Fast-track Criteria** | No major technical complications; AP certifies compliance with all PNAPs and Regulations. |

---

## 4. Realistic Project Programming (The "Road to Site Start")
Standard timelines for a private sector development in Hong Kong.

### 4.1 Typical Submission Milestones
1.  **General Building Plan (GBP) Phase:** 2–4 months (Preparation + 60-day BD/Referral cycle).
2.  **Structural/Foundation Phase:** Parallel to GBP; requires RSE coordination.
3.  **Hoarding & Excavation (E&S):** Usually the first "Consent" obtained.
4.  **Site Start:** Typically **6–9 months** from the first formal GBP submission for a greenfield site.

### 4.2 Departmental Response Times (Referral System)
* **FSD (Fire Services):** Parallel 60-day cycle with BD; critical for Fire Service Notes/FSI plans.
* **LandsD (Land Administration):** Often slower; Lease Interpretation/Modifications can take **6–18 months**.
* **PlanD (Planning):** s.16 Applications (if required) take **2 months** for a decision.

## 5. Electronic Submission Hub (ESH)
The BD is transitioning to a digital-only submission environment.

* **Format:** Digital signatures (e-Cert) and BIM/CAD files via the ESH portal.
* **Efficiency:** Eliminates physical printing/delivery; automatic distribution to referral departments (DSD, WSD, FSD).
* **Batching:** Large sets are broken down by file size limits (check latest ESH technical guidelines).

---

## 6. Renewal and Re-submission Strategies
* **Renewal of Consent:** Consent expires if works do not commence within **3 months** or are suspended for more than **3 months**. Use Form BA8 to renew.
* **Resubmission Strategy:** If a "Fundamental Issue" is raised, withdraw and resubmit before the 60-day mark to reset the clock and avoid a formal "Disapproval" record.
* **Modification/Exemption (s.42):** Applied alongside GBP to bypass rigid interpretations (e.g., non-compliant headrooms for specific designs).

## 7. Completion Certification (BA14)
* **The Gatekeeper:** Form BA14 serves as the formal certification of completion for specific work packages (Foundation, E&S, A&A) that do not result in a new building.
* **Sequential Logic (s.16(3)(b) BO):** Under the "Chain of Consent," the BD typically refuses Superstructure Consent until the Foundation BA14 has been acknowledged.
* **Statutory Timelines:** * **Standard:** Administrative target is **28 days**. 
* **Fast-track (PNAP APP-13):** Use **Form BA14C** for A&A works to trigger a **14-day** streamlined cycle (if not selected for audit).
* **Post-OP Rectification:** Minor "cosmetic" rectifications after an Occupation Permit can be handled via the **PRWP** (Post-OP Rectification Works Procedure) instead of a formal BA14.

## 8. Record Plan & Minor Deviations (PNAP APP-117)
A **Record Plan** must be submitted with the BA14. If deviations from the approved plans are "Minor," they can be reflected directly on the Record Plan. If they are "Major," an **Amendment Plan** must be approved *prior* to filing BA14.

### 8.1 Minor Deviation Criteria (The "Record Plan only" Zone)
| Category | Minor Deviation (Record Plan OK) | Major Deviation (Requires Amendment) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GFA / Plot Ratio** | No change in GFA or PR. | Any increase in GFA or PR. |
| **Structural** | Slight shifting of reinforcement; change in non-structural partitions. | Change in structural system, member sizes, or loading. |
| **Fire Safety (MOE)** | Increase in exit width; decrease in travel distance. | **Decrease** in exit width; **Increase** in travel distance. |
| **L&V (Reg. 30)** | Increase in window area/openable area. | **Decrease** in window area below statutory 1/10. |
| **Site Coverage** | Minor reduction in site coverage. | Any increase in site coverage. |
| **Positioning** | Shifting of internal walls within the same unit. | Shifting of building block or boundary setbacks. |

### 8.2 Record Plan Checklist for Submission
To ensure the BA14 is not rejected, the Record Plan must include:
* **Color Coding:** Generally submitted in "Black & White" or "Monochrome" showing the final as-built status, with major changes from the last approved plan clearly noted.
* **Certifications:** * **AP/RSE/RGE:** Standard endorsement certifying works are in "substantial accordance" with the BO.
    * **RPS (Surveyor):** Required for building height, boundary setbacks, and site levels (PNAP APP-40).
* **Supporting Docs:** * **Form 201/202:** Summary of steel/concrete test results.
    * **Piling/Drilling Logs:** Final Founding Levels (FFL) for foundation packages.
    * **Photographic Records:** Clear visual evidence of completed works.

## 9. ESH (Electronic Submission Hub) Protocol
The Record Plan is the final "Legal Snapshot" in the ESH database.
* **Metadata:** Ensure CAD/BIM metadata tags match the **BA14** form data exactly.
* **Layering:** Follow the *Technical Manual for ESH* for layer naming (e.g., `BD_GFA_EXEMPT`, `BD_MOE_PATH`).
* **Signatures:** Digital signatures (e-Cert) are required for every sheet.

---

### *Refined Programming Tip*
> **The 28-Day Buffer:** When drafting a project master program, never place "Superstructure Site Start" immediately after "Foundation Completion." Always insert a **28-day "BA14 Acknowledgement" period** to account for the BD's review of the Record Plans and Test Reports.
