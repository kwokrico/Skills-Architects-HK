# HK Design Theory

Design theory applied to Hong Kong's high-density, subtropical, post-colonial urban context.

---

## 0. Human Scale (Mandatory in Every Suggestion)

HK design theory is only useful when it lands in **human-scale** decisions: what bodies can reach, how crowds pass, how stairs feel after 10 floors, whether an edge feels safe, and whether rain/heat make a route unusable.

When you make *any* design suggestion, include a **Human scale check** with at least **1–3 dimensions (mm)** relevant to the suggestion (width/height/riser+tread/turning/guard height), using the reference table:

- `references/hk-human-scale-dimensions.md`

If you can’t justify the suggestion with human-scale numbers and experience (comfort, effort, crowding, fear-of-height, heat/rain exposure), revise the suggestion until you can.

## 1. Critical Regionalism in HK

Kenneth Frampton's critical regionalism — resisting placeless modernism through tectonic culture — is directly applicable to HK's curtain-wall tower monoculture.

| Principle | HK Application |
|---|---|
| Tectonic expression | Expose structural logic (RC shear walls, transfer slabs) rather than concealing behind generic curtain wall |
| Climate response | Shading devices, ventilated facades, and operable windows as cultural/climatic expression |
| Local material | Granite, GRC, and ceramic tile as regional material language |
| Topographic engagement | Respond to HK's dramatic hillside topography; section as design generator |

Human-scale implications (always state these when suggesting an element):
- Shading/brise-soleil: check **reach/operability** and maintenance access (avoid “designed but untouchable”).
- Ventilated/operable facades: check **handle heights** and safe **edge/guard** conditions for operable panels.

## 2. Vertical Urbanism (Koolhaas / HK Context)

Rem Koolhaas's "Bigness" and congestion theory finds its fullest expression in HK:

- Programme stacking: residential, retail, office, carpark, MTR concourse in a single section
- Elevator as urban street: vertical circulation replaces horizontal movement
- Podium as urban ground: artificial ground plane at L3–L5 creates new public realm
- Composite building as urban block: single building performs the role of an entire city block

Human-scale implications (always state these when suggesting stacking/section moves):
- Elevator lobbies and transfer levels: check **two-way passing widths** and **queue depth**.
- Podium “streets”: check **weather-protected walking width** and **resting cadence** (benches/landings).

## 4. Heritage Conservation Theory (HK)

| Concept | HK Application |
|---|---|
| AAB grading | Grade 1 (highest significance) / Grade 2 / Grade 3; informs intervention level |
| AMO consultation | Declared monuments and proposed Grade 1: AMO consultation mandatory |
| Adaptive reuse | Preferred over demolition; Lacaton & Vassal principle applied to HK heritage stock |
| Reversibility | New interventions should be reversible; do not damage historic fabric |
| Key precedents | PMQ (Police Married Quarters), Tai Kwun (Central Police Station), Blue House (Wan Chai) |

Human-scale implications (always state these when suggesting interventions):
- Adaptive reuse: check **door clear openings**, **step-free routes**, and **stair comfort** before celebrating “authenticity”.
- Reversibility: avoid “museum plinth” solutions that break **everyday usability** (circulation widths, reach, seating).

## 5. Sustainability Philosophy (BEAM Plus / HKGBC)

- BEAM Plus is not a checklist exercise — it is a framework for integrated design
- Credits requiring early design decisions (orientation, shading, natural ventilation) cannot be retrofitted
- HKGBC promotes whole-life thinking: operational carbon + embodied carbon + user wellbeing
- Post-occupancy evaluation (POE) increasingly expected for BEAM Plus Platinum projects

Human-scale implications (always state these when suggesting sustainability moves):
- Natural ventilation and shading: check **operability/reach**, **glare**, and **thermal comfort** at seating/working heights.
- “Green” circulation: check **ramp gradients**, **stair comfort**, and **rest points** so low-carbon routes are actually used.

## 6. Compositional Principles (HK High-Density)

| Principle | Application |
|---|---|
| Section as generator | In HK, the section (podium height, transfer level, sky garden) is more generative than the plan |
| Threshold design | Public → semi-public → private transitions compressed in HK; design each threshold deliberately |
| View as amenity | Harbour/mountain views are primary drivers of unit value and orientation |
| Ground activation | Blank podium walls are the dominant failure mode; active frontage is a design obligation |

Human-scale implications (always state these when suggesting compositional moves):
- Thresholds: specify **clear widths** and **door openings** (don’t design “tight drama” that becomes daily friction).
- Ground activation: specify **walkway width**, **queue spill**, and **edge safety** (guards/parapets) at any level change.
