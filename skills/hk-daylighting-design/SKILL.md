# HK Daylighting Design

Compliance baseline: BO Reg. 30/30A (window area ≥ 1/10 floor area). Performance target: BEAM Plus NB IEQ daylight credits. Climate: CZ 1A subtropical, 22.3°N.

---

## 1. BO Minimum Daylighting Compliance

| Use | Min Window Area | Min Headroom | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic (residential) | 1/10 of floor area | 2.5 m | BO Reg. 30 |
| Non-domestic | 1/10 of floor area | 3.0 m | BO Reg. 30A |

- Window area measured as openable area (not fixed glazing) for ventilation compliance
- Rooms without direct external windows require BD approval (e.g. internal bathrooms via mechanical ventilation)

---

## 2. BEAM Plus IEQ Daylight Credits

| Credit | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Daylight Factor (DF) | DF ≥ 2% in ≥ 75% of regularly occupied floor area |
| Illuminance-based | 300 lux for ≥ 50% of occupied hours in ≥ 75% of area |
| Glare control | External or internal shading to limit ASE > 1000 lux |

---

## 3. Solar Geometry (HK, 22.3°N)

| Season | Solar Noon Altitude | Sunrise/Sunset Azimuth |
|---|---|---|
| Summer solstice (Jun 21) | ~91° (sun passes north of zenith) | ~60° N of E / W |
| Equinox (Mar/Sep 21) | ~68° | Due E / W |
| Winter solstice (Dec 21) | ~45° | ~30° S of E / W |

Key implications:
- **East/West facades**: high solar gain in morning/afternoon; horizontal fins less effective than vertical fins or deep reveals
- **North facade**: receives direct sun in summer (sun north of zenith); do not assume north = shaded
- **South facade**: lower solar altitude in winter; horizontal overhangs effective

---

## 4. Urban Canyon Context

HK typical street H/W ratios of 3:1 to 8:1 severely limit sky component. Simulation is essential for dense urban sites.

| Metric | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Sky Component (SC) | Use Radiance or validated simulation; hand calculation unreliable in deep canyons |
| Externally Reflected Component (ERC) | Low in HK urban context; dark facades opposite reduce ERC |
| Borrowed light | Glazed partitions to internal corridors acceptable for BEAM Plus if primary space has direct window |

---

## 5. Shading Strategies (CZ 1A)

| Facade | Preferred Strategy |
|---|---|
| East | Vertical fins or deep reveals; egg-crate for combined H+V |
| West | Same as East; consider opaque spandrel panels to reduce WWR |
| North | Horizontal overhangs (summer sun from north); light shelf for daylighting |
| South | Horizontal overhangs; most effective orientation for shading control |

---

*Sources: Buildings Ordinance Cap. 123 Reg. 30/30A, BEAM Plus NB v2.0 IEQ credits, CIBSE LG10, HK Observatory solar data.*
