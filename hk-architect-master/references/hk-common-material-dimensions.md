## How to use this reference

- **Design to modules early**: set grids, opening sizes, and panelization to common board/block sizes to minimize cutting/wastage.
- **Prefer repeatable bays**: facade, ceiling, partitions, and tiling layouts should align to stock dimensions (or integer fractions).
- **Confirm “real” sizes**: many products have **nominal** vs **actual** sizes (e.g., timber, blocks, boards). Always confirm datasheets.

## Typical stock sizes (HK / regional supply)

### Boards & sheets (interior + facade backing)

| Material | Common stock size (L × W) | Common thicknesses (mm) | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Gypsum plasterboard | 2400 × 1200; 2700 × 1200; 3000 × 1200 | 9.5; 12.5; 15 | Fire/moisture boards vary; check BEAM/VOC docs where applicable. |
| Fibre cement board | 2440 × 1220 | 4.5; 6; 8; 9; 12; 16 | Widely used for wet areas / soffits; confirm edge treatment and coating system. |
| Plywood (general) | 2440 × 1220; 1830 × 915 | 3–25 (varies) | 4×8 ft and 3×6 ft equivalents are common; FSC/low-formaldehyde grades vary. |
| OSB (where used) | 2440 × 1220 | 9; 11; 15; 18 | Less common than plywood in some HK fit-outs; verify availability. |
| Aluminium composite panel (ACP) | 2440 × 1220; 3050 × 1220 (varies) | 3; 4; 6 | Coil coating / PVDF specs matter for marine exposure. |

### Masonry & walling units

| Material | Typical unit size (L × H × T, mm) | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Concrete block (hollow) | 390 × 190 × 190; 390 × 190 × 90 | Nominal “400 × 200” module; mortar joints affect coordination. |
| AAC block (autoclaved aerated concrete) | 600 × 200 × (75–200) | Thickness varies by partition/thermal need; confirm density/strength class. |
| Clay brick (modular) | ~215 × 102.5 × 65 | Many variants exist; confirm local preferred format and mortar joint assumptions. |

### Rebar, mesh & reinforcement (RC works)

| Material | Typical stock / standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Rebar lengths | 12 m (typical) | Bar lengths and bend schedules drive wastage; coordinate lap/splice and bar mark repetition. |
| Common bar diameters | 10, 12, 16, 20, 25, 32 mm | Grade and standard depend on project/spec; confirm with engineer and supplier. |
| Welded mesh sheets | 2400 × 6000 (common); other formats exist | Mesh opening sizes vary; verify structural requirements and handling constraints. |

### Metal sections & cladding support

| Material | Typical stock / standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hot-rolled sections (UB/UC/angles/channels) | Typically supplied in **12 m** lengths | Section series depend on standard (EN/GB/China); confirm availability and mill origin. |
| Cold-formed steel studs/tracks (drywall) | Stud lengths often 2.4 / 3.0 / 3.6 m (varies) | Coordinate floor-to-ceiling heights to reduce splicing and waste. |
| Aluminium extrusions (curtain wall) | Commonly supplied in **~6 m** lengths | Supplier-specific; mullion/transom cut-optimization is a major cost driver. |
| Stainless fasteners (316/316L) | Standard metric series (M4–M16 common) | For marine exposure, focus on grade + isolation to avoid galvanic corrosion. |

### Glass & glazing (typical fabrication limits)

| Material | Typical stock / limit (indicative) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Float glass “jumbo” sheets | Sizes vary; large-format fabrication is supplier-dependent | Confirm max sheet, tempering limits, IGU handling, and site access/hoisting constraints early. |
| Laminated glass build-ups | Thickness build-ups vary (e.g., 6+6, 8+8, etc.) | Typhoon impact and facade wind load will drive selection; confirm local code requirements. |

### Tiles & stone (planning module)

| Material | Common module sizes (mm) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Porcelain tiles | 300×300; 300×600; 600×600; 600×1200; 200×1200 | Coordinate setout with movement joints, drains, and threshold details. |
| Stone slabs/tiles | Supplier-driven (often cut-to-order) | For BEAM regional sourcing, confirm quarry and processing location (e.g., Fujian/Guangdong). |

### Pipes & conduits (MEP coordination)

| Material | Typical stock length | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| uPVC / HDPE pipes | Often 6 m | Diameter series varies; confirm fittings availability and pressure class. |
| EMT / GI conduits | Often 3 m / 3.75 m (varies) | Coordinate bend radii, containment, and accessible junction strategy. |
