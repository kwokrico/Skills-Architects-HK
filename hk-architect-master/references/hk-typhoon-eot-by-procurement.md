# Typhoon and Weather Delay — EOT by Procurement Route (Hong Kong)

Reference for `hk-procurement-strategy`. **CA administration:** `hk-tender-contract-administration` §12.8. **QS quantum:** `hk-cost-consultancy` §7.3.

When **Typhoon Signal No. 8 (T8) or higher** is hoisted, site work typically stops under safety regulations. Entitlement to **time** and **money** depends on procurement route and executed contract clauses.

**Disclaimer:** Clause numbers below are illustrative (e.g. SFBC delay provisions, NEC4 clause 60.1(13)). Verify the signed contract, edition, and particular conditions.

---

## 1. Traditional Procurement (Design-Bid-Build)

**Contract context:** HKIA/HKIS Standard Form (With Quantities) or Government GCC for Building Works.

### Mechanism

- Typhoon treated as **Excusable, Non-Compensable** event (often under force majeure / **inclement weather**).
- Under standard HKIA contracts (e.g. delay provisions around **exceptionally inclement weather**), T8+ is a listed **Specified Event** (verify clause numbers on project).

### AP / Architect role

- Contractor serves **written notice of delay** within contractual period (commonly **28 days** of the event — verify contract).
- **Architect / AP** assesses impact on **critical path** of master programme.
- Grant **extension of time (EOT)** where entitlement proven; **no** loss and expense for prolonged site overheads, idle plant, or scaffolding during downtime.

### Financial handling

| Element | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Time | Contractor safe from **liquidated damages** for entitled days |
| Money | **Zero** recovery for site overheads / preliminaries during stoppage |
| Risk split | Developer loses time; contractor loses operational cost |

## 2. Design and Build (D&B)

**Contract context:** ArchSD D&B Conditions, DEVB Standard D&B Form, or bespoke private D&B.

### Mechanism

- Physical site closure due to T8 recognised as **excusable delay** for construction works (similar surface outcome to traditional).
- Contractor responsible for **both design progression and site operations**.

### Structural risk trap

| Claim type | Typical employer's representative response |
|---|---|
| 3 days site halt from T8 | **3 days EOT** for construction works |
| Delay to consultant design submissions to BD during typhoon | Generally **rejected** — office/design scheduling is contractor risk |

### Verdict

- **Time:** Granted for **direct physical site disruption** only.
- **Money:** No compensation for design knock-on or administrative delays from office closures.

## 3. Management Contracting / Construction Management

**Contract context:** Bespoke trade package frameworks (`MC-HK`, `CM-HK`).

### Mechanism

- T8 during foundation phase: **Piling Works Contractor** claims EOT from **Management Contractor** (MC) or, under CM, from **Employer**.
- MC/CM must **aggregate** claims and pass to Employer.

### Interface risk (critical)

| Event | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Piling contractor granted **4-day EOT** (typhoon damage to sheet piles) | Superstructure contractor may claim **delay to site handover** |
| Handover delay classification | May be **Employer-caused delay** |

### Financial handling

| Element | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Typhoon itself | Often **non-compensable** for the affected package |
| Consequential delay to **next package** | May become **excusable AND compensable (Time + Money)** if Employer failed to provide site access on schedule |
| Developer exposure | Typhoons can **inadvertently cost large sums** if package interfaces are tightly scheduled |

### Pre-typhoon season controls

- [ ] Package handover matrix with float and responsible party
- [ ] Agreed definition of "site available" per package
- [ ] Single **EOT/Claim Register** for all packages (`hk-tender-contract-administration` §12.8)
- [ ] Scenario plan: piling EOT → superstructure notification template

## 4. Collaborative Procurement — NEC4 (Target Cost Options C and D)

**Contract context:** DEVB standard amendments for Hong Kong public works; MTR / AA NEC projects.

### Mechanism

- NEC4 does **not** rely on traditional "Force Majeure" or "Inclement Weather" labels alone.
- Typhoon assessed as **Compensation Event (CE)** under **clause 60.1(13)** (weather measurement — verify NEC4 edition and Z clauses).

### Data-driven test

To qualify as a CE for weather:

1. Event must occur, on average, **less than once every 10 years** in that **calendar month**.
2. Compare **Hong Kong Observatory** historical data (past decade) against actual storm metrics (e.g. rainfall volume, wind speed) per contract measurement rules.

### Standard August T8 (example)

| Test | Result |
|---|---|
| 1-in-10-year threshold | Typical seasonal T8 often **fails** test |
| Treatment | **Normal business risk** — absorb in programme float; **no EOT**; Completion Date unchanged |

### Catastrophic super typhoon (example)

If CE qualifies:

| Element | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Time | Completion Date / Key Dates extended |
| Money | Defined cost of managing site during delay feeds **Price for Work Done to Date (PWDD)** |
| Option C | Actual costs raise projected expenditure; overrun shared via **pain/gain** per agreed ratio |

### Early warning

- Notify and record in **Early Warning Register** even if CE threshold uncertain.
- Programme revision and CE quotation deadlines are strict (`hk-tender-contract-administration` §10).

## 5. Summary Matrix — Typhoon EOT Handling

| Procurement strategy | Time (EOT)? | Money (delay costs)? | Operational assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Traditional** | Yes, if critical path hit | No | Subjective assessment of exceptionally inclement weather; notice time-bars |
| **Design & Build** | Yes, site works only | No | Physical stoppage only; design delays excluded |
| **Management contracting / CM** | Yes; downstream risk | **Risk of yes** for next package | Package interface and site-handover claims |
| **NEC4 (target cost)** | Only if > 1-in-10-year event | Shared via pain/gain if CE qualifies | HKO 10-year meteorological comparison |
