## 1. Core Position
- Treat Practical Completion as a contractual threshold, not a ceremonial event.
- Certify PC only when the Works are fit for intended use with minor defects outstanding.
- Convert snagging into a controlled workflow with owners, deadlines, and evidence.
- During DLP, protect both quality and contractual rights through disciplined records.

---

## 2. PC (Practical Completion) in HKIA Context
Under HKIA Standard Form logic, PC is generally the point where:
1. The Works are substantially complete.
2. Remaining defects are minor and do not prevent beneficial occupation/use.
3. Testing/commissioning and statutory readiness are sufficiently advanced for handover intent.

PC implications to manage:
- Start of Defects Liability Period (DLP).
- Release or partial release mechanics for retention (subject to contract terms).
- Transfer in responsibility/risk profile as defined in the contract.
- Commencement of formal defects tracking and closeout cycle.

Architect/AP responsibilities at PC stage:
- Verify completion status against drawings, specifications, and contract deliverables.
- Confirm outstanding items are genuinely minor and listable.
- Issue PC certificate only with a structured snag schedule and deadlines.
- Record basis of certification to protect professional position.

---

## 3. Pre-PC Readiness Gate (Before Certificate Issue)
Use a formal readiness gate before recommending/issuing PC:

1. **Functional readiness**
- Core building functions operate safely and as intended.
- Major systems commissioned to usable status.

2. **Compliance and authority interface**
- No unresolved non-compliance that blocks occupation intent.
- Statutory submissions/inspections are aligned with handover strategy.

3. **Quality readiness**
- Public areas, arrival sequence, and representative typical units meet agreed quality benchmark.
- Remaining defects are local, non-critical, and rectifiable without major dismantling.

4. **Documentation readiness**
- As-built status, O&M documents, warranties/manuals at agreed minimum handover level.
- Defect recording protocol and responsibility matrix approved.

5. **Commercial/contract readiness**
- Clear statement of what is included in PC and what remains as outstanding minor works.
- DLP start date, defect notice process, and response SLA agreed with all parties.

## 4. Snagging Workflow (Luxury Finishes)
For high-end residential, hospitality, and premium common areas, use a zone-based and finish-critical workflow.

### 4.1 Snag Categories
- `A - Critical`: Safety/operation risk, water ingress, life-safety or major functional defect.
- `B - Major`: Significant visual/functional defect affecting handover quality.
- `C - Minor`: Localized cosmetic issue, touch-up, alignment, tolerance correction.
- `D - Observation`: Monitor-only item; no immediate rectification required.

### 4.2 Zone Logic
Inspect and close by zone:
- Apartment/room level.
- Typical floor common area.
- Lift lobby and vertical circulation.
- Podium/clubhouse/amenity.
- Façade-interface and external works.

### 4.3 Luxury Finish Control Points
Check systematically for:
- Stone grain continuity and book-match logic.
- Joint alignment, width consistency, and edge straightness.
- Metal trim levelness, sharpness control, and coating integrity.
- Timber veneer direction, tone consistency, and edge detailing.
- Paint finish uniformity under daytime and nighttime lighting.
- Silicone/sealant cleanliness, color match, and profile consistency.
- Sanitaryware and ironmongery installation tolerances.

## 5. De-Snagging and Verification Cycle
Closeout is a repeatable cycle, not a one-off walk.

Cycle standard:
1. Initial snag walk and issue register within 24-48 hours.
2. Contractor rectification window by category (`A`: immediate, `B`: 3-7 days, `C`: 7-14 days unless agreed otherwise).
3. De-snag inspection with photo evidence and pass/fail status.
4. Re-open item automatically if defect recurs or finish degrades.
5. Weekly dashboard review until closure rate stabilizes above target.

Evidence standard per item:
- Unique ID.
- Zone/location code.
- Category and trade owner.
- Before/after photos with date/time.
- Target date and actual close date.
- Verification by architect/authorized inspector.

## 6. DLP Management Framework
Treat DLP as active quality assurance.

### 6.1 Intake and Classification
- Log all defects notices from client/FM/end users in a single register.
- Classify by severity, trade, and recurrence pattern.
- Separate workmanship defects from wear-and-tear or operational misuse.

### 6.2 Response SLAs (Typical)
- `Emergency`: attend same day.
- `Urgent`: attend within 24-48 hours.
- `Routine`: attend within 5-10 business days.

### 6.3 Governance Rhythm
- Weekly defects coordination meeting (high-volume period).
- Fortnightly trend review by trade and location.
- Monthly summary to Employer on open/closed/overdue/repeat defects.

### 6.4 DLP Endgame
Before DLP expiry:
1. Conduct final DLP inspection and issue defects list.
2. Require closure evidence and re-inspection of repeat failures.
3. Confirm residual defects handling mechanism under contract.
4. Support final certificate/release steps per contract conditions.

---

## 7. Common Failure Modes and Controls
Failure modes:
- PC issued with unresolved major defects disguised as minor items.
- Snag list created without ownership or due dates.
- De-snag done visually without measurable acceptance criteria.
- Repeated defects not tracked as systemic trade issue.
- High-visibility luxury finishes inspected under poor or inconsistent lighting.

Controls:
- Define non-negotiable no-PC blockers (waterproofing failure, life-safety, critical MEP reliability).
- Use a standardized snag coding system and dashboard.
- Lock inspection conditions (lighting level, viewing distance, mock-up benchmark).
- Escalate repeat defects to root-cause corrective action, not repeated patchwork.

## 10. Certificate Chain (PC → Making Good Defects → Final)

Align with Contract Administrator duties in `hk-tender-contract-administration` Section 12.9 and QS final account in `hk-cost-consultancy`.

### 10.1 Sequence overview

```
Interim Certificates (construction)
        ↓
Practical Completion Certificate + snag schedule
        ↓
Defects Liability Period (snagging / de-snag)
        ↓
Certificate of Making Good Defects (defects corrected)
        ↓
Final Certificate (after final account agreed)
```

### 10.2 Practical Completion certificate
- Issue only after Section 3 readiness gate is satisfied.
- Attach **Schedule of Outstanding Minor Defects** with rectification dates.
- Confirm DLP start date and retention release mechanics per contract.
- Notify QS for valuation adjustment if contract ties payment to PC.

### 10.3 Defect lists during DLP
- Compile and issue defect lists after inspections (initial, periodic, final).
- Use defect IDs linked to snag register; track contractor response and re-inspection.
- Distinguish **defect** (contractual remedy) from **damage** or **maintenance** (may not be contractor obligation).

### 10.4 Certificate of Making Good Defects
**Purpose**: Certify contractor has made good defects notified during DLP per contract.

Pre-issue checklist:
1. Final DLP inspection completed with client/PM.
2. All contractual defects closed or accepted with written agreement.
3. Repeat-defect root causes addressed (not cosmetic re-patch only).
4. Outstanding items explicitly listed if contract permits residual settlement.
5. QS confirms no outstanding contra-charges or set-off disputes blocking certificate.

**Issue basis**: Record inspection date, defect list reference, and any agreed exceptions.

### 10.5 Final certificate
**Purpose**: Close the building contract; typically triggers final retention release subject to contract.

Pre-issue checklist:
1. Certificate of Making Good Defects issued (or equivalent contract milestone).
2. **Final account** agreed between QS and contractor (`hk-cost-consultancy`).
3. All interim certificates and adjustments reconciled.
4. Bonds/insurances released or extended per contract.
5. Client confirms no outstanding claims barred only by late notice.

**CA action**: Issue Final Certificate only when contractual prerequisites are met; maintain written record of basis.

### 10.6 Role interfaces at closeout

| Action | CA | QS | PM |
|---|---|---|---|
| Final defect inspection | Chairs / certifies | — | Attends |
| Making Good Defects cert | Issues | Confirms account items | Client sign-off |
| Final account | Issues Final Certificate after | Agrees final account | Validates for client payment |
| Client handover | Coordinates docs | — | Leads occupation transition |

**Construction sequencing:** **Fit-out lane 7** (partitions, wet-area waterproofing, 24hr flood test, tiles, ceilings) and **acceptance lane 8** (material → sectional → final) feed directly into pre-PC readiness (§3) and snag categories (§4). Distinguish contract sectional completion from BD OP. Load `hk-construction-programme` for fit-out and acceptance swimlanes.
