# Cap 123C — Building (Demolition Works) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 13 May 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. C

> Scope note: Cap 123C is the **on-site demolition safety and sequence rulebook** — not the BD plan package (that is Cap 123A reg. 8(3)–(4)), not structural construction quality (Cap 123Q), and not minor works. Use this before schematic design whenever the scheme includes strip-out, partial demolition, or full clearance of an existing building / substantial part of a building.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123C applies to the **demolition of a building or of any substantial or significant part of a building** (reg. 2). It sets pre-start duties for the Authorized Person (AP) and registered specialist contractor (demolition), public-protection hardware on street frontages, reverse-order sequencing, shoring / collapse control, supervision by technically competent persons (TCPs), and restrictions on mechanical plant and structural cutting.

---

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. When this Cap bites at schematic design (reg. 2)

| Situation | Cap 123C applies? | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| Full building demolition | **Yes** | Budget RSC (demolition category), TCP supervision, street fans/screens, reverse-order method statement |
| Substantial / significant **part** of a building (heavy A&A strip-out, floor removal, structural demolition) | **Yes** | Treat as demolition works early — not as “fit-out”; link to Cap 123A demolition plan set + stability certs |
| Cosmetic strip-out with no structural / significant fabric removal | Likely **no** | Confirm with AP; do not assume Cap 123C alone governs |

> Also lock Cap 123A demolition plans (existing fabric + surrounds survey, sequence, temporary support stages, public protection, stability reports) before massing assumes a cleared site.

### 2. Pre-commencement checklist — AP vs registered specialist contractor (reg. 3)

Nothing starts until these are done.

#### 2.1 Authorized Person must arrange (reg. 3(1))
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| Meters | Remove **all gas, electricity, water and other meters** |
| Supply cutoff | **Disconnect gas and electricity** to the building — except what is still needed for the demolition works themselves |
| Service fittings on the building | Remove fittings tied to **tramway**, **street lighting**, **electricity supply**, or other services attached to the building |

#### 2.2 Registered specialist contractor must arrange (reg. 3(2))
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| Public frontage protection | If a wall **abuts / fronts a street, service lane, or other public open area** → fans, catch platforms, and dust screens (see §3) |
| Drainage | **Seal all sewer and drainage connections** |
| Openings | **Remove all glazed sashes and doors** |

**SD takeaway:** Early survey must flag street-front walls, public lanes, attached utilities, and active drains — these drive hoarding / fan line, utility diversion programme, and strip-out logistics.

---

### 3. Street / public open-area protection geometry (reg. 3(2)(a)) — design hard numbers

Where a wall faces a street, service lane, or public open area, the RSC must erect:

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| First catch level | Fans or catch platforms along that wall at the level of the **first floor above street level** |
| Vertical intervals | Additional fans / catch platforms as needed for dust / debris / material danger, at vertical intervals of **not more than 10 m** |
| Uppermost fan | Not more than **10 m below the working level** |
| Dust control | **Dust screens covering the whole of that wall** |

**SD takeaway:** Working height and street frontage length control temporary-works cost and public interface; model fan stack early if demolition height is significant. Also show these in Cap 123A demolition public-protection drawings.

---

### 4. Collapse control & sequence (regs. 4 & 4A) — method statement drivers

#### 4.1 Shoring / safeguards (reg. 4)
- Before start, and whenever needed during works: provide **adequate shoring or other precautions** to prevent, so far as practicable, accidental collapse of:
  - any part of the building being demolished; **and**
  - any **adjoining or other building** whose collapse could endanger persons
- Exception: persons placing the shoring itself, if practicable precautions are taken for *their* safety

#### 4.2 Demolition sequence (reg. 4A) — RSC duty
1. Carry out works in the **reverse order of construction**, unless that is not appropriate; **and**
2. Any collapse or fall of a part of the building must be **designed for in the demolition plans** and **controlled** so as not to endanger any person

**SD takeaway:** Do not assume “top-down freestyle.” Adjacent buildings, party walls, shared structure, sloping ground, and retained façades force designed temporary works and may rule out reverse-order as inappropriate — document that justification in the plans.

### 5. Live site hazards that affect phasing (regs. 5–7)

| Hazard | Rule | Architect / AP note |
|---|---|---|
| Live electrics (reg. 5) | No electric cable / apparatus that could be a danger may remain charged during works (except plant used *for* demolition) | Coordinate full de-energising with utilities before structural work |
| Fire / explosion (reg. 6(a)(i)) | RSC must take all practicable steps against gas / vapour leakage or accumulation | Prior gas purge / tank / pipe risk assessment — especially industrial / restaurant / LPG histories |
| Flooding (reg. 6(a)(ii)) | Same duty against flooding from **water mains, sewers, or culverts** | Map basement / nullah / main interfaces; sealing drains (reg. 3) is not enough alone |
| External openings (reg. 6(b)) | All external openings to/from the building must be protected to prevent danger to persons on the works | Site access / exit strategy is a safety design issue |
| Debris shutes (reg. 7) | If provided, situate and construct to avoid danger to workers and the public so far as practicable | Locate chute drops away from street crossings and neighbour openings |

### 6. Supervision structure (regs. 8–9) — team & plant constraints

#### 6.1 Continuous TCP ownership (reg. 8)
| Rule | Practical effect |
|---|---|
| Demolition + incidental works under **personal supervision** of a TCP appointed by the RSC | No anonymous site control — name a competent TCP |
| Two or more RSCs on the same works | Each appoints TCPs as appropriate; **no operation** until contractors have **consulted and agreed method and timing** |
| Site notice | Specified-form notice with TCP name(s) posted in a **prominent position** on site |

#### 6.2 Operations that demand elevated supervision (reg. 9(2))
These may only be done under **immediate TCP supervision**, or by **experienced demolition workmen under TCP supervision**:

| Crit ops | Why it matters at SD |
|---|---|
| (a) Actual demolition of **framework**, or any **floor / wall / roof / staircase** — unless no foreseeable collapse risk that endangers workers | Default: almost all structural strip-out is controlled |
| (b) Any part with a **special risk of collapse** (of that part or another part) | Party walls, deep beams, transfer structures, unstable façades |
| (c) Cutting / breaking / dismantling **RC, steelwork, or ironwork** forming part of the structure | Hot works / diamond cutting / torching need method + TCP |
| (d) Use of any **powered mechanical plant or equipment** in actual demolition | Excavators, smashers, etc. are regulated ops — not optional extras |

#### 6.3 Mechanical plant operator bars (reg. 9(3))
Operator of powered mechanical plant used in demolition must:
- have **≥ 3 years** experience operating **that particular plant/equipment**;
- meet any further BA-specified training / knowledge / skills / experience; **and**
- operate under **immediate TCP supervision**

**SD takeaway:** If programme or cost assumes long-reach / high-reach / crushers on constrained urban sites, confirm plant feasibility, neighbour vibration/risk, and TCP coverage — Cap 123C does not treat mech demo as a free default.

---

### 7. Debris, plant loads & temporary enclosure (reg. 10)

| Ban | Rule |
|---|---|
| Floor / roof overload by debris | No floor, roof, or other part shall be so loaded with debris/materials as to be unsafe or liable to become unsafe |
| Debris against edges | No debris/materials may accumulate against any **fence, hoarding, or wall** so as to make it unsafe or liable to become unsafe |
| Plant overload | No floor / roof / part shall be so overloaded with **plant or equipment** as to be unsafe or liable to become unsafe |

**SD takeaway:** Staging floors, debris stockpile zones, and plant sitting positions must be designed with load paths — especially for progressive floor-by-floor demo and street-edge hoardings.

---

### 8. Structural cutting & framed buildings (regs. 11–12)

| Operation | Precaution |
|---|---|
| Cutting / releasing / dismantling steelwork or ironwork (reg. 11) | Prevent danger from **sudden twist, spring, or collapse**, so far as practicable |
| Removing any part of framing from a framed / partially framed building (reg. 12) | All practicable precautions against **collapse of the structure** |

**SD takeaway:** Steel / RC framed buildings need an engineered dismantling sequence in the Cap 123A demolition plans; “cut and hope” fails both Cap 123C and Cap 123A plan requirements.

---

### 9. Offences that signal real enforcement weight (reg. 13) — why SD cannot freestyle

| Who / what | Liability signal |
|---|---|
| AP breach of reg. 3(1) (meters / disconnect / service fittings) | Fine at **level 4** |
| RSC breach of reg. 3(2) (fans / screens / sealed drains / glazing) | Fine at **level 4** |
| RSC breach of reg. 6(a) (fire/flood worker danger) or reg. 8(2) (multi-contractor sync) | Fine at **level 6** + up to **2 years** imprisonment |
| Breach of shoring, sequence, electrics, openings, shutes, TCP supervision / notice, critical ops / plant operator rules, overload, steel cutting, framing (regs. 4(1), 4A, 5, 6(b), 7, 8(1)/(3), 9(1)/(3), 10–12) | RSC **and any person directly concerned** — fine at **level 6** + up to **2 years** |

### 10. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing a cleared-site massing or A&A strip-out assumption:

1. **Confirm Cap 123C applies** — full demo or substantial/significant part (reg. 2).
2. **Appoint pathway** — AP for demo works + registered specialist contractor (demolition); TCPs named on site before / with start.
3. **Survey streets & public open areas** — first-floor fans + ≤10 m fan stack + full-wall dust screens (reg. 3(2)(a)).
4. **Utility kill list** — meters, gas/electric disconnect, attached service fittings, sealed sewers/drains, glazed sashes/doors removal (reg. 3).
5. **Adjoining building / party-wall risk** — shoring & collapse design before excavation/new footings rely on the cleared plot (reg. 4).
6. **Sequence principle** — reverse order of construction unless justified; any intentional fall must be **designed and controlled** on the demo plans (reg. 4A).
7. **Frame type** — steel / RC / framed = special cutting & framing precautions + elevated TCP ops (regs. 9, 11–12).
8. **Plant strategy** — if mechanical demolition is assumed, budget ≥3-year plant-experienced operators under immediate TCP (reg. 9(3)).
9. **Debris & plant load management** — no floor/hoarding overload; design staging & chute locations (regs. 7, 10).
10. **Cross-link Cap 123A** — demolition plan package, stage-by-stage temporary support, public protection drawings, and stability reports for demo building + affected assets; Cap 123C executes what those plans design.
