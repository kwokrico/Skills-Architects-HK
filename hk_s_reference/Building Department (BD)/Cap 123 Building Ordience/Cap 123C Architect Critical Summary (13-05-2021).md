# Cap 123C — Building (Demolition Works) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 13 May 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. C

> Scope note: Cap 123C is the **on-site demolition safety, sequence, supervision and public-protection statute**. It does **not** prescribe the BD drawing package (that is Cap 123A reg. 8(3)–(4)), structural performance criteria (Cap 123Q), or minor-works routes (Cap 123N). Cap 123C is short (regs. 1–13) but every duty is operational — breach of most provisions is a **level 6 fine + up to 2 years’ imprisonment**.  
> Read with: Cap 123A demolition plans + stability reports; **Code of Practice for Demolition of Buildings 2004** (2016/2023 amendments); PNAP APP-21, APP-71 / PNRC-6, APP-75 / PNRC-4.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123C applies to the **demolition of a building or of any substantial or significant part of a building** (reg. 2). It allocates pre-start duties between the Authorized Person (AP) and the registered specialist contractor (demolition) (RSC), mandates street-front fans / catch platforms / dust screens with hard geometry, requires reverse-order sequencing and designed collapse control, and controls TCP supervision, mechanical plant operators, debris/plant loading, and steel / framing dismantling.

At schematic design, treat Cap 123C as the reason you cannot assume a “cleared site on day one.” Street setback vs height, adjoining buildings, utilities, glazing strip-out, fan stacks, plant-on-slab vs ground methods, and multi-contractor interfaces must be locked before massing and programme are frozen.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Application threshold — what “substantial / significant part” means for SD (reg. 2)

| Situation | Cap 123C applies? | What to lock at schematic |
|---|---|---|
| Full building demolition | **Yes** | RSC (Demolition), TCP regime, street fans/screens, reverse-order method, Cap 123A demo plan set |
| Partial demolition of a **substantial / significant part** (floors removed, structural walls/frames cut, roof/stair demolition, large A&A strip-out) | **Yes** | Same pathway — do **not** treat as fit-out or ordinary A&A labour |
| Cosmetic strip-out / non-structural partitions only | Usually **no** | Confirm with AP; Cap 123C alone may not govern, but Cap 123 / Cap 123A may still apply |
| Basements, tanks, silos, industrial plant, piers demolished as part of “building” works | **Yes** if within BO demolition of a building / significant part | CoP Demolition expands appraisal; Cap 123C still supplies the safety duties |

**SD gate:** If the scheme’s cost, programme, or foundation design assumes the existing fabric is gone, Cap 123C + Cap 123A demo package are on the critical path — not “contractor means-and-methods only.”

### 2. Pre-commencement duties — split of ownership (reg. 3)

Nothing may start until **both** AP and RSC have discharged their pre-start lists. These are separate offence tracks (reg. 13(1)–(2)).

#### 2.1 Authorized Person — before demolition starts (reg. 3(1))

| Duty | Exact requirement | Schematic / programme implication |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Meters** | Cause **all** gas, electricity, water **and other** meters to be removed from the building | Early utility survey: who owns each meter; lead time for company removal; temporary meters for site power |
| **(a) Supply disconnect** | Cause gas **and** electricity supply to the building to be disconnected — **except** what may still be used in the demolition works | Cap 123C allows temporary construction power/gas for demo itself; CoP Demolition typically keeps weather-proofed temp electricity (lighting/plant), water (dust spray), telecom (security) — design temporary supply locations |
| **(b) Attached service fittings** | Cause removal of any fitting attached to the building in connexion with **tramway**, **street lighting**, **electricity supply**, or **other service** | Street-light brackets, overhead line attachments, tramway fittings, utility risers on façades — survey elevations facing streets early |

**Architect note:** Reg. 3(1) is an **AP personal duty**. Programme cannot say “contractor to arrange utilities” and leave AP out. At SD, put utility termination / meter removal on the AP-led lead-in programme with named utility companies.

#### 2.2 Registered specialist contractor — before demolition starts (reg. 3(2))

| Duty | Exact requirement | Schematic / programme implication |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Public-front protection** | Where a wall **abuts or fronts** a street, **service lane**, or other open area **accessible to the public** → fans / catch platforms + dust screens (full geometry in §3) | Any wall facing public realm triggers the stack — including rear lanes and public open areas, not only main roads |
| **(b) Drainage seal** | **Seal all** sewer and drainage connections | Prevents sewage backup / odour / flooding path into the shell; coordinate with reg. 6(a)(ii) flooding duty |
| **(c) Glazing / doors** | **Remove all glazed sashes and doors** from the building | Pre-demo soft strip; glass falls are a classic public/worker hazard — schedule before structural works |

**SD takeaway:** Soft-strip package (meters, glazing, doors, drain sealing, service fittings) is a **statutory precondition**, not optional early works. Show it on the Cap 123A sequence drawings.

### 3. Street / public open-area protection — hard geometry (reg. 3(2)(a))

Triggered when a wall of the building **abuts or fronts upon** a street, service lane, or other open area accessible to the public. All three layers below are mandatory together.

#### 3.1 Catch / fan stack

| Layer | Rule | Design reading |
|---|---|---|
| **First level** | Erect fans or catch platforms along that wall at the level of the **first floor of the building above the level of the street** | First catch line is tied to **building first floor**, not an arbitrary scaffold lift |
| **Additional levels** | Erect further fans / catch platforms at such other floors as necessary to prevent **nuisance from dust** or **danger from debris or materials** | Density of fan levels is risk-driven, but capped by the spacing rules below |
| **Vertical spacing** | Fans / catch platforms at vertical intervals of **not more than 10 m** | Max 10 m between catch levels |
| **Uppermost fan** | Uppermost fan / catch platform **not more than 10 m below the working level** | As working floor rises/falls, the top catch must stay within 10 m of work — temporary-works design must track the sequence |
| **Dust screens** | Erect dust screens to **cover the whole of the wall** so as to prevent any nuisance from dust | Full-height wall coverage — not patch screens at work zones only |

#### 3.2 What this forces at schematic design

| Design question | Why Cap 123C cares |
|---|---|
| How tall is the building / part being demolished? | Fan count ≈ height ÷ ≤10 m, plus first-floor starter |
| Which façades face street / lane / public open area? | Each such wall gets the full treatment |
| Is clear space (building line → lot boundary) enough for walkway / catch platform width? | Cap 123C sets **fans + screens**; CoP Demolition / APP-75 add **hoarding / covered walkway** when clear space < height (or < ½ height → walkway + catch platform). Cap 123C alone does not replace those street occupation rules |
| Will progressive top-down work keep the uppermost fan ≤10 m below working level? | Sequence drawings must update temporary works stage-by-stage |
| Partial demolition of upper floors only? | Still check whether remaining street-front walls need screens/fans during works |

**Show on Cap 123A public-protection drawings:** fan/catch platform levels, dust screens covering whole wall, relationship to street level and working floors — Cap 123C is what BA/FSD/public safety inspectors will check on site.

### 4. Shoring and collapse prevention (reg. 4)

#### 4.1 Core duty (reg. 4(1))

**Before** commencing demolition, **and** at such times during the works as may be necessary:

- Take such precautions as may be necessary, by **adequate shoring or otherwise**, to prevent so far as practicable the **accidental collapse** of:
  1. any part of the **building being demolished**; **or**
  2. any **adjoining or other building**, the collapse of which may endanger any person.

| Trigger at SD | Action |
|---|---|
| Party walls / shared structure | Assume temporary support + neighbour stability report (Cap 123A) |
| Truncating continuous frames | Temporary continuity / bracing before cut |
| Building that retains adjacent ground / slope | RGE + shoring / ground support — Cap 123C “or otherwise” includes geo precautions |
| Dilapidated adjoining buildings | Pre-strengthen / shore **before** vibration / plant starts |
| Cantilever canopies / balconies over street | Support or cut-and-lift before removing anchorage (CoP fleshes method; Cap 123C requires collapse prevention) |

#### 4.2 Shoring-crew exception (reg. 4(2))

Reg. 4(1) does **not** apply to a person actually placing shoring / safeguards **if** practicable precautions are taken to ensure **that person’s** safety. This is a narrow site-safety carve-out — it does **not** exempt the project from providing shoring.

**SD takeaway:** Adjoining-building risk and party-wall strategy belong in the concept risk register. Do not freeze new foundations that rely on a neighbour’s wall until Cap 123C shoring + Cap 123A stability reports are feasible.

### 5. Sequence of demolition works (reg. 4A) — RSC duty with plan linkage

The RSC appointed for demolition works **must ensure** works are carried out:

| Limb | Requirement | Schematic reading |
|---|---|---|
| **(a)** | In the **reverse order of construction** of the building to be demolished, **unless it is not appropriate** | Default = top-down, last-built-first-removed. If bottom-up, cut-and-lift, or mechanical top-down with early GF removal is proposed, the plans must justify why reverse order is “not appropriate” |
| **(b)** | So that any **collapse or fall** of a part of the building is **designed for in the plans for demolition works** and **controlled** so as not to endanger any person | Intentional falls (e.g. designed drop of a bay) are only lawful if shown on approved demo plans and controlled — freestyle “let it fall” is an offence |

**Link to Cap 123A:** Reg. 4A(b) expressly ties Cap 123C site practice to the **demolition plans**. Cap 123A reg. 8(3)–(4) is where those plans live:

- Existing construction & slopes; survey of surrounds  
- Sequence / plant / procedures  
- Temporary support **stage-by-stage**  
- Public protection (dust screens, catch fans, hoardings, covered walkways)  
- Support to affected neighbours  
- Stability reports for demo building + affected assets; calcs proving plant will not damage neighbours  

**SD takeaway:** Method statement is a **design deliverable**, not a post-tender afterthought. Massing that assumes instant clearance without a reverse-order (or justified alternative) narrative will fail BD and Cap 123C together.

### 6. Live electrics during works (reg. 5)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Ban | No electric cable or other apparatus that is liable to be a source of danger may **remain electrically charged** at any time during the works |
| Exception | Cable / apparatus **used in the demolition works** themselves |

**Distinct from reg. 3(1):** Reg. 3 is pre-start disconnect of building supply. Reg. 5 is a **continuous** duty during works — stray live feeds, neighbour-shared risers, temporary site wiring that becomes dangerous, abandoned switchgear, etc.

**SD / AP note:** Confirm whether any services serving adjoining occupied buildings run through the demo building. Cap 123C forbids leaving dangerous charges in place; utility diversion design may be needed before structural works can start.

### 7. Fire / explosion, flooding, and external openings (reg. 6)

Applies **at all times** during demolition works.

#### 7.1 RSC duty to persons employed (reg. 6(a))

| Risk | Duty |
|---|---|
| **(i) Fire / explosion** | Take **all practicable steps** to prevent danger to persons employed from fire or explosion through **leakage or accumulation of gas or vapour** |
| **(ii) Flooding** | Same duty against flooding from **water mains, sewers or culverts** |

| Typical SD flag | Why |
|---|---|
| Former restaurant / LPG / industrial / DG use | Gas-free / purge / tank removal before hot works |
| Basement near mains / nullah / culvert | Flood risk during drain sealing or main damage |
| Non-ventilated light wells / enclosed voids | Gas accumulation (CoP survey item; Cap 123C supplies the duty) |
| Sealed drains (reg. 3(2)(b)) alone | Not enough — reg. 6(a)(ii) is ongoing operational vigilance |

**Enforcement weight:** Reg. 6(a) breach by RSC → fine at **level 6 + up to 2 years** (reg. 13(3)) — one of the heaviest Cap 123C offences.

#### 7.2 External openings (reg. 6(b))

All external openings **to or from** the building shall be so protected as to prevent danger to persons employed in the demolition works.

| Opening type | Design implication |
|---|---|
| Street doors / shopfronts after glazing removed | Temporary barriers / screens — openings are hazard edges |
| Light-well / courtyard openings | Fall / falling-object protection for workers |
| Roof / floor edges created mid-sequence | Protect as works create new openings |
| Access/egress for workers | Safe protected route in and out of the shell |

### 8. Debris shutes (reg. 7)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| When | **Where shutes are provided** for removal of materials or debris (not mandatory to use shutes, but if used…) |
| How | Situated and constructed to avoid, so far as practicable, danger to persons employed **or any other person** (public / neighbours) |

**SD implications:**

| Decision | Cap 123C angle |
|---|---|
| Internal staircase / shaft used as chute | Still a “shute” in substance — locate drop zones away from public and worker paths |
| External chute on street façade | Interface with fans / dust screens / covered walkway; discharge point is a public-danger zone |
| No chute — floor-by-floor stockpile then crane | Reg. 7 silent, but reg. 10 overload rules still bite |

Show chute positions (or “no chute / alternative routing”) on Cap 123A debris-handling drawings.

### 9. Technically competent person (TCP) supervision (reg. 8)

#### 9.1 Personal supervision of all demolition + incidental works (reg. 8(1))

- The demolition **and all works incidental thereto** shall be specifically placed under the **personal supervision** of a TCP appointed by the RSC (demolition).
- “Incidental” catches soft strip, temporary works erection, debris handling, plant mobilisation — not only the smash.

#### 9.2 Two or more RSCs on the same demolition (reg. 8(2))

Each such contractor must:

| Duty | Effect |
|---|---|
| **(a)** Appoint such number of TCPs as appropriate to supervise personally the demolition works | Multiple named TCPs if multiple RSCs |
| **(b)** Ensure his workmen do **not** carry out an operation until he has **consulted and agreed** with the other contractors as to the **method** and the **time** of that operation | No unilateral ops — written interface agreement on method + timing |

**SD / procurement implication:** Split packages (e.g. asbestos abatement specialist + demolition RSC, or façade retention RSC + main demo RSC) create a **statutory coordination hold point**. Programme must show agreed sequencing between contractors — reg. 8(2) breach is level 6 + 2 years (reg. 13(3)).

#### 9.3 Site notice (reg. 8(3))

A notice in the **specified form** naming the TCP(s) appointed under reg. 8(1) must be posted in a **prominent position** on the demolition site.

**SD takeaway:** Supervision plan / TCP nomination is a start-on-site gate (also Cap 123A / TMSS territory) — budget competent demolition TCPs early.

### 10. Operations that may only be done under elevated supervision (reg. 9)

#### 10.1 Supervision standard (reg. 9(1))

Specified operations may be carried out **only**:

| Option | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **(a)** Under the **immediate** supervision of a TCP appointed by the RSC; **or** | TCP physically directing the op |
| **(b)** By workmen **experienced in demolition works** and under the supervision of such a TCP | Experienced crew + TCP still supervising (not unsupervised “experienced” labour alone) |

#### 10.2 The four critical operation classes (reg. 9(2))

| Class | Operation | When the elevated rule drops away | SD reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a)** | Actual demolition of the **framework** of a building, or of any **floor, wall, roof or staircase** | Only if there is **no** risk of collapse of any part of a building (in the course of or as a result of the works) that would endanger persons employed — other than a risk that could not reasonably have been foreseen | Default for almost all structural strip-out: elevated supervision. The carve-out is narrow |
| **(b)** | Actual demolition of any part where there is a **special risk of collapse** (of that part **or** any other part) endangering persons employed | No carve-out — always elevated | Party walls, transfer structures, unstable façades, fire-damaged members, truncated continuous frames |
| **(c)** | **Cutting, breaking up or dismantling** of **reinforced concrete, steelwork or ironwork** forming part of the structure | Always elevated | Hot works, diamond sawing, torching, breaker work on structural members |
| **(d)** | Use of any **powered mechanical plant or equipment** in actual demolition work | Always elevated | Excavators, high-reach, crushers, pulverisers — not “optional extras” |

#### 10.3 Mechanical plant operator bars (reg. 9(3))

The operator of powered mechanical plant / equipment used in demolition work shall:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **(a)** Experience | At least **3 years** experience operating **the particular plant or equipment** (not “3 years on site” generally) |
| **(b)** BA standards | Possess such other training, knowledge, skills and experience as the **Building Authority may specify from time to time** |
| **(c)** Supervision | Operate under the **immediate** supervision of a TCP appointed by the RSC |

**SD takeaway:** If cost plan / programme assumes long-reach or slab-mounted plant, confirm: (i) plant type named in Cap 123A mechanical plant plan; (ii) floor capacity vs reg. 10; (iii) operator market with ≥3 years on *that* plant; (iv) TCP immediate cover. Cap 123C makes unqualified plant use a criminal exposure for RSC and persons directly concerned (reg. 13(4)).

### 11. Floors, roofs, fences, hoardings — overload bans (reg. 10)

Three separate bans — all absolute (“shall not”):

| Limb | Ban | Typical failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| **(1)** | No floor, roof or other part being demolished shall be so overloaded with **debris or materials** as to render it unsafe or liable to become unsafe | Stockpiling debris on upper floors beyond original design load; progressive floor failure |
| **(2)** | No debris / materials shall accumulate against any **fence, hoarding or wall** so as to render it unsafe or liable to become unsafe | Debris piled against street hoarding → public collapse; debris against party wall → neighbour distress |
| **(3)** | No floor, roof or other part shall be so overloaded with any **plant or equipment** as to render it unsafe or liable to become unsafe | Excavator / breaker on suspended slab without propping / load check |

**SD implications:**

| Decision | What to lock |
|---|---|
| Plant-on-slab vs ground-based demolition | Reg. 10(3) + Cap 123A stability calcs for plant |
| Debris holding zones | Permissible temporary accumulation per floor (CoP / Cap 123A debris plan); clear limits in notes |
| Hoarding line | Keep debris clear of fence/hoarding/wall — design holding bays **inside** the site, not against the boundary enclosure |
| Early GF / cockloft removal for vehicle access | May be justified under reg. 4A “not appropriate” reverse order — but must be on plans with temporary stability |

### 12. Cutting steelwork / ironwork (reg. 11)

Where steelwork or ironwork is being **cut, released or dismantled** during demolition:

- Precautions shall be taken, so far as practicable, to prevent any danger from a **sudden twist, spring or collapse**.

| Member type | Cap 123C risk | Design response |
|---|---|---|
| Beams in bending / continuous steel | Stored energy → spring when cut | Sequence cuts; temporary props; restrain before release |
| Tension members / hangers | Sudden collapse of supported floors | De-tension procedure on plans |
| Portal / braced frames | Frame instability when one member released | Temporary bracing before member removal (links to reg. 12) |
| Composite RC + steel | Unpredictable residual forces | Treat as both reg. 9(2)(c) elevated op and reg. 11 precaution |

Cross-link: reg. 9(2)(c) already requires elevated TCP supervision for this work; reg. 11 adds the **physical** sudden-release hazard.

---

### 13. Removing framing from framed / partially framed buildings (reg. 12)

Where any part of the **framing** is being removed from a framed or partially framed building:

- All practicable precautions shall be taken to prevent danger from **collapse of the structure**.

| Building type | Implication |
|---|---|
| Full steel / RC frame | Frame stability is a system property — removing one bay can collapse another |
| Partially framed (e.g. frame + loadbearing walls) | Hybrid behaviour — identify which walls are still loadbearing before removing frame members |
| Transfer frames / hanging structures | Highest risk — must appear as unconventional elements on Cap 123A demo plans |

**SD takeaway:** Regs. 11–12 together mean framed buildings need an **engineered dismantling sequence** on the demolition plans. “Floor-by-floor breaker” without frame analysis fails Cap 123C even if reverse-order is claimed.

### 14. Offences and penalties (reg. 13) — who is exposed

| Provision breached | Who is guilty | Max penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Reg. **3(1)** (meters / disconnect / service fittings) | **Authorized Person** | Fine at **level 4** |
| Reg. **3(2)** (fans / screens / sealed drains / glazing) | **RSC** | Fine at **level 4** |
| Reg. **6(a)** (fire/flood worker danger) or **8(2)** (multi-RSC sync) | **RSC** | Fine at **level 6** + imprisonment **2 years** |
| Regs. **4(1), 4A, 5, 6(b), 7, 8(1) or (3), 9(1) or (3), 10, 11, 12** | **RSC** appointed in respect of the works **and any person directly concerned** with the demolition works | Fine at **level 6** + imprisonment **2 years** |

**Architect / consultant note:** “Person directly concerned” under reg. 13(4) is broad. Cap 123C is not “contractor-only.” AP-led design that ignores designed collapse (4A), shoring (4), or plant overload (10) can put the project team in the exposure zone. At SD, document constraints so the approved plans carry the Cap 123C duties.

### 15. Cap 123C → Cap 123A / CoP map (what the statute forces onto drawings)

Cap 123C does not list drawing scales; it **requires behaviours that only work if Cap 123A plans exist**:

| Cap 123C duty | Where it must appear / be proven |
|---|---|
| Fans / catch platforms / dust screens (reg. 3(2)(a)) | Cap 123A public-protection drawings; CoP §§3.2–3.4 geometry/loads |
| Shoring / adjoining collapse prevention (reg. 4) | Cap 123A temporary support + neighbour stability report |
| Reverse order / designed fall (reg. 4A) | Cap 123A sequence / procedure plan |
| Utilities disconnect (regs. 3(1), 5) | Cap 123A / CoP utility termination schedule |
| Drain seal + flood risk (regs. 3(2)(b), 6(a)(ii)) | Drainage notes; temp drainage strategy |
| Shutes (reg. 7) | Debris handling plan |
| TCP / multi-RSC (reg. 8) | Supervision plan / site notice; interface method statements |
| Crit ops + plant (reg. 9) | Mechanical plant plan; operator competency; TCP cover |
| No overload (reg. 10) | Debris accumulation limits; plant load calcs on slabs |
| Steel / framing (regs. 11–12) | Member-by-member dismantling sequence for framed buildings |

### 16. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing cleared-site massing, A&A strip-out, or foundation sequence:

1. **Confirm application** — full demo or substantial/significant part (reg. 2); if yes, Cap 123C + Cap 123A demo package are mandatory path items.
2. **Appoint pathway** — AP for demolition works + RSC (Demolition); name TCP(s); if multiple RSCs, lock method+time interface (reg. 8).
3. **Map every public-facing wall** — street, service lane, public open area → first-floor fans + ≤10 m fan stack + uppermost ≤10 m below working level + full-wall dust screens (reg. 3(2)(a)).
4. **Overlay CoP street occupation** — clear space vs height → hoarding / covered walkway / catch platform (Cap 123C fans alone are not the full public-protection design).
5. **Utility kill list (AP-owned)** — meters removed; gas & electricity disconnected (except demo use); tramway / street-light / electricity / other attached fittings removed (reg. 3(1)); confirm no live dangerous cables remain during works (reg. 5).
6. **Soft strip (RSC-owned)** — seal all sewer/drainage connections; remove all glazed sashes and doors (reg. 3(2)(b)–(c)).
7. **Adjoining / party-wall / slope risk** — shoring or other precautions before start and as needed (reg. 4); Cap 123A neighbour stability report.
8. **Sequence principle** — reverse order of construction unless justified as inappropriate; any intentional collapse/fall designed on plans and controlled (reg. 4A).
9. **Hazard uses** — gas/vapour fire-explosion and mains/sewer/culvert flood risks assessed continuously (reg. 6(a)); protect all external openings (reg. 6(b)).
10. **Debris strategy** — chute location/construction if used (reg. 7); no floor/roof overload by debris or plant; no debris against fence/hoarding/wall (reg. 10).
11. **Frame / RC / steel** — elevated TCP ops for framework/floors/walls/roofs/stairs, special collapse risk, RC/steel/iron cutting, and powered plant (reg. 9); sudden-twist and framing-collapse precautions (regs. 11–12).
12. **Mechanical plant** — operator ≥3 years on **that** plant + BA-specified competency + immediate TCP (reg. 9(3)); plant-on-slab proven safe under reg. 10(3).
13. **Cap 123A package complete** — existing fabric & surrounds, stage-by-stage temporary support, public protection, debris system, stability reports — Cap 123C executes what those plans design.
14. **Completion path** — Cap 123A: AP/RSE/RGE certify within **14 days** that demo followed approved plans; remaining structures safe; affected land/street adequate margin of safety.
