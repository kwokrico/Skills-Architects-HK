# Cap 123J — Building (Ventilating Systems) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 13 May 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. J

> Scope note: Cap 123J is the **fire-services-facing rulebook for ducted inter-compartment ventilating systems** — intake siting, duct materials, fire dampers at compartment penetrations, filter combustibility, and annual RSC inspection. It is **not** the full FS Code / FRC package, not Cap 123F (planning), not Cap 132 scheduled-premises HVAC rules, and not a substitute for FS Notes on means of escape or smoke control. Use this at SD when any scheme uses ducts/trunks crossing fire compartments.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123J applies to every **ventilating system that uses ducting or trunking passing through a wall, floor or ceiling from one compartment of a building to another** (reg. 3(1)). It does **not** apply to ventilating systems in buildings that are **scheduled premises** under the Public Health and Municipal Services Ordinance (Cap. 132) (reg. 3(2)).

---

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. When Cap 123J bites at schematic design (reg. 3)

| Situation | Cap 123J applies? | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| Duct / trunk crosses wall, floor or ceiling **between compartments** | **Yes** | Lock intake location, duct material, fire damper + FR rating at every penetration, cleaning access, filter type |
| Wholly within one compartment (no inter-compartment duct) | **No** (by reg. 3(1)) | Still satisfy FS Code / Cap 123 elsewhere; Cap 123J threshold not triggered |
| Building is Cap 132 **scheduled premises** | **Excluded** (reg. 3(2)) | Different regime — do not assume Cap 123J alone governs |
| Pre-23 Oct 1964 system left untouched | Reg. 4 deferred until alter / remove / FSD fire-hazard finding (reg. 7) | A&A that “touches” the system can suddenly bring full reg. 4 compliance |

**SD takeaway:** Compartment strategy = duct strategy. Every fire compartment boundary the MVAC crosses is a Cap 123J damper + FR continuity point.

### 2. Hard design rules — intakes, access, materials (reg. 4(1))

#### 2.1 Air intakes — siting & screens (reg. 4(1)(c)–(d))

| Rule | Hard number / limit | Architect action at SD |
|---|---|---|
| No intake where Director of Fire Services considers a **fire hazard** | Discretionary FSD opinion | Keep intakes away from refuse rooms, kitchens exhaust confluence, LPG/stores, transformer yards, carpark exhaust, hot work / ignition sources |
| No intake where **waste or rubbish is likely to accumulate** | Site judgment | Avoid lane refuse corridors, loading-bay trash zones, dead corners at grade |
| Intake opening screen | Corrosion-resistant mesh **≤ 12 mm** | Specify mesh early; coordinate with insect / weather louvre composition |

#### 2.2 Access for inspection & measurement (reg. 4(1)(a)–(b))

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Moving parts | Securely **fenced** |
| Whole system | Accessible for inspection |
| Fan spindle | Guard removable; tachometer can be applied |
| Every air intake **and** exhaust | Accessible for **measurement** |

**SD takeaway:** Do not bury fans, intakes, or exhausts behind sealed soffits / unopenable plant screens without a maintenance route. Plant room / roof access paths are a Cap 123J compliance item, not only an O&M courtesy.

#### 2.3 Duct construction & cleaning (reg. 4(1)(e)(i)–(ii), (f))

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Material | Wholly **non-combustible**, strength/durability **not less than galvanized sheet-iron or steel** |
| Cleaning | Accessible for cleaning **throughout its entire length** |
| One building only | **No duct shall serve more than one building** |

**SD takeaway:** Linked towers / podium–tower shared MVAC that pushes a single duct run across legally separate buildings is forbidden under Cap 123J. Split systems or stop-and-restart at the building boundary. Cleaning access (doors / removable panels / straighteners for length) must fit the architecture — long buried ducts with no access fail reg. 4(1)(e)(ii).

#### 2.4 Fire dampers at every compartment penetration (reg. 4(1)(e)(iv)) — critical geometry

At the point where a duct passes through any **floor, wall or ceiling**:

| Requirement | Spec |
|---|---|
| Damper | Fitted at the penetration |
| Operation | **Fusible links** of a type **approved by the Director** (FSD) |
| Design temperature | Operate up to **69°C** |
| Fire resistance | Constructed or protected to resist fire for **not less than** the FR period of the floor / wall / ceiling penetrated |

**SD takeaway:** Compartment FR rating (e.g. 1-hr / 2-hr walls and floors) drives damper FR. Coordinate early with fire compartment drawings — damper count and locations scale with every shaft and horizontal duct crossing.

#### 2.5 Filters & precipitators (reg. 4(1)(g)–(h))

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Every filter | Wholly **non-combustible** — **steel wool prohibited** |
| Electrostatic filter / precipitator | Must be a type **approved by the Director** |

### 3. FSD exemption path (reg. 4(2))

Director of Fire Services may, by written notice and subject to conditions, exempt a system from all or any of reg. 4(1) if an **application in writing** is made and the Director thinks fit.

**SD takeaway:** Do not design on the assumption of exemption. Treat exemption as a last-resort variance with conditions — base the scheme on full reg. 4 compliance.

---

### 4. Owner / O&M duties that affect handover design (regs. 5 & 5A)

| Duty | Interval / timing | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Keep system in safe and efficient working order | Continuous | Building owner |
| Inspect every **damper, filter and precipitator** | Not exceeding **12 months** | Registered specialist contractor (appropriate category) |
| Certificate after inspection | Within **14 days**; copy to Director | RSC → instructing person + FSD |

Certificate must state: address of inspection; whether dampers / filters / precipitators are in safe and efficient working order; RSC name and address.

**False certificate / failure to issue** → offence (fine at level 1 + up to 6 months imprisonment under reg. 5A(3)).

**SD takeaway:** Provide safe, repeatable access to every damper, filter, and precipitator for annual RSC inspection — not only construction commissioning access.

---

### 5. Existing / heritage systems (reg. 7)

Systems installed **before 23 October 1964** need **not** comply with reg. 4 until either:

1. The system is **removed or altered in any way**; or  
2. The Director opines it constitutes a **fire hazard**

**SD takeaway:** On A&A / adaptive reuse, any meaningful alteration to a grandfathered ducted system can trigger full modern Cap 123J compliance (materials, dampers, intakes, access). Survey early and decide: keep untouched vs redesign to current rules.

---

### 6. Enforcement touchpoints (regs. 5–6)

| Actor | Power / duty |
|---|---|
| FSD authorized officer | May test any Cap 123J system to confirm safe and efficient working order (reg. 6) |
| Owner | Offence for failure to maintain / annual inspect (reg. 5(2) — fine at level 1) |

Enforcing authority for this Cap is the **Director of Fire Services**, not BD plan checking alone — align FS submission / FRC narrative with Cap 123J duct and damper locations.

### 7. Schematic-design checklist (print this)

- [ ] Confirm scheme uses inter-compartment ducts/trunks → Cap 123J **on**
- [ ] Confirm building is **not** Cap 132 scheduled premises (else different rules)
- [ ] Intake clear of fire hazard / refuse accumulation; mesh **≤ 12 mm**
- [ ] Exhaust and intake reachable for measurement; fans reachable for tachometer
- [ ] All ducts non-combustible (≥ GI / steel durability); cleaning access full length
- [ ] **No shared duct across more than one building**
- [ ] Fire damper + FSD-approved fusible link (**69°C**) at **every** floor / wall / ceiling penetration; FR ≥ penetrated element
- [ ] Filters non-combustible (no steel wool); electrostatic types FSD-approved
- [ ] Annual RSC inspection access designed in for dampers / filters / precipitators
- [ ] If pre-1964 system: alteration strategy locked before SD freezes MVAC

| Need | Instrument |
