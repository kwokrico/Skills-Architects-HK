# Cap 123A — Building (Administration) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 30 June 2024 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. A

> Scope note: Cap 123A is the **BD submission, signing, consent, supervision, and certification rulebook** — not planning volume control (Cap 123F), not construction performance (Cap 123Q), and not demolition site-safety detail (Cap 123C). Use this before schematic design to lock consultant registration roles, conflict disclosures, every prescribed plan sheet, scales/colouring, scheduled-area geo packs, 60/30/28-day clocks, start/stop hold points, TCP / supervision-plan duties, and the OP-path certificate chain (including water).

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123A governs how building works and street works under the Buildings Ordinance are **documented, signed by the correct registered person, submitted for Building Authority (BA) approval, consented for commencement, supervised on site, and certified on completion**. It also sets registration entry rules for Authorized Persons (AP), Registered Structural Engineers (RSE), Registered Geotechnical Engineers (RGE), Registered Inspectors (RI), and registered contractors, plus the fee table for plan submissions and register matters.

It applies to the full Cap 123 plan/consent pathway. It **does not apply** to minor works commenced under the simplified requirements (reg. 48). At SD: decide BO pathway → appoint AP/RSE/RGE to the reg. 12 signing matrix → list every reg. 8 sheet the massing will need → programme 60-day first approval + 28-day consent → bake in GI/SF/ELS/water-cert critical path.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Key definitions that change site strategy (reg. 2)

| Term | Meaning | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Bulk excavation** | All excavation **except** excavation for ground investigation, public utility trenches, drains, sewers, piles or caissons | In Scheduled Area 1, the SF plan must show the **envelope of all bulk excavation** (plan + sections with levels above HKPD). Basement / mass dig extents are BA-controlled envelopes — not free design choices |
| **Geotechnical design assumption** | Assumption on geology; groundwater & surfacewater; site history; location/nature of services, utilities, drains, sewers; existing site formation; foundations of existing buildings (on site or adjacent/nearby); shear strength; effect of proposed works; any other geo matters — used to demonstrate **adequate margin of safety** of any building, street, or natural/formed/man-made land | SF and ELS packs must schedule these assumptions **and** outline what changes if an assumption is later proven wrong (still must satisfy Cap 123Q s.21 for SF / s.14 for ELS) |
| **Site formation plans** | The SF plans prescribed in reg. 8 | Triggers the full reg. 8(1)(bb) supporting documentation pack (A–F) |
| **Hard copy form** | Paper or similar form readable without equipment | Hard-copy plan submissions still need duplicates and durable media (regs. 11, 14) |
| **Water authority / waterworks** | As in Waterworks Ordinance Cap 102 | Reg. 25A OP-path water certificates depend on this |

### 2. Who can be on the team — registration gates (Part 2, regs. 3–4A)

Lock appointments against these lists before fee proposals or schematic consultant briefs.

#### 2.1 Authorized Persons’ register — three lists (reg. 3(1)–(3))

| List | Minimum professional status | Plus |
|---|---|---|
| Architects | Registered architect | Continuous **1 year** HK practical experience within the **3 years** before application, as Registration Committee considers appropriate (reg. 3(6)) |
| Engineers | Registered professional engineer in **civil or structural** discipline | Same 1-year HK experience rule |
| Surveyors | Registered professional surveyor | Same 1-year HK experience rule |

#### 2.2 Structural engineers’ register (reg. 3(4))
- Registered professional engineer in **structural** discipline; **or**
- Civil discipline **with** structural engineering practical experience approved by the Structural Engineers Registration Committee  
- Plus the same reg. 3(6) experience rule

#### 2.3 Geotechnical engineers’ register (reg. 3(4A))
- Registered professional engineer in the **geotechnical** discipline  
- Plus reg. 3(6) experience rule

#### 2.4 Inspectors’ register — three lists (regs. 3(4B)–(4E), (8)–(10))
Used for mandatory inspection schemes (MBIS etc.), not day-to-day GP submissions — but relevant if the project later involves RI roles.

| Path into RI register | Experience gate |
|---|---|
| Already an AP (or RSE for engineers list) on the matching list | Within **7 years** before application: appropriate HK practical experience in building repair/maintenance as AP / RSE / RA / RPE / RPS |
| RA / RPE (building or structural) / RPS (building surveying) without being AP/RSE on that path | Aggregate **≥ 1 year** within **3 years** before application in HK building construction, repair & maintenance (Inspectors Registration Committee) |
| RPE in building services (building), civil, or materials (building); or RPS quantity surveying | Aggregate **≥ 3 years** of which **≥ 1 year** falls within the **3 years** before application |
| Nominated under Cap 123 s.3(7AA) | As provided in Ordinance; may enter without Inspectors Registration Committee recommendation in specified cases |

**Note:** Reg. 3(6) (the general 1-year AP/RSE/RGE experience rule) **does not** apply to inspectors’ register applicants (reg. 3(7)).

#### 2.5 Application mechanics (regs. 4–4A)
- Most AP / RSE / RGE / RI applicants: documentary proof to Registration Committee + suitability; may be required to attend **professional interview**
- Specified RI shortcuts (reg. 3(4B)(a)/(c) etc.): evidence to BA instead
- General building contractors / specialist contractors: qualifications, experience, competence evidence to Contractors Registration Committee; body corporate must evidence directors/officers/appointed persons; interview if required

#### 2.6 Conflict-of-interest disclosure (regs. 5–5A) — practice risk at appointment
- **AP / RSE / RGE** must not act as contractor, deal in building materials, or receive any payment / commission / advantage / benefit from any contractor, sub-contractor, or supplier of materials/goods used in the works **without written disclosure to the client**
- **RI** same rule for dealing in materials / receiving benefits from contractors/suppliers

**SD takeaway:** Put written conflict disclosure into appointment letters; D&B / package-contractor interfaces need early clarity.

### 3. Who must sign what — the schematic signing matrix (reg. 12)

Signature = **assumption of all responsibility** for that plan / detail / calculation.

| Document | Who must prepare & sign | Exception / note |
|---|---|---|
| All plans, structural details & calculations generally | **AP** (reg. 12(1)) | Subject to RSE/RGE rules below |
| Foundation plans, structural details & calcs under regs. 8(1)(d), 8(1)(i), 9(f) | **RSE** (reg. 12(2)) | AP may sign alone only if **all** of reg. 12(4) apply |
| Geotech plans, assessments, details/calcs, geo reports, site investigation or ground investigation reports under regs. 8(1)(b)(iv), (ba), (bb), (bc), (d), (l), 8(3), 8(4)(c) | **RGE** (reg. 12(5)) | No AP substitution |

#### 3.1 When AP may sign foundation / structural docs without RSE (reg. 12(3)–(4))
**All** of the following must be true:
1. Construction height ≤ **10 m**
2. Span of any structural element ≤ **6 m**
3. Structural elements of timber, masonry, steel, plain concrete, or reinforced concrete only
4. Foundations are **spread footings**, ground bearing pressure ≤ **300 kPa**, founding ≤ **2 m** below existing ground level
5. No structural alteration to any existing structural elements designed by an RSE

> **SD default:** Anything taller, longer-span, piled, deep, or altering RSE-designed structure → budget RSE from day one. Slopes / retention / SF / ELS / scheduled-area GI → budget RGE.

#### 3.2 One-team rule (reg. 15)
BA may refuse to accept plans from **two or more** APs, RSEs, or RGEs in respect of works at the **same premises**. Do not run parallel competing submissions.

#### 3.3 Compliance certificate with every plan submission (reg. 18A)
With plans submitted for approval, the AP / RSE / RGE who prepared them (or under whose supervision they were prepared) must submit a specified-form certificate that:
- (a) the plans were prepared by him or under his supervision/direction; **and**
- (b) to the best of his knowledge and belief the plans comply in all respects with the Ordinance and regulations

#### 3.4 Stability certificate for A&A / repairs (reg. 18)
Where works are repairs, alterations or additions to an existing building, the AP who prepared the plans must, at submission, send a specified-form certificate that he has **inspected** the building and that it is capable of bearing the loads/stresses that may be increased or altered by the works.  
If the building was constructed so as to require an RSE → certificate must be **signed by RSE and countersigned by AP**.

### 4. Prescribed plan package for building works (reg. 8(1)) — full sheet list

Design schematic so every future BD sheet has a clear home. These are the plans **prescribed** for Cap 123 purposes.

#### 4.1 Floor & roof plans — reg. 8(1)(a)
Must show:
- Full dimensions
- Thickness of walls
- Position of all windows
- Soil fitments, waste fitments, water storage tanks
- **Intended use of every part of the building**

#### 4.2 Elevations, levels, streets, wells — reg. 8(1)(b)
Must show:
- (i) All elevations of the building
- (ii) Level of adjacent streets relative to a **known datum** and to site/building levels
- (iii) Width of every street adjacent to the site
- (iv) Position, depth and construction of any well

#### 4.3 Geotechnical assessment (when BA requires) — reg. 8(1)(ba)
- Assessment of adequacy of the site for the proposed building works
- Must accompany the plans in (a), (b), (c) or (h)
- Must include plans and sections of proposed **site formation** and **foundation** works  
**SD trigger:** sloping sites, retained ground, filled ground, adjacent sensitive buildings, complex groundwater — assume BA will ask.

#### 4.4 Site formation plan — reg. 8(1)(bb)
The plan itself must show:
1. All SF works and associated surfacewater drainage works incorporating the requirements of the supporting report (E)
2. Existing nature of site and surrounds — accurate survey with ground-level contours, geological conditions, groundwater & surfacewater conditions; particulars of structures, foundations, public utilities, drains, sewers and other services **on and adjacent to** the site
3. Safety precautions whilst carrying out building works during **periods of heavy rainfall**
4. Schedule of **geotechnical design assumptions**
5. Sequence of building works and methods to be employed
6. If BA requires: specifications and other relevant particulars
7. **In Scheduled Area No. 1:** envelope of all **bulk excavation** by cross-sections and horizontal plan contours marked with levels above the **Hong Kong Principal Datum**

**Supporting documentation that must accompany the SF plan:**

| Ref | Content |
|---|---|
| **(A)** | Explanatory guide to the submission |
| **(B)** | Study report: topography, geology, groundwater, surfacewater, site history, public utilities, drains/sewers/other services, local geotechnical records |
| **(C)** | Ground investigation report with comprehensive details of equipment and procedures |
| **(D)** | Site monitoring of groundwater conditions |
| **(E)** | Critical examination & interpretation of (B)(C)(D); schedule of geo design assumptions; discussion of anticipated geo problems; **outline of variations** if an assumption is revealed erroneous so Cap 123Q s.21 compliance can no longer be demonstrated; geo requirements for design/construction including testing, inspecting, maintenance |
| **(F)** | Stability analyses sufficient to demonstrate Cap 123Q s.21; design calcs for SF works; calcs of effect of works on groundwater, the site, and any building/structure/land; other relevant geo calcs — **all paginated and prefaced by a comprehensive index** |

#### 4.5 Excavation & lateral support (ELS) plan (when BA requires) — reg. 8(1)(bc)
The plan must show:
1. All excavation and lateral support work (including ground treatment and dewatering) incorporating report (E) requirements
2. Existing site & surrounds — accurate survey with contours, geology, groundwater & surface water; particulars of streets, structures, foundations, public utilities, water mains, drains, sewers, other services; **dimensioned location** of water-carrying services and foundations on and adjacent to the site
3. Schedule of geotechnical design assumptions
4. Sequence of all **sub-surface** building works and methods
5. Particulars of monitoring for building & ground movements and variations in piezometric levels
6. Other specifications / relevant particulars

**Supporting documentation:**
| Ref | Content |
|---|---|
| **(A)** | Explanatory guide |
| **(B)** | Study: topography, geology, groundwater, surface water, site history, utilities, water mains, drains/sewers/services, local geo records |
| **(C)** | Site investigation & laboratory testing report (equipment & procedures) |
| **(D)** | Groundwater monitoring results |
| **(E)** | Interpretation of (B)(C)(D); design assumptions; anticipated problems; outline of variations if assumption fails so Cap 123Q **s.14** can no longer be demonstrated; geo requirements including testing, inspecting, **monitoring** |
| **(F)** | Analyses demonstrating Cap 123Q s.14; design calcs for lateral support systems; calcs of effect on groundwater, site, buildings, structures, streets, land or services; other geo matters — **paginated + comprehensive index** |

#### 4.6 Sections — reg. 8(1)(c)
One or more sections showing:
- Foundations
- Levels of the site and the lowest floor
- **Height of the building**
- **Clear height of every storey**
- Position of windows and floors
- Level of the roof of every adjoining or adjacent building
- Maximum gradient boundary-to-boundary across the site
- Maximum depth of excavation
- Maximum thickness of filling
- Volume of all excavation and all filling

> **SD takeaway:** Floor-to-floor narrative, basement dig depth, and cut/fill volumes are statutory drawing content — freeze them early.

#### 4.7 Foundation plan — reg. 8(1)(d)
- Size and position of foundations
- Full report of site investigation work undertaken
- Details of any proposed ground grouting

**In Scheduled Areas Nos. 2 or 4**, also accompany with:
| Ref | Content |
|---|---|
| (A) | Explanatory guide |
| (B) | Desk study (topo, geology, groundwater, surfacewater, site history, utilities, drains/sewers/services, local geo records) |
| (C) | Ground investigation report (equipment & procedures) |
| (D) | Groundwater monitoring |
| (E) | Interpretation + design assumptions + anticipated problems + geo requirements for design/construction including testing, inspecting, maintenance |
| (F) | Design calculations for foundation works |

#### 4.8 Drainage plan — reg. 8(1)(e)
- Size and depth of drains or sewers
- Connexions to public sewer / drain / nullah, **or** any other disposal method
- Position of disconnecting traps
- Means of ventilation

#### 4.9 Block plan & key plan — reg. 8(1)(f)–(g)
- Block plan: size and position of the building and of other buildings in the immediate vicinity, and neighbouring streets
- Key plan: if site position is not clearly identifiable from the block plan

#### 4.10 Plot ratio / site coverage / height / open-space diagram — reg. 8(1)(h)
Diagrammatic plan with **full dimensions and calculations** showing:
1. Height, site coverage, plot ratio and disposition per Cap 123F regs **19, 20 and 21**
2. Any open space required by Cap 123F regs **25, 27 and 28**

> This is the statutory PR/SC sheet — schematic massing must be calculable against Cap 123F (and OZP/lease) before GP freeze.

#### 4.11 Structural details & calculations — reg. 8(1)(i)
Plans showing structural details **together with one set of structural calculations** for the structural works.

#### 4.12 Projections over streets / unleased Government land — reg. 8(1)(j)
For any canopy, bridge or other projection: plans clearly showing nature and dimensions.

#### 4.13 Sanitary / UFA particulars — reg. 8(1)(k)
Details showing:
1. **Usable floor area** (meaning as in Cap 123I reg. 3)
2. Number and type of soil fitments and waste fitments to be provided

#### 4.14 Ground investigation plan (scheduled areas) — reg. 8(1)(l)
Must show:
1. Location, size and depth (or criteria for determining them) of all exploratory works — boring, drilling, probing, testing, instrument installation/measurement, soil/rock sampling — plus equipment and procedures
2. Equipment and procedures for laboratory testing and criteria for selecting test samples
3. If BA requires: specifications, reporting format, other particulars

#### 4.15 Barrier-free access plans — reg. 8(1)(m)
Where Cap 123F reg. **72** applies: plans showing in detail the provision made for persons with a disability in accordance with that regulation (DMBA / Third Schedule path).

#### 4.16 Cross-reference definitions (reg. 8(2))
*Soil fitment* and *waste fitment* take their meanings from Cap 123I.

### 5. Demolition plan package (reg. 8(3)–(4))

If strip-out / demo is part of the scheme, prescribed plans are:

| Plan | Must show |
|---|---|
| (a) | Construction and condition of building to be demolished, including sections showing slopes and ground supported by it, and all affected buildings, structures, streets, land and services |
| (b) | Existing nature of site and surrounds (accurate survey with contours; sections showing slopes/ground supported); all necessary removal of ground |
| (c) | Sequence, equipment and procedures for demolition |
| (d) | Support to be provided to the building being demolished and any slopes/ground it supports, and to all affected assets, **at each stage** |
| (e) | Precautionary measures for public protection — dust screens, catch fans, hoardings, covered walkways |
| (f) | Support to all affected buildings, structures, streets, land and services **at each stage** |

**Must be accompanied by:**
- (a) Stability report of building to be demolished **and** all affected buildings/structures/streets/land/services
- (b) Structural calculations for supports where BA considers appropriate
- (c) Stability report with calculations demonstrating demolition will not render inadequate the margin of safety of, or cause damage to, any building/structure/street/land/services
- (d) Stability report with calculations demonstrating proposed use of equipment or mechanical plant will not render inadequate margin of safety or cause damage

**Consent add-on (reg. 31(2)):** Application for consent to commence demolition must include personal particulars, qualifications and experience of operators of any powered mechanical plant/equipment proposed to be used. Changes of such operators → notify BA within **7 days** with new particulars (reg. 22(7)).

### 7. Submission mechanics — delivery, copies, scales, colouring (regs. 6–7, 10–14)

#### 7.1 How to deliver (reg. 6)
- Notices, forms, certificates, plans and other documents to BA: by **post** or delivery to BA office during office hours
- If plans (or works shown) do not comply → BA may charge prescribed fee for **every further submission**
- Fee **not** chargeable if BA is satisfied further submission was caused by a **bona fide misinterpretation** of the Ordinance or regulations

#### 7.2 Forms must be correctly completed and signed (reg. 7)
Every prescribed/specified notice, form, certificate or other document must be clearly and correctly completed to supply the required information and duly signed.

#### 7.3 Copies (reg. 11)
- Hard-copy plan or related document for approval → submit in **duplicate**
- BA may require any additional hard copies it considers necessary
- **Related document** = details of ground treatment proposed in connection with a plan, **or** a site investigation report in connection with a plan

#### 7.4 Scheduled Area 1 coupling (reg. 11A)
For building works in **Scheduled Area No. 1**: foundation plan under reg. 8(1)(d) **shall be submitted together with** the site formation plan under reg. 8(1)(bb).  
You cannot split SF approval from foundation on Area 1.

#### 7.5 Scales — building works (reg. 13(1), (3))
| Drawing | Minimum ratio |
|---|---|
| Plans and sections of building works | **1:100** (BA may accept ≥ **1:200** only for very extensive works) |
| Block plan | **1:500** |

#### 7.6 Clarity, media, colouring (reg. 14)
- Drawn/reproduced in a clear and intelligible manner
- Hard copy: on suitable and durable material
- Engineering drawings must **not** be reproduced on **ferro-prussic** prints for BA submission
- **One copy** of every plan so coloured as to clearly differentiate **existing work from new work**, and one part of any new work from other parts

#### 7.7 BA power to demand more (reg. 10)
BA may require additional plans, enlarged details, other documents, and information on water supply, specifications/use of special materials, or any other particulars it considers necessary.

### 8. Approval & consent procedure (Part 4, regs. 29–34)

#### 8.1 Application for approval (reg. 29)
- Specified form + documents required under these regulations
- BA may charge prescribed fee
- Subject to reg. 11A, any one or more plans may be submitted for approval **separately**
- Further particulars or other plans after BA refusal under Cap 123 s.16(1)(i) or (2)(f) = **fresh application** for the refused plans (subject to reg. 30(3))

#### 8.2 How approval is signified (reg. 30(1)–(2))
1. Written notice served on the applicant; **and**
2. Stamping, signing and dating of the plan (**not** structural or geotechnical calculations)

Stamped approved plan is returned / made available to the AP / RSE / RGE concerned. BA may give **separate approval** for any one or more of the plans submitted.

#### 8.3 Deemed-approval clocks (reg. 30(3)–(4)) — Cap 123 s.15 periods

| Situation | Deemed approval after |
|---|---|
| Plans submitted for the **first time** | **60 days** from submission date |
| Re-submitted so extensively revised as to constitute, in BA’s opinion, a **major revision** | **60 days** from last submission |
| Any other re-submission of previously submitted plans | **30 days** from last submission |

If BA considers a re-submission a major revision → must notify the appointed AP / RSE / RGE accordingly **not later than 30 days** from the last submission date.

#### 8.4 Consent to commence (regs. 31–32)
- Application in specified form (demo: plus plant operators’ particulars — see §5)
- Consent signified by written notice on the applicant
- BA may consent to commencement of **any part** of works whose plans have been approved
- Deemed consent period under Cap 123 s.15: **28 days**

#### 8.5 Alterations after consent (reg. 33)
Where it is desired to alter or add to works already consented:
- Apply in writing with (a) plans showing alterations/additions, and (b) the original notice of consent
- BA may charge prescribed fee
- Cap 123 s.15 applies; deemed period **28 days**
- BA may approve and consent by stamping plans per reg. 30 **and** adding those plans to the list of plans in the consent notice

#### 8.6 Resumption of suspended work (reg. 34)
When BA renews consent under Cap 123 s.20, it endorses a note of renewal on the notice of consent.

### 9. Notices, appointments, and stop-work triggers (regs. 19–24, 28)

| Event | Who acts | Deadline / effect |
|---|---|---|
| AP / RSE / RGE who prepared submitted plans ceases to act for the client | That AP / RSE / RGE | Notify BA in writing within **7 days** (reg. 19) |
| Before commencement — contractor appointment & start date | AP notifies; contractor accepts & undertakes compliance | **≥ 7 days before** commencement, specified form (reg. 20(1)) |
| Before commencement — TCP appointments (if supervision plan required) | AP / RSE / RGE **and** registered contractor each notify BA of their TCPs + personal particulars, qualifications, experience | **≥ 7 days before** commencement (reg. 20(2)–(3)) |
| Client appoints a replacement AP / RSE / RGE / RGBC / RSC / RMWC | Person for whom works are carried out | Notify BA of name within **7 days** of appointment (reg. 22(1)) |
| Appointed person becomes unable/unwilling to act during works | — | **That discipline’s works must cease** until replacement appointed and BA notified: RSE → structural work stops; RGE → geotech work stops; AP → **all works** stop; contractor → building/street works stop (reg. 22(2)) |
| Change of TCP (AP/RSE/RGE side) | AP / RSE / RGE | Notify BA within **7 days** + new TCP particulars (reg. 22(3)); works under that TCP **cease** until replaced (reg. 22(5)) |
| Change of TCP (contractor side) | RGBC / RSC / RMWC | Notify within **7 days** + particulars (reg. 22(4)); works under that TCP **cease** until replaced (reg. 22(6)) |
| Change of demo plant operator | Registered specialist contractor | Notify within **7 days** + particulars (reg. 22(7)) |
| First appointment of AP / RSE / RGE under Cap 123 s.4(1) | Client furnishes notice of appointment + appointee’s acceptance | Specified form (reg. 23(1A)) |
| AP / RSE / RGE ceases to be appointed | That person | Notify BA within **7 days** (reg. 23(1)) |
| Nomination of another AP / RSE / RGE to act in stead (Cap 123 s.4(2)) | Appointed person | Notify BA within **7 days** with nominee’s acceptance (reg. 23(2)) |
| Contractor ceases to be appointed | Contractor | Within **7 days**, deliver specified-form notice to AP for submission under reg. 38, certifying works so far complied with Ordinance/regs (reg. 24) |
| Emergency works engagement (Cap 123 s.19) | Every AP / RSE / RGE supervising and every registered contractor carrying out | Certify on specified form within **48 hours** (reg. 28) |

### 10. On-site duties after consent (Part 5, regs. 35–41)

Duties in this Part are **additional** to any other Ordinance/regulation duties (reg. 35).

#### 10.1 AP must supply approved plans to contractor (reg. 36)
- Supply stamped/signed/dated copy of every BA-approved plan, and any supervision plan
- Where structural or geotechnical details were prepared by RSE/RGE and approved → AP must ensure contractor also receives a copy

#### 10.2 Periodical vs continuous supervision (regs. 37, 41)

| Party | Supervision standard | Must ensure works follow |
|---|---|---|
| **AP** | Periodical supervision + inspections as necessary | Ordinance/regs; approved plans; supervision plan (if required under Cap 123 s.39A Technical Memorandum); BA orders/conditions |
| **RSE / RGE** | Same, for structural / geotechnical works respectively | Same for their discipline |
| **Registered contractor (RGBC / RSC / RMWC)** | **Continuous** supervision | Ordinance/regs; approved plans; BA orders/conditions; supervision plan (if required) |

#### 10.3 Technically Competent Persons (TCPs)
- Where supervision plan required: AP, RSE, RGE each appoint appropriate number of TCPs for supervision on each site (reg. 37(3))
- Contractor likewise appoints TCPs (reg. 41(2))
- BA may **reject or revoke** TCP appointment if qualifications/experience insufficient (regs. 37(4), 41(3))

#### 10.4 Contractor site document & record duties (regs. 40–41)
- Keep on site all approved plans and supervision plans supplied under reg. 36; produce to BA when required
- Keep records of activities and information relevant to supervision
- BA may inspect records at any reasonable time
- Retain records **≥ 12 months** after submission of the certificate on completion of the **final stage** of the works

#### 10.5 AP duty on contractor cessation notice (reg. 38)
AP must submit to BA, within **7 days** of receipt, any reg. 24 notice delivered by a ceasing contractor.

#### 10.6 Extra inspection fees (reg. 39)
If on completion BA needs more than one inspection because of Ordinance/regulation contravention or incorrect information → AP / RSE / RGE may be required to pay the prescribed fee for every additional inspection.

### 11. Completion certificates & OP-path water proofs (regs. 25–26, 25A)

#### 11.1 Building works completion chain (reg. 25)

| Step | Who | Timing | Certifies |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | RGBC / RSC / RMWC | Within **7 days** of completion | New building / works carried out per Ordinance, regs, and approved plans → deliver form to AP (or to RSE/RGE as applicable) |
| 2a | AP | Within **14 days** of completion | Compliance + opinion that building/works are **structurally safe** → send to BA |
| 2b | RSE or RGE (if form delivered to them) | Within **7 days** of receipt of contractor cert | Compliance + structurally / geotechnically safe (as applicable) → deliver to AP; AP sends to BA within further **7 days** |
| Demo | AP, RSE and RGE | Within **14 days** of demo completion | (a) demo completed per approved plans; (b) any remaining structure/building on site is structurally safe; (c) any land/street affected has adequate margin of safety |

Use the specified form for works resulting in a new building, or not so resulting, as applicable; demolition has its own specified form.

#### 11.2 Street works completion (reg. 26)
- Contractor: within **7 days**, certify compliance → deliver to AP
- AP: within **14 days**, certify works per approved plans → send to BA

#### 11.3 Water supply certificates (reg. 25A) — OP killers if missed

**Trigger A — new building with WC / trough WC / latrine / urinal** (Cap 123I):  
AP must send **together with** the reg. 25(2) certificate:

| Water source | Required certificate(s) |
|---|---|
| Permanent connexion from waterworks | Certificate by **Water Authority** that permanent connexion made per Cap 123I reg. 10A |
| Well within the site (BA permitted under Cap 123I reg. 10A(3)(b)) | (i) AP cert that permanent well connexion made per reg. 10A; **and** (ii) AP cert in respect of such well per Cap 123I reg. 10A(7) |
| Other supply (BA permitted/directed under Cap 123I reg. 10A(3)(c)) | AP cert that permanent connexion of that supply made per reg. 10A |

**Trigger B — new building with waste fitment or shower** (Cap 123I):  
Same parallel certificate structure (Water Authority / well / other supply) sent with reg. 25(2).

Water Authority must issue a required certificate within **10 days** of receiving written application (reg. 25A(3)).

> **SD implication:** Coordinate WSD connection design and programme from concept; without these certs the completion/OP path stalls wherever sanitary or waste fittings are provided.

### 14. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing massing, consultant briefs, and fee proposals:

1. **Confirm BO pathway** — full Cap 123 plan/consent vs minor works simplified route (reg. 48).
2. **Map scheduled area number** — Area 1 couples SF + foundation (reg. 11A) and requires bulk-excavation envelope; Areas 2/4 thicken foundation geo packs; GI plan mandatory for scheduled areas (reg. 8(1)(l)).
3. **Appoint / budget AP + RSE + RGE** using the reg. 12 signing matrix; confirm reg. 12(4) exemption only if the scheme truly stays ≤10 m / ≤6 m span / shallow spread footings.
4. **Write conflict disclosures** into appointments (regs. 5–5A); avoid multi-AP same-premises conflict (reg. 15).
5. **List every reg. 8 sheet** the massing will need — floors with intended uses, elevations/levels/street widths, sections (height + clear storeys + dig/fill volumes), drainage outfall, block/key, **PR/SC/open-space diagram**, structural + foundation, BFA (if Cap 123F reg. 72), street projections, sanitary/UFA.
6. **If basement / retention / slopes / fill** — assume SF and/or ELS packs with full A–F supporting docs, design-assumption schedule, and Cap 123Q s.14/s.21 contingency narrative.
7. **Programme clocks** — 60 days first/major approval; 30 days minor re-sub; 28 days consent; BA has 30 days to flag “major revision.”
8. **Bake in SI / GI / groundwater monitoring** before foundation schema freezes.
9. **If demo / A&A** — budget stability cert (reg. 18), full demo plan set (reg. 8(3)–(4)), and plant-operator particulars for consent.
10. **Plan OP cert chain** — contractor (7 days) → AP (14 days) / RSE-RGE (7+7) → BA; plus Water Authority / well / alternative water certs under reg. 25A.
11. **Scale & colour discipline** — ≥1:100 (or agreed 1:200), block ≥1:500, one coloured exist/new copy, no ferro-prussic prints, hard copy in duplicate.
12. **Supervision plan / TCP budget** — if TM under Cap 123 s.39A will require a supervision plan, TCP CVs must be ready ≥7 days before start; contractor keeps plans on site and records for ≥12 months after final completion cert.
