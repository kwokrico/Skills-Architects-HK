# Cap 123H — Building (Refuse Storage and Material Recovery Chambers and Refuse Chutes) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 9 February 2012 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. H

> Scope note: Cap 123H is the **refuse / recycling spatial rulebook** — chamber sizing, vehicular-access triggers, floor-by-floor recovery rooms, and refuse-chute geometry. It is **not** planning volume control (Cap 123F), drainage standards (Cap 123I), or construction performance (Cap 123Q).  
> **Lock refuse strategy before freezing** ground-floor GFA, service lanes, podium ramps, domestic cores, and typical-floor service rooms.  
> Companion practice: **PNAP APP-35** (Refuse Storage and Collection, Dec 2022) — RCV envelope, GFA exclusion, FRR for rooms, and the optional RSR + centralised recycling-corner modification of reg. 3A.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123H requires BD plans for buildings listed in the Schedule to show **refuse storage and material recovery chambers** (or **material recovery chambers** for industrial) sized from usable floor space, and — for domestic / domestic part of composite — **refuse storage and material recovery rooms on every floor** (with listed exemptions). Where a chamber, MRC, or room is provided, and where any refuse chute is provided, the design must meet hard dimensional, access, construction, drainage, ventilation, and hopper constraints in regs 4–25.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Definitions that drive schematic decisions (reg. 2)

Wrong vocabulary = wrong facility type and wrong Schedule row. Lock these before massing.

#### 1.1 Three facility types

| Term | Statutory meaning | Typical SD placement |
|---|---|---|
| **Refuse storage and material recovery chamber (RS&MRC)** | Single chamber where refuse containers are stored **and** reusable/recyclable materials are separated, sorted, and stored | G/F or podium — domestic, non-domestic (non-industrial), composite |
| **Material recovery chamber (MRC)** | Chamber for separating / sorting / storing reusable or recyclable materials **only** (no refuse-container store wording) | Industrial buildings per Schedule |
| **Refuse storage and material recovery room (RS&MRR)** | Single **room on a floor** for separation, sorting, and storage of refuse + recovered materials | Every domestic floor under reg. 3A (not the central chamber) |

#### 1.2 “With vehicular access” variants

| Term | Meaning | SD consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **RS&MRC with vehicular access** | RS&MRC with ingress/egress adequate for a **refuse and material recovery collection vehicle of a type customarily used by the collection authority** | Full RCV bay — not a widened pedestrian door |
| **MRC with vehicular access** | Same idea for material-recovery collection vehicle | Same — industrial large UFS |

**Collection authority** = Director of Food and Environmental Hygiene (FEHD). Cap 123H does **not** publish vehicle dims — use **APP-35** (§10 below).

#### 1.3 Floor-area metrics for Schedule triggers

| Metric | Formula / rule | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Usable floor space (UFS)** | Floor space **excluding** staircases, staircase halls, lift landings, WC/urinal/lavatory-basin space, and space occupied by lift / air-conditioning / similar service machinery | Domestic, non-domestic, industrial Schedule columns |
| **Aggregate usable floor space** (composite only) | Domestic-component UFS + **one third** of non-domestic-component UFS | Whether a composite **triggers** a chamber and which vehicular band |
| **Floor area** (reg. 2) | Composite → aggregate UFS; any other building → UFS | Cross-ref wording in Schedule |

**UFS ≠ GFA ≠ Cap 123F open space ≠ Cap 123I UFA.** Build a Cap 123H UFS layer early; APP-35 requires UFS / aggregate UFS + min chamber size + proposed chamber size on plans.

#### 1.4 Material vocabulary (recyclables briefing)

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Recovered materials** | Reusable or recyclable materials recovered from refuse |
| **Recyclable materials** | Capable of being regenerated into raw materials for new products |
| **Reusable materials** | Capable of being used again in current form without breaking down into raw materials |
| **Reusable or recyclable materials** | Composition including **paper, paperboard, plastic, glass, wood, metal and textile** |

#### 1.5 Building-type definitions that unlock exemptions / Schedule rows

| Term | Cap 123H meaning | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Industrial building** | A **godown**, or a building where articles are manufactured, altered, cleaned, repaired, ornamented, finished, adapted for sale, broken up or demolished, or materials transformed | Uses **MRC** Schedule row (÷2 320), not RS&MRC |
| **Church** | Building constructed/intended principally for **religious worship** | Reg. 3 chamber duty **off** |
| **School** | Building principally for primary / secondary / tertiary education; includes day nursery, kindergarten, play group, play school, day-time care/education of children, and buildings principally for other **full-time** education | Reg. 3 chamber duty **off** |
| **External wall** | Whole or part of an **outer wall** of a building, **not** a party wall, even if adjoining another building’s wall | Chamber door **must** sit in a true external wall (regs 7, 10) |
| **Hopper** | Fitment on a refuse chute into which refuse is placed and from which it is projected into the chute | Chute design only |
| **Open air** | Same meaning as Cap **123F** | Hopper location (reg. 22) |
| **Foul water / soil fitment** | Same meaning as Cap **123I** | Chamber drainage + flushing-water tee (regs 11–12) |
| **Plan** | Plan submitted under Cap 123 **s.14** | Regs 3 / 3A bite on **approval plans**, not “concept sketches” |

**Multi-floor unit (reg. 3A(7)):** a domestic unit occupying space on **2 or more consecutive floors** (duplex / triplex / maisonette).

### 2. When a chamber is mandatory on plans (reg. 3 + Schedule)

#### 2.1 Trigger rule

Every plan for a building of a Schedule description (or **2+ buildings all of the same description**) with total floor area in the second column **must show** a chamber of the third-column type with **minimum floor space** from the fourth column.

| Building | Threshold → ordinary chamber | Threshold → **vehicular-access** chamber | Min floor space of chamber |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Domestic** | UFS ≥ **1 320 m²** and < **13 200 m²** | UFS ≥ **13 200 m²** | UFS ÷ **347** |
| **Non-domestic (except industrial)** | UFS ≥ **3 960 m²** and < **39 600 m²** | UFS ≥ **39 600 m²** | UFS ÷ **925** |
| **Industrial** | UFS ≥ **3 960 m²** and < **39 600 m²** → **MRC** | UFS ≥ **39 600 m²** → **MRC with vehicular access** | UFS ÷ **2 320** (ordinary MRC **not less than 2.25 m²**) |
| **Composite** | Aggregate UFS ≥ **1 320 m²** and < **13 200 m²** | Aggregate UFS ≥ **13 200 m²** | **(Domestic UFS ÷ 347) + (Non-domestic UFS ÷ 925)** |

**Below-threshold = no reg. 3 chamber.** Domestic floors may still need RS&MRR under reg. 3A.

#### 2.2 Multi-building plans (reg. 3(2)) — easy to get wrong

| Situation | How to size |
|---|---|
| Plan covers **2+ buildings of the same** Schedule description | Aggregate their UFS as one building of that description |
| Plan covers buildings of **2+ different** Schedule descriptions | Treat as one **composite** building — use composite aggregate UFS for trigger + composite sizing formula |

#### 2.3 Absolute exemptions from reg. 3 (reg. 3(3))

Reg. 3 **does not apply** to a building constructed or adapted for use principally as a:
- **church**; or
- **school**; or
- **car park**.

If you still **voluntarily** provide a chamber or chute, regs 4–25 still apply to what you build.

#### 2.4 Worked sizing examples

| Scheme | Calc | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic 5 000 m² UFS | 5 000 ÷ 347 | ≈ **14.4 m²** RS&MRC (no vehicle) |
| Domestic 15 000 m² UFS | 15 000 ÷ 347 | ≈ **43.2 m²** RS&MRC **with vehicular access** |
| Office 10 000 m² UFS | 10 000 ÷ 925 | ≈ **10.8 m²** RS&MRC |
| Office 45 000 m² UFS | 45 000 ÷ 925 | ≈ **48.6 m²** RS&MRC **with vehicular access** |
| Godown 8 000 m² UFS | 8 000 ÷ 2 320 = 3.45 → but floor ≥ 2.25 | **≈ 3.45 m² MRC** |
| Godown 4 000 m² UFS | 4 000 ÷ 2 320 ≈ 1.72 → lift to 2.25 | **2.25 m² MRC** (statutory floor) |
| Composite: 4 000 domestic + 6 000 non-dom | Trigger aggregate = 4 000 + 6 000/3 = **6 000** (≥1 320, <13 200) → ordinary chamber. Size = 4 000÷347 + 6 000÷925 ≈ 11.5 + 6.5 | **≈ 18.0 m²** RS&MRC |
| Composite: 10 000 domestic + 12 000 non-dom | Aggregate = 10 000 + 4 000 = **14 000** ≥ 13 200 → **vehicular**. Size = 10 000÷347 + 12 000÷925 ≈ 28.8 + 13.0 | **≈ 41.8 m²** RS&MRC with vehicular access |

**Composite trap:** the **trigger** uses ⅓ non-domestic UFS; the **chamber area** uses **full** non-domestic UFS ÷ 925. Do not divide non-domestic by 3 when sizing the chamber.

#### 2.5 Plan submission content (APP-35 §5)

On plans for Schedule buildings, show:
1. UFS or aggregate UFS calculation;
2. Minimum floor space required for the chamber;
3. Actual proposed chamber size (must be ≥ minimum).

### 3. Floor-by-floor RS&MRR — domestic / domestic part of composite (reg. 3A)

#### 3.1 Default rule (reg. 3A(1))

Every plan for:
- a **domestic building**; or
- the **domestic part** of a composite building,

must show an RS&MRR on **every floor** of that domestic building / domestic part.

#### 3.2 Multi-floor units (duplex / triplex) — regs 3A(2)–(3)

Where **2+ consecutive floors** comprise only one or more multi-floor units:
- for each multi-floor unit, show RS&MRR on **at least one** of the floors that unit occupies;
- **unless** a reg. 3 chamber / MRC is already shown on any of those floors.

#### 3.3 Floor-level skips (reg. 3A(4)) — no room required on

| Floor type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Floor **not designed for habitation** | Skip (plant, transfer, sky lobby with no dwellings, etc.) |
| Floor that already has a reg. 3 **chamber or MRC** shown | Skip on that floor only |

#### 3.4 Partial carve-outs (reg. 3A(5))

Where part of a domestic building / domestic part is designed as a **hotel, guest-house or boarding house**, reg. 3A **does not apply to that part**. Remaining pure-residential floors still need rooms.

#### 3.5 Whole-building / site exemptions (reg. 3A(6)) — all-or-nothing for that building

Reg. 3A does **not** apply to the plan to the extent it relates to:

| # | Exemption | Typical scheme |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | Domestic or composite with **one staircase only** | Small walk-up / single-stair tower |
| (b) | Domestic for **one single family** only, with **≤ 3 floors** designed for habitation | Large house / villa |
| (c) | Composite whose domestic part has **≤ 3 floors** and is for **one single family** only | Shop house + family flat above |
| (d) | Domestic or composite on a site of area **≤ 500 m²** | Tight urban plot |

**SD takeaway:** Single-stair / single-family / ≤500 m² site is the early escape hatch. Multi-stair domestic towers → budget an RS&MRR on every typical floor from first core diagram.

#### 3.6 Alternative to every-floor RS&MRR — modification via APP-35 Appendix C

BA may favourably consider **modification of reg. 3A** for domestic / domestic part with **TUFS ≥ 1 320 m²**, replacing every-floor RS&MRR with:
- a **refuse storage room (RSR)** on every domestic floor (refuse only); **plus**
- a **centralised recycling corner (RC)** for all recyclables.

| Element | APP-35 hard numbers |
|---|---|
| **RSR** min dimension | ≥ **1 m** |
| **RSR** min floor area | ≥ **1.5 m²** |
| **RSR** standards | Still meet Cap 123H regs **12A, 12B, 12C** + APP-35 fire / layout |
| **RC** location | Main entrance/exit level (podium or G/F); one RC per tower (shared RC rules below) |
| **RC** size by TUFS per tower / shared group | Low 1 320–7 260 → **10–14 m²**; Medium >7 260–13 200 → **14–16 m²**; High >13 200 → **16–21 m²** |
| **RC** min dimension | ≥ **1.5 m**; manoeuvre ≥ **1.5 × 1.5 m** inside |
| **RC** BFA | Readily accessible to persons with a disability |
| **Shared RC** | Only if each served building has TUFS **< 1 320 m²**, combined TUFS of served buildings **≥ 1 320 m²**, and horizontal distance from each building main entrance/exit to RC **≤ 75 m** |
| **RC** common parts | Must be DMC / Cap 344 common parts (or owner undertaking if no DMC yet) |
| **RC** wet services | Water point(s) + sink; floor outlet → ≥100 mm pipe → back-inlet trapped gully immediately outside → foul water (mirrors chamber drainage) |
| **RC** vent / light / fire | ≥3 ACH + purify; ≥120 lux; FRR walls **-/120/120**, door **-/60/60**, self-close not hold-open; fire alarm + sprinkler; socket(s) for future smart bins |

**Do not assume this alternative is automatic** — it needs a **modification application**. Default statutory path remains every-floor RS&MRR.

### 4. Which physical standards apply to which facility (regs. 4–4B)

| Facility | Must comply with | Does **not** automatically need |
|---|---|---|
| **RS&MRC** (reg. 4) | Regs **5–12A** full package | — |
| **MRC** (reg. 4A) | Same as RS&MRC: regs **5–12A** | — |
| **RS&MRR** (reg. 4B) | Reg **8(1)** (any dim ≥1.5 m), **12A** (mech vent + purify), **12B** (readily accessible), **12C** (≥120 lux) | Regs 5–7, 8(2), 9–12 (external wall, 2 m height, steel door, drainage, flushing water) — **not** imposed by 4B |
| **Refuse chute** (reg. 13) | Vertical (subject to 14); terminate in an **RS&MRC**; regs **14–25** | — |

**APP-35 overlay on rooms:** even though Cap 123H does not prescribe FRR for RS&MRR, APP-35 requires walls **-/120/120**, door **-/60/60**, no hold-open, plus fire alarm and sprinkler — treat as BD expectation at SD.

### 5. Chamber location, access & envelope (regs. 5–8, 10) + APP-35 RCV

#### 5.1 Location & ready access (reg. 5)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | BA-**approved** design |
| Location | BA-**approved** location with **ready access** for removing refuse containers and recovered materials |
| Pedestrian approach (chamber **without** vehicular access) | Passage / alley / similar way ≥ **1.5 m** wide, **without steps**, **paved**, longitudinal gradient **≤ 1 in 20** |

**APP-35 location rule for vehicular chambers:** place RS&MRC intended for RCV visit on **G/F or podium** with **direct vehicular access from the street without passing through intervening floors**. Show RCV access and exit routes from public street on building plans.

#### 5.2 Enclosure & external wall (regs. 6–7)

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Openings allowed | Access door (reg. 10); refuse-chute opening; hopper opening — **nothing else** |
| External wall | **≥ 1** wall of every chamber must be an **external wall** (not party wall) |

**SD takeaway:** Cannot bury chamber mid-plan with only an internal door. Door must be in an outer wall — drives podium edge, lane frontage, and service-yard disposition.

#### 5.3 Minimum dimensions (reg. 8)

| Item | Chambers (RS&MRC / MRC) | Rooms (via 4B) |
|---|---|---|
| Any plan dimension | ≥ **1.5 m** | ≥ **1.5 m** (8(1) only) |
| Clear height to ceiling | ≥ **2 m** throughout | **Not** imposed by Cap 123H for rooms |

Schedule floor-space formula is a **minimum area**; the 1.5 m rule still forces usable proportions (e.g. 1.5 × 1.5 = 2.25 m² footprint before area formula bites).

#### 5.4 Access door (reg. 10) — chambers only

| Item | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Type | Close-fitting **steel** door |
| Location | In an **external wall** of the chamber |
| Height | ≥ **1.8 m** |
| Width | ≥ **1.25 m** |
| Security | Lock or other means to prevent unauthorised access |
| Internal face | **Without projections** |

Vehicular chambers will usually need openings / clearances **larger than** this pedestrian door to pass an RCV — the 1.25 × 1.8 m rule is a floor, not the RCV envelope.

#### 5.5 RCV limiting envelope (APP-35 §1) — design to worst case

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Overall length | **11 m** |
| Overall width (mirror to mirror) | **3 m** |
| Overall height | **4 m** |
| Min headroom for bin-lifting | **4.5 m** |
| Min ground clearance | **0.24 m** |
| Min angle of departure (laden) | **10°** |
| Turning circle diameter (wall to wall) | **20 m** |
| Gross vehicle weight | **26 tonnes** |
| Max access gradient | **1:10** |
| Min access-road width | Preferably **4 m**, not less than **3.5 m** |
| Parking space for collection | **5 m × 12 m** |
| Hammer-head (if no continuing forward exit) | Basic **27 m × 9.5 m**, approach **10.9 m** wide (or alternative proven from above envelope) |

**Structural note:** 26 t GVW on podium / transfer slabs is an early RSE load case — do not leave to DD.

### 6. Chamber construction, wet services & air (regs. 9, 11–12A)

#### 6.1 Fabric (reg. 9)

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Walls / structure | Brickwork, concrete, or other BA-approved material |
| Internal wall faces | Lined with **glazed bricks, glazed tiles**, or other approved |
| Ceiling | Cement rendered, **smooth** finish |
| Floor slab | Concrete **≥ 100 mm** thick |
| Floor falls | Laid to fall toward the reg. 11 gully |
| Floor finish | Quarry tiles or other approved **hard impervious** material |
| Wall–floor junction | **Coved** |

#### 6.2 Drainage (reg. 11) — foul, not surface water

| Step | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1 | Floor **outlet drain** with **grating** inside chamber |
| 2 | Connect by pipe ID ≥ **100 mm** to a **back-inlet trapped gully** |
| 3 | Gully **immediately outside** the chamber |
| 4 | Gully fitted with **airtight cover** for inspection / cleaning |
| 5 | Gully connected to a drain for **foul water** (Cap 123I sense) |

#### 6.3 Cleaning water (reg. 12)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Provision | Water supply point **inside** every chamber |
| Source | Connected to the building’s supply of water provided for **flushing soil fitments** — not potable drinking water as the statutory default |

Coordinate with Cap 123I flushing system / flushing-water tank early.

#### 6.4 Mechanical ventilation & air purifying (reg. 12A) — chambers **and** rooms

Every RS&MRC / MRC / RS&MRR (via 4B) must have:
1. Mech ventilation supplying fresh air to **all parts** at **≥ 3 air changes per hour**; **and**
2. Air purifying facilities on the **exhaust**, to BA satisfaction.

**APP-35 practice (chambers / rooms / RC):**
- Centralised system → single purifier before final atmospheric discharge is acceptable;
- No particular odour problem → mech fan + particulate filter at each MRC / RS&MRR may be considered;
- Main exhaust preferably at **upper roof**, away from other buildings; if surrounded by taller buildings, discharge may be at the main RS&MRC;
- Noise to Cap **400** Technical Memorandum;
- Fire dampers if exhaust grilles / ducting at each floor;
- RS&MRC air-purifying devices: chemical scrubber, bio-oxygen generator, photo-oxidation generator, or other appropriate devices.

**SD takeaway:** Reserve vertical exhaust duct + roof terminal (or justify main-chamber discharge). Odour / neighbour / FS damper coordination is a concept-stage shaft claim.

#### 6.5 Room-only duties (regs. 12B–12C)

| Reg | Rule |
|---|---|
| **12B** | Every RS&MRR **readily accessible by any occupier at all times** — not locked staff-only; not behind a tenancy that residents cannot enter |
| **12C** | Illumination ≥ **120 lux** measured at finished floor level |

### 7. Refuse chutes — optional, but fully prescribed if used (regs. 13–25)

Cap 123H **does not require** a chute. If provided:

| Reg. 13 baseline | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Alignment | **Vertical**, save as permitted by reg. 14 |
| Termination | At lower level in a **refuse storage and material recovery chamber** (not an MRC-only space, not a floor room) |
| Compliance | Must meet regs **14–25** |

#### 7.1 Geometry & structure (regs. 14–18)

| Topic | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Bends / offsets (14(1)) | **Prohibited** except at the **foot** or **above the highest hopper** |
| Foot offset (14(2)) | Slope ≥ **60°** to horizontal; replaceable **galvanized or stainless steel ≥ 3 mm** thick |
| Top above hoppers (15) | Chute top ≥ **300 mm** above top of highest hopper |
| Lining & bore (16) | Lined with glazed ware or other impervious tubes; internal diameter ≥ **450 mm** |
| Walls (17) | Solid brick or concrete ≥ **100 mm** thick **exclusive of lining** |
| Base shutter (18) | Galvanized or stainless steel shutter at lower end to close the chute when needed |

**Shaft claim at SD:** ≥450 mm clear bore + ≥100 mm wall + lining ≈ significant core width; stack vertically onto the RS&MRC.

#### 7.2 Ventilation, cleanout & internal surface (regs. 19–21)

| Topic | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Vent pipe (19) | At top of chute; internal area ≥ **0.05 m²**; carried up ≥ **1 m above roof**; end grille with apertures aggregate area ≥ pipe section; **least dimension of each aperture ≤ 10 mm** |
| Inspection opening (20) | Above highest hopper; ≥ **225 mm** diameter; close-fitting metal cover |
| Internal surface (21) | Smooth and impervious; glazed ware or approved; if pipes — jointed in cement mortar **1:1** (cement:sand) or approved material; pipes securely and solidly bedded into chute walls |

#### 7.3 Hoppers (regs. 22–25) — typical-floor design killers

| Topic | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Situation (22) | Every hopper in a place **permanently ventilated to the open air** (Cap 123F open-air meaning) — **not** a sealed AC corridor |
| Mouth size (23(1)) | Clear opening **not less than 250 × 150 mm** and **not more than 350 × 250 mm** |
| Positions (23(2)(a)) | Remains only **fully closed** or **fully open**; will **not** open of its own accord |
| Containment (23(2)(b)) | Prevents escape of dust or fumes when closed **and** when open |
| Lodging (23(3)) | Constructed so refuse cannot lodge in hopper/frame |
| Material (23(4)) | Galvanized or stainless mild steel plate ≥ **3 mm**, or approved |
| Inner plate (23(5)) | Projects downward at ≥ **45°** to horizontal when hopper closed |
| Recess (24) | Recess large enough that **no part** of frame or hopper projects into the chute |
| Junction (25) | Continuous **smooth and impervious** surface into the chute |

**SD takeaway:** Hopper lobby / refuse room on the typical floor needs a permanent open-air ventilation strategy (open corridor, ventilated lobby, external wall opening). Do not place hoppers in pressurised or fully enclosed AC common areas without a Cap 123F open-air solution.

### 8. GFA, fire, and submission interfaces (APP-35 + Cap 123A / 123F)

| Topic | Practice |
|---|---|
| **GFA exclusion** | Subject to satisfactory planning, area of MRC, RS&MRC, RS&MRR, refuse chutes, and hopper rooms (where provided) **may be excluded from GFA** under Cap **123F** reg. **23(3)(b)** — also RSR + RC under the APP-35 alternative if modification accepted |
| **Plan calcs** | Show UFS / aggregate UFS, min chamber floor space, and proposed chamber size (APP-35 §5) |
| **RCV routes** | Indicate access and exit from public street on building plans |
| **RS&MRR fire** | Walls FRR ≥ **-/120/120**; door ≥ **-/60/60**; self-close without hold-open; fire alarm + sprinkler (APP-35 §6) |
| **Cap 123A** | Refuse facilities appear on general building / floor plans and drainage; chamber wet connection is a Cap 123I foul-drainage design |
| **Cap 123I** | Foul gully / flushing-water tee; do not confuse Cap 123H UFS with Cap 123I UFA for toilet counts |

### 9. Common schematic failure modes

| Failure | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Chamber with only an **internal** door | Regs 7 + 10 require door in an **external wall** |
| Approach with **steps** or slope steeper than 1:20 | Reg. 5(2) |
| Using **⅓ non-domestic** in the chamber **area** formula | ⅓ is for **aggregate trigger** only; size uses full non-domestic ÷ 925 |
| Industrial scheme drawn as RS&MRC | Schedule requires **MRC** (recyclables), not refuse+recovery chamber |
| Chute terminating in floor RS&MRR or MRC-only | Reg. 13 — must terminate in **RS&MRC** |
| Hopper in sealed AC corridor | Reg. 22 — permanent open-air ventilation |
| Mid-height chute offset for architectural “jog” | Reg. 14 — only foot or above highest hopper |
| Domestic typical floor with no RS&MRR and no 3A exemption | Reg. 3A(1) |
| Assuming APP-35 RSR+RC without modification | Still need BA modification of reg. 3A |
| Vehicular chamber via basement through intervening floors | APP-35 — direct G/F / podium access from street |
| Ignoring 26 t RCV on podium | Structural / headroom / turning failure at DD |

### 10. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing G/F service layout, cores, and domestic typical floors:

1. **Classify** — domestic / non-domestic / industrial / composite; confirm church / school / car-park exemption from reg. 3 if claimed.
2. **Compute Cap 123H UFS** — exclude stairs, staircase halls, lift landings, sanitary fitment space, lift/AC plant; for composite also compute **aggregate UFS** (domestic + ⅓ non-dom).
3. **Schedule trigger** — if above threshold, reserve chamber area (÷347 / ÷925 / ÷2320) and flag **vehicular access** at 13 200 / 39 600 / 13 200 aggregate.
4. **Industrial?** — use **MRC** row, not RS&MRC; enforce ≥2.25 m² floor on ordinary MRC.
5. **Multi-building plan** — same description → aggregate; different descriptions → treat as composite.
6. **Domestic floors** — every habitable floor gets RS&MRR unless 3A(4)–(6) exemption or duplex “≥1 floor per unit”; or pursue APP-35 RSR+RC **modification**.
7. **Chamber envelope** — ≥1 external wall; steel door ≥1.25 × 1.8 m in that wall; clear height ≥2 m; every plan dim ≥1.5 m; no stray openings.
8. **Access** — non-vehicular: ≥1.5 m, step-free, paved, ≤1:20; vehicular: APP-35 RCV envelope, 4.5 m bin-lift headroom, ≤1:10 gradient, 5×12 m parking, preferably forward-out or hammer-head.
9. **Wet + air** — foul gully immediately outside, ≥100 mm, flushing-water point, ≥3 ACH + exhaust purify + roof (or justified) discharge.
10. **Rooms** — ≥1.5 m min dim; 12A vent/purify; 24/7 occupier access; ≥120 lux; APP-35 FRR -/120/120 + door -/60/60 + sprinkler/alarm.
11. **Chute (if any)** — vertical to RS&MRC; ≥450 mm ID; ≥100 mm walls; open-air hoppers; roof vent ≥0.05 m² / ≥1 m above roof; no mid-height bends; shutter at foot.
12. **GFA narrative** — claim Cap 123F reg. 23(3)(b) exclusion for compliant refuse facilities; keep sizes honest on plans.
13. **Cross-links** — Cap 123A plan package; Cap 123I foul/flushing; Cap 123F open air + GFA; APP-35 RCV / modification / fire.
