# Cap 123H — Building (Refuse Storage and Material Recovery Chambers and Refuse Chutes) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 9 February 2012 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. H

> Scope note: Cap 123H is the **refuse / recycling spatial rulebook** — chamber sizing, vehicular access triggers, floor-by-floor recovery rooms, and refuse-chute geometry. It is **not** planning volume control (Cap 123F), drainage standards (Cap 123I), or construction performance (Cap 123Q). Lock refuse strategy before freezing ground-floor GFA, service lanes, and cores.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123H requires BD plans for scheduled building types to show **refuse storage and material recovery chambers** (or **material recovery chambers** for industrial) sized from usable floor space, plus **refuse storage and material recovery rooms on every domestic floor** (with listed exemptions). Where provided, chambers/rooms and any refuse chutes must meet hard dimensional, access, construction, drainage, and ventilation constraints.

---

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Three facility types — pick the right one at concept (reg. 2)

| Facility | What it is | Typical SD use |
|---|---|---|
| **Refuse storage and material recovery chamber** | Single chamber for refuse containers **plus** separating / sorting / storing recovered materials | Domestic, non-domestic (non-industrial), composite — Schedule |
| **Material recovery chamber** | Chamber for separating / sorting / storing reusable or recyclable materials only (no refuse-container store wording) | **Industrial** buildings only per Schedule |
| **Refuse storage and material recovery room** | Single room on a floor for separation, sorting, and storage of refuse + recovered materials | Every domestic floor under reg. 3A (rooms, not the ground/central chamber) |

**“With vehicular access”** = chamber designed so a **collection-authority-type** refuse / material recovery vehicle can enter and leave (reg. 2). This is a site-layout and doorway / ramp / headroom design problem — not a late furniture layout.

**Floor area for Schedule thresholds (reg. 2):**
- Non-composite → **usable floor space** (UFS)
- Composite → **aggregate usable floor space** = domestic UFS + **¹⁄₃** of non-domestic UFS

**UFS excludes:** staircases, staircase halls, lift landings, WC/urinal/basin space, and plant space for lifts / AC / similar (reg. 2).

### 2. When a chamber is mandatory on plans (reg. 3 + Schedule) — hard SD triggers

Every plan for a Schedule building (or multiple buildings of the **same** description counted together) must show a chamber of the required type with **minimum floor space** from the Schedule formulae.

| Building | Threshold → ordinary chamber | Threshold → **vehicular-access** chamber | Min floor space |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Domestic** | UFS ≥ **1 320 m²** and < **13 200 m²** | UFS ≥ **13 200 m²** | UFS ÷ **347** |
| **Non-domestic (not industrial)** | UFS ≥ **3 960 m²** and < **39 600 m²** | UFS ≥ **39 600 m²** | UFS ÷ **925** |
| **Industrial** | UFS ≥ **3 960 m²** and < **39 600 m²** → **material recovery chamber** | UFS ≥ **39 600 m²** → **MRC with vehicular access** | UFS ÷ **2 320** (ordinary MRC **≥ 2.25 m²** minimum) |
| **Composite** | Aggregate UFS ≥ **1 320 m²** and < **13 200 m²** | Aggregate UFS ≥ **13 200 m²** | **Domestic UFS ÷ 347** + **non-domestic UFS ÷ 925** |

**Multi-building plans (reg. 3(2)):** If the plan covers **2+ buildings of different Schedule descriptions**, treat them as one **composite** building for chamber sizing.

**Exempt from reg. 3 chamber duty (reg. 3(3)):** buildings constructed/adapted principally as a **church**, **school**, or **car park**.

**Quick sizing examples (ordinary chamber):**
- Domestic 5 000 m² UFS → 5 000 ÷ 347 ≈ **14.4 m²** chamber
- Office 10 000 m² UFS → 10 000 ÷ 925 ≈ **10.8 m²** chamber
- Composite 4 000 m² domestic + 6 000 m² non-domestic → (4 000÷347) + (6 000÷925) ≈ **11.5 + 6.5 = 18.0 m²**
- Aggregate UFS for composite trigger = 4 000 + (6 000÷3) = **6 000 m²** → ordinary chamber (≥1 320, <13 200)

**SD takeaway:** Below Domestic 1 320 / Non-dom 3 960 / Aggregate 1 320 there is **no reg. 3 chamber**, but domestic floors may still need **rooms** under §3.

### 3. Floor-by-floor refuse rooms — domestic / composite domestic (reg. 3A)

#### 3.1 Default rule
Plans for a **domestic building** or the **domestic part of a composite** must show a **refuse storage and material recovery room on every floor** of that domestic building / domestic part (reg. 3A(1)).

#### 3.2 Built-in skips (do not over-provide)
| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| Floor **not designed for habitation** | No room required (reg. 3A(4)(a)) |
| Floor already has a reg. 3 chamber / MRC | No room required on that floor (reg. 3A(4)(b)) |
| **Multi-floor unit** (duplex / triplex spanning consecutive floors) | Room on **at least one** of those floors for that unit — unless a chamber/MRC is already shown on any of those floors (reg. 3A(2)–(3)) |
| Hotel / guest-house / boarding-house part | Reg. 3A **does not apply** to that part (reg. 3A(5)) |

#### 3.3 Whole-building / site exemptions from reg. 3A (reg. 3A(6))
Reg. 3A does **not** apply (to the extent of that building) where:
1. Domestic or composite with **one staircase only**; **or**
2. Domestic for **one single family** with **≤ 3 floors** designed for habitation; **or**
3. Composite whose domestic part has **≤ 3 floors** and is for **one single family** only; **or**
4. Domestic or composite on a site of area **≤ 500 m²**

**SD takeaway:** Core planning must reserve a recovery room on each typical domestic floor (min dim still ≥1.5 m per reg. 8(1) via 4B) unless an exemption clearly fits — single-stair / single-family / small-site is the early escape hatch.

### 4. Which physical standards apply to which facility (regs. 4–4B)

| Facility | Must comply with |
|---|---|
| Refuse storage & material recovery **chamber** | Regs **5–12A** (full chamber package) |
| **Material recovery chamber** | Same as chambers: regs **5–12A** |
| Floor **room** (reg. 3A) | Reg **8(1)** (min dimension ≥1.5 m), **12A** (mech vent + air purifying), **12B** (readily accessible), **12C** (≥120 lux) — **not** the full enclosure / external-wall / steel-door / drainage / flushing-water package |

### 5. Chamber location, access & envelope — SD geometry (regs. 5–8, 10)

Bake into ground-floor / G/F service planning:

| Item | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Design + location | BA-**approved** design and location with **ready access** for removing containers and recovered materials (reg. 5(1)) |
| Pedestrian approach (non-vehicular chamber) | Passage / alley ≥ **1.5 m** wide, **no steps**, paved, gradient ≤ **1 in 20** (reg. 5(2)) |
| Openings | Enclosed except: access door (reg. 10), refuse-chute opening, hopper opening — **no other openings** (reg. 6) |
| External wall | **≥ 1** wall must be an **external wall** (reg. 7) |
| Min plan dimension | Any dimension ≥ **1.5 m** (reg. 8(1)) — also applies to rooms via 4B |
| Clear height | Ceiling ≥ **2 m** throughout (reg. 8(2)) — chambers |
| Door | Close-fitting **steel** door in an **external wall**; height ≥ **1.8 m**; width ≥ **1.25 m**; lockable; internal face **without projections** (reg. 10) |

**SD takeaway:** You cannot bury a chamber deep in the plan with only an internal door. External wall + 1.25 × 1.8 m steel door + ≤1:20 approach corridor (or true FEHD-vehicle access for “with vehicular access”) locks podium / G/F disposition early. Coordinate with Cap 123F open-air / lane / lane-widening assumptions.

**Vehicular-access chambers (Schedule triggers):** size the bay for collection vehicles used by the **Director of Food and Environmental Hygiene** — turning, headroom, door width beyond the 1.25 m pedestrian door, and structural floor loading. Agree vehicle envelope with FEHD / AP early; Cap 123H states the duty, not a drawing template.

### 6. Chamber construction, wet services & air (regs. 9, 11–12A)

| Topic | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Structure | Brick / concrete / other BA-approved (reg. 9(1)) |
| Wall finish | Internal faces lined with **glazed brick/tile** or approved (reg. 9(2)) |
| Ceiling | Cement rendered, smooth (reg. 9(3)) |
| Floor | Concrete ≥ **100 mm**; fall to gully; quarry tile / hard impervious finish; wall–floor junction **coved** (reg. 9(4)–(5)) |
| Drainage | Floor outlet with grating → pipe ID ≥ **100 mm** → **back-inlet trapped gully immediately outside** chamber with airtight access cover → **foul-water** drain (reg. 11) |
| Cleaning water | Water supply point inside chamber, fed from the building’s **flushing water** to soil fitments (reg. 12) — not potable mains as default narrative |
| Mech ventilation | ≥ **3 air changes / hour** fresh air to all parts (reg. 12A(a)) |
| Exhaust | Air purifying on the exhaust, to BA satisfaction (reg. 12A(b)) |

**Rooms (3A)** also need **12A** vent/purifying + **readily accessible at all times** by any occupier (12B) + illumination ≥ **120 lux** at finished floor (12C).

**SD takeaway:** Chamber = wet foul room + ME vent shaft / purify kit + flushing tee. Show plant route and exhaust discharge early (odour / FS Code interface).

### 7. Refuse chutes — optional but fully prescribed if used (regs. 13–25)

Chutes are **not** mandated by Cap 123H; if you provide one, it **must** be vertical (subject to foot/top offsets), terminate in a **refuse storage and material recovery chamber**, and meet regs 14–25.

| Topic | Hard number / rule |
|---|---|
| Alignment | Vertical; **no bend/offset** except at foot or **above** highest hopper (reg. 14(1)) |
| Foot offset (if any) | Slope ≥ **60°** to horizontal; replaceable **galv. or stainless** steel ≥ **3 mm** thick (reg. 14(2)) |
| Top above hoppers | Chute top ≥ **300 mm** above highest hopper (reg. 15) |
| Bore | Lined with glazed / impervious tubes; ID ≥ **450 mm** (reg. 16) |
| Walls | Solid brick or concrete ≥ **100 mm** exclusive of lining (reg. 17) |
| Base shutter | Galv. or stainless steel shutter to close chute (reg. 18) |
| Vent pipe | At top; area ≥ **0.05 m²**; extend ≥ **1 m above roof**; grille apertures aggregate ≥ pipe section, each aperture ≤ **10 mm** least dim (reg. 19) |
| Cleanout | Access ≥ **225 mm** dia above highest hopper + close-fitting metal cover (reg. 20) |
| Internal surface | Smooth, impervious; glazed ware / approved; pipe joints cement mortar 1:1 or approved, solidly bedded (reg. 21) |
| Hopper location | In a place **permanently ventilated to the open air** (reg. 22) |
| Hopper mouth | Clear opening between **250 × 150 mm** and **350 × 250 mm** (reg. 23(1)) |
| Hopper behaviour | Only fully open / fully closed; no self-open; dust/fume tight; no lodging of refuse; plate ≥ **3 mm** galv./stainless; inner plate ≥ **45°** down when closed (reg. 23) |
| Recess | Frame/moving parts fully housed — **nothing** projects into the chute (reg. 24) |
| Junction | Continuous smooth impervious surface into chute (reg. 25) |

**SD takeaway:** Chute is a vertical shaft claim (450+ mm ID + 100 mm wall + hoppers in permanently open-air spaces + roof vent ≥1 m). Prefer chute-free bag / cart systems only if operationally clear; if chute is in the concept, stack it on the chamber and reserve open-air hopper lobbies (not sealed AC corridors).

### 8. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing G/F service layout, cores, and domestic typical floors:

1. **Classify the building** — domestic / non-domestic / industrial / composite; compute **UFS** (and for composite, **aggregate UFS** = domestic + ⅓ non-domestic).
2. **Hit Schedule thresholds?** — if yes, reserve chamber floor space (÷347 / ÷925 / ÷2320) and flag **vehicular access** at 13 200 / 39 600 / 13 200 aggregate as applicable.
3. **Church / school / car park?** — reg. 3 chamber duty off; still confirm any voluntary chute/chamber meets regs 4–25 if provided.
4. **Domestic floors** — unless reg. 3A(5)–(6) exemption fits, put a recovery **room** on every habitable domestic floor (duplex: ≥1 floor per multi-floor unit).
5. **Chamber envelope** — ≥1 external wall, steel door ≥1.25 × 1.8 m, clear height ≥2 m, every plan dim ≥1.5 m, no stray openings.
6. **Access path** — non-vehicular: ≥1.5 m, step-free, paved, ≤1:20; vehicular: FEHD vehicle envelope + in/out.
7. **Wet + air** — foul gully immediately outside, 100 mm drain, flushing water point, ≥3 ACH + exhaust purify.
8. **Rooms** — ≥1.5 m min dim, 12A vent/purify, 24/7 accessible, ≥120 lux.
9. **Chute (if any)** — vertical to chamber; 450 mm ID; open-air hoppers; roof vent ≥0.05 m² / ≥1 m above roof; no mid-height bends.
10. **Cross-links** — UFS definition overlaps Cap 123I sanitary counting; foul connection Cap 123I; plan presentation Cap 123A; do not confuse with Cap 123F PR/SC.
