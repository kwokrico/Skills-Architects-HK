# Cap 123F — Building (Planning) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 16 June 2024 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. F

> Scope note: Cap 123F is the **BO planning rulebook** — site class, site coverage (SC), plot ratio (PR), open space, lanes/setbacks, street projections, natural light & air, stair / MOE / firefighting access / EVA envelopes, domestic & cinema planning, temporary buildings / hoardings, and barrier-free access (reg. 72 + Third Schedule). It is **not** OZP / lease development intensity (often more restrictive), not Cap 123A submission package detail, and not FS Code MOE geometry detail. At SD: classify the site → freeze SC/PR/height band → lock open space & light wells → size MOE / fireman’s lift / EVA → design BFA routes.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123F applies to **building works under the Buildings Ordinance** and sets how a building may occupy its lot: site classification A/B/C, permitted SC/PR by height, domestic open space and service lanes, street projections, habitable/office lighting & ventilation, staircases and emergency access (including fireman’s lifts, firefighting & rescue stairways, EVA), kitchens/tenement rules, places of public entertainment / cinemas, temporary buildings / hoardings, and mandatory design for persons with a disability.

It governs **all classes of buildings** that need BD approval; SC/PR tables and open-space rules are keyed to **domestic / non-domestic / composite** use and **class A, B or C sites** (or BA discretion if the site is none of these). Always take the **lowest** of Cap 123F First Schedule, OZP Notes, and lease / Conditions of Sale.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 0. Definitions that drive SD decisions (reg. 2)

Lock these before area schedules and light-well calcs:

| Term | Statutory meaning (design implication) |
|---|---|
| **Site coverage** | Area of site covered by the building (or by that part, for composite parts) |
| **Plot ratio** | GFA ÷ site area (reg. 21(3)) |
| **Storey** | Space between upper surface of a floor and upper surface of floor above; top storey = floor to mean height of ceiling/roof |
| **Usable floor space** | Floor space **excluding** staircases, staircase halls, lift landings, WC/urinal/lavatory-basin space, and machinery for lift / AC / similar |
| **Open air** | Vertically uncovered & unobstructed; every horizontal dim ≥ **1.5 m**; if enclosed on 4 sides → horizontal area ≥ **1 m² per 6 m** mean enclosing-wall height |
| **Balcony** | Projects from wall; carries floor/roof load (cantilever or brackets) |
| **Canopy** | Projects **> 500 mm**, height ≤ **7.5 m** above ground, rain/sun protection, **no floor load** |
| **Verandah** | Projects from wall; supported on piers/columns |
| **Detached building** | Clear open space ≥ **2.3 m** along entire depth **and** ≥ **2.3 m** full-width rear |
| **Semi-detached** | Pair linked by party wall; same 2.3 m side + rear open-space rule |
| **Room** | Portion subdivided by partitions from floor to ceiling |
| **External wall** | Outer wall (or part), not a party wall — even if adjoining another building |
| **Street / road** | Street includes footpath and private/public street; **road** = street excluding footpath |
| **Access facilities** | Telecom/broadcasting rooms, ducts, risers for cables/wires/ancillary plant |
| **Disability** | Impairment in vision, hearing or locomotion (injury, disease or congenital deformity) |
| **Cinema** | Building/part designed for cinematographic displays, with projection room & apparatus |
| **Hotel** (reg. 23A) | Premises held out as providing sleeping accommodation to any person presenting themselves for a fee |

### 1. Lock the site class before any massing (reg. 18A)

| Class | Definition | Abutting % rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Class A** | Abuts **one** (or more) specified street(s) ≥ **4.5 m** wide — and is **not** B or C | Default / mid-block |
| **Class B** | **Corner** site abutting **2** specified streets, each ≥ 4.5 m | ≥ **40%** of site boundary must abut those streets |
| **Class C** | **Corner** site abutting **3** specified streets, each ≥ 4.5 m | ≥ **60%** of site boundary must abut those streets |

**Specified street** (reg. 18A(3)) — only counts if the whole street is composed of parts that each meet one of:
1. vested in Government **and** maintained by Highways Department; **or**
2. private street on land held under the **same Government lease** as the site, and lease requires surrender of that street land when required; **or**
3. street required to be constructed on **unleased Government land** by the lessee under the site’s lease; **or**
4. street on land over which the site owner has an express **right of way exercisable at all times**; **or**
5. street on land held under a Government lease **by the owner of the site**

**SD kill switches:**
- Site is **not** A/B/C → height, SC and PR are **entirely BA-determined** (reg. 19(3)) — get in-principle advice early
- Incomplete corner frontage (< 40% / 60%) → stays class A — lose B/C SC & PR uplift
- BA alone determines street width for Cap 123F purposes (reg. 6)
- Every building needs access from a street; BA may require on-site **access lane or access road** (reg. 5)

### 2. Site coverage & plot ratio — First Schedule + maths (regs. 19–23A)

#### 2.1 How SC/PR are chosen (regs. 19–21)
- Class A/B/C → SC from reg. 20 + First Schedule; PR from reg. 21 + First Schedule  
- SC/PR **depend on building height band** (not storey count alone)  
- Domestic part of composite uses **domestic** SC columns; ND part uses **ND** columns  
- Non-class site → BA sets height, SC and PR (reg. 19(3))

#### 2.2 Height measurement for SC/PR (reg. 23(1))
From **mean level of the specified street(s) ≥ 4.5 m** that the building fronts/abuts  
→ to **mean height of roof over the highest usable floor space**.  
If those streets have different levels → measure from the **lower / lowest** street mean level.

#### 2.3 Site area for SC/PR (reg. 23(2))
| Include | Exclude |
|---|---|
| Areas **dedicated to the public for passage** | Any part of a **street** or **service lane** |

#### 2.4 GFA definition & BA discounts (reg. 23(3))
**GFA** = area within external walls at each floor level (**including floors below ground**)  
+ area of each balcony (overall dimensions including thickness of sides)  
+ thickness of external walls.

BA **may disregard** floor space **solely** for:
- parking motor vehicles  
- loading / unloading of motor vehicles  
- refuse storage chambers; refuse storage & material recovery chambers; material recovery chambers; refuse storage & material recovery rooms; refuse chutes; refuse hopper rooms; other facilities to facilitate refuse separation (to BA satisfaction)  
- **access facilities** for telecommunications and broadcasting services  
- space occupied solely by machinery/equipment for any lift, air-conditioning or heating system or similar service  

> Not automatic freebies — design so BA will accept disregard. OZP / lease GFA definitions may still count these areas.

#### 2.5 Non-domestic SC privilege to 15 m (reg. 20(3)–(4))
On class A/B/C, ND building (or ND part of composite) may **exceed** First Schedule SC **up to 15 m above ground**, whatever the building height — but:
- above that level, SC must return within permitted SC (reg. 20(1) or (2)); **and**
- **whole-building PR** must still respect permitted PR (subject to reg. 22)

**Classic podium-tower move:** near-full-coverage retail/office podium ≤ 15 m, then tower on domestic or ND SC limit.

#### 2.6 Composite domestic PR formula (reg. 21(2))
\[
PR_{dom} = \frac{(PR_{ND,perm} - PR_{ND,actual}) \times PR_{D,perm}}{PR_{ND,perm}}
\]
where \(PR_{D,perm}\) / \(PR_{ND,perm}\) = First Schedule permitted PR if the building were wholly domestic / wholly non-domestic at that height and site class.

#### 2.7 Tiny caretaker / top-storey residence — ND treatment (reg. 23(4))
BA may treat as non-domestic a composite whose **only** domestic part is:
- caretaker / service residence ≤ **50 m²** usable floor space; and/or  
- residence comprising the **top storey** of the building only  

#### 2.8 Dedication / street-widening bonuses (reg. 22) — explicit caps

**Trigger A — dedication (reg. 22(1)):** between ground and ≥ **5.5 m** (or ≥ **3.3 m** if BA satisfied no obstruction to vehicular traffic), building set back from a lot boundary abutting a street, and the set-back land is dedicated (with Government consent) for public passage.

**Trigger B — Government acquisition (reg. 22(2)):** part of lot abutting a street acquired by Government for street widening (agreement or Cap 124 resumption).

**SC bonus (either trigger):**
\[
SC_{max} = SC_{perm} + \frac{1500 \times A_{ded/acq}}{A_{site} \times H}
\]
(as percentage of site)

**PR bonus (either trigger)** — take the **lesser** of:
- \(PR_{perm} + 20\%\); or  
- \(PR_{perm} + \dfrac{5 \times A_{ded/acq}}{A_{site}}\)

**Hard limits:**
- Bonus SC on **one part** of the building → other parts still capped at normal SC  
- Does **not** waive domestic open space under reg. 25  
- Dedication land still counts in site area (reg. 23(2)(b))

#### 2.9 Hotels (reg. 23A) — optional ND treatment + GFA disregard
BA **may** treat hotel building (or hotel part) as **non-domestic**, and may disregard GFA for:
- place for picking up / setting down persons arriving or departing by vehicle  
- laundry; carpentry workshop; mechanical or electrical workshop  
- storage of dry goods, food, beverages, linen or furniture  
- staff canteen, changing room, rest room  
- other supporting facilities BA approves  

**Enforcement traps (regs. 23A(4)–(9)):**
- Cannot change use away from hotel without prior BA approval  
- No Cap 349 licence in effect, or Cap 349 exclusion order in effect → deemed change of use  
- Disregarded floors cannot be used for other purposes without BA approval  
- Offence: fine at level 6 + 2 years; failure to comply with BA discontinue-use order: fine at level 5 + 1 year + $5,000/day

### 4. Open space, storey height, setbacks, cuttings, service lanes (regs. 24–28)

#### 4.1 Storey height — habitation / office (reg. 24)
| Rule | Dimension |
|---|---|
| Floor to ceiling | ≥ **2.5 m** |
| Floor to underside of any beam | ≥ **2.3 m** |
| Sloping ceiling | measure to **mean** height; **no portion** < **2.0 m** |

#### 4.2 Domestic open space (reg. 25 + Second Schedule)
Every domestic building on class A/B/C must have open space **at the rear, or partly at rear and partly at the side**, at a level ≥ **150 mm below** the floor of the lowermost storey:

| Site class | Open space ≥ |
|---|---|
| A | **one-half** of the roofed-over area of the building |
| B | **one-third** of the roofed-over area |
| C | **one-quarter** of the roofed-over area |

**Geometry traps (regs. 25(1)(b)–(6)):**
- No part of building bounding the open space may be within **1.5 m** horizontally of a vertical line drawn from a point on the **opposite** open-space boundary  
- No part of any domestic building within **1.5 m** of the **rear boundary** (that strip counts toward open space)  
- Existing domestic with open space ≤ required → cannot reduce existing amount  
- Existing domestic with open space > required → cannot reduce below required  
- Level difference > **600 mm** between adjoining open spaces → safe parapets / railings / fences by the person creating the difference  
- **Access** shall be provided to every open space  
- BA may require **more** than Second Schedule for proper/equitable development of an adjacent site  
- Reg. 22 dedication / acquisition bonuses **do not** waive reg. 25

#### 4.3 Narrow street setback (reg. 26)
Where an existing street in front of a **new** building is < **4.5 m** wide → no part of the building nearer than **2.25 m** to the street centre line.

#### 4.4 Cuttings (reg. 27)
- No building abutting a cutting (including a toe wall supporting a cutting)  
- Clear intervening space at ground-floor level ≥ **¼ of cutting height**, and in no case < **2.5 m**  
- Cutting height = vertical from toe up to intersection with a line drawn **downward at 30° to horizontal** from the top of the cutting

#### 4.5 Service lanes (reg. 28)
Every domestic building needs a service lane at rear or side, **except** when:
- a public lane ≥ **3 m** wide or a street already exists; **or**
- detached or semi-detached building; **or**
- BA exemption  

Lane must be accessible from an existing street (or would become accessible on future redevelopment of other lots in the block). Alignment, width and levels = BA decision. If BA requires lane > **1.5 m**, BA may modify reg. 25.

### 5. Projections & street interface (regs. 3–7, 10–15)

#### 5.1 Balcony / opening protection
| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Upper-storey balcony clear height (reg. 3(3)) | ≥ clear height of the storey from which it projects |
| External openings above GF (reg. 3A) | Barrier ≥ **1 100 mm** high; lowest **150 mm** built solid; design to minimise falling / rolling / sliding / climbing |

#### 5.2 Nuisance / access (regs. 4–5)
- No building/fixture may obstruct or endanger users of adjacent footpath/street, create nuisance, or discharge noxious gases/exhaust over street/footpath at < **2.5 m** height  
- Every building: means of obtaining access from a street; BA may require access lane/road within the site of a new building

#### 5.3 Projections over streets (reg. 7) — all soffits ≥ 2.5 m unless noted

| Element | Max projection | Other |
|---|---|---|
| Architectural projection (eaves, cornice, moulding) | **500 mm** | soffit ≥ 2.5 m |
| Pipe or gutter (incl. appurtenances) | **300 mm** | soffit ≥ 2.5 m |
| Specified structure = drying rack **or** supporting frame for AC unit / light fitting / antenna or transceiver for public telecom | **750 mm** | soffit ≥ 2.5 m |
| Retractable awning | Retracted **500 mm** / fully extended **2.5 m** | soffit ≥ 2.5 m; ≥ **600 mm** horizontal clearance from pavement kerb (if carriageway); ≥ **1.5 m** from centre line of footpath-only street |
| Metal ventilation duct (+ supporting frame) | **750 mm** | soffit ≥ 2.5 m; must **not** project beyond edge of balcony / verandah / canopy if hung on/under them |

#### 5.4 Canopies over streets (reg. 10)
| Condition | Clear space beneath |
|---|---|
| Within **600 mm** of outer edge of footpath, **or** projecting over a road | ≥ **5.5 m** |
| Over a footpath (general) | ≥ **3.3 m** |

- Adequate surface-water drainage required  
- Max projection (incl. cornices/mouldings/features) = **lesser of 1/10 street width or 3 m**  
- No portion within **4.5 m** horizontally of a vertical from the street centre line  

#### 5.5 Use / doorway restrictions (regs. 12–15)
- **No doorway** giving direct access onto the top of any canopy (reg. 12)  
- Verandah/balcony built over a street: **not** for factory, workshop, storeroom, kitchen, lavatory, bathroom, WC, urinal or latrine (reg. 13)  
- Doors / gates / windows / shutters must not project over street below **2.5 m** — **except** emergency exit doors, electrical transformer room doors, plant room doors, refuse storage chamber doors, and similar utility doors, if when fully opened they do not obstruct persons or vehicles (reg. 15)

### 6. Lighting & ventilation — room depth & light-well geometry (regs. 29–37, 71)

#### 6.1 Habitation / office / kitchen (regs. 29–30)
Every storey used as office or for habitation needs effectual lighting & ventilation. Every room for habitation, office or kitchen needs **natural** lighting & ventilation by windows that:
| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Glass area | ≥ **1/10** of room floor area |
| Openable area | ≥ **1/16** of floor area |
| Top of opening | ≥ **2.0 m** above floor (**1.9 m** for detached / semi-detached) |
| Prescribed window | The 1/10 glass area must face **external air** |

#### 6.2 Room depth (reg. 32)
No part of any room used for habitation > **9 m** (measured within the room) from a prescribed window facing external air — or, if window opens onto enclosed/unenclosed verandah/balcony/conservatory (regs. 33 / 71), from the **outer edge** of that verandah/balcony/etc.

#### 6.3 What counts as “facing external air” (reg. 31)

A prescribed window faces external air only if **one** of:
**(a)** faces a street ≥ **4.5 m** wide; **or**  
**(b)+(c)** faces uncovered unobstructed space above a **rectangular horizontal plane (RHP)** and clears the inclined-plane test for an **internal** light well; **or**  
**(d)** opens onto an area bounded opposite by the **site boundary**, and clears the boundary inclined-plane test (with lane adjustments).

**RHP at sill level** (sill deemed **1 m** above floor whether or not it is):
| Parameter | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Area | **21 m²** |
| Base (along sill) | **2.3 m** |
| Depth to opposite wall/building within site | **4.5 m** |
| Depth to site boundary (boundary case) | **2.3 m** |
| Depth to line 1.5 m beyond boundary / lane centre (narrow lane case) | **2.3 m** |

**Inclined clear planes — no building (on site) may protrude above:**

| Case | Habitation | Office / kitchen |
|---|---|---|
| Internal light well — plane from RHP base (reg. 31(1)(c)) | **71½°** from horizontal | **76°** |
| Boundary opposite — plane from projected RHP (reg. 31(1)(d)) | **80½°** | **83°** |

**Narrow lane / street < 4.5 m** adjacent to and parallel with the boundary:
- For inclined-plane (1)(d): treat boundary as **1.5 m beyond** actual boundary  
- For RHP depth: sides measured to a line **1.5 m beyond** boundary; if lane/street < **3 m** wide → to the **centre line** of the lane/street

#### 6.4 Window onto enclosed verandah / balcony / conservatory (reg. 33)
Deemed to comply with regs. 30–31 if:
- the verandah/balcony/conservatory itself has a window that would satisfy 30–31 for a room whose floor area = **room + verandah/balcony/etc.**; **and**
- the room’s own glass & openable area would satisfy reg. 30(2)(a) for that same aggregate floor area

#### 6.5 Offices without natural compliance (reg. 34)
If, owing to position/level/surroundings, reg. 31 cannot be met to BA satisfaction → provide:
- mechanical ventilation ≥ **5 air changes per hour** fresh air to all parts of the room; **and**
- BA-approved artificial lighting

#### 6.6 Additional vent (reg. 35)
BA may require extra ventilation (intended use / surroundings) via:
- aperture or airshaft to open air ≥ **0.015 m²** unobstructed sectional area; **or**
- fanlight to ventilated lobby/corridor; **or**
- other BA-approved means  

Laundry part of / used with a building is deemed used for habitation for regs. 29–35.

#### 6.7 Room-sealed gas water heater (reg. 35A)
Applies unless building plans approved (or deemed approved) on/before **20 May 1983**, or building designed (to BA satisfaction) with centralised hot water supply.  
Every bathroom (or place where the heater serving it is installed) needs **suitable provision** = adequate aperture in an **external wall** giving direct access to external air for a room-sealed gas water heater, sealable with a readily removable cover when no heater is installed.

#### 6.8 Soil / waste fitment rooms (reg. 36)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Window or lantern light | Required |
| Glass area | ≥ **1/10** floor |
| Openable area | ≥ **1/10** floor, **directly into open air** |
| Top of openable part | ≥ **2.0 m** above floor |
| Soil-fitment room | Must **not** open directly into food manufacture/prep/storage room, or into PPE auditorium / waiting space |

Soil fitment = WC fitment, trough WC, or urinal.

#### 6.9 Neighbour light & air (reg. 37) + BA discretion (reg. 71)
- New work must not reduce light/air available to another Cap-123F-compliant building below Cap 123F minima  
- BA may permit windows/vents/apertures to face/ventilate into an **unenclosed** verandah/balcony/place — only if standards of lighting/ventilation and health of occupiers are not prejudiced; may impose conditions on plan approval

### 7. Stairs, MOE, firefighting access, EVA (regs. 39–44)

Cap 123F sets **triggers & minima**; detail geometry lives in FS Code / CoP MOE / EVA PNAPs.

#### 7.1 Main staircase (reg. 39)
Every building > 1 storey needs stair(s) to upper floors unless separate access exists.

| Item | Minimum / maximum |
|---|---|
| Clear height | ≥ **2.0 m** |
| Clear width | ≥ **900 mm** |
| Tread (centre of flight) | ≥ **225 mm** face-to-face of risers |
| Riser | ≤ **175 mm** |
| Steps per flight | ≤ **16** without a landing |
| Handrail | One or both sides; tubular Ø **38–50 mm** external, or rectangular **40–50 mm** wide with depth (or to deep groove) ≤ **50 mm**, or analogous grip |
| Arrangement | Access to a street or to open space leading thereto |
| Roof continuation | Main stair of buildings > **4 storeys** must continue to roof **unless** a secondary fire-escape stair is provided |
| Roof escape door | If stair continued to roof as fire escape → door with **glazed upper panels** |

#### 7.2 Common stairs & escalators (regs. 40–40A)
- Building for separate occupation by > **2 tenants**: common stair needs adequate natural lighting at each storey above GF + ventilation at least at its highest point  
- Escalator is **in addition to** reg. 39 stairs and reg. 41 MOE / second stair — never a substitute  
- Escalator is **not** a staircase/passage/normal means of egress for reg. 43

#### 7.3 Means of escape (regs. 41–44)
| Trigger / rule | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Every building | MOE as required by intended use |
| Second staircase | Mandatory if > **6 storeys** **or** uppermost floor > **17 m** above ground at main-stair discharge |
| Travel distance (reg. 43) | Every part intended for habitation, or any school, or place of public assembly ≤ **24 m** from staircase / passage / other normal egress |
| Landings, lobbies, passages (reg. 42) | Min dimension ≥ width of the staircase they lead to |
| Revolving doors / turnstiles (reg. 44) | Alternative exit in close proximity; in PPE, turnstiles kept clear of exit line |

#### 7.4 Access staircases for firemen (reg. 41A)
- Every building > 1 storey: adequate number of access staircases for safe unobstructed firemen access to all floors (number by intended use + floor size); protected from fire & smoke  
- **Exempt:** solely domestic single-household buildings ≤ **3 main storeys**

#### 7.5 Fireman’s lifts (reg. 41B)
**Required** if building > **2 storeys**, such that no part of floors served is > **60 m** from a fireman’s lift.

Design / install so that:
- at least every **alternate floor** is served by ≥ 1 fireman’s lift  
- adequate size (car + doors) for firemen with equipment  
- enclosed by liftwell with ≤ **3** fireman’s lifts and **no other** lifts  
- firemen protected from fire/smoke (esp. when leaving lifts)  
- exclusive firemen control in fire  
- safe unobstructed access to lifts and to floors served  

**Cubical extent** (for exemptions / 41C): space within external surfaces of walls & roof and upper surface of lowest-storey floor — exclude roof enclosures used exclusively for water tank / lift gear / other services; unenclosed side deemed enclosed by wall from outer edge of roof.

**Exempt if any of:**

| Exemption | All conditions |
|---|---|
| Single-stair low-rise | Only **1** staircase **and** ≤ **6** storeys **and** uppermost floor ≤ **17 m** above stair discharge |
| Domestic | Height ≤ **30 m** (height per reg. 23(1)) |
| Non-domestic / ND part of composite | No industrial undertaking; not bulk storage / warehouse / PPE / hotel or guesthouse / hospital; **and** either height ≤ **15 m**, **or** height ≤ **30 m** **and** cubical extent ≤ **7 000 m³** |

#### 7.6 Firefighting & rescue stairway (reg. 41C)
Combined **access staircase + fireman’s lift** in one stairway.

**Applies to:**
1. Building used/intended for industrial undertaking, bulk storage or warehouse, with height > **30 m**, **or** cubical extent > **7 000 m³** and height > 1 storey; **and**
2. Every basement with cubical extent > **7 000 m³** **and** either > **2 storeys** **or** depth > **9 m** (ground-storey floor → lowest basement floor)

**Rules:**
- Number such that no part of building (incl. basement) / basement is > **60 m** from such a stairway  
- No lift other than fireman’s lift in the stairway; ≤ 3 fireman’s lifts  
- If roof accessible by staircase → access staircase of the FR stairway must serve the roof  
- Fireman’s lift need not serve top floor if BA satisfied machine room must be on that floor (no practicable alternative)  

**Basement** = any storey below ground storey from which any required exit route is upward.  
**Ground storey** = storey with entrance from a street; if multiple street levels with entrances on different storeys → each such storey is a ground storey.

#### 7.7 EVA (reg. 41D)
- **Every building** needs emergency vehicular access for safe unobstructed FSD vehicle access and safe operation in fire/emergency  
- Design & construction per BA requirements for the intended use  
- BA may exempt any/all design requirements if compliance is impracticable (topography) or unwarranted (low fire risk) — **only if** compensating fire-safety measures are imposed so safety is not prejudiced

### 8. Domestic planning rules (regs. 45–49)

#### 8.1 Kitchens (reg. 45)
- Every domestic building, and (unless BA exempts) any part intended to be separately let for dwelling, needs kitchen accommodation  
- Internal wall surface to ≥ **1.2 m** above floor: tiles, or cement mortar ≥ **12.5 mm**, or other non-absorbent material  
- Proper fireplace or cooking slab **unless** cooking by gas, oil or electricity  
- Sink + fittings for water supply  

#### 8.2 Tenement houses (reg. 46)
**Tenement house** = domestic part in which any living room (cooking or sleeping) is intended/adapted for use of more than one tenant/sub-tenant.

| Rule | Limit |
|---|---|
| Depth (front main wall, or balcony front if projected → nearest rear main wall) | ≤ **10 m** unless BA exempts |
| Rear window each storey | Glass ≥ **1.5 m²**; openable ≥ **1.5 m²** into open air; top of opening ≥ **2 m** above floor |
| Windows required under Cap 123F | Must not be obstructed by any structure inside or outside |
| Kitchen area (unit ≤ 45 m²) | ≥ **3.75 m²** |
| Kitchen area (unit > 45–70 m²) | ≥ **4.5 m²** |
| Kitchen area (unit > 70 m²) | ≥ **5.5 m²** |
| Kitchen smaller dimension | ≥ **1.5 m** |

#### 8.3 Retaining walls (regs. 47–48)
- No domestic building against a retaining wall > **4.5 m** high  
- Leave ≥ **1.5 m** between domestic building and toe of such wall  
- Massive rock face = retaining wall for this regulation  
- RW forming part of a domestic building (subject to reg. 27): properly waterproofed against dampness; properly insulated against condensation on internal face of any habitable room  

#### 8.4 Dangerous trades + domestic (reg. 49)
No building used/designed for domestic purposes or intended for habitation may also be used for (unless BA exempts with prescribed structural/other requirements):
- manufacture of dangerous goods (Cap 295); or storage of DG to which Cap 295 s.6 applies  
- motor repair shop; vulcanizing shop  
- automobile or carriage painting  
- paint shop where paint/varnish is manufactured or mixed  
- dry-cleaning  

**Exception:** in a building used for those purposes, ≤ **50 m²** may be used as residence for a caretaker or person employed in connection with maintenance/services for the building.

### 10. Barrier-free access — reg. 72 + Third Schedule

#### 10.1 When reg. 72 applies
Where persons with a disability have, or may reasonably be expected to have, access → design to BA satisfaction to facilitate access to and use of the building and its facilities.  
**Deemed compliance** = Third Schedule **Part 2**.

**Exempt (reg. 72(4)):**
- buildings ≤ **13 m** above ground used/intended for occupation by a **single family**  
- temporary buildings or contractor’s sheds (Part VII)

#### 10.2 Extent by building category (Third Schedule Part 4)

**Domestic buildings**
- If > **4 storeys**: all **common areas**  
- If ≤ 4 storeys: main entrance + common areas of GF + means of access  
- Also: all accessible guest rooms required under s.7 (hotels etc. in domestic context where applicable)  
- **Exclude:** plant/maintenance-only spaces (plant, cooling tower, power plant, lift motor / transformer / switch / battery / machinery / plant / pump / boiler rooms; ladder/catwalk/crawl-only non-tenantable spaces; maintenance access routes, pits, lift shafts, ventilation shafts; sub-station, telecom equipment room, metering area); storage/plant mezzanines; security/safety raised platforms (guard tower, fixed lifeguard stand); swimming-pool water only; paths serving only those exclusions  

**Non-domestic buildings**
- **All parts**, excluding: commercial kitchen, cold room, cinema projector room; same plant/maintenance list as above; hazardous or non-public bulk/raw-material storage (waste containment, chemical store, etc.); storage/plant mezzanines; security platforms; pool water; paths only to those  

**Composite buildings**
- Domestic rules for domestic parts **+** non-domestic rules for ND parts **+** accessible guest rooms under s.7  

#### 10.3 Auditoria (Division 1) — theatres, cinemas, concert halls, stadiums, game halls, lecture/conference halls
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| ≤ 800 fixed seats at spectator level | ≥ **4** wheelchair spaces at spectator level |
| > 800 fixed seats | ≥ **2** wheelchair spaces per **400** seats (or part) |
| Adjacency | Each wheelchair space adjacent to ≥ 1 other wheelchair space **and** to fixed seats |
| Size | Side facing activity ≥ **800 mm**; other side ≥ **1 300 mm** |
| Approach passage | ≥ **1 500 mm** from accessible entrance |
| Removable seats | Allowed in wheelchair spaces when unused |
| Accessible rooms | Auditorium, stage, backstage, changing, rehearsal, dressing, rest, toilets, showers — all wheelchair-accessible; connecting routes accessible |
| Wayfinding | Braille & tactile fire-exit maps at all auditorium entrances; tactile warning strips at top & bottom of stairs to stage |

#### 10.4 Hotels / hostels / guesthouses (Division 2)
**2 accessible guest rooms per 100 guest rooms** (or part thereof).  
Accessible guest room = accessible location + all sleeping, bathing and sanitary facilities accessible.

#### 10.5 Carparks (Division 3)
| Total parking spaces | Min accessible spaces |
|---|---|
| 1–50 | 1 |
| 51–150 | 2 |
| 151–250 | 3 |
| 251–350 | 4 |
| 351–450 | 5 |
| Above 450 | 6 |

- Accessible route from accessible bays to lobby with accessible lift or entrance  
- Bay width ≥ **3 500 mm**; **or** ≥ **2 500 mm** each if shared loading strip ≥ **1 200 mm** (yellow hatched) between two accessible bays  
- Floor marked with bay number + international symbol of accessibility ≥ **1 500 × 1 500 mm**

#### 10.6 Access route (Division 4)
- From a **prominent point on the lot boundary** accessible to a public street/pedestrian way, directly to a commonly used public entrance (or point adjacent) **and** to an accessible lift  
- Exception: impracticable due to difficult terrain / unusual site characteristics → provide vehicular access route + conspicuous directional signs from main entrance to accessible entrance  
- Clear width ≥ **1 050 mm**; firm surface; free of wheelchair barriers (except Schedule-compliant features)  
- **Tactile guide path** required on access routes for building uses in Division 21 items **1, 4, 5, 7, 10, 11, 13** (see §10.15)

#### 10.7 Ramps (Division 5)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| When required | All level changes not served by accessible lift / accessible lifting mechanism |
| Width | ≥ **1 050 mm** |
| Max slope | **1:12**; minor single rises: **1:10** (length ≤ 1 500 mm, rise ≤ 150 mm) or **1:8** (length ≤ 600 mm, rise ≤ 75 mm) |
| Head/foot landing | ≥ **1 500 × 1 500 mm** clear; no door swings into landing |
| If gradient ≥ 1:20 | Intermediate landing ≥ **1 200 mm** long each 10 m horizontal run; handrails both sides (Div. 8); tactile warning strips at head, foot & landings — **except** ramp to lift, or ramp < 300 mm long |
| Edge protection | Kerb ≥ **100 mm** or rail **200 mm** above ramp, both sides |
| Vehicular hazard | Rise > 200 mm leading toward vehicular area → railing/barrier ≥ **1 500 mm** from foot of ramp across full width |
| Projections | No wall projections > **90 mm** below **2 000 mm** above ramp (unless unavoidable → extend to ramp level or guide by tactile flooring) |
| Contrast | Floor and wall along ramps in contrasting colours; avoid raised traction strips on ramp surface |

#### 10.8 Dropped kerbs (Division 6)
At pedestrian crossings and each end of private-street / access-road footpath; also any kerb separating footpath/ramp from vehicular area.
- ≥ **1 200 × 1 200 mm**; back-of-footway clearance ≥ **800 mm**  
- Gradient ≤ **1:10**; level difference to vehicular area ≤ **15 mm**  
- Tactile warning strip 300 mm from vehicular area; tactile strip nominal width **600 mm** at the ramp  
- Unobstructed view of approaching traffic; no raised traction strips on surface  

#### 10.9 Steps & staircases (Division 7) — BFA required staircases
Applies to required staircases (access stair in FR stairway, or MOE stair).
- Non-slip nosing (not projecting); nosing/tread contrasting; tread/wall contrasting  
- Internal riser ≤ **175 mm**; external riser ≤ **160 mm**  
- Riser vertical **or** receding ≤ **15 mm** from perpendicular at next nosing  
- Handrails **both sides**; ≤ **16** steps per flight  
- Tactile warning strips at landings and top/bottom: **300 mm** wide if landing enclosed/leads to floor; **600 mm** if landing leads to open space or building entrance/exit (then Braille & tactile info on adjacent wall for the opening)  

#### 10.10 Handrails (Division 8)
| Item | Dimension |
|---|---|
| Clear of wall | **30–50 mm** |
| Bracket clear height | ≥ **70 mm** from top of bracket to top of handrail |
| Top height | **850–950 mm** above nosing / floor / landing |
| Profile | Tubular (or similar grip); external Ø **32–50 mm** |
| Extension | ≥ **300 mm** beyond first/last nosing or ramp ends (may shorten to ≥ **100 mm** if door opening in place); closed end turned down or returned to post/wall — must not project into travel route |
| Loading | Resist ≥ **1.3 kN** vertical or horizontal; no rotation in fixings |
| Braille / tactile | Directional arrows & floor numbers on handrails every floor; where directional signs exist, Braille/tactile per Fig. 6 |

#### 10.11 Corridors, lobbies, paths (Division 9)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Clear width | ≥ **1 050 mm** |
| Dead-end / one-direction exit | ≥ **1 500 × 1 500 mm** space within **3 500 mm** |
| Lobby in corridor | Length ≥ **1 200 mm** (excluding door swings) |
| Building entrance | Level area ≥ **1 200 mm** beyond door swings **and** ≥ **1 500 mm** wide, both sides |
| Exception | Does not apply to lobbies leading to staircases only |
| Channel covers | Flush; holes ≤ **20 mm** |
| Gratings | Slots ≤ **13 mm**; not parallel to travel |

| Controlled passage (cashier / anti-theft / turnstile) | Wheelchair path ≥ **800 mm** marked with accessibility symbol — or adjacent alternative path |
| Low headroom | If headroom ≤ **2 000 mm** → warning guardrail/barrier with leading edge ≤ **680 mm** AFFL |

#### 10.12 Doors on accessible routes (Division 10)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Clear width | ≥ **800 mm** |
| Leading-face unobstructed strip | ≥ **330 mm** beside handle |
| Door near corner | If < 330 mm from corner → swing from the side nearer that corner |
| Double-action self-closing | Check mechanism preventing swing beyond closed; transparent vision panel bottom ≤ **1 000 mm**, top ≥ **1 500 mm** AFFL |
| Thresholds | As limited in Schedule (keep flush / minimal — check s.42 for operative limit) |

#### 10.13 Accessible toilets (Division 11) — SD space claim
**Does not apply to domestic buildings / domestic parts of composites.**

### 12. Temporary buildings & contractor sheds (Part VII, regs. 50–58)

#### 12.1 What is temporary (reg. 50)
Building for which a BA permit is issued on a temporary basis because it is required only for a short time, constructed of **short-lived materials**, or a contractor’s shed for permanent works. BA may also include buildings on land held on licence.

**Short-lived materials** (weather-resisting roof/external wall context) include e.g.: thin / feather-edge boarding; fibre board / chipboard / compressed straw; wood-wool slabs; non-external plywood; plasterboard; fibrous plaster; lime/gypsum plaster on lath; thin cement plaster on lath; unprotected sheet iron/steel; organic-based felt; canvas/cloth; palm leaves/matting; unprotected softwood roof boarding; other combustible material.

#### 12.2 SD / site constraints
| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Permit (reg. 51) | Application in specified form + plans BA requires; permit may fix period & conditions |
| Combustible temporary building (reg. 52) | ≥ **3 m** from any other building **and** from site boundary; keep that space clear |
| Contractor sheds (reg. 53) | Application required (except sheds in contractors’ yards); state situation, dims, construction, duration, use; if for habitation — number of persons. Habitation floor: properly concreted **or** raised ≥ **1 m** above ground |
| Welfare on site (reg. 54) | Adequate kitchens & latrines for workmen; drainage to public drain/sewer where one exists on/near site |
| Live wires (reg. 55) | Contractor must arrange with owners to make nearby live wires/cables safe |
| Security deposit (reg. 57) | BA may require ≤ **$500** per shed deposited in Treasury |
| Cancellation (reg. 58) | BA may cancel for regulation breach or failure to maintain |

### 14. Schematic-design decision sequence (use this order)

1. **Confirm streets** → class A/B/C (abutting %, specified-street tests) or BA special determination  
2. **Cross-check OZP Notes + lease** PR/SC/height against First Schedule — adopt the **strictest**  
3. Fix **height band** (reg. 23(1)) → read full First Schedule; decide podium ≤ **15 m** ND SC strategy; test dedication/acquisition bonus if street setback is real  
4. Allocate **GFA discounts** (carpark, loading, refuse, plant, telecom) and **open space** / service lane / rear 1.5 m  
5. Test **prescribed windows** / light wells (RHP 21 m²; 71½° / 76° / 80½° / 83°) and **9 m** habitable room depth  
6. Size **stair cores**: second stair / fireman’s lift / FR stairway / EVA triggers by height, use, basement, cubical extent  
7. Embed **BFA**: continuous access route, ramps, accessible WC, lift car size (watch >3 lifts rule), parking, hotel rooms, Div. 21 extras  
8. Resolve **projections**, canopies, AC/drying frames, exhaust outlets ≥ 2.5 m; no canopy roof doors  
9. Overlay **hotel / cinema / PPE / tenement / retaining-wall / dangerous-trade** rules if applicable  
10. Feed Cap 123A package: PR/SC diagram (reg. 8(1)(h)), open space (regs. 25, 27, 28), BFA plans (reg. 8(1)(m))
