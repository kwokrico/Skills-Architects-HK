# Cap 123F — Building (Planning) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 16 June 2024 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. F

> Scope note: Cap 123F is the **BO planning rulebook** — site class, site coverage (SC), plot ratio (PR), open space, lanes/setbacks, natural light & air, stair / MOE / firefighting access / EVA envelopes, domestic & cinema planning, and barrier-free access (reg. 72 + Third Schedule). It is **not** OZP / lease development intensity (often more restrictive), not Cap 123A submission package detail, and not FS Code MOE geometry detail. At SD: classify the site → freeze SC/PR/height band → lock open space & light wells → size MOE / fireman’s lift / EVA → design BFA routes.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123F applies to **building works under the Buildings Ordinance** and sets how a building may occupy its lot: site classification A/B/C, permitted SC/PR by height, domestic open space and service lanes, street projections, habitable/office lighting & ventilation, staircases and emergency access (including fireman’s lifts, firefighting & rescue stairways, EVA), kitchens/tenement rules, places of public entertainment / cinemas, temporary buildings / hoardings, and mandatory design for persons with a disability.

It governs **all classes of buildings** that need BD approval; SC/PR tables and open-space rules are keyed to **domestic / non-domestic / composite** use and **class A, B or C sites** (or BA discretion if the site is none of these). Always take the **lowest** of Cap 123F First Schedule, OZP Notes, and lease / Conditions of Sale.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Lock the site class before any massing (reg. 18A)

| Class | Definition | Abutting % rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Class A** | Abuts **one** (or more) specified street(s) ≥ **4.5 m** wide — and is **not** B or C | Default / mid-block |
| **Class B** | **Corner** site abutting **2** specified streets, each ≥ 4.5 m | ≥ **40%** of site boundary must abut those streets |
| **Class C** | **Corner** site abutting **3** specified streets, each ≥ 4.5 m | ≥ **60%** of site boundary must abut those streets |

**Specified street** (reg. 18A(3)) — only counts if it is (any mix of parts composing the whole street):
- vested in Gov + maintained by HyD; **or**
- private street on same GL as the site that must be surrendered when required; **or**
- street required to be built on unleased GLA under the lease; **or**
- land over which the owner has an express **all-times right of way**; **or**
- street on land held under a GL by the site owner

**SD kill switches:**
- Site is **not** A/B/C → height, SC and PR are **entirely BA-determined** (reg. 19(3)) — get in-principle advice early
- Fake “wide street” or incomplete corner frontage → class A PR/SC, not B/C bonus
- BA alone determines street width for Cap 123F purposes (reg. 6)

### 2. Site coverage & plot ratio — First Schedule + composite maths (regs. 19–23)

#### 2.1 How to read the First Schedule
SC and PR **depend on building height band** and site class. Caps for buildings **over 61 m**:

| Use | Class A | Class B | Class C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic SC / PR | **33.33% / 8.0** | **37.5% / 9.0** | **40% / 10.0** |
| Non-domestic SC / PR | **60% / 15** | **62.5% / 15** | **65% / 15** |

Low towers get **higher SC, lower PR**; as height rises, SC drops and PR rises (see First Schedule full table at end of this file). Mixed height bands: you design to the height of the building, not storey count alone.

#### 2.2 Height measurement for SC/PR (reg. 23(1))
From **mean level of the specified street(s) ≥ 4.5 m** that the building fronts/abuts  
→ to **mean height of roof over the highest usable floor space**.  
If streets have different levels → use the **lower / lowest** street mean level.

#### 2.3 Site area (reg. 23(2))
- **Exclude** any part of a street or service lane  
- **Include** areas dedicated to the public for passage (dedication areas still count in site area)

#### 2.4 GFA definition & BA discounts (reg. 23(3))
**GFA** = area within external walls at each floor (including below ground) + balcony area (overall dims incl. side thickness) + external wall thickness.

BA **may disregard** floor space solely for:
- parking / loading-unloading of motor vehicles  
- refuse storage / material recovery chambers & rooms, refuse chutes, hopper rooms, refuse separation facilities  
- telecommunications & broadcasting **access facilities**  
- plant solely for lift, AC/heating or similar service  

> SD practice: park / plant / refuse / MDF-telecom floors are **not** automatic freebies — structure them so BA will accept disregard; OZP/lease GFA definitions may still count them.

#### 2.5 Non-domestic SC privilege to 15 m (reg. 20(3)–(4))
Non-domestic (or non-domestic part of composite) on class A/B/C may **exceed** First Schedule SC **up to 15 m above ground**, whatever the building height — but:
- above that level, SC must return within permitted SC; **and**
- **whole-building PR** must still respect permitted PR (subject to reg. 22)

**Classic podium-tower move:** full/near-full coverage retail/office podium ≤ 15 m, then tower on domestic or ND SC limit.

#### 2.6 Composite building domestic PR (reg. 21(2)) — formula
\[
PR_{dom} = \frac{(PR_{ND,perm} - PR_{ND,actual}) \times PR_{D,perm}}{PR_{ND,perm}}
\]
where perm = First Schedule permitted for that height/class if the building were wholly ND or wholly D.

#### 2.7 Tiny caretaker flat may keep ND treatment (reg. 23(4))
BA may treat as non-domestic a composite whose **only** domestic part is:
- caretaker / service residence ≤ **50 m²** UFS; and/or  
- residence comprising the **top storey** only  

#### 2.8 Dedication / street-widening bonuses (reg. 22) — max ~20% PR
Where ground level to ≥ **5.5 m** (or **3.3 m** if BA accepts no vehicular obstruction) is set back and dedicated for public passage (or land acquired for street widening):
- SC bonus formula based on dedicated area × 1500 / (site × height)  
- PR may exceed permitted by **min( +20%, dedicated×5 / site area )**  
- Does **not** reduce domestic open space under reg. 25  
- Bonus SC on one part of the building → other parts still capped at normal SC

#### 2.9 Hotels (reg. 23A) — optional ND treatment
BA **may** treat hotel (or hotel part) as **non-domestic**, and may disregard GFA for:
- pick-up / drop-off for hotel guests by vehicle  
- laundry, carpentry, M&E workshops  
- dry goods / food / beverage / linen / furniture stores  
- staff canteen, changing, rest rooms  
- other supporting facilities BA approves  

**Trap:** changing use away from hotel (or losing Cap 349 licence / exclusion order) without prior BA approval is an offence; disregarded floors cannot be reused for other purposes without BA approval.

### 4. Open space, setbacks, cuttings, service lanes (regs. 24–28)

#### 4.1 Storey height — habitation / office (reg. 24)
| Rule | Dimension |
|---|---|
| Floor to ceiling | ≥ **2.5 m** |
| Floor to underside of beam | ≥ **2.3 m** |
| Sloping ceiling | measure to **mean** height; **no part** < **2.0 m** |

#### 4.2 Domestic open space (reg. 25 + Second Schedule)
Every domestic building on class A/B/C must have open space **at rear, or rear + side**, ≥ **150 mm below** floor of lowermost storey:

| Site class | Open space ≥ |
|---|---|
| A | **½** of roofed-over area |
| B | **⅓** of roofed-over area |
| C | **¼** of roofed-over area |

**Geometry traps:**
- No part of building bounding the open space may be within **1.5 m** horizontally of a vertical line from the opposite open-space boundary  
- No domestic building within **1.5 m** of the **rear boundary** (that strip counts toward open space)  
- Existing buildings: cannot reduce open space below the larger of existing or required  
- Level drop > **600 mm** between open spaces → parapet / railing / fence required  
- Access to every open space required  
- BA may demand **more** than Second Schedule for equitable development of neighbouring sites  
- Reg. 22 dedication bonuses **do not** waive reg. 25 open space

#### 4.3 Narrow street setback (reg. 26)
If existing street in front of a **new** building is < **4.5 m** wide → no part of building closer than **2.25 m** to the street centre line.

#### 4.4 Cuttings (reg. 27)
Do not abut a cutting (incl. toe wall). Clear intervening space at GF ≥ **max(¼ cutting height, 2.5 m)**. Cutting height measured from toe, vertically to intersection with a **30°** line down from the top of the cutting.

#### 4.5 Service lanes (reg. 28)
Every domestic building needs a rear/side **service lane**, **except** when:
- a public lane ≥ **3 m** or a street already exists; **or**
- detached / semi-detached building; **or**
- BA exemption  

Lane must be (or become via neighbour redevelopment) accessible from an existing street. Alignment/width/levels = BA decision; if BA requires lane > **1.5 m**, may modify reg. 25.

### 5. Projections & street interface (regs. 3–7, 10–15)

Bake into elevations and GF doors early:

| Element | Hard limits |
|---|---|
| External opening barriers above GF (reg. 3A) | Barrier ≥ **1 100 mm**; lowest **150 mm** solid; design against fall / climb / roll-through |
| Exhaust / noxious discharge over street (reg. 4) | Outlet soffit ≥ **2.5 m** above adjacent footpath/street |
| Access (reg. 5) | Every building needs access from a street; BA may require on-site access lane/road |
| Architectural projection over street (reg. 7) | ≤ **500 mm**; soffit ≥ **2.5 m** |
| Pipe/gutter over street | ≤ **300 mm**; soffit ≥ **2.5 m** |
| Drying rack / AC frame / antenna frame (“specified structure”) | ≤ **750 mm**; soffit ≥ **2.5 m** |
| Retractable awning | Retracted ≤ **500 mm**, extended ≤ **2.5 m**; soffit ≥ **2.5 m**; ≥ **600 mm** from kerb (carriageway) or ≥ **1.5 m** from footpath centre line |
| Metal ventilation duct over street | ≤ **750 mm**; soffit ≥ **2.5 m**; must not project beyond balcony/verandah/canopy edge |
| Canopy clear height (reg. 10) | ≥ **5.5 m** if within 600 mm of footpath outer edge or over a road; ≥ **3.3 m** over footpath |
| Canopy max projection | **min(1/10 street width, 3 m)**; no portion within **4.5 m** horizontally of centre-line vertical |
| No doorway onto canopy roof (reg. 12) | Absolute |
| Verandah/balcony over street (reg. 13) | Not for factory, workshop, store, kitchen, lavatory, bathroom, WC, urinal, latrine |
| Doors/windows/shutters over street (reg. 15) | Must not project below **2.5 m** — except emergency exits, transformer / plant / refuse chamber / similar utility doors that open fully without obstructing persons/vehicles |

**Canopy / balcony definition reminders (reg. 2):** canopy projects > **500 mm** and ≤ **7.5 m** above ground, no floor load; balcony carries floor/roof load.

### 6. Lighting & ventilation — Room & block form drivers (regs. 29–37, 71)

#### 6.1 Habitation / office / kitchen windows (regs. 30–32)
| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Glass area | ≥ **1/10** of room floor area |
| Openable area | ≥ **1/16** of floor area; top of opening ≥ **2.0 m** above floor (**1.9 m** for detached / semi-detached) |
| Prescribed window | The glass area required by 1/10 must face **external air** |
| Room depth | No part of a **habitable** room > **9 m** from a prescribed window (or from outer edge if opening onto verandah/balcony per regs. 33 / 71) |

#### 6.2 What counts as “facing external air” (reg. 31) — **light-well / setback geometry**
A prescribed window faces external air only if **one** of:
- faces a street ≥ **4.5 m** wide; **or**
- faces an uncovered, unobstructed space above a **rectangular horizontal plane (RHP)** and clears the inclined plane tests below

**RHP minimums (at sill level; sill deemed 1 m above floor):**
- Area ≥ **21 m²**
- Base ≥ **2.3 m**
- Depth (base → opposite wall/building within site) ≥ **4.5 m**; **or** ≥ **2.3 m** to site boundary / adjusted centre-line of narrow lane (reg. 31(2)(d)–(e))

**Inclined clear planes (no building protrusion above):**

| Case | Habitation | Office / kitchen |
|---|---|---|
| Internal light well (reg. 31(1)(c)) | **71½°** from horizontal | **76°** |
| Window onto site boundary opposite (reg. 31(1)(d)) | **80½°** | **83°** |

Narrow service lane / street < 4.5 m beside boundary: for (1)(d), treat boundary as **1.5 m beyond** actual boundary (or to lane centre if lane < 3 m for RHP depth).

#### 6.3 Offices without natural compliance (reg. 34)
If reg. 31 cannot be met → mechanical ventilation ≥ **5 air changes/hour** fresh air + BA-approved artificial lighting.

#### 6.4 Soil / waste fitment rooms (reg. 36)
- Window or lantern: glass ≥ **1/10** floor; openable ≥ **1/10** floor **directly into open air**; top of openable ≥ **2.0 m**  
- Soil-fitment room must **not** open directly into food prep/storage rooms, PPE auditorium, or PPE waiting space  

#### 6.5 Gas water heaters (reg. 35A)
Post–20 May 1983 buildings without centralised hot water: every bathroom needs suitable provision (adequate external-wall aperture, sealable with removable cover) for **room-sealed** gas water heater — design riser / external wall penetrations at SD.

#### 6.6 Neighbouring rights (reg. 37)
New work must not reduce light/air available to another compliant building below Cap 123F minima — massing studies must include **neighbour** prescribed windows.

### 7. Stairs, MOE, firefighting access, EVA (regs. 39–44)

Coordinate with FS Code / MOE Code / EVA PNAP geometry — Cap 123F sets **triggers & minima**.

#### 7.1 Main staircase geometry (reg. 39)
| Item | Minimum / maximum |
|---|---|
| Clear height | ≥ **2.0 m** |
| Clear width | ≥ **900 mm** |
| Tread (centre of flight) | ≥ **225 mm** |
| Riser | ≤ **175 mm** |
| Steps per flight | ≤ **16** without landing |
| Handrail | One or both sides; tubular Ø **38–50 mm** or rectangular **40–50 mm** wide / ≤ 50 mm deep |
| Roof continuation | Main stair of buildings > **4 storeys** must continue to roof **unless** a secondary fire-escape stair is provided |
| Roof escape door | If stair continues to roof as escape → glazed upper panels |

Common stair serving > 2 tenants: natural lighting each storey above GF + ventilation at highest point (reg. 40).  
**Escalators** are **extra** — never replace required stairs / MOE / second stair; not a means of egress under reg. 43 (reg. 40A).

#### 7.2 Means of escape (reg. 41)
- Every building: MOE as required by intended use  
- **Second staircase** mandatory if building **> 6 storeys** **or** uppermost floor > **17 m** above ground at main stair discharge  

Travel: every part intended for habitation, or any school, or place of public assembly ≤ **24 m** from a staircase / passage / normal egress (reg. 43). Revolving doors / turnstiles need alternate exit nearby (reg. 44). Landings/lobbies/passages ≥ width of the stair they serve (reg. 42).

#### 7.3 Access staircases for firemen (reg. 41A)
Required for every building > 1 storey (adequate number by use & floor size; protected for firemen) — **except** solely domestic single-household buildings ≤ **3 main storeys**.

#### 7.4 Fireman’s lifts (reg. 41B)
Required if building > **2 storeys**, placed so no served floor part is > **60 m** from a fireman’s lift; at least every alternate floor served; dedicated liftwell (≤ 3 fireman’s lifts, no other lifts); exclusive firemen control.

**Exempt if any of:**
| Exemption | Conditions |
|---|---|
| Single-stair low-rise | Only 1 stair **and** ≤ 6 storeys **and** uppermost floor ≤ 17 m above discharge |
| Domestic | Height ≤ **30 m** (height per reg. 23(1)) |
| Non-domestic / ND part of composite | No industry / bulk storage / warehouse / PPE / hotel-guesthouse / hospital; **and** (height ≤ 15 m **or** height ≤ 30 m **and** cubical extent ≤ **7 000 m³**) |

#### 7.5 Firefighting & rescue stairway (reg. 41C)
Combined **access stair + fireman’s lift** required when:
- Industrial / bulk storage / warehouse building with height > **30 m**, **or** cubical extent > **7 000 m³** and height > 1 storey; **or**
- Basement with cubical extent > **7 000 m³** **and** (> 2 storeys **or** depth > **9 m** from ground-storey floor to lowest basement floor)

Place so no part of building/basement is > **60 m** from such a stairway. If roof is stair-accessible, the access stair must serve the roof.

#### 7.6 EVA (reg. 41D)
**Every building** needs emergency vehicular access designed for FSD vehicle approach & safe operation. Design to BA-specified requirements for the use; BA may exempt for topography or low fire risk **only if** compensating fire-safety measures are imposed.

### 8. Domestic planning rules (regs. 45–49)

| Topic | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Kitchen (reg. 45) | Every domestic building (and separately lettable dwelling part unless BA exempts) needs a kitchen: wall finish ≥ **1.2 m** non-absorbent; fireplace/cooking slab **or** gas/oil/electric cooking; sink + water supply |
| Tenement depth (reg. 46) | Depth front main wall (or balcony front) → nearest rear main wall ≤ **10 m** unless BA exempts; rear window ≥ **1.5 m²** glass & openable, top ≥ **2 m**; kitchen area by unit size (3.75 / 4.5 / 5.5 m² bands) with min dimension ≥ **1.5 m** |
| Retaining walls (regs. 47–48) | No domestic building against retaining wall > **4.5 m**; leave ≥ **1.5 m** to toe of such wall (rock face = retaining wall). RW forming part of building: waterproof + insulated against condensation in habitable rooms |
| Dangerous trades (reg. 49) | No domestic / habitational use combined with DG manufacture/storage (as specified), motor repair, vulcanizing, auto/carriage painting, paint manufacture/mixing, or dry-cleaning — unless BA exempts with prescribed structural measures. Caretaker flat ≤ **50 m²** UFS allowed in such buildings |

**Detached / semi-detached open space definition (reg. 2):** clear unobstructed open space ≥ **2.3 m** along the entire depth **and** ≥ **2.3 m** full-width at the rear — used for service-lane exemption and some window opening heights.

### 9. Places of public entertainment & cinemas (regs. 49A–49E)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| PPE location (49A) | Must be in a building used **only** for that PPE — **except** cinema regime below |
| Cinema in mixed building (49B) | Must sit in **non-domestic** building or ND part of composite; total cinema accommodation in that building ≤ **2 000** persons (one or many cinemas aggregated) |
| Incompatible co-locations | Cinema **cannot** share a building with: motor vehicle repair workshop, petrol filling station, godown/warehouse, factory/industrial undertaking, school, child care centre, elderly home, PWD residential care home |
| Shared facilities | MOE, entrance, foyer, waiting space may be shared only if BA is satisfied |
| No domestic in PPE (49C) | Habitation prohibited inside a PPE |
| Floors / tiers (49D) | Gradient ≤ **35°**; clear height floor → underside of tier/ceiling above ≥ **3 m** |
| Waiting (49E) | Adequate waiting accommodation for persons awaiting admission |

### 10. Barrier-free access — reg. 72 + Third Schedule (schematic must-haves)

#### 10.1 When it applies
If persons with a disability have, or may reasonably be expected to have, access → design to BA satisfaction so they can access and use the building and facilities. **Deemed compliance** = Third Schedule **Part 2**.

**Exempt (reg. 72(4)):**
- buildings ≤ **13 m** above ground used / intended for **single family** occupation  
- temporary buildings / contractor’s sheds (Part VII)

#### 10.2 Extent by building type (Third Schedule Part 4)
| Category | BFA applies to |
|---|---|
| **Domestic** | Common areas if > **4** storeys; if ≤ 4 storeys → main entrance + GF common areas + means of access. Excludes plant/maintenance-only, storage mezzanines, security platforms, pool water, paths only to those. Guest rooms under s.7 still apply where relevant |
| **Non-domestic** | **All parts**, except commercial kitchen / cold room / cinema projector room, plant/maintenance-only, hazardous or non-public bulk storage, storage mezzanines, security platforms, pool water, paths only thereto |
| **Composite** | Domestic rules for domestic parts **+** non-domestic rules for ND parts **+** accessible guest rooms where hotels/hostels/guesthouses |

#### 10.3 Core SD dimensions (Third Schedule Part 2 — extract)
| Element | Critical number |
|---|---|
| Access route clear width | ≥ **1 050 mm** from lot-boundary prominent point to public entrance **and** to accessible lift |
| Ramp width | ≥ **1 050 mm**; max slope **1:12** (minor rises: 1:10 ≤ 1.5 m / 150 mm rise; 1:8 ≤ 600 mm / 75 mm rise) |
| Ramp landings | ≥ **1 500 × 1 500 mm** at head & foot; intermediate landing ≥ **1 200 mm** long every 10 m run if ≥ 1:20; handrails both sides; edge kerb ≥ 100 mm or rail 200 mm above ramp |
| Door clear width | ≥ **800 mm**; leading-edge unobstructed strip ≥ **330 mm** |
| Manoeuvring / corridors | Plan **1 500 mm** turning spaces at changes of direction & in front of accessible WC approaches |
| Accessible WC cubicle | Internal ≥ **1 500 × 1 750 mm**; manoeuvring ≥ **1 500 × 1 500 mm** at 350 mm AFFL; if ≤ 20 WCs on floor → ≥ 1 accessible; if > 20 → ≥ 2 (cubicle count can substitute floor count inside multi-cubicle toilets) |
| Accessible lift | Car & door clear entrance ≥ **850 mm**; lobby illumination ≥ **85 lux** |
| Accessible parking | ≥ 1 per first 50 spaces, then stepped to 6 above 450; stall ≥ **3 500 mm** wide (or ≥ 2 500 mm + shared **1 200 mm** hatched loading strip between two); route to accessible lift lobby |
| Hotels / hostels / guesthouses | **2 accessible guest rooms per 100** (or part thereof) |
| Auditoria | Wheelchair spaces: ≥ 4 if ≤ 800 fixed seats; then ≥ 2 per 400 seats; space ≥ **800 × 1 300 mm**; approach aisle ≥ **1 500 mm**; stage/backstage/toilets accessible |

Tactile guide paths / assistive listening / Braille maps apply to building types listed in **Division 21** (department stores, hotels, worship, cinemas/theatres, schools, factories, sports complexes, restaurants, markets, hospitals, residential homes, clubhouses, transport stations, carparks — check Division 21 for each provision).

> Also coordinate **Design Manual: Barrier Free Access** (current edition) practice notes — Third Schedule is the statutory floor; DM/BFA is the usual detailing path BA expects on drawings (Cap 123A reg. 8(1)(m) BFA plans).

### 11. Telecom / broadcasting access facilities (reg. 28A)

Provide Cap-123F “access facilities” (rooms, ducts, risers for telecom / broadcasting cables & plant) for every:
- commercial building  
- industrial building  
- residential building **other than** single-family residence  
- hotel building  

Design to BA-specified requirements (typically vertical risers + equipment rooms sized for operator co-location) — treat as early shaft/plant claim; GFA disregard possible under reg. 23(3)(b).

| Hoardings / covered walkways / gantries (regs. 64–70) | Required plans + permit for erect / alter / demolish / excavate works affecting street passers-by — programme hold point before site works |

### 13. Schematic-design decision sequence (use this order)

1. **Confirm streets** → class A/B/C or BA special determination  
2. **Cross-check OZP Notes + lease** PR/SC/height against First Schedule — adopt the **strictest**  
3. Fix **height band** (reg. 23(1)) → read SC/PR; decide podium ≤ **15 m** ND SC strategy  
4. Allocate **GFA discounts** (carpark, refuse, plant, telecom) and **open space** / service lane  
5. Test **prescribed windows** / light wells (71½° / 76° / 80½° / 83°) and 9 m room depth  
6. Size **stair cores**: second stair / fireman’s lift / FR stairway / EVA triggers by height, use, basement  
7. Embed **BFA** continuous route, accessible WC, lift, parking, hotel rooms  
8. Resolve **projections**, canopies, AC frames, exhaust outlets ≥ 2.5 m  
9. If hotel / cinema / PPE / tenement — apply Part VI / VIA overlays  
10. Feed Cap 123A package: PR/SC diagram (reg. 8(1)(h)), open space, BFA plans
