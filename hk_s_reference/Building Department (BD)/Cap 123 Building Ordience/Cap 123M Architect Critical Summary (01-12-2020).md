# Cap 123M — Building (Energy Efficiency) Regulation
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 1 December 2020 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. M

> Scope note: Cap 123M is the **OTTV statutory hook** for commercial buildings and hotels. It requires external walls and roofs to achieve a **suitable Overall Thermal Transfer Value** to BA’s satisfaction. Cap 123M itself does **not** publish numeric W/m² caps, calculation methods, A&A triggers, open-front shop carve-outs, or submission timing — those live in BD’s *Code of Practice for Overall Thermal Transfer Value in Buildings 1995* (OTTV Code) and **PNAP APP-67** (last revision Sep 2025). This summary merges Cap 123M design constraints with the APP-67 / OTTV Code practice an architect must know before freezing façade WWR, glass, orientation, roof build-up, or shading.  
> Not this Cap: BEAM Plus, EMSD BEC (lighting/HVAC), Cap 123F planning geometry (except s. 6 + APP-67 sunshade concessions), or residential RTTV (that is APP-156 / Guidelines — separate track; hotels/guesthouses with A/C profiles unlike normal domestic are out of APP-156 and back under Cap 123M / APP-67).

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123M applies only to Schedule buildings — **commercial buildings** and **hotels** — and requires them to be designed and constructed for energy efficiency to the Building Authority’s satisfaction, primarily by giving external walls and roofs a **suitable OTTV** (ss. 3–4). On mixed-use buildings it bites only on Schedule-use parts (and ancillary spaces), and expressly **excludes refuge floors and car parks**. In practice, “suitable OTTV” = the tower / podium W/m² caps and calculation methodology in the OTTV Code as amended by APP-67.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Does Cap 123M apply to *this* scheme? (s. 3 + Schedule + s. 2)

| Building / part | Cap 123M? | SD action |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial building (see §1.1) | **Yes** | Lock OTTV envelope strategy from day one of façade studies |
| Hotel (see §1.2) | **Yes** — Schedule item 2 (separate from “commercial”) | Same; treat hotel rooms as tower OTTV use class on Summary Sheet |
| Domestic, industrial, school | **No** (not in Schedule; also carved out of “commercial”) | Cap 123M off — but residential may still need APP-156 RTTV / OTTVᴿᴿᶠ for GFA concessions |
| Carpark (standalone or podium) | **Excluded** from Cap 123M “building” on mixed-use (s. 3(2)(a)) | Keep carpark out of OTTV calculation boundary |
| Refuge floor | **Excluded** (s. 3(2)(a)) | Do not pull refuge-floor envelope into OTTV area |
| Mixed commercial + domestic / industrial | **Partial** — only Schedule parts + ancillary to that use | Split the envelope model; annotate OTTV-applicable storeys on GAs |
| Open-front G/F shop ≤ 50 m² UFA (APP-67 §6) | May be **disregarded** from OTTV calc (conditions) | See §7 |
| A&A — new building extension / full façade replacement (APP-67 §5) | **Yes** | Cap 123M / APP-67 applies — see §8 |
| A&A — partial window swap / local cladding patch | Usually **no** under APP-67 §5 | Confirm with AP; do not assume OTTV package |

#### 1.1 “Commercial buildings” include / exclude filter (s. 2)

**Includes:** offices, shops, department stores, places of public entertainment, places of public assembly, restaurants, and any other buildings used for commercial purposes.

**Does not include** (even if colloquially “commercial”):
- hotels (covered separately via Schedule item 2)
- domestic premises
- industrial buildings
- schools
- carparks
- places used for bulk storage
- utility buildings (e.g. power stations, sub-stations)

**APP-67 Summary Sheet building-type ticks** (practical classification for submission): Hotel / Office (including industrial/office) / Shops / Others (specify). Footnote: “other commercial” may include department stores, places of public entertainment / assembly, restaurants, etc.

**SD takeaway:** Wrong use-classification at concept = wrong façade brief. Confirm occupancy early; a “serviced apartment / hotel / hostel / RCHE” call can flip Cap 123M on or off (and APP-156 off if A/C profile ≠ normal domestic).

#### 1.2 Hotel definition (s. 2)

**Hotel** = premises held out as providing **sleeping accommodation** to any persons presenting themselves who are willing to pay a fee for that accommodation.

#### 1.3 Refuge floor (s. 2)

**Refuge floor** = a protected floor that serves as a refuge for occupants to assemble in case of fire — excluded from Cap 123M application on mixed-use (s. 3(2)(a)).

### 2. Design duty — energy efficiency via OTTV (s. 4)

| Duty | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| General (s. 4(1)) | Building must be designed and constructed to achieve energy efficiency **to BA’s satisfaction** |
| Deemed compliance path (s. 4(2)) | Compliance with s. 4(1) is regarded as satisfied if **external walls and roofs** have a **suitable overall thermal transfer value** |

**Policy intent (APP-67 intro):** reduce heat transfer through the building envelope → cut electricity for air-conditioning.

#### 2.1 What “OTTV” means in statute (s. 2)

OTTV = heat transferred through the building envelope, expressed in **W/m²**, calculated having regard to factors such as:
- area of the building envelope
- materials of construction
- thermal properties of those materials
- **orientation** of the building
- area of **openings** in the envelope
- **shading effect of projections** from the envelope

**SD schematic levers Cap 123M elevates:** orientation, WWR / openings by elevation, wall–roof U-value build-up, glass type / SC / VLT, and shading projections. Own these at SD — not as late GS polish.

#### 2.2 What is an “external wall” for Cap 123M (s. 2)

| Counts as external wall | Does **not** count |
|---|---|
| Whole or part of an **outer wall** | Wall (or part) that is **underground** |
| Wall adjoining a wall of another building | — |

**SD takeaway:** Basement perimeter walls drop out of the Cap 123M external-wall OTTV set; focus budget on above-ground commercial / hotel façade and roof (including skylights — Summary Sheet tracks skylight area / SC / VLT separately).

#### 2.3 Numeric OTTV caps — Cap 123M says “suitable”; APP-67 sets the numbers

Cap 123M does **not** state max W/m². APP-67 §3 (as amended Sep 2025) amends the OTTV Code control as follows:

| Envelope zone | Max OTTV (current APP-67) | Previous |
|---|---|---|
| **Building tower** | **≤ 20 W/m²** | 21 W/m² |
| **Podium** | **≤ 40 W/m²** | 50 W/m² |

**Implementation (APP-67 §12):** revised caps apply to all **new building plans or major revision** of building plans for development or A&A proposals submitted for approval **on or after 31 December 2025**.

**SD takeaway:** For schemes submitting on/after that date, design to **20 / 40**, not the old 21 / 50. Client / BEAM Plus targets may still be stricter — Cap 123M / APP-67 is the BA floor, not the ceiling.

#### 2.4 Tower vs podium split — notional podium height (APP-67 §4)

For buildings with varying site coverage at different levels under **PNAP APP-132**, the **notional height of the podium** for OTTV calculation may be regarded as **20 m above mean street level** of the site. To simplify computation, the notional podium may be demarcated at a level **lower than 20 m**.

**SD takeaway:** Lock the podium / tower OTTV cut-line early when massing uses stepped SC under APP-132 — wrong cut-line wrongly assigns area to the tighter tower 20 W/m² band or the looser podium 40 W/m² band.

### 3. Mixed-use envelope boundary (s. 3(2)) — calculation hygiene

Where parts of a building have different purposes:

1. Cap 123M applies **only** to Schedule-use parts + spaces ancillary to that use  
2. **Exclude** use as **refuge floor** or **car park**  
3. Every reference to “building” in Cap 123M = those included parts only  

| Include in Cap 123M OTTV model | Exclude |
|---|---|
| Office / retail / F&B / cinema / hotel floors | Pure domestic towers |
| Genuine ancillary plant / toilets serving Schedule use | Industrial floors / schools |
| — | Carpark floors / levels |
| — | Refuge floors |

**APP-67 Summary Sheet designated uses (design classification cues):**
- **Podium:** Shops / Offices / Restaurants / Cinema / Plant Rooms / Others  
- **Tower:** Office / Hotel Rooms / Others  

**SD takeaway:** Draw the OTTV calculation boundary on the GAs at schematic stage. Composite podium–tower schemes fail late when carpark / refuge façade is wrongly left in (or commercial area left out), or when podium plant rooms are mis-assigned.

### 4. What Cap 123M s. 5 demands on Ordinance s. 14 plans

If Cap 123M applies, any plan for building works submitted under **Ordinance s. 14** must include:

| Plan content (s. 5) | What to lock |
|---|---|
| **(a) Wall & roof material diagrams** | Dimensions, colours, arrangements, and other details of materials for **external walls and roofs** |
| **(b) Windows table (every floor)** | In **tabular form**, per orientation: number of windows; size of each; **glass thickness**; **type of glass** |
| **(c) Composite walls / roofs** | Thermal transmittance value + **calculations** |
| **(d) OTTV statement** | Statement + **calculations** of OTTV of external walls and roofs |

**SD takeaway:** Curtain-wall packaging, spandrel build-ups, roof insulation, skylights, and glass schedules are statutory plan content — freeze N/E/S/W labels, WWR per elevation, and glass type early enough for the OTTV calc.

### 5. Submission timing & package — Cap 123M s. 5 vs APP-67 / Cap 123A practice

Cap 123M s. 5 reads as if OTTV info must be on s. 14 plans. **APP-67 §§7–8** softens / sequences this in practice:

| Stage | What BA accepts / requires |
|---|---|
| **First GBP submission** | Façade may not be finalised → BA accepts first plans **without** full Cap 123M s. 5 information and calculations |
| **After GBP approval, before consent to commence** | Submit **OTTV Summary Sheet** (APP-67 Appendix A) under **Cap 123A / B(A)R reg. 10**. OTTV must be calculated per OTTV Code |
| **Before OP** (or Form BA14 for A&A) | Incorporate **finalised OTTVs** of external walls and roofs **and shading coefficient of glass** into GBPs for record |
| **At OP / BA14** | Submit **OTTV Report**: detailed calcs + Forms **OTTV1–OTTV4** (schedule to OTTV Code) + record plans + test certificates / published specs for materials (esp. fenestration / façade glass) + finalised OTTV Summary Sheet — again under B(A)R reg. 10 |

**Who may calculate (Summary Sheet):** Registered Professional Engineer (BS/Mechanical) / Architect / Others (specify).

**SD takeaway:** You can submit massing GAs without a locked façade — but you **cannot** get consent without an OTTV Summary Sheet, and you **cannot** get OP without the full OTTV Report + glass SC on record plans. Programme the façade consultant and glass procurement against the consent / OP gates, not only against DD.

### 6. OTTV Summary Sheet — data the architect must own by consent (APP-67 App. A)

Treat this as the SD → DD data checklist (podium column + tower column):

| Field group | What to capture |
|---|---|
| Identity | Address, BD Ref., building type (hotel / office incl. I/O / shops / others) |
| Who calculated | RPE (BS/Mech) / Architect / Other |
| Scale | No. of storeys (excl. G/F); GFA; UFA |
| Envelope areas | Total external wall area (incl. windows); total window area; **WWR**; total skylight area |
| Weighted avg U-value | Opaque wall / window / opaque roof / skylight (W/m²K) |
| Glass (windows & skylights) | Reflective / tinted / clear; area; **SC**; **VLT**; double glazing Y/N |
| External shading | Overhang Y/N; sidefin Y/N (windows & skylights) |
| Weighted absorptivity | Wall / roof |
| Weighted density | Wall / roof (kg/m²) |
| **OTTV results** | Wall W/m²; roof W/m²; **overall average** W/m² — split podium vs tower |

**SD takeaway:** If your façade concept cannot state provisional WWR, glass SC/VLT, overhang/fin strategy, and separate podium/tower OTTV targets by mid-DD, the consent package will stall.

### 7. Open-front shops (APP-67 §6) — G/F retail carve-out

| Shop condition | OTTV treatment |
|---|---|
| Open-front shop (or the like) on G/F, **UFA ≤ 50 m²**, and any A/C for the shop is **separated** from the main building A/C | May be **disregarded** from the OTTV calculation |
| Open-front shop with **UFA > 50 m²** | May demonstrate compliance with tower/podium caps by a **notional OTTV calculation based on clear glass** construction for the shopfront |

**SD takeaway:** Small open-front retail can stay out of the envelope model if A/C is independent. Larger open fronts still need a notional clear-glass OTTV proof — do not assume “no façade = no OTTV.”

---

### 8. Alterations & additions — when Cap 123M bites again (APP-67 §5)

For A&A to existing buildings, B(EE)R / Cap 123M applies to:

| A&A trigger | Applies? |
|---|---|
| Horizontal or vertical extension which constitutes a **new building** | **Yes** |
| **New external walls / façades covering the entire tower or podium** | **Yes** |
| Localised A&A (partial windows, non-entire façade) | Not listed in APP-67 §5 — typically outside this trigger; confirm with AP |

**SD takeaway:** Full re-skin / new wing = treat as Cap 123M project (Summary Sheet → OTTV Report → BA14 path). Cosmetic or partial façade works usually do not restart the full Cap 123M package.

### 9. Shading projections — Cap 123M s. 6 + APP-67 §§10–11 (design hard numbers)

#### 9.1 Statutory disregard (Cap 123M s. 6)

Any projection from the external wall that:
1. BA is satisfied **contributes to achieving the suitable OTTV**; **and**
2. does **not project more than 1.5 m** from the external wall  

shall **not** be taken into account when checking Cap 123F **regs. 20, 22 and 31**.

| Cap 123F reg. disregarded for that projection | What it normally controls |
|---|---|
| **Reg. 20** | Site coverage / PR (incl. non-domestic SC privilege band) |
| **Reg. 22** | Dedication / street-widening PR bonuses |
| **Reg. 31** | “Facing external air” / prescribed-window lighting-ventilating geometry |

#### 9.2 APP-67 sunshade practice (operational overlay on s. 6)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| GFA | **Genuine sunshades** used to reduce OTTV are **not accountable for GFA** |
| Site coverage | By virtue of Cap 123M s. 6, not included in SC if projection **≤ 1.5 m** |
| > **750 mm** projection | Submit **quantitative assessment** to BA that the sunshade assists OTTV reduction |
| ≤ **750 mm** projection | Regarded as **not causing obstruction** to prescribed windows |
| Projection over streets | **Not allowed** under Cap 123 **s. 31(1)**; exemptions may be considered case-by-case if special circumstances justify |
| Innovative / non-Code designs (APP-67 §11) | BA may accept designs other than those in the OTTV Code if comparable or better in energy efficiency; effective OTTV-reducing innovative designs **not penalised** in PR / SC |

**SD takeaway:**
- Design sunshades as **real OTTV tools** (documented in calc), not decoration.
- Prefer ≤ **750 mm** where prescribed-window obstruction is a risk; between 750 mm and 1.5 m budget a quantitative OTTV assessment.
- Never assume street overhang sunshades are free — Cap 123 s. 31(1) still bites unless BA exempts.
- Keep ≤ **1.5 m** to claim Cap 123F regs 20 / 22 / 31 disregard + SC exclusion.

### 10. Building materials & alternative designs (APP-67 §§9, 11)

| Situation | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Materials **listed** in OTTV Code | Use Code properties |
| Materials **not** listed in OTTV Code | Obtain OTTV-related properties from **reliable sources**; facilitate consent / OP / BA14 by detailing **source of information** and **suitability for local conditions** |
| Designs other than OTTV Code stipulations | BA may accept if **comparable or better** in energy efficiency |
| Innovative OTTV-reducing designs | Not to be penalised in PR / SC if effectiveness is demonstrated |

**SD takeaway:** Exotic glass, composite cladding, or green-wall / double-skin concepts need a properties dossier early — do not discover “not in OTTV Code tables” at consent.

### 11. Parallel regimes — do not confuse (architect map)

| Regime | What it controls | Typical trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Cap 123M + APP-67 + OTTV Code** | Commercial & hotel envelope OTTV | Schedule use / hotel |
| **APP-156 + Residential Energy Guidelines** | Residential RTTV wall/roof, OTTVᴿᴿᶠ, NVTC, glass VLT/ER — often as **GFA concession pre-requisite** under APP-151 | Domestic (excl. hotel / guesthouse / RCHE-type A/C profiles) |
| **EMSD BEC** | Lighting, HVAC, electrical, lift energy | Building services |
| **BEAM Plus** | Voluntary / client credits (often stricter OTTV) | Client / funding |

Residential RRF (residents’ recreational facilities) OTTV is controlled like commercial/hotel under APP-156 (same **20 / 40** tower/podium caps) — that is **not** Cap 123M, but the numbers and Forms OTTV1–4 methodology align with the OTTV Code.

### 12. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing massing, orientation, WWR, or façade system:

1. **Confirm occupancy** — commercial (s. 2) and/or hotel in Schedule? If neither → Cap 123M off for this Cap (check APP-156 if domestic).
2. **Split mixed use** — carve out domestic / industrial / school / carpark / refuge from the Cap 123M “building” (s. 3(2)).
3. **Lock podium vs tower cut-line** — APP-132 varying SC → notional podium ≤ **20 m** above mean street level (APP-67 §4); assign **≤ 20 W/m²** tower / **≤ 40 W/m²** podium targets (post-31 Dec 2025 submissions).
4. **Set OTTV strategy** — orientation, WWR by elevation, wall/roof U-build-up, glass type / SC / VLT, overhangs & sidefins (ss. 2 & 4 + Summary Sheet fields).
5. **Underground walls out** — no Cap 123M external-wall effort on underground perimeter (s. 2).
6. **Open-front G/F retail** — ≤ 50 m² UFA + separate A/C → may disregard; > 50 m² → notional clear-glass OTTV (APP-67 §6).
7. **A&A path** — new-building extension or entire tower/podium re-skin → full Cap 123M / APP-67 package (APP-67 §5).
8. **Plan / consent / OP sequence** — first GBP may omit full s. 5 pack; **Summary Sheet before consent**; final OTTV + glass SC on plans + OTTV Report (Forms OTTV1–4, specs, certs) at OP / BA14 (APP-67 §§7–8).
9. **Sunshades** — genuine OTTV devices ≤ **1.5 m** → Cap 123F regs 20/22/31 disregard + not in SC/GFA; > **750 mm** needs quantitative OTTV proof; ≤ **750 mm** OK for prescribed-window obstruction; no street projection without Cap 123 s. 31(1) exemption (s. 6 + APP-67 §10).
10. **Non-Code materials / innovative envelope** — source dossier + local-suitability narrative; BA may accept comparable-or-better designs without PR/SC penalty (APP-67 §§9, 11).
11. **Do not confuse regimes** — Cap 123M ≠ EMSD BEC ≠ BEAM Plus ≠ APP-156 RTTV.
