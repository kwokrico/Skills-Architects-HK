# Cap 123M — Building (Energy Efficiency) Regulation
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 1 December 2020 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. M

> Scope note: Cap 123M is the **OTTV (Overall Thermal Transfer Value) statutory hook** for commercial buildings and hotels — not BEAM Plus, not EMSD BEC lighting/HVAC, and not Cap 123F planning geometry (except the shading-projection concession in s. 6). Cap 123M itself does **not** publish the numeric W/m² OTTV caps; those live in BD’s *Code of Practice for Overall Thermal Transfer Value in Buildings* (and related PNAPs). Use this before schematic design freezes façade WWR, glass type, orientation strategy, roof build-up, or shading projections.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123M applies only to buildings in the Schedule — **commercial buildings** and **hotels** — and requires them to be designed and constructed for energy efficiency to the Building Authority’s satisfaction, primarily by giving external walls and roofs a **suitable overall thermal transfer value (OTTV)**. On mixed-use buildings it bites only on Schedule-use parts (and ancillary spaces), and expressly **excludes refuge floors and car parks** from the “building” for Cap 123M purposes.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Does Cap 123M apply to *this* scheme? (s. 3 + Schedule + s. 2)

| Building / part | Cap 123M? | SD action |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial building (see definition below) | **Yes** | Lock OTTV envelope strategy from day one of façade studies |
| Hotel | **Yes** | Same — hotel is Schedule item 2 (separate from “commercial”) |
| Domestic, industrial, school | **No** (not in Schedule; also carved out of “commercial buildings”) | Do not size façade for Cap 123M OTTV — other energy regimes may still apply |
| Carpark (standalone or podium) | **Excluded** from Cap 123M “building” on mixed-use (s. 3(2)(a)) | Keep carpark out of OTTV zone / calculation boundary |
| Refuge floor | **Excluded** (s. 3(2)(a)) | Do not pull refuge-floor envelope into OTTV area |
| Mixed commercial + domestic / industrial | **Partial** — only Schedule parts + ancillary to that use | Split the envelope model; annotate OTTV-applicable storeys on plans |

#### 1.1 What counts as a “commercial building” (s. 2) — include / exclude filter

**Includes:** offices, shops, department stores, places of public entertainment, places of public assembly, restaurants, and any other buildings used for commercial purposes.

**Does not include (even if sometimes “commercial” in ordinary speech):**
- hotels (covered separately via Schedule item 2)
- domestic premises
- industrial buildings
- schools
- carparks
- places used for bulk storage
- utility buildings (e.g. power stations, sub-stations)

**SD takeaway:** Wrong use-classification at concept stage = wrong façade brief. Confirm occupancy early; a “serviced apartment / hotel / hostel” call can flip Cap 123M on or off depending on the *hotel* definition below.

#### 1.2 Hotel definition (s. 2)

**Hotel** = premises held out as providing **sleeping accommodation** to any persons presenting themselves who are willing to pay a fee for that accommodation.

### 2. Design duty — energy efficiency via OTTV (s. 4)

| Duty | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| General (s. 4(1)) | Building must be designed and constructed to achieve energy efficiency **to BA’s satisfaction** |
| Deemed compliance path (s. 4(2)) | Compliance with s. 4(1) is regarded as satisfied if **external walls and roofs** have a **suitable overall thermal transfer value** |

#### 2.1 What “OTTV” means in statute (s. 2)

OTTV = heat transferred through the building envelope, expressed in **W/m²**, calculated with regard to:
- area of the building envelope
- materials of construction
- thermal properties of those materials
- **orientation** of the building
- area of **openings** in the envelope
- **shading effect of projections** from the envelope

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123M elevates four schematic-design levers above fittings-out: **orientation, WWR / openings by elevation, wall–roof U-value build-up, and shading projections**. Glass specification and WWR by compass point must be owned at SD — not left to a late GS detail.

#### 2.2 What is an “external wall” for Cap 123M (s. 2)

| Counts as external wall | Does **not** count |
|---|---|
| Whole or part of an **outer wall** | Wall (or part) that is **underground** |
| Wall adjoining a wall of another building | — |

**SD takeaway:** Basement perimeter walls drop out of the Cap 123M external-wall OTTV set; focus the budget on above-ground commercial / hotel façade and roof.

#### 2.3 Numeric OTTV limits — not inside Cap 123M

Cap 123M says **“suitable”** OTTV; it does **not** state max W/m². For schematic targets, pull the current numeric criteria from BD’s OTTV Code of Practice / current PNAP practice — do not invent caps from this Regulation alone. (Separate client / BEAM Plus targets may be stricter than code minimum.)

### 3. What must appear on Cap. 123 s. 14 building plans (s. 5) — submission package drivers

If Cap 123M applies, any plan for building works submitted under **Ordinance s. 14** must include:

| Plan content (s. 5) | What the architect / façade consultant must lock |
|---|---|
| **(a) Wall & roof material diagrams** | Dimensions, colours, arrangements, and other details of materials for **external walls and roofs** |
| **(b) Windows table (every floor)** | In **tabular form**, per orientation: number of windows; size of each; **glass thickness**; **glass type** |
| **(c) Composite walls / roofs** | Thermal transmittance value + **calculations** |
| **(d) OTTV statement** | Statement + **calculations** of OTTV of external walls and roofs |

**SD takeaway:** Curtain-wall packaging, spandrel build-ups, roof insulation, and glass schedules are not “DD polish” — Cap 123M makes them a **statutory plan prerequisite**. Freeze orientation labels (N/E/S/W), WWR per elevation, and glass type early enough that the OTTV calc can sit in the GA/façade package.

### 4. Shading projections & Cap 123F interaction (s. 6) — the 1.5 m concession

Even though Cap 123F normally controls projections against planning metrics, Cap 123M s. 6 creates a **targeted disregard**:

| Condition | Rule |
|---|---|
| Projection from the external wall | Must contribute to achieving the **suitable OTTV** (BA satisfaction) |
| Max projection | **Not more than 1.5 m** from the external wall |
| Planning regs disregarded for that projection | Cap 123F **regs. 20, 22 and 31** |

**Meaning for schematic design:**
- Cap 123F **reg. 20** → site coverage / PR (non-domestic SC privilege band)
- Cap 123F **reg. 22** → dedication / street-widening PR bonuses
- Cap 123F **reg. 31** → “facing external air” / window lighting-ventilating geometry

If BA accepts the projection as an OTTV contributor and it is ≤ **1.5 m**, it is **not taken into account** when checking compliance with those three Cap 123F regulations.

**SD takeaway:** Horizontal shading fins / light shelves / eaves ≤ 1.5 m can be used to cut solar gain **and** ease Cap 123F SC/PR / light-well clashes — but only if they are real OTTV contributors (documented in the OTTV calc) and BA agrees. Do not assume every decorative ledge qualifies; do not exceed 1.5 m and expect the disregard. Street-overhang / canopy rules elsewhere in Cap 123F still need a separate check.

### 5. Mixed-use envelope boundary (s. 3(2)) — calculation hygiene

Where parts of a building have different purposes:

1. Cap 123M applies **only** to Schedule-use parts + spaces ancillary to that use  
2. **Exclude** use as **refuge floor** or **car park**  
3. Every reference to “building” in Cap 123M = those included parts only  

| Include in Cap 123M OTTV model | Exclude |
|---|---|
| Office / retail / F&B / hotel floors (and genuine ancillary plant / toilets serving them) | Pure domestic towers |
| — | Industrial floors |
| — | Schools |
| — | Carpark floors / levels |
| — | Refuge floors |

**SD takeaway:** Draw the OTTV calculation boundary on the GAs at schematic stage. Composite podium–tower schemes fail late when carpark / refuge façade area is incorrectly left in (or commercial area left out).

### 6. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing massing, orientation, WWR, or façade system:

1. **Confirm occupancy** — commercial (per s. 2) and/or hotel in Schedule? If neither → Cap 123M off; stop here for this Cap.
2. **Split mixed use** — carve out domestic / industrial / school / carpark / refuge from the Cap 123M “building” (s. 3(2)).
3. **Set OTTV strategy early** — orientation, WWR by elevation, wall/roof U-build-up, glass type & shading (ss. 2 & 4).
4. **Underground walls out** — do not spend Cap 123M envelope effort on underground perimeter (s. 2 external wall definition).
5. **Numeric target from Code / PNAP** — Cap 123M only says “suitable”; adopt current BD OTTV Code figures (and any stricter client / BEAM Plus target).
6. **Plan-pack readiness** — wall/roof material diagrams; per-floor window tables by orientation (size, glass thickness, type); composite U-value calcs; OTTV statement (s. 5).
7. **Shading ≤ 1.5 m** — if fins/overhangs are part of the OTTV solution, keep projection ≤ **1.5 m** and claim Cap 123F regs 20 / 22 / 31 disregard only where BA accepts OTTV contribution (s. 6).
8. **Do not confuse regimes** — Cap 123M = envelope OTTV for commercial & hotels; EMSD BEC / lighting–HVAC and BEAM Plus are parallel tracks, not substitutes for s. 4–5.
