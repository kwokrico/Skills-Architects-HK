# Cap 123L — Building (Appeal) Regulation
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 13 May 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. L

> Scope note: Cap 123L is the **Appeal Tribunal procedure rulebook** — how to serve a notice of appeal, what must go in the statement of particulars, timelines for BA representations and document exchange, hearing publicity, summons, withdrawal, and dismissal for non-compliance. It is **not** a plan-approval design code, not Cap 123A (administration / plans), and not the substantive right of appeal (that sits in Cap 123 ss. 47–53). Use this the moment a BA decision threatens the scheme (refusal, disapproval, order, or other appealable determination) — appeal clocks start immediately and missed filings can kill the challenge.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123L governs procedure before the **Appeal Tribunal** constituted under Cap 123 for appeals against Building Authority decisions under **section 47 of the Ordinance**. It applies to any person who has given notice of appeal (the **appellant**) and to the Building Authority as respondent — there is no occupancy-type or construction-class filter; the trigger is an appealable BA decision, not a building use.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. When Cap 123L bites relative to schematic design

| Situation | Cap 123L relevant? | SD / project implication |
|---|---|---|
| BA refuses / disapproves plans, or issues an order / determination you intend to challenge | **Yes** — appeal machinery activates | Protect programme: diary Cap 123 s.47 notice clock + Cap 123L statement clock on Day 0 of the BA decision |
| Routine plan submission / negotiation with BA (no appeal yet) | **No** (procedure dormant) | Still design to Cap 123A / F / J etc.; Cap 123L only if the decision is contested |
| Awaiting BA reply on a voluntary amendment | Usually **not yet** | Do not confuse resubmission talks with an appeal; once notice of appeal is given, Cap 123L deadlines bind |
| Multi-party / joint venture with one “appealing owner” | **Yes** if that person is the appellant | Confirm who has standing / interest in the land (s. 4(1)(e)); wrong appellant = wasted appeal |

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123L has **no geometry or occupancy rules**. Its “design constraints” are **hard process deadlines and evidence-package contents**. If the massing or A&A strategy depends on overturning a BA position, treat appeal timing as a critical-path work package equal to redesign.

### 2. Kick-off — notice of appeal (s. 3) — Day-0 actions

| Requirement | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| Form | Notice **in writing**, served on the **Secretary** (to the Appeal Tribunal) |
| Service on Secretary (s. 3(2)) | Registered or ordinary mail to office, **facsimile**, or **personal** service |
| Copy to BA (s. 3(3)) | Appellant **must** serve a copy of the notice on the Building Authority **within the period in Cap 123 s. 47** for giving notice of appeal |
| Failure to serve BA copy | Tribunal **may dismiss** the appeal (s. 11(a)) |

**SD / AP takeaway:** Serving the Secretary alone is not enough — BA must get a copy inside the Cap 123 s. 47 window. Put both acts on the same desk instruction the day the BA decision lands. Cap 123L itself does not restate the s. 47 period — check the Ordinance (and any extension machinery there), not this Regulation alone.

---

### 3. Statement of particulars (s. 4) — the evidence package that wins or loses

Within **28 days** beginning on the date notice of appeal is given (or any extended period under s. 13), the appellant must furnish to the Secretary a statement containing **all** of:

| Item | What the Tribunal expects | Architect / AP content to lock early |
|---|---|---|
| (a) Decision particulars | Exact BA decision under challenge | Quote reference no., date, operative paragraphs |
| (b) Grounds of appeal | If not already in the notice | Map each ground to Ordinance / regulation / PNAP / Code clause — not vague “unreasonable” |
| (c) Detailed description of the matter | Full factual narrative | Site history, submission chronology, what was refused and why it matters to the scheme |
| (d) Document list | Every document intended at hearing | Plans, calc packages, minutes, emails, photos, expert reports — list now; late ambush is exposed |
| (e) Property / land (where relevant) | Address, description, **appellant’s interest** | Lease / ownership / AP appointment chain — standing proofs |
| (f) Witness particulars | Who will give evidence | AP, RSE, fire engineer, traffic, heritage — name roles early |

**Mandatory dual service:** Copy of the full statement → Building Authority within the same 28-day / extended period (s. 4(2)).

**Failure:** No statement (or no copy to BA) → Tribunal **may dismiss** (s. 11(b)).

**SD takeaway:** The 28-day clock is shorter than most redesign cycles. From the moment appeal is contemplated, assemble rejected drawings, annotated BA comments, lease extracts, and expert outlines — do not wait until after a “hopeful” resubmission talk.

---

### 4. BA reply & preliminary-hearing materials (s. 5)

| Actor | Deadline | What they must do |
|---|---|---|
| Building Authority | **28 days** from receiving the appellant’s s. 4(2) statement copy (or extended period) | Furnish Secretary with **written representations** + any documents in BA possession/custody that would assist the Tribunal to determine or dispose of the appeal |
| Building Authority | Same window | Serve copies of those representations and documents on the **appellant** |

**SD takeaway:** This is how BA frames “need for a preliminary hearing” material. Expect the BA package to include internal notes, previous refusals, and comparative cases. Your team must be ready to answer that package within the next further-particulars window (s. 6).

---

### 5. Further particulars & document inspection (s. 6) — discovery clocks

Either party may serve notice requesting:
1. **Further particulars** on any matter relevant to the appeal; and/or  
2. **Inspection** of (or a copy of) any relevant document in the other party’s custody, control, or possession.

| Party requesting | When request may be made | Compliance by other party |
|---|---|---|
| Building Authority | Within **14 days** of receiving appellant’s s. 4(2) statement copy | Within **14 days** of the request notice (unless Tribunal finds request unreasonable / reasonable grounds not to comply) |
| Appellant | Within **14 days** of receiving BA’s s. 5(b) representations | Same 14-day compliance duty |
| Either | Or within any **extended period** under s. 13 | Copies of particulars / documents also go to the **Secretary** |

**Failure by appellant** to comply with a s. 6(2) request → Tribunal **may dismiss** the appeal (s. 11(c)).

**SD takeaway:** Document hygiene matters before SD freezes a “we’ll appeal if refused” strategy. Privilege claims at hearing are questions of law (s. 9(5)); messy files and undisclosed drawings create dismissal and credibility risk.

---

### 6. Hearing logistics (ss. 7–9) — what client / counsel must expect

| Topic | Rule | Practical note |
|---|---|---|
| Notice of hearing (s. 7) | Secretary notifies each party **not less than 21 days** before the fixed date — date, time, place | Do not schedule irreversible site works / contracts that assume a later result without contingency |
| Witness summons (s. 8) | On party application, Tribunal may issue Chairman-signed summons to appear and give evidence / produce documents | Use for third-party surveyors, ex-BA officers, utility owners where voluntary attendance fails |
| Default publicity (s. 9(1)) | Hearing is **in public** | Commercial sensitivity / neighbour PR is a real exposure — brief client early |
| Private hearing (s. 9(2)–(4)) | Tribunal may order private hearing (in whole or part) after consulting parties, if satisfied it is desirable; may restrict publication/disclosure; must weigh parties’ views, private interests, and privilege claims | Apply early if lease strategy, valuations, or security layouts must stay closed |
| Privilege (s. 9(5)) | Privilege claim at hearing = **question of law** | Legal advice privilege / without-prejudice material needs counsel preparation, not ad-hoc AP assertions |

### 7. Withdrawal, dismissal & record (ss. 10–12)

#### 7.1 Withdrawal (s. 10)
- Appellant may withdraw or abandon any part by **written notice to the Secretary**.
- Copy must go to BA **as soon as practicable**.

#### 7.2 Automatic kill-switches — dismissal (s. 11)
Tribunal may dismiss if satisfied the appellant failed to:

| Failure | Section |
|---|---|
| Serve BA a copy of the notice of appeal | s. 3(3) |
| Furnish / serve the s. 4 statement | s. 4(1)/(2) |
| Comply with a further-particulars / document request | s. 6(2) |

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123L dismissals are mostly **self-inflicted administration**, not merits. Treat the three duties above as non-negotiable AP/legal admin, with named owner and deadline on the project programme.

#### 7.3 Decision package (s. 12)
- Chairman records grounds, names, witnesses, evidence, decision, reasons, and costs order.
- Signed decision, reasons, and costs order → served on **both** appellant and BA.
- Tribunal **may publish** the decision even if the hearing (or part) was private.

**SD takeaway:** Even “private” hearings can yield a public written decision — do not assume commercial secrecy survives outcome publication (s. 12(3)).

### 8. Time extensions & service formalities (ss. 13–14)

#### 8.1 What can be extended (s. 13)
Tribunal (or Chairman alone) may extend, on party application, the clocks in:
- s. 4(1) — appellant’s statement of particulars  
- s. 5(a) — BA representations  
- s. 6(2)/(3) — further-particulars request and compliance  

**Not** listed here: Cap 123 s. 47 primary notice-of-appeal period (governed by the Ordinance, not this Regulation’s s. 13 list).

#### 8.2 How to serve (s. 14) — after notice of appeal

| Serve on | Method |
|---|---|
| Tribunal / Secretary | Personal on Secretary, **or** registered post to Secretary’s office *(note: s. 3(2) specially allows ordinary mail / fax for the initial notice of appeal only)* |
| Building Authority | Personal, **or** registered post to BA’s office |
| Appellant / other person (individual) | Personal, **or** registered post to last known residence |
| Appellant / other person (not individual) | Registered post to registered address or last known place of business |

**SD takeaway:** After kick-off, prefer **registered post or personal service** for everything — do not assume fax/ordinary mail under s. 3(2) still works for later filings.

### 9. Architect’s timeline cascade (programme tool)

Use from the date the Cap 123 s. 47 notice of appeal is given (= Day 0 of Cap 123L s. 4):

| Clock | Days | Actor | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cap 123 s. 47 | Per Ordinance | Appellant | Give notice of appeal + serve BA copy (Cap 123L s. 3) |
| Cap 123L s. 4 | **+28** from notice | Appellant | Statement of particulars → Secretary + BA |
| Cap 123L s. 5 | **+28** from BA’s receipt of statement | BA | Representations + documents → Secretary + appellant |
| Cap 123L s. 6 | **+14** from receiving opposite package | Either | Request further particulars / documents |
| Cap 123L s. 6 | **+14** from request | Recipient | Comply (or justify refusal to Tribunal) |
| Cap 123L s. 7 | **≥21** before hearing | Secretary | Hearing notice (date / time / place) |
| Cap 123L s. 13 | On application | Tribunal / Chairman | May extend s. 4 / 5 / 6 clocks only |

### 10. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before relying on “we can appeal the BA decision” as a massing or A&A assumption:

1. **Confirm the decision is appealable** under Cap 123 s. 47 — Cap 123L only runs the procedure.
2. **Diary Cap 123 s. 47 + Cap 123L s. 3** — notice to Secretary **and** copy to BA inside the Ordinance period.
3. **Start the s. 4 evidence pack on Day 0** — grounds mapped to clauses, document list, land interest, intended witnesses (AP / RSE / specialists).
4. **Budget the 28 + 28 + 14 cascade** — redesign, negotiation, and appeal prep cannot share the same undefined float.
5. **Appoint counsel early** if publicity, privilege, or private-hearing applications are foreseeable (ss. 9, 12).
6. **Never miss s. 11 kill-switches** — BA copy of notice, statement of particulars, and further-particulars compliance.
7. **Do not invent extension comfort** — s. 13 does not extend the Cap 123 s. 47 primary notice period; apply for extensions only on listed Cap 123L clocks.
8. **Parallel path** — keep a compliant redesign option live; an appeal protects rights, it does not pause statutory stop-work / order enforcement unless separately secured under Cap 123.
