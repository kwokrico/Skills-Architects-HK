# Cap 123L — Building (Appeal) Regulation
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 13 May 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. L  
Made under Cap. 123, section 38(1B) | In force from 16 November 1994

> Scope note: Cap 123L is the **Appeal Tribunal procedure rulebook** — cast of parties, how to serve a notice of appeal, what must go in the statement of particulars, BA representations tied to the need for a **preliminary hearing**, further-particulars / document inspection, ≥21-day hearing notice, witness summons, public vs private hearing, privilege as a question of law, withdrawal / partial abandonment, dismissal for non-compliance, the hearing record, publication of decisions, time extensions, and service formalities. It is **not** a plan-approval design code, not Cap 123A (administration / plans), and not the substantive right of appeal (that sits in Cap 123 ss. 46–53). Use this the moment a BA decision threatens the scheme — appeal clocks start immediately and missed filings can kill the challenge on procedure alone.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123L governs procedure before the **Appeal Tribunal** constituted under Cap 123 for appeals against Building Authority decisions under **section 47 of the Ordinance**. It applies to any person who has given notice of appeal (the **appellant**) and to the Building Authority as respondent — there is no occupancy-type or construction-class filter; the trigger is an appealable BA decision, not a building use. Every “hearing” in this Regulation includes a **preliminary hearing** under Cap 123 s. 49, so the same notice, publicity, summons, record, and costs rules can bite before any full merits hearing.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Cast of parties & defined terms (s. 2) — who owns which duty

| Term | Means | Why the architect / AP must care |
|---|---|---|
| **appellant** | Person who has given notice of appeal | Once notice is given, *this person* (not “the project team”) owns Cap 123L clocks and s. 11 dismissal risk |
| **Secretary** | Secretary to the Appeal Tribunal under Cap 123 s. 46 | Primary filing address for notice, statement, withdrawal — wrong office = no valid start |
| **Tribunal** | Tribunal appointed under Cap 123 s. 48(1) for *that* appeal | Case-specific panel — not a standing “BD appeals desk” |
| **Chairman** | Chairman of that Tribunal under Cap 123 s. 48(1) | Signs summons (s. 8); records the hearing (s. 12); may alone extend time (s. 13(2)); co-signs decision with other members |
| **hearing** | Hearing of an appeal **including a preliminary hearing** | All Cap 123L hearing rules (ss. 7–9, 12) apply to Cap 123 s. 49 preliminary hearings too |
| **preliminary hearing** | Hearing held under Cap 123 s. 49 | Filter / disposal hearing — BA’s s. 5 package is expressly “as regards need for” this |
| **notice of appeal** | Notice under Cap 123L s. 3 | Day-0 trigger for the 28-day statement clock |
| **extended period** | Period allowed under Cap 123L s. 13 | Only for clocks listed in s. 13 — not a general “ask for more time” for Cap 123 s. 47 |

**SD takeaway:** Lock who is the named appellant (owner / developer / AP as agent with clear authority) before notice goes out. Joint-venture ambiguity here is a standing / interest problem under s. 4(1)(e), not a drafting nicety.

### 2. When Cap 123L bites relative to schematic design

| Situation | Cap 123L relevant? | SD / project implication |
|---|---|---|
| BA refuses / disapproves plans, or issues an order / determination you intend to challenge | **Yes** — appeal machinery activates | Protect programme: diary Cap 123 s. 47 notice clock + Cap 123L statement clock on Day 0 of the BA decision |
| Routine plan submission / negotiation with BA (no appeal yet) | **No** (procedure dormant) | Still design to Cap 123A / F / J etc.; Cap 123L only if the decision is contested |
| Awaiting BA reply on a voluntary amendment / resubmission | Usually **not yet** | Do not confuse resubmission talks with an appeal; once notice of appeal is given, Cap 123L deadlines bind even if talks continue |
| Multi-party / joint venture with one “appealing owner” | **Yes** if that person is the appellant | Confirm standing / interest in the land (s. 4(1)(e)); wrong appellant = wasted appeal |
| Strategy relies on overturning BA interpretation (PR, SC, MOE, street, OP condition, order) | **Yes** | Treat appeal prep as a parallel work package equal in weight to redesign |
| Minor Works / designated exempted works track (no Cap 123 s. 47 decision) | Usually **no** | Cap 123L only runs after an appealable Ordinance decision |

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123L has **no geometry or occupancy rules**. Its “design constraints” are **hard process deadlines and evidence-package contents**. If massing or A&A depends on overturning a BA position, diary Cap 123L as critical path.

### 3. Kick-off — notice of appeal (s. 3) — Day-0 actions

#### 3.1 What must happen

| Requirement | Hard rule | Section |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Notice of appeal **for Cap 123 s. 47** | s. 3(1) |
| Form | **In writing** | s. 3(1) |
| Primary service | Served on the **Secretary** | s. 3(1) |
| Copy to BA | Appellant **must** serve a copy on the Building Authority **within the period specified in Cap 123 s. 47** for giving notice of appeal | s. 3(3) |
| Failure to serve BA copy | Tribunal **may dismiss** the appeal | s. 11(a) |

#### 3.2 How to serve the *initial* notice on the Secretary (s. 3(2)) — special wider methods

Unlike later filings under s. 14, the **notice of appeal to the Secretary** may be served by **any** of:

| Method | Allowed for initial notice to Secretary? |
|---|---|
| Registered mail to Secretary’s office | **Yes** |
| **Ordinary mail** to Secretary’s office | **Yes** (s. 3(2) only) |
| **Facsimile transmission** | **Yes** (s. 3(2) only) |
| Personal service on Secretary | **Yes** |

**SD / AP takeaway:** Serving the Secretary alone is not enough — BA must get a copy inside the Cap 123 s. 47 window. Put both acts on the same desk instruction the day the BA decision lands. Cap 123L does **not** restate the s. 47 period length — check the Ordinance (and any extension machinery *there*), not this Regulation alone. Prefer registered post or personal service even for the initial notice so you can prove service if BA disputes receipt.

### 4. Statement of particulars (s. 4) — the evidence package that wins or loses

#### 4.1 Clock

| Clock | Rule |
|---|---|
| Start | Date notice of appeal is **given** |
| Duration | **28 days** beginning on that date, **or** any **extended period** under s. 13 |
| Recipients | Furnish to **Secretary** (s. 4(1)) **and** serve copy on **BA** within the same window (s. 4(2)) |
| Failure | No statement, or no BA copy → Tribunal **may dismiss** (s. 11(b)) |

#### 4.2 Mandatory contents — expand each limb (s. 4(1)(a)–(f))

The statement must contain **all** of the following:

| Limb | Statute text (essence) | Architect / AP content to lock early |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Decision particulars** | Particulars of the decision to which the appeal relates | Exact BA decision under challenge: reference no., date, operative paragraphs, which plans / which order / which condition. Attach or quote the decision letter. Ambiguous “the disapproval” invites BA to say you appealed the wrong thing |
| **(b) Grounds of appeal** | Grounds **if they have not been specified in the notice of appeal** | Map each ground to Ordinance / regulation / Code / PNAP clause — not vague “unreasonable.” Strategy choice: put headline grounds in the s. 3 notice (to fix the frame early) **or** hold detail for the s. 4 statement — but by Day 28 the grounds must exist somewhere |
| **(c) Detailed description of the matter** | Detailed description of the matter to which the appeal relates | Full factual narrative: site history, submission chronology, what was refused, scheme impact (GFA, height, MOE, street works, OP path). This is the story the Tribunal reads first |
| **(d) Document list** | Description of **each document** the appellant intends to produce at the hearing | Plans (all revisions), structural / geo calcs, minutes, emails, photos, expert reports, lease extracts, prior BA approvals on comparable schemes. **List now** — late ambush documents create credibility and further-particulars risk |
| **(e) Property / land (where relevant)** | Address and description of any property or land to which the appeal relates **and** a statement of the **interest of the appellant** in that property or land | Lot / address / DD / Lot no.; owner vs purchaser vs AP agent; lease / assignment / authorization chain. Standing proofs belong here |
| **(f) Witness particulars** | Particulars of the witnesses the appellant intends to call | Name / role for AP, RSE, RGE, fire engineer, traffic, heritage, valuation — whoever will speak. Unnamed “experts later” is weak under (f) |

#### 4.3 Dual-service discipline

| Duty | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Statement → Secretary | Within 28 days / extended period |
| **Same** statement copy → BA | Within **same** 28 days / extended period |

Missing either limb is independently enough for s. 11(b) dismissal (“furnish … **or** serve a copy”).

**SD takeaway:** The 28-day clock is shorter than most redesign cycles. From the moment appeal is contemplated, assemble rejected drawings, annotated BA comments, lease extracts, and expert outlines — do not wait until after a “hopeful” resubmission talk. Treat s. 4 as a **design archive freeze**: if a drawing is not listed under (d), assume you may not get to rely on it cleanly at hearing.

### 5. BA reply & materials “as regards need for a preliminary hearing” (s. 5)

Section 5’s title is deliberate: BA’s package is not only a defence brief — it is material the Tribunal uses to decide whether a **Cap 123 s. 49 preliminary hearing** is needed, and/or whether the appeal can be determined or otherwise disposed of.

| Actor | Deadline | What they must do |
|---|---|---|
| Building Authority | **28 days** beginning on the date of receiving the appellant’s s. 4(2) statement copy (or extended period) | Furnish Secretary with **written representations** **and** documents (if any) **in BA’s possession or custody** that BA **considers would assist** the Tribunal to **determine or otherwise dispose of** the appeal |
| Building Authority | Same window | Serve on the appellant: copy of the representations **and** a copy of **each** document furnished under (a) |

#### 5.1 Practical implications of BA’s discretion

| Point | Effect on the project team |
|---|---|
| BA chooses what “would assist” | Expect selective disclosure of internal notes, prior refusals, comparative cases — not necessarily the whole BD file |
| Package can drive preliminary hearing | Be ready for an early Cap 123 s. 49 filter hearing under the same Cap 123L hearing rules (ss. 7–9, 12) |
| Clock starts on BA’s **receipt** of your s. 4(2) copy | Prove BA service date; soft “we emailed someone at BD” is not s. 14 service |
| Appellant’s next discovery window (s. 6(2)(b)) starts when BA serves under s. 5(b) | Diary **+14 days** the day BA’s package lands |

**SD takeaway:** When BA’s s. 5 pack arrives, do not only argue merits — also assess whether BA is steering toward summary disposal / preliminary hearing. Align counsel and experts within the next 14-day further-particulars window.

### 6. Further particulars & document inspection (s. 6) — discovery clocks

#### 6.1 Two kinds of request (s. 6(1))

Either party may, by serving a notice:

| Type | What you can demand |
|---|---|
| **(a) Further particulars** | More particulars on **any matter relevant** to the appeal |
| **(b) Document access** | Make available for **inspection**, **or** furnish a **copy** of, any document the requester considers relevant that is in the other party’s **custody, control or possession** |

#### 6.2 Who may request when (s. 6(2))

| Party requesting | Window | Trigger event |
|---|---|---|
| Building Authority | **14 days** beginning on service of appellant’s s. 4(2) statement (or extended period) | After BA receives your statement |
| Appellant | **14 days** beginning on service of BA’s s. 5(b) representations (or extended period) | After you receive BA’s package |
| Either | — | Requests **outside** these windows are not authorised by s. 6(2) unless an extension under s. 13 applies to that window |

#### 6.3 Compliance duty & escape valves (s. 6(3))

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Default | Recipient must comply within **14 days** of the request notice (or extended period) |
| Escape 1 | Tribunal satisfied the request was **unreasonable** |
| Escape 2 | Tribunal satisfied **reasonable grounds** exist for not complying |
| Secretary copy (s. 6(4)) | Any particulars or document copies furnished under (3) must **also** go to the Secretary within the same compliance window |

#### 6.4 Asymmetric enforcement under Cap 123L

| Failure | Cap 123L consequence |
|---|---|
| **Appellant** fails to comply with a s. 6 request | Tribunal **may dismiss** the appeal (s. 11(c)) |
| **BA** fails to comply with a s. 6 request | **No** Cap 123L dismissal mirror — remedy is Tribunal direction / adverse inference / costs under Ordinance powers, not automatic Cap 123L dismissal |

**SD takeaway:** Document hygiene before SD freezes a “we’ll appeal if refused” strategy. Privilege claims at hearing are questions of law (s. 9(5)); messy files and undisclosed drawings create dismissal and credibility risk. When BA requests inspection of your working calcs / emails, escalate to counsel immediately — do not freestyle withhold or over-produce.

### 7. Notification of hearing (s. 7)

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Who notifies | **Secretary** |
| Whom | **Each party** |
| Form | **In writing** |
| Minimum lead time | **Not less than 21 days** before the date fixed |
| Content | Date, **time**, and **place** of the hearing |

Because “hearing” includes preliminary hearing (s. 2), this ≥21-day notice applies to Cap 123 s. 49 preliminary hearings as well as full hearings.

**SD takeaway:** Do not schedule irreversible site works, demolition, or main-contract awards that assume a later result without contingency. From notice of hearing, budget prep time for witnesses listed under s. 4(1)(f) and any summons under s. 8.

---

### 8. Witness summons (s. 8)

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Who applies | A **party** to the appeal |
| Who issues | Tribunal, on that application |
| Form | Summons **signed by the Chairman** |
| What it can compel | Person to **appear** at a hearing **and** give evidence **or** produce any document in that person’s **custody, control or possession** |

**Typical architectural uses:** reluctant third-party surveyors; utility / adjacent-owner witnesses; authors of reports you need in evidence; production of documents held by someone who will not voluntarily release them.

**SD takeaway:** Summons is not automatic discovery — you must **apply**. Identify non-voluntary witnesses in the s. 4(1)(f) list early enough to apply before the hearing date fixed under s. 7.

### 9. Public hearing, private hearing, non-disclosure & privilege (s. 9)

#### 9.1 Default rule (s. 9(1))

Subject to (2) and (3), the hearing of an appeal **shall be in public**.

#### 9.2 Private hearing order (s. 9(2))

Tribunal **may**, by order:

| Power | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Direct that a hearing **or part of a hearing** be held **in private** |
| Condition | After **consulting the parties**, Tribunal is satisfied it is **desirable** |
| Attendance | Give directions as to **who may be present** |

#### 9.3 Publication / disclosure bans (s. 9(3))

Where a private-hearing order is made under (2), Tribunal **may** also direct that evidence or document contents **must not be published or disclosed** to:

- some or all of the **parties**; and/or  
- some or all of the **persons permitted to be present** under (2)

— covering evidence given before the Tribunal **or** any matter in a document lodged with / received in evidence by the Tribunal.

#### 9.4 Mandatory factors (s. 9(4))

In exercising powers under (2) and (3), Tribunal **shall** take into account (in addition to any other matter):

| Factor | Practical meaning |
|---|---|
| Views of any party | Client / BA positions on open vs closed |
| **Private interests** of any party | Commercial strategy, security layouts, valuation, lease negotiations |
| Any **claim as to privilege** by any party | Legal advice / without-prejudice / other privilege assertions |

#### 9.5 Privilege = question of law (s. 9(5))

Any claim as to privilege arising at a hearing **shall be treated as a question of law**.

**SD takeaway:** Brief the client early that BA appeals are **public by default** — neighbour PR, press, and competing developers can sit in. If lease strategy, security layouts, or valuations must stay closed, apply for a s. 9(2)/(3) order with counsel — do not assume the Tribunal will volunteer privacy. Privilege is not something the AP can “assert from the box”; prepare the legal claim before hearing day. Also note: even a private hearing can still yield a **publishable written decision** under s. 12(3).

### 10. Withdrawal & partial abandonment (s. 10)

| Action | How | Extra duty |
|---|---|---|
| Withdraw the **whole** appeal | Written notice to the **Secretary** | Serve copy on BA **as soon as practicable** after giving it |
| Abandon **any part** of the appeal | Same written notice to Secretary | Same BA copy duty |

**SD / commercial use cases for partial abandonment:**

- BA accepts one ground via parallel negotiation (e.g. agrees a MOE revision) while another ground (e.g. PR / street) remains live — abandon the settled limb, keep the rest  
- Client elects to redesign part of the scheme and drop that attack line to save costs / hearing time  
- Narrow the issues before a preliminary hearing to improve disposal prospects

**SD takeaway:** Partial abandonment is expressly allowed — use it as a live case-management tool, not only as full retreat. Always copy BA promptly; silent “we’re dropping ground 2” at the hearing is not s. 10 compliance.

### 11. Dismissal kill-switches (s. 11) — procedure that ends the appeal without merits

Where the Tribunal is satisfied the appellant failed to do any of the following, it **may** dismiss the appeal:

| Failure | Section | Typical real-world cause |
|---|---|---|
| Serve BA a copy of the notice of appeal | s. 3(3) | Notice only filed with Secretary; BA never served inside Cap 123 s. 47 period |
| Furnish the s. 4(1) statement **or** serve the s. 4(2) copy on BA | s. 4(1)/(2) | Statement late; or filed with Secretary but BA copy missed |
| Comply with a further-particulars / document request under s. 6(2) | s. 6 | Ignored BA discovery request; incomplete production without Tribunal escape ruling |

#### 11.1 What s. 11 does *not* say (important)

| Point | Implication |
|---|---|
| “**May** dismiss” | Discretionary — not automatic — but do not rely on mercy |
| Only **appellant** failures listed | BA procedural defaults are not Cap 123L dismissal triggers |
| No merits threshold | Dismissal can kill a strong technical case on admin alone |

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123L dismissals are mostly **self-inflicted administration**, not merits. Name an owner for each of the three duties on the project programme, with proof-of-service files.

### 12. Record of hearing, signed decision & publication (s. 12)

#### 12.1 What the Chairman must record (s. 12(1))

At **any** hearing (including preliminary), the Chairman shall record or cause to be recorded:

| Record item | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Grounds of appeal | Fixes the issues decided |
| Name of the appellant | Identity of the party bound |
| Names of **all** witnesses summoned **or** who appeared | Completeness of the evidentiary record |
| Evidence given | Basis for reasons / later challenge |
| Decision of the Tribunal | Operative outcome |
| Reasons for the decision | Explains win / loss for redesign |
| Any order for costs | Commercial exposure |

#### 12.2 Signed decision package & service (s. 12(2))

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| What is signed | Decision at conclusion of hearing + reasons + any costs order |
| Who signs | **Chairman and the other members** of the Tribunal |
| Who receives copies | **Both** appellant and Building Authority |

#### 12.3 Publication even after private hearing (s. 12(3))

Tribunal **may publish**, in a manner it thinks fit, any decision under (2) — **including** a decision from a hearing the whole or any part of which was held in private under s. 9(2).

**SD takeaway:** Privacy at the hearing ≠ secrecy of the written outcome. Assume published decisions can be read by the market, neighbours, and future BA case officers. Draft grounds and evidence with that publication risk in mind. After a loss, the written reasons become the redesign brief; after a win, they become precedent leverage on later submissions.

### 13. Extension of time limits (s. 13) — what can and cannot be stretched

#### 13.1 Extendable clocks only

On application by a party, the Tribunal may extend (for that case) any time limit in:

| Section | Clock |
|---|---|
| s. 4(1) | Appellant’s statement of particulars (28 days) |
| s. 5(a) | BA’s representations / documents (28 days) |
| s. 6(2) | Window to **make** a further-particulars / document request (14 days) |
| s. 6(3) | Window to **comply** with such a request (14 days) |

Test: Tribunal (or Chairman) considers extension **appropriate**.

#### 13.2 Who may grant (s. 13(2))

The power under s. 13(1) **may be exercised by the Chairman** alone — useful when a full Tribunal is not yet sitting.

#### 13.3 What Cap 123L s. 13 does **not** extend

| Clock | Where it lives |
|---|---|
| Cap 123 **s. 47** primary notice-of-appeal period | Buildings Ordinance — **not** on the s. 13 list |
| s. 7 ≥21-day hearing notice | Not listed as extendable under s. 13 (Secretary’s diary duty) |
| s. 3 service methods / BA copy duty tied to s. 47 | Tied to Ordinance period |

**SD takeaway:** Do not invent extension comfort for the Cap 123 s. 47 notice period from Cap 123L. If you need more time for the **statement** or discovery, apply under s. 13 early — preferably before the original window expires — and diarise that Chairman-alone relief is available.

### 14. Service formalities (s. 14) — after the initial notice

Section 14 applies to service of any notice, statement, order, summons, particulars in writing, or other document — **subject to s. 3(2)** (which keeps the wider methods for the *initial* notice of appeal to the Secretary only).

| Serve on | Valid methods under s. 14 |
|---|---|
| Tribunal / Secretary | Personal on Secretary, **or** **registered post** to Secretary’s office |
| Building Authority | Personal, **or** **registered post** to BA’s office |
| Appellant / other person — **individual** | Personal, **or** registered post to **last known place of residence** |
| Appellant / other person — **not an individual** (company / body) | Registered post to **registered address** or **last known place of business** |

#### 14.1 Method contrast — initial notice vs everything else

| Method | Initial notice to Secretary (s. 3(2)) | Later filings (s. 14) |
|---|---|---|
| Ordinary mail | Allowed | **Not** listed |
| Facsimile | Allowed | **Not** listed |
| Registered post | Allowed | Allowed |
| Personal service | Allowed | Allowed (where stated) |

**SD takeaway:** After kick-off, prefer **registered post or personal service** for everything. Do not assume fax / ordinary mail under s. 3(2) still works for the statement, withdrawal, further particulars, or BA service. Keep proof-of-service (receipts, acknowledgements) in the appeal file — s. 11 fights are often about whether service happened, not about architecture.

### 15. Architect’s timeline cascade (programme tool)

Use from the date Cap 123 s. 47 notice of appeal is given (= Day 0 of Cap 123L s. 4):

| Step | Clock | Actor | Action | Kill risk if missed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Cap 123 s. 47 period | Appellant | Notice → Secretary + copy → BA (s. 3) | Appeal may never validly run; s. 11(a) if BA copy missed |
| 1 | **+28** from notice | Appellant | Statement of particulars → Secretary + BA (s. 4) | s. 11(b) dismissal |
| 2 | **+28** from BA receipt of statement | BA | Representations + docs → Secretary + appellant (s. 5) | Preliminary-hearing / disposal framing delayed |
| 3a | **+14** from BA receiving statement | BA may request | Further particulars / documents from appellant (s. 6(2)(a)) | — |
| 3b | **+14** from appellant receiving BA pack | Appellant may request | Further particulars / documents from BA (s. 6(2)(b)) | Missed window = no statutory s. 6 request |
| 4 | **+14** from request | Recipient | Comply (unless Tribunal escape) + copy Secretary (s. 6(3)/(4)) | Appellant non-compliance → s. 11(c) dismissal |
| 5 | **≥21** before hearing | Secretary | Hearing notice — date / time / place (s. 7) | — |
| 6 | On application | Party / Tribunal | Witness summons signed by Chairman (s. 8) | Critical witness may not attend |
| 7 | Hearing | Tribunal | Public by default; private / non-disclosure / privilege as ordered (s. 9) | PR / confidentiality exposure |
| 8 | Anytime | Appellant | Withdraw / abandon part — notice Secretary + BA ASAP (s. 10) | — |
| 9 | Conclusion | Tribunal | Record, signed decision + reasons + costs → both parties; may publish (s. 12) | — |
| * | On application | Tribunal / Chairman | Extend only s. 4(1) / 5(a) / 6(2)/(3) clocks (s. 13) | Cannot stretch Cap 123 s. 47 via Cap 123L |

### 16. AP office checklist — documents to freeze before Day 0

Build this pack so s. 4 can be filed inside 28 days without scrambling:

1. BA decision letter / order (full text) — for s. 4(1)(a)  
2. Chronology of submissions, amendments, and BA comments — for s. 4(1)(c)  
3. Full drawing register (all revisions under appeal) — for s. 4(1)(d)  
4. Supporting calcs, FS/MOE/traffic/heritage reports relied on — for s. 4(1)(d)  
5. Lease / ownership / AP appointment / authorization chain — for s. 4(1)(e)  
6. Named witness list (AP / RSE / RGE / specialists) — for s. 4(1)(f)  
7. Grounds mapped clause-by-clause (Ordinance / Cap 123 sub-leg / Code / PNAP) — for s. 4(1)(b)  
8. Parallel compliant redesign option (alive, costed) — commercial fallback if appeal fails or is dismissed  
9. Proof-of-service protocol (registered post / personal) for every Cap 123L filing  
10. Counsel brief on public hearing / privilege / private-hearing application (s. 9)

### 17. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before relying on “we can appeal the BA decision” as a massing or A&A assumption:

1. **Confirm the decision is appealable** under Cap 123 s. 47 — Cap 123L only runs the procedure.  
2. **Name the appellant** and prove land / property interest for s. 4(1)(e).  
3. **Diary Cap 123 s. 47 + Cap 123L s. 3** — notice to Secretary **and** copy to BA inside the Ordinance period; prefer provable service.  
4. **Start the s. 4 evidence pack on Day 0** — all six limbs (a)–(f), not just “grounds.”  
5. **Budget the 28 + 28 + 14 cascade** — redesign, negotiation, and appeal prep cannot share the same undefined float.  
6. **Plan for preliminary hearing** — s. 5 materials + Cap 123 s. 49; same Cap 123L hearing rules apply.  
7. **Appoint counsel early** if publicity, privilege, private hearing, or summonses are foreseeable (ss. 8–9, 12).  
8. **Never miss s. 11 kill-switches** — BA copy of notice, statement of particulars (both filings), and further-particulars compliance.  
9. **Do not invent extension comfort** — s. 13 does not extend Cap 123 s. 47; apply early only for listed Cap 123L clocks; Chairman alone may grant.  
10. **Use partial abandonment (s. 10)** when BA settles part of the dispute — narrow the live issues.  
11. **Assume publication risk** even after a private hearing (s. 12(3)).  
12. **Parallel path** — keep a compliant redesign option live; an appeal protects rights, it does not pause statutory stop-work / order enforcement unless separately secured under Cap 123.
