# Cap 123I — Building (Standards of Sanitary Fitments, Plumbing, Drainage Works and Latrines) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 23 April 2020 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. I

> Scope note: Cap 123I is the **sanitary provision, plumbing, and drainage geometry rulebook** — not BD plan administration (Cap 123A), not volume/open-space control (Cap 123F), and not construction site safety (Cap 123Q). Use this before schematic design to lock toilet counts from usable floor area (UFA), male/female split, flushing-water routing, foul/surface water outfalls, and septic/cesspool siting. Cap 123A reg. 8(1)(k) requires UFA + soil/waste fitment schedules on plans — Cap 123I is what those numbers must satisfy.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123I sets the **minimum numbers of waterclosets, urinals, lavatory basins, and baths/showers** by occupancy class, plus how to **derive design population from UFA or seats**, the **plumbing/drainage hard dimensions** (pipe sizes, traps, falls, vents, rainwater sizing), and **septic tank / cesspool / latrine** standards where public foul sewer is absent or soil fitments are prohibited. It applies to residential buildings, workplaces, places of public entertainment, sports stadia, cinemas, shopping arcades / department stores, religious institutions, funeral parlours, restaurants, and to essentially every building’s foul and surface water disposal system.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. First schematic move — classify the occupancy correctly (regs. 3–8)

Wrong class = wrong toilet count and wrong M:F split. Lock classification before block planning toilet cores.

| Occupancy class | Cap 123I reg. | Excludes / carve-outs that trip SD |
|---|---|---|
| **Residential building** | 4 | Domestic building; **excludes** school pupil hostel / dormitory / lodging (reg. 3) |
| **Workplace** | 5 | Office; industrial undertaking; **shop not inside** arcade/dept store; food room of restaurant **only if** restaurant population > **300** |
| **Place of public entertainment** | 6 | Cap 172 PPE meaning; **excludes** sports stadium and cinema |
| **Sports stadium** | 6A | Sporting activities building / part |
| **Cinema** | 7 | Cinematographic displays |
| **Shopping arcade / department store** | 7A | Arcade includes **passageways** connecting shops |
| **Religious institution** | 7B | Congregational worship / Chinese temples (Cap 153) |
| **Funeral parlour** | 7C | Funeral services building / part |
| **Restaurant** | 8 | Cap 132X restaurant meaning; person calc **excludes food room** |

**SD takeaway:** Separate tenancies by class even inside one tower (office + restaurant + shop). Food rooms of large restaurants are **additional workplace** toilets under reg. 5, not covered by the dining-side restaurant count alone.

### 2. Usable floor area definition — what feeds the person count (reg. 3)

**UFA (general, Part II):** aggregate floor areas **excluding**:
- staircases, public circulation, lift landings;
- kitchens in self-contained flats;
- lavatories;
- space occupied by lift / A/C / utility machinery for the building.

**Arcade special rule (reg. 7A(4)):** for shopping-arcade person calc, UFA excludes staircase, lift landing, lavatory, and lift/AC/utility machinery — but **arcade passageways remain in** (they are the occupancy).

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123I UFA ≠ GFA and ≠ Cap 123F open space. Build a dedicated Cap 123I UFA layer early; Cap 123A sanitary schedule depends on it.

#### 3.1 Person-density rates (use these, not “client headcount” assumptions)

| Flatted factory | **1 / 4.5 m²** UFA | 5(5) |
| Warehouse / godown / storage | **1 / 30 m²** UFA | 5(5) |
| Shop (not in arcade/dept store) | **1 / 15 m²** UFA | 5(5) |
| Restaurant food room (restaurant >300 persons) | **1 / 4.5 m²** UFA of food room | 5(5) |

| Assembly / auditorium — fixed seats | **1 / seat** | 6(3) |
| Sports stadium — no / movable seats | **1 / 0.5 m²** spectator UFA | 6A(3) |
| Sports stadium — fixed seats | **1 / seat** | 6A(3) |

| Shopping arcade / dept store — B/G/1/2 | **1 / 3 m²** UFA | 7A(3) |

| Funeral parlour | **1 / 0.5 m²** UFA | 7C(3) |
| Restaurant (dining side) | **1 / 1.5 m²** UFA **excluding food room** | 8(2) |

#### 3.2 Default male:female proportions (unless BA accepts otherwise)

| Class | Default M:F | Reg. |
|---|---|---|
| Residential; workplace (non-Labour industrial); sports stadium | **1 : 1** | 4(4), 5(5), 6A(3) |
| Public entertainment; cinema; arcade/dept store; religious; funeral; restaurant | **1 : 1.5** | 6(3), 7(3), 7A(3), 7B(3), 7C(3), 8(2) |
| Other industrial | Commissioner for Labour | 5(5) |

**SD takeaway:** At M:F = 1:1.5, female WCs dominate core area. Do not assume 50/50 for cinema, mall, PPE, restaurant, religious, or funeral.

### 4. Minimum sanitary provision — scannable ratios by class

“Or part thereof” always rounds **up**. Provide sex-separated rooms where required (almost everywhere except tiny residential / tiny restaurant exceptions).

#### 4.1 Residential (reg. 4)

| Provision | Rule |
|---|---|
| Shared WCs (not sex-separated) | ≤8 persons → **1**; then **+1 / 12** over 8 |
| Sex-separated WCs | Same per sex: ≤8 → **1**; then **+1 / 12** over 8 |
| Male WCs + urinals (sex-separated) | ≤12 male → **1 WC + 1 urinal**; then **+1 WC and +1 urinal / 12** over 12 |
| Basins + baths/showers | ≤8 → **1 each**; then **+1 / 12** over 8 for each |
| Tenement alternative | Water supply points may replace basins **and** baths/showers (1 point = 1 basin + 1 bath/shower); points in separate compartment **≥ 0.75 m²** or in a WC; **kitchen sink is not** a water supply point |

### 5. Equivalents, prohibitions & water supply (regs. 9–10A)

| Rule | Hard number / effect |
|---|---|
| Soil fitments prohibited (reg. 89 order) | Provide **latrine fitments** / **bucket urinals** at same counts as WCs / urinals (reg. 9) |
| Trough WC | **1 m trough = 1 WC** |
| Trough urinal | **0.5 m trough = 1 urinal** |
| Flush water | Permanent satisfactory supply required to every WC / trough WC / latrine / urinal |
| Waste / shower water | Permanent satisfactory supply required |
| Source priority | Waterworks; or BA-permitted **well on site**; or BA-permitted other source — well needs **WSD certificate** + dry-season (1 Dec–30 Apr) yield certified by AP |

**SD takeaway:** No public salt/fresh flushing means — budget tank rooms, risers, and (if remote) well / tanker strategy before locking upper-floor massing. All flushing plumbing materials must suit **salt water** (reg. 17).

### 6. Plumbing geometry that fixes wet stacks & duct sizes (Part III)

#### 6.1 Routing principles
| Item | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Soil | Via soil pipes → foul drains (reg. 11) |
| Waste default | Discharge in open air over/into channel within **1 m** of trapped gully; into gully above water; or into hopper head (reg. 12) |
| Combined system | Waste may connect to soil pipe only with **≥80 mm seal**, ≥32 mm trap ID, and vented / seal-preserving trap (regs. 12(3), 25(3)) |
| Salt-water flushing plant | Entire flushing system (incl. flushing storage) = salt-water suitable materials (reg. 17) |

#### 6.2 Flush volumes & pipe diameters
| Fitment | Flush | Flush pipe min ID |
|---|---|---|
| WC / slop sink | **9–14 L** per use; cistern refill ≤ **2 min** | **32 mm** |
| Trough WC | Auto discharge ≥ **9 L / m** of channel | **32 mm** |
| Urinal | Auto ≥ **4.5 L** per basin/stall or per metre trough | **15 mm** per basin/stall or per metre |
| Pressure valve alternative | Allowed (except trough WC & urinals) if suitable head — no cistern (reg. 20) |
| Waterworks-flushed soil fitments | regs. 19–23 **do not apply** (reg. 91) |

#### 6.3 Flushing storage tanks (reg. 22)
- Required wherever WC / trough WC / urinal / slop sink installed
- Position: **above highest fitment** or on suitable water tower
- Close-fitting cover + access to enter and clean
- Overflow: ≥1 commercial size larger than supply; dip to ≤**150 mm** above tank bottom; discharge **conspicuously** (reg. 23)

#### 6.4 Trap seals & sizes
| Fitment | Min trap ID | Min water seal |
|---|---|---|
| WC / slop sink / urinal >3 basins or channel >2 m | **80 mm** | **50 mm** (soil) |
| Urinal 2–3 basins or channel ≤2 m | **50 mm** | 50 mm |
| Bidet / single urinal basin or stall | **40 mm** | 50 mm |
| Trough WC | **100 mm** | 50 mm |
| Waste fitment (normal) | **32 mm** | **40 mm** |
| Waste connected to soil | **32 mm** | **80 mm** + vented / anti-siphon |

Trap exemptions for short wastes ≤1 m to trapped gully; lavatory ranges under reg. 12(2); shower ranges with channel trap (reg. 25).

#### 6.5 Vertical stacks, anti-syphonage & ventilation
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Soil pipe from WC/slop | ≥ **80 mm**, or ≥ trap outlet — whichever greater; connect to covered drain **without intervening trap** |
| Waste termination above gully/channel | ≤ **150 mm** above |
| Bends | Only if unavoidable; obtuse; large radius; constant section; cleaning eye at/near bend |
| Anti-syphonage | Required when >1 trap shares a soil or waste pipe (waste traps that inherently hold seal may omit unless BA requires) |
| AS pipe take-off | ≤ **300 mm** from trap outlet (or integral trap take-off) |
| AS pipe ID | If main ≥80 mm → AS ≥**50 mm**; if main <80 mm → ≥ max(⅔ main, **32 mm**) |
| Vent pipe height | ≥ **1 m** above roof (or above eaves if pitched) |
| Vent / soil-as-vent ID | ≥ **80 mm**; grated open end ≥ pipe sectional area; **no surface water** in vent pipes; no foul air into building |

#### 6.6 Rainwater — size & separation (regs. 32–33, 38)
| Item | Hard number |
|---|---|
| Rainwater pipe area | **700 mm² pipe / 10 m²** horizontal roofed surface |
| Min rainwater pipe OD | **65 mm** (verandah/balcony exception if street-side discharge: ≤ **40 mm** ID) |
| Separation | Rainwater **must not** connect to soil, waste, or ventilating pipes |
| Footpath discharge | Via **cast iron conductor** — no free discharge across footpath (regs. 32(5), 42) |
| Eaves gutters | Fall ≥ **1 in 100**; size **700 mm² gutter / 7 m²** roof |
| Verandah/balcony RW | Prefer connect to roof RW stack where available |

#### 6.7 Duct access (reg. 37)
Any soil / waste / AS / vent / rainwater pipe in an internal duct needs access panels to **all pipe connexions**.

### 7. Site drainage — outfalls that fix site layout (Part IV)

#### 7.1 Where water must go
| Stream | Rule (regs. 39–41) |
|---|---|
| Foul | Connect to public foul sewer if available at suitable level **within 30 m of lot boundary**; else BA may order septic tank / cesspool / other approved facility |
| Surface | To public surface sewer within **30 m of building** if available; else to public channel / street side channel; else to stream / nullah to BA satisfaction |
| Sub-soil | May connect to private/public **surface** sewer/drain |

#### 7.2 Drain sizing, falls, bed
| Item | Hard number |
|---|---|
| Min covered drain ID | **100 mm** |
| Falls (Table 29) | 100 → **1/40**; 150 → **1/70**; 225 → **1/100**; 300 → **1/150** |
| Pipe bed | Concrete ≥ **100 mm** thick, ≥ **150 mm** wider than pipe; haunched to springing |
| Soft ground | Surround with ≥ **100 mm** concrete (except sub-soil) |
| Under buildings | **Straight** alignment; **cast iron** sewage pipes; relieving arches/beams so building load not transmitted (reg. 54) |

#### 7.3 Gathering grounds (reg. 47A) — water catchment sites
Foul drains/sewers in gathering grounds must be pressure-capable & watertight (cast iron or approved); hatch boxes ≤ **30 m** intervals + at direction changes (>10° without purpose bend); **≤1 manhole** per drain/sewer.

#### 7.4 Access & disconnection
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Manholes | At direction changes (unless ≤45°, radius ≥6× ID, purpose-made bend); MH or cleaning eyes ≤ **60 m** apart |
| MH under/inside building | **Double-sealed** cast iron airtight cover |
| Disconnecting trap | Required on foul drains/sewers, and on surface drains unless to open nullah/stream; inside MH; seal ≥ **75 mm**; sit on owner’s land as near public sewer / covered nullah as practicable — **BA installs** trap + MH and recovers cost (regs. 57, 76) |
| Public sewer / nullah connexion | **BA makes connexion** and recovers cost (reg. 75) — design programme must allow BD drain test before trench backfill (Part VII) |
| Drain inlets | Trapped, seal ≥ **75 mm** (except soil/vent inlets, trade effluent, roof-only rainwater, or where impracticable) |
| Foul vent at high point | ≥ **50 mm** ID ventilating pipe |
| Surface channels | Fall ≥ **1 in 100**; silt grilles; footpath covers flush, gaps ≤ **20 mm** |

**SD takeaway:** Reserve owner’s land at the lot boundary for disconnecting MH; measure 30 m to public sewer early — if absent, budget septic/cesspool land and effluent strategy now.

### 8. Off-sewer facilities — septic tanks & cesspools (Parts V–VI)

#### 8.1 Septic tank
| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Siting | ≥ **18 m** from spring / stream / well used (or likely used) for drinking, domestic, or food prep |
| Capacity | 1-day soil+waste storage by water-consumption rate BA accepts; **min 2.3 m³**, **max 41 m³** |
| Geometry | Depth invert-to-floor **1.2–1.8 m**; length **3–4 ×** width |
| Walls / floor | Brick ≥215 mm or concrete ≥125 mm walls; floor/foundation concrete ≥150 mm; smooth impervious finish |
| Access / vent | Access to each chamber; headspace vented or means to draw off gases; dip-pipe inlets/outlets |
| Approvals | Submit effluent + sludge disposal method for BA approval before install |

#### 8.2 Cesspool
| Constraint | Value |
|---|---|
| Siting | ≥ **20 m** from drinking/domestic/food water sources; ≥ **15 m** from any occupied/work building |
| Emptying | Removable without carrying contents through occupied/work buildings |
| Capacity | **1 month** storage at **135 L/person/day** (BA sets person count) |
| Construction | Impervious; RC cover; access; ventilation |

### 9. Latrines (where soil fitments banned / no flush) — Part IX

Applies when latrine fitments are used (reg. 9 / 89 pathway). Key SD envelopes:

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Door | Full-height self-closing (except chemical closet) |
| Door swing | Directly to **open air** (chemical closet: need not, but **must not** open into food rooms) |
| “Open air” | Vertically uncovered; min dimension **1.5 m**; if enclosed 4 sides → ≥ **1 m²** horizontal per **6 m** mean enclosing-wall height |
| Vent/light opening | ≥ **0.2 m²** permanent open/openable; near ceiling; to open air; flyscreened |
| Floor | ≥ **150 mm** above external ground; fall ≥ **1:25** (squatting surround ≥ **1:10**) |
| Wall finish | Tile or non-absorbent render ≥ **1.2 m** high |
| Receptacle | Non-absorbent; leak-proof; capacity ≤ **50 L** |

BA may allow door/vent to unenclosed verandah/balcony if still opens to / communicates with open air without prejudicing health (reg. 88).

### 10. Trade effluent & industrial kitchens (reg. 90) — plant room triggers

If discharge may include trade effluent, chemicals, waste steam, liquid > **40°C**, petroleum, calcium carbide, acid, grease/oil, or matter likely to injure sewers — BA may require **neutralizing tanks, cooling tanks, petrol interceptors, grease traps**, or similar.

**SD takeaway:** Restaurants, F&B, workshops, car parks, and process rooms need space for grease / interceptor plant at SD — not as fit-out afterthoughts.

### 11. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing floor plates and cores:

1. **Classify every use** under regs. 4–8 (and workplace carve-outs for arcade shops / large restaurant food rooms).
2. **Compute Cap 123I UFA** per use (apply arcade-specific UFA if mall).
3. **Apply density + default M:F** → separate male/female (and urinal) counts; round every “or part” up.
4. **Locate sex-separated toilet rooms** on every relevant floor/zone; for malls overweight B–2/F female capacity.
5. **Confirm flushing water strategy** — salt-compatible tanks above highest WC, risers, WSD connexion / well.
6. **Draw foul & surface paths** — ≤30 m to public sewers; reserve disconnecting MH on owner land; separate rainwater stacks (≥700 mm² / 10 m² roof).
7. **If no foul sewer** — allocate septic (≥18 m from wells) or cesspool (≥20 m wells, ≥15 m buildings) footprint + emptying route.
8. **Internal wet ducts** — stacks, anti-syphonage, ≥80 mm vents terminating ≥1 m above roof, connexion access panels.
9. **Kitchen / industrial** — grease / interceptor / cooling / neutralising plant space (reg. 90).
10. **Cross-link Cap 123A** — show UFA and sanitary fitment schedules on plans (reg. 8(1)(k)); drainage plans with sizes, falls, traps, vents, and outfalls.
