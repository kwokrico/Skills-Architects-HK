# Cap 123I — Building (Standards of Sanitary Fitments, Plumbing, Drainage Works and Latrines) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 23 April 2020 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. I

> Scope note: Cap 123I is the **sanitary provision, plumbing, and drainage geometry rulebook** — not BD plan administration (Cap 123A), not volume/open-space control (Cap 123F), and not construction site safety (Cap 123Q). Use this before schematic design to lock toilet counts from usable floor area (UFA), male/female split, flushing-water routing, foul/surface water outfalls, and septic/cesspool siting. Cap 123A reg. 8(1)(k) requires UFA + soil/waste fitment schedules on plans — Cap 123I is what those numbers must satisfy.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123I sets the **minimum numbers of waterclosets, urinals, lavatory basins, and baths/showers** by occupancy class, plus how to **derive design population from UFA or seats**, the **plumbing/drainage hard dimensions** (pipe sizes, traps, falls, vents, rainwater sizing, manholes, disconnecting traps), and **septic tank / cesspool / latrine** standards where public foul sewer is absent or soil fitments are prohibited. It applies to residential buildings, workplaces, places of public entertainment, sports stadia, cinemas, shopping arcades / department stores, religious institutions, funeral parlours, restaurants, and to essentially every building’s foul and surface water disposal system.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Vocabulary that drives design decisions (reg. 2)

Do not confuse these streams — they control which pipe, trap, and outfall you draw.

| Term | Means | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Soil fitment** | WC, trough WC, urinal, slop sink, bidet, or similar | Feeds **soil pipe** → foul drain |
| **Waste fitment** | Bath, lavatory basin, or sink (**not** a slop sink) | Feeds **waste pipe**; default discharge to open-air gully/hopper, or to soil only under reg. 25(3) rules |
| **Soil** | Discharge from a soil fitment | Foul system only |
| **Waste** | Used water from a waste fitment | Separate from rainwater |
| **Foul water** | Water contaminated by soil, waste, **or trade effluent** | Public foul sewer / septic / cesspool path |
| **Surface water** | Rain from building + paved/unpaved ground appurtenant | Rainwater pipes / surface drains / nullah — **never** into soil/waste/vent |
| **Trade effluent** | Liquid (with/without suspended matter) from trade or industry | Triggers reg. 90 interceptors / neutralising plant |
| **Slop sink** | Sink for solid or liquid excrement | Treated as **soil** fitment (not “waste”) |
| **Private sewer** | Sewer that is **not** a public sewer | Still needs BA connexion to public sewer/nullah (reg. 75) |
| **Public sewer / public channel** | Vested in & maintained by Government | Primary outfall targets within 30 m rules |
| **Nullah** | Includes any trained streamcourse | Surface / some foul outfall option |
| **Septic tank** | Tank receiving soil + waste where organic solids liquefy | Off-sewer foul treatment |
| **Cesspool** | Underground chamber for reception + **storage** of foul water | Off-sewer storage only (no treatment implied) |
| **Latrine / latrine fitment** | Room / fitment with **removable** excrement receptacle | Used when soil fitments prohibited (regs. 9, 89) |
| **Chemical closet** | Latrine fitment with chemically treated receptacle | Door/vent rules relaxed vs ordinary latrine |
| **Anti-syphonage pipe** | Pipe preventing loss of trap water seal | Required when multiple traps share one stack |
| **Rain water pipe** | Carries surface water **directly** from roofs, verandahs, balconies | Sized by roof area; street discharge via cast-iron conductor |

### 2. First schematic move — classify the occupancy correctly (regs. 3–8)

Wrong class = wrong toilet count and wrong M:F split. Lock classification before block planning toilet cores.

| Occupancy class | Cap 123I reg. | Statutory meaning / carve-outs that trip SD |
|---|---|---|
| **Residential building** | 4 | Domestic building; **excludes** school pupil hostel / dormitory / lodging whether in same building as classrooms or not (reg. 3) |
| **Tenement house** | 4(2) | Domestic part where any living room is for **more than one** tenant/sub-tenant — unlocks water-supply-point alternative |
| **Workplace** | 5 | (a) office; (b) industrial undertaking; (c) **shop not inside** arcade/dept store; (d) restaurant **food room only if** restaurant population > **300** |
| **Industrial undertaking** | 5 | Cap 59 meaning, but **does not include** a restaurant food room |
| **Place of public entertainment** | 6 | Cap 172 PPE meaning; **excludes** sports stadium and cinema |
| **Sports stadium** | 6A | Building / part designed for sporting activities |
| **Cinema** | 7 | Building / part designed for cinematographic displays |
| **Shopping arcade** | 7A | Includes the **passageways** connecting the shops |
| **Department store** | 7A | Cap 502 Schedule 1 meaning |
| **Religious institution** | 7B | Congregational worship / prayers; includes Cap 153 Chinese temple paragraph (b) places |
| **Funeral parlour** | 7C | Building / part for funeral services |
| **Restaurant** | 8 | Cap 132X s.31(2) restaurant; person calc **excludes food room** |
| **Food room** | 5 / 8 | Cap 132X s.3(1); if restaurant >300 persons → **extra workplace** toilets for the food room |

**Composite / multi-use SD rule:** Split every tenancy by class even inside one tower (office + restaurant + shop + mall). A shop **inside** an arcade uses **reg. 7A**, not workplace reg. 5. A standalone street shop uses **reg. 5**. Food rooms of large restaurants need **both** dining-side (reg. 8) and kitchen-side (reg. 5) provision.

**School pupil hostels** are carved out of “residential building” — do not auto-apply reg. 4 tables; seek BA / Cap 123A sanitary schedule advice for that use class.

### 3. Usable floor area — what feeds the person count (regs. 3, 7A(4))

#### 3.1 General UFA (Part II, except arcade person-calc under 7A(3)(a))

**UFA** = aggregate of floor areas in the building, **excluding**:
1. staircases;
2. **public circulation space**;
3. lift landings;
4. kitchens in self-contained flats;
5. lavatories;
6. space occupied by machinery for lift, A/C, utility, or similar building services.

#### 3.2 Arcade-specific UFA (reg. 7A(4)) — for shopping-arcade person calc only

Aggregate of floors in the arcade, **excluding**:
1. staircase;
2. lift landing;
3. lavatory;
4. lift / A/C / utility machinery space.

**Critical difference:** general UFA excludes **public circulation**; arcade UFA does **not** exclude arcade passageways — and reg. 3 already says shopping arcade **includes** those passageways. So mall circulation **counts** toward population.

#### 3.3 What Cap 123I UFA is not
- Not Cap 123F GFA / site coverage maths
- Not saleable area / CFA for leasing
- Not “net office” after fit-out partitions alone — start from Cap 123I exclusions

**SD takeaway:** Build a dedicated Cap 123I UFA layer early; Cap 123A sanitary schedule depends on it. For malls, measure B/G/1/2 and 3/F+ separately (different densities).

#### 4.3 Worked quick examples (round every “or part” **up**)

**A. Grade-A office floor — 1 800 m² Cap 123I UFA**  
Persons = 1 800 / 9 = **200**; M:F 1:1 → 100 male + 100 female.  
Male (11–100 band scales… here 100): WC = 100/25 = **4**; urinals = 100/50 = **2**; basins = 100/25 = **4**.  
Female: WC = 2 + (100−25)/25 = 2 + 3 = **5**; basins = **4**.  
Sex-separated exclusive rooms required.

**B. Restaurant dining UFA 300 m² (food room separate)**  
Persons = 300 / 1.5 = **200**; M:F 1:1.5 → male 80, female 120.  
Male: WC **1**, urinals **2**, basins **1**.  
Female: WC **2**, basins **1**.  
If total restaurant persons >300, **also** size food-room toilets as workplace at 1/4.5 m².

**C. Mall G/F arcade UFA 1 200 m²**  
Persons = 1 200 / 3 = **400**; M:F 1:1.5 → male 160, female 240.  
Male ≤500: WC = 160/125 → **2**; urinals = 160/250 → **1**; basins = 160/125 → **2**.  
Female 126–1 800: WC = 3 + (240−125)/80 = 3 + 2 = **5** (round parts up carefully: 115/80 → 2 parts); basins = 240/125 → **2**.

**D. Cinema 600 seats**  
Persons = 600; M:F 1:1.5 → male 240, female 360.  
Male 201–500: WC **2**, urinals = 240/100 → **3**.  
Female 251–420: WC = 5 + (360−250)/85 = 5 + 2 = **7**.  
Basins: male 240/100 → **3**; female 360/100 → **4**.

“Or part thereof” always rounds **up**. Sex-separated exclusive rooms are mandatory for almost all non-residential classes (exceptions noted).

| Mode | Provision rule |
|---|---|
| Shared WCs (not sex-separated) — Table 1 | ≤8 persons → **1**; >8 → **1 + 1/12** over 8 |
| Sex-separated WCs — Table 2 | Same per sex: ≤8 → **1**; >8 → **1 + 1/12** over 8 |

### 6. Equivalents, BA prohibition & permanent water supply (regs. 9–10A, 89)

#### 6.1 When soil fitments are prohibited (regs. 9, 89)
If BA orders that drains / private sewers / disposal means are unsatisfactory → **no soil fitments**. Then:
- **Latrine fitments** in lieu of WCs — same count as the relevant occupancy table
- **Bucket urinals** in lieu of urinals — same count
- Part IX latrine geometry then applies (see §12)

#### 6.2 Trough equivalents (reg. 9(2)–(3))
| Fitment | Equivalence |
|---|---|
| Trough watercloset | **Every 1 m** of trough = **1 WC** |
| Trough urinal | **Every 0.5 m** of trough = **1 urinal** |

#### 6.3 Permanent water supply (reg. 10A) — OP / consent killer
| Required for | Supply duty |
|---|---|
| Every WC / trough WC / latrine / urinal | Permanent connexion; supply **satisfactory and sufficient** for flushing + all other uses of that supply |
| Every waste fitment / shower | Permanent connexion; satisfactory & sufficient for those uses |

**Source hierarchy (reg. 10A(3)):**
1. **Waterworks** (WSD); **or**
2. BA written permission — **well within the site**; **or**
3. BA permit/direct other source (only if waterworks + on-site well are not reasonable)

**Well path (reg. 10A(5)–(7)):**
- Owner must produce **WSD certificate** whether waterworks supply is available
- WSD must issue certificate within **10 days** of written application
- Yield must be sufficient for Dec–30 Apr dry season; AP ascertains by recognised method after first BA consent to commence (or in that dry period / as BA directs) and **certifies** yield

**Sufficiency test (reg. 10A(4)):** BA looks at nature, type, size, purpose of building, and all likely water uses.

### 7. Plumbing — fitment construction & flushing plant (Part III, regs. 11–25)

#### 7.1 Soil vs waste routing (regs. 11–12)
| Stream | Rule |
|---|---|
| Soil | Conduct via suitable soil pipes to **foul** drains |
| Waste (default) | Discharge in **open air**: (a) over/into channel within **1 m** of properly trapped gully; (b) into gully **above** water level; or (c) into suitable hopper head |
| Lavatory basins (alt.) | Into drainage channel immediately below basins that discharges to trapped gully; **or** into common waste pipe through a trap with adequate cleaning access |
| Waste → soil (combined) | Allowed subject to reg. 25(3) trap rules (80 mm seal + ventilated / seal-preserving) |

#### 7.2 Fitment materials & geometry (regs. 13–16, 18)
| Item | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Soil fitment material | Glazed earthenware, enamelled fireclay, or other approved |
| Pedestal WC | Shape/capacity to retain water & receive deposits without undue soiling; hinged seat or seat rim/inset (squatting type exempt) |
| Flushing rim | Required on WC, bidet, basin-type urinal, slop sink |
| Trough WC channel | Length ≤ **5 m**; even fall ≥ **1 in 30** to outlet trap |
| Stall / trough urinal channel | Even fall ≥ **1 in 120**; no part of channel > **6 m** from its trap |

#### 7.3 Flushing water system — always salt-water ready (regs. 17–23)
| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Scope | Plumbing for flushing to every WC, trough WC, urinal, slop sink |
| Materials | **Every part** incl. flushing-only storage tank = suitable for **salt water** |
| Cistern required | Default for WC, trough WC, urinal, slop sink — unless pressure valve (reg. 20) |
| WC / slop flush volume | **9–14 L** per use; ball valve refill ≤ **2 minutes** |
| Trough WC auto flush | Discharge at **highest point** of channel; ≥ **9 L per metre** of channel; intervals as needed for cleaning |
| Urinal auto flush | Into every basin/stall or every metre of trough; ≥ **4.5 L**; intervals as needed |
| Pressure valves | Allowed instead of cistern **except** trough WC and urinals, if suitable head |
| Flush pipe min ID | WC / trough WC / slop → **32 mm**; urinal basin/stall → **15 mm** each; trough urinal → **15 mm per metre** |
| Flushing storage tanks | Required in every building with WC / trough WC / urinal / slop sink |
| Tank position | **Above highest fitment**, or on suitable water tower |
| Tank access | Close-fitting cover; access to **enter and clean** |
| Overflow (cistern & tank) | Required; tank overflow ID = **≥1 commercial size larger** than supply; dip to ≤ **150 mm** above tank bottom; discharge in **conspicuous** position |

**Exemption (reg. 91):** regs. 19–23 do **not** apply to soil fitments flushed with water **from the waterworks**.

#### 7.4 Trap seals & sizes (regs. 24–25)

**Soil traps — seal ≥ 50 mm; min ID:**

| Fitment | Min trap ID |
|---|---|
| WC / slop sink / urinal >3 basins or stalls / channel >2 m | **80 mm** |
| Urinal 2–3 basins/stalls or channel ≤2 m | **50 mm** |
| Bidet / single urinal basin or stall | **40 mm** |
| Trough WC | **100 mm** |

**Waste traps — under fitment; min ID 32 mm; seal ≥ 40 mm** (or ≥ **80 mm** if connected to soil pipe, plus ventilated or constructed to prevent seal loss).

**Waste trap not required when:**
1. waste pipe ≤ **1 m** and discharges over/into suitable trapped gully; **or**
2. lavatory basins discharge per reg. 12(2); **or**
3. shower range has trapped channel at lowest point

### 8. Vertical stacks, vents, rainwater & ducts (Part III, regs. 26–38A)

#### 8.1 Soil & waste pipes (regs. 26–29)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Soil from WC/slop | ID ≥ **80 mm**, or ≥ trap outlet ID — whichever greater |
| Soil from urinal/bidet | ID ≥ trap outlet ID of connected fitment |
| Soil to drain | Connect to covered drain **without intervening trap** |
| Single waste fitment pipe | ID ≥ **32 mm**, or ≥ trap outlet — whichever greater |
| Multi-fitment waste pipe | Adequate diameter for all waste |
| Waste lower end | Terminate ≤ **150 mm** above channel or trapped gully |
| Bends | Only if unavoidable; obtuse angle; largest practicable radius; **no change** of cross-section; cleaning eye at/near bend |
| Access | Cleaning eyes / approved method; sited for easy rod entry |

#### 8.2 Anti-syphonage (reg. 30)
**Trigger:** more than one soil/waste trap on one soil pipe; **or** more than one waste trap on one waste pipe.  
**Waste exception:** traps constructed to prevent seal loss need no AS pipe unless BA requires.

| Detail | Rule |
|---|---|
| Take-off | Integral to trap; **or** on branch soil/waste on seal side nearest main, ≤ **300 mm** from trap outlet |
| AS pipe ID if main ≥80 mm | ≥ **50 mm** |
| AS pipe ID if main <80 mm | ≥ max(**⅔ of main**, **32 mm**) |
| Main AS to venting soil stack | Connect only **above flood level** of highest fitment on that soil pipe |

#### 8.3 Ventilating pipes (reg. 31)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Height | ≥ **1 m** above roof; or above **eaves** if pitched roof |
| Foul air | Must not escape into any building |
| Soil/waste as vent | May be carried up to act as drain/sewer vent |
| Min ID of vent (and of soil/waste acting as vent) | **80 mm** |
| Open end | Grating with aggregate aperture area ≥ pipe sectional area |
| Ban | Ventilating pipes **must not** carry surface water |

#### 8.4 Rainwater pipes & eaves gutters (regs. 32–33, 38)
| Item | Hard number / rule |
|---|---|
| Sizing | **700 mm²** of pipe / **10 m²** horizontal roofed-over surface |
| Min diameter | ≥ **65 mm** (general) |
| Verandah/balcony RW to street side channel (or to such a pipe) | ID ≤ **40 mm**; prefer connect to roof RW stack where available |
| Outside RW to surface public sewer drain | Discharge ≤150 mm above ground over trapped gully; **or** into trapped gully below grating but above water |
| Inside RW (not roof-only) to surface sewer drain | Trap with seal ≥ **75 mm** |
| RW to channel | Discharge ≤ **150 mm** above channel top |
| Street side-channel via conductor | Cast-iron shoe at foot so water enters conductor directly |
| Separation | RW **must not** connect to soil, waste, or ventilating pipes |
| Across footpath | Must use **cast iron conductor** (also reg. 42 — no free discharge across footpath surface) |
| Eaves gutters | Cast iron or approved; fall ≥ **1 in 100**; size **700 mm²** gutter / **7 m²** roof; properly connected to RW pipes |

#### 8.5 Pipe materials, fixing, ducts, standards (regs. 34–38A)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Soil / waste / AS / vent / foul overflow & foul-drain connexions | Circular; cast iron, steel, copper, or approved; sound & defect-free |
| Rainwater pipes | Cast iron, copper, or approved |
| Connexions | Suitable to materials; watertight |
| Fixing | Soil / waste / RW securely fixed to wall; floor/wall penetrations filled & sealed |
| Pipes in ducts | Access panels sized/sited for **all pipe connexions** in the duct |
| BS / national standards | BA may permit in writing materials/workmanship per relevant BS Spec / BS CP / other accepted national standard (reg. 38A) |

### 9. Site drainage — outfalls that fix site layout (Part IV, regs. 39–46)

#### 9.1 Mandatory drainage of the building (reg. 39)
Every building must have pipes, drains and channels necessary to dispose of **all foul water and surface water**.

#### 9.2 Where each stream must go
| Stream | Rule | Reg. |
|---|---|---|
| **Foul** | If public foul sewer at suitable level/position within **30 m of lot boundary** → connect to it | 40(1) |
| **Foul (no sewer)** | BA may order septic tank, cesspool, or other approved facility | 40(2) |
| **Surface** | If public surface sewer at suitable level/position within **30 m of the building** → rainwater pipes + surface drains to it | 41(1) |
| **Surface (no public sewer)** | To public channel or street side channel to BA satisfaction | 41(1) |
| **Surface (no channel either)** | To stream course or nullah to BA satisfaction | 41(2) |
| **Sub-soil** | May connect to private/public **surface** sewer or drain | 43 |
| **Footpath ban** | No surface-water pipe may discharge across footpath surface | 42 |

**SD takeaway:** Measure 30 m to foul sewer from **lot boundary**, but 30 m to surface sewer from **the building** — different reference points. Reserve owner’s land for disconnecting MH near the connexion.

#### 9.3 Drain materials & size (regs. 44–46)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Covered drains/sewers (not sub-soil) | Glazed earthenware, stoneware, concrete, cast iron, or approved |
| Above-ground drains/sewers | Cast iron or approved |
| Pipe quality | True bore; smooth internal surface; impervious |
| CI sewage pipes | Protect against rust/corrosion with suitable asphaltic coating |
| Earthenware/stoneware/concrete wall thickness | ID ≤150 mm → thickness ≥ **1/9** of diameter; ID >150 mm → ≥ **1/12** |
| Sub-soil drains | Clay field pipes or suitable; adequate falls |
| Size | Adequate but not excessive; covered drain ID ≥ **100 mm** |

#### 9.4 Laying beds & soft ground (reg. 47)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Bed (all drains/sewers except sub-soil, + traps) | Concrete ≥ **100 mm** thick; ≥ **150 mm** wider than pipe/trap diameter; haunched to springing then splayed to meet barrel |
| Support | Full length of barrels — **not** on sockets |
| Soft/yielding ground | Adequate support; surround with ≥ **100 mm** concrete (except sub-soil) |
| Above-ground CI | May sit on piers/supports at **each joint** |

### 10. Gathering grounds, falls, junctions, joints, vents (regs. 47A–53)

#### 10.1 Gathering grounds (reg. 47A) — Cap 102 catchment sites
Foul drains/sewers from buildings in gathering grounds must be **watertight** and pressure-capable:

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pipes | Cast iron or approved; design/construction to resist likely pressure |
| Hatch boxes | Same pressure capability; cast iron or approved; bolted hatch/door on top |
| Joints | Lead or approved; flanges bolted with metallic/asbestos gasket (or other approved pressure joint) |
| Hatch box spacing | ≤ **30 m**; at gradient changes if BA requires; at every direction change (except ≤10° via purpose-made bend) |
| Manholes | **Not more than one** manhole permitted in each such drain/sewer |

#### 10.2 Minimum falls — Table 29 (reg. 48)

| Pipe diameter | Minimum fall |
|---|---|
| 100 mm | **1 in 40** |
| 150 mm | **1 in 70** |
| 225 mm | **1 in 100** |
| 300 mm | **1 in 150** |

Fall is from highest inlet to public sewer / other outlet connexion.

#### 10.3 Junctions (reg. 49)
| Case | Method |
|---|---|
| Single branch at ≤ **45°** in flow direction | Purpose-made **Y** junction allowed |
| Any other branch | Inside **manhole**; obliquely ≤ **60°** in flow direction; junction **above** invert of main |

#### 10.4 Joints — watertightness test head (reg. 50)
Joints must resist water head of **max(1.5 m, vertical distance from joint invert to top of immediately preceding manhole)**. No joint material may project into pipe bore.

| Pipe type | Joint method |
|---|---|
| CI socketed | Hemp/yarn gasket + metallic lead (or approved) caulking |
| CI flanged | Bolted flanges + metallic/asbestos gasket |
| Earthenware / stoneware / concrete | Hemp/yarn gasket + cement mortar (or approved) |

#### 10.5 Inlets, drain ventilation, free air path (regs. 51–53)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Drain/sewer inlets | Trapped; seal ≥ **75 mm** |
| Inlet trap **not** required for | Soil pipe inlet; ventilating pipe inlet; trade effluent inlet; impracticable cases; drain used **solely** for roof rainwater |
| Foul drain high-point vent | ≥ **50 mm** ID ventilating pipe |
| Disconnecting-trap MH | Must be adequately ventilated |
| Branch drains | Ventilate at highest point if BA requires |
| Free air path | **No trap or obstruction** between lower and higher ventilation openings of any drain/sewer |

### 11. Under-building drains, manholes, disconnecting traps, gullies (regs. 54–61)

#### 11.1 Under / through buildings (reg. 54)
- Under a building: drains/sewers in **straight lines** + **cast iron** sewage pipes only
- Through a building, or building over a drain/sewer: provide **relieving arches or beams** so building load is **not** transmitted to the drain/sewer

#### 11.2 Manholes & cleaning eyes (regs. 55–56, 59)
**Provide manholes:**
- at every direction change, **unless all** of: change ≤ **45°**; bend radius ≥ **6 ×** ID; purpose-made pipe; **and**
- at gradient changes if BA requires

**Also:** MH or cleaning eyes at intervals ≤ **60 m**.

**MH construction:**
| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| Walls | Brick ≥ **215 mm**; or concrete ≥ **125 mm**; or approved impervious |
| Foundation | Concrete ≥ **150 mm** |
| Benching | Fall toward channel at **1 in 2**; cement-rendered smooth/impervious with all internal faces |
| Channel | ≥ half-round glazed earthenware or cement rendered; diameter ≥ largest inlet and ≤ outlet |
| Inlets | Discharge into channel via bends formed in benching |
| Cover (general) | CI airtight, ground-flush, adequate strength |
| Cover (inside/under building) | **Double-sealed** CI airtight |

**Cleaning eyes:** airtight + watertight cover; size for easy rod entry.

#### 11.3 Disconnecting traps (reg. 57) — site-boundary must-have
| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Required on | Every foul drain/sewer; every surface drain/sewer **except** when connected to stream / **open** nullah |
| Location | On land owned by the building owner(s), as near as practicable to public sewer (or covered nullah for surface) — unless Cap 28 permit to occupy Government land |
| Installation | Fixed in a manhole; seal ≥ **75 mm**; integral cleaning eye |
| Who builds it | **Building Authority** fixes the trap and constructs the MH; recovers cost from owner(s) (reg. 76) |

#### 11.4 Gullies, trench backfill, surface channels (regs. 58, 60–61)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Open trapped gully | Grating openings area ≥ trap outlet cross-section |
| Sealed trapped gully | Ventilate space above trap water level |
| Trench backfill | Careful rammed earth; no stones that fail a **50 mm** ring within **300 mm** of pipe top |
| Surface channels | Adequate size; approved impervious; smooth; fall ≥ **1 in 100** |
| Debris control | Grilles to keep sand/silt out of public sewer/channel/nullah/stream |
| Footpath channel covers | Flush with path; holes/gaps ≤ **20 mm** in one dimension |

### 12. Off-sewer facilities — septic tanks & cesspools (Parts V–VI)

#### 12.1 Septic tank (regs. 62–68)
| Constraint | Value / rule |
|---|---|
| Pre-approval | Submit effluent **and** sludge disposal methods to BA; refuse if nuisance / health injury likely |
| Siting from water sources | ≥ **18 m** from spring / stream / well used or likely used for drinking, domestic, or food prep / vessel cleansing |
| Sludge access | Situate so sludge removal can use an approved method |
| Design | Tank + ancillary effluent works as BA approves (beyond these regs) |
| Capacity basis | Store **1 day** of soil + waste at BA’s potable+flushing consumption rate for the building type/size/use |
| Capacity limits | **Min 2.3 m³**, **max 41 m³** |
| Persons for sizing | Determined by BA |
| Depth (inlet invert → floor) | **1.2–1.8 m** |
| Plan proportion | Length **3–4 ×** width |
| Access | Adequate means to inspect/clean **each chamber** |
| Walls | Brick ≥215 mm; or concrete ≥125 mm; or approved |
| Foundation & floor | Concrete ≥ **150 mm** |
| Finish | All internal faces (incl. floor) cement-rendered or approved facing — smooth, impervious |
| Inlets/outlets | **Dip pipes** deep enough not to disturb top scum |
| Headspace | Adequately ventilated **or** means to draw off gases |

#### 12.2 Cesspool (regs. 69–72)
| Constraint | Value / rule |
|---|---|
| Siting from water sources | ≥ **20 m** from drinking/domestic/food water spring/stream/well |
| Siting from occupied buildings | ≥ **15 m** from any building where persons reside or work |
| Emptying route | Contents removable **without** carrying through any reside/work building |
| Capacity basis | Store **1 month** of soil + waste |
| Daily rate | **135 L / person / day** (BA sets person count) |
| Construction | Brick / concrete / approved; impervious inside & out; **RC cover**; cleaning access; ventilation; internal faces cement-rendered |

**SD takeaway:** Septic and cesspool footprints + tanker turning/access are site-planning items — not MEP afterthoughts. Check gathering-ground / well offsets early on NT / village / remote sites.

### 13. Latrines (Part IX) — when soil fitments are banned or unavailable

Applies to rooms housing latrine fitments (regs. 9 / 89 pathway). Chemical closets have limited relaxations.

#### 13.1 “Open air” for Part IX (reg. 79)
A space that is:
1. vertically uncovered and unobstructed; **and**
2. not less than **1.5 m** in any dimension; **and**
3. if enclosed on four sides → horizontal area ≥ **1 m² per 6 m** of mean enclosing-wall height

#### 13.2 Door, light, air, fabric (regs. 80–87)

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Door | Full-height **self-closing** (except chemical closet) |
| Door swing (ordinary latrine) | Directly into **open air** |
| Door swing (chemical closet) | Need not open to open air, but **must not** open into any food manufacture / prep / storage room |
| Light & ventilation opening | ≥ **0.2 m²** permanently open or designed to open; as near ceiling as practicable; communicates directly with open air; metal/approved mesh **flyscreen** |
| Floor level | ≥ **150 mm** above external ground |
| Floor finish | Non-absorbent, smooth |
| Floor fall | ≥ **1:25** to outlet; squatting surround to aperture ≥ **1:10** |
| Wall finish | Tiles, or cement mortar ≥ **12.5 mm**, or other non-absorbent — to ≥ **1.2 m** above floor |
| Receptacle chamber walls | Non-absorbent (except access opening) |
| Receptacle access | Adequate, fly-proof covered, for cleaning and placing/removing receptacle |
| Fitment design | Retain receptacle so excrement deposits into it so far as possible |
| Receptacle | Galvanized metal or non-absorbent; leak-proof; capacity ≤ **50 L** |

#### 13.3 BA discretion (reg. 88)
BA may permit door / reg. 82 opening to communicate with **unenclosed** verandah, balcony, or other unenclosed space if that space itself opens to / communicates with open air — and only if lighting, ventilation, and occupier health are not prejudiced.

### 14. Trade effluent & industrial / F&B plant (reg. 90)

BA may require the owner to provide **neutralizing tanks, cooling tanks, petrol intercepting traps, grease traps**, or other suitable provision if discharge into any drain/sewer may include:

| Trigger | Example SD spaces |
|---|---|
| Trade effluent | Workshops, process rooms, industrial units |
| Chemical refuse / waste steam / liquid > **40°C** that is dangerous, nuisance, or prejudicial to health (alone or with sewer contents) | Laundries, plant rooms, process cooling |
| Petroleum spirit, calcium carbide, acid, grease or oil | Car parks, petrol filling, commercial kitchens |
| Matter likely to injure sewer, impede free flow, or prejudice treatment/disposal | F&B grease, solids, silt |

**SD takeaway:** Budget plant rooms / external tank zones and drainage falls for grease / petrol interceptors at schematic — especially restaurants, food courts, car parks, and industrial floors.

### 15. Testing, BA-built works & programme hold points (Parts VII–VIII)

These are not “legal fluff” — they fix construction sequencing and site set-outs.

#### 15.1 Drainage testing before backfill (regs. 73–74)
1. On completion of drainage works, **before** filling trenches, RGBC / RSC applies in writing for BA test
2. BA inspects/tests → notify pass, or order remedial works with a time limit
3. After order period, BA re-tests; repeated failures → further tests at **prescribed fees** paid by contractor
4. If BA does **not** test within **4 days** of application → trenches **may** be filled
5. If trenches filled before BA pass notice (and not under the 4-day rule) → BA may require opening up for inspection/test

#### 15.2 Works only BA may execute (regs. 75–78)
| Work | Who does it | Cost recovery |
|---|---|---|
| Connexion of drain / private sewer to **public sewer or nullah** | **BA only** — and only after drainage works comply | From building owner; or apportioned equally among owners of multi-owner private sewer |
| Disconnecting trap + its manhole (reg. 57) | **BA only** | Same owner / apportionment rules |
| Any Cap 123I work on **unleased Government land** | **BA only** | Recover from person for whom work is done |
| Unauthorised private execution of the above | BA may demolish/remove/alter + do necessary works + recover as if BA had done the work | regs. 75–77 cost rules |

**SD / programme takeaway:** Draw the private drain to a clear terminus on **owner land**; leave the public connexion and disconnecting MH for BA. Do not assume the contractor can “just connect to the public sewer” under a street excavation permit alone.

### 16. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing floor plates, cores, roofscape, and site levels:

1. **Classify every use** under regs. 4–8 (arcade shop vs standalone shop; PPE vs cinema vs stadium; restaurant dining vs food room if >300 persons; school hostels ≠ residential).
2. **Compute Cap 123I UFA** per use (apply arcade-specific UFA; exclude kitchens only in self-contained flats under general UFA).
3. **Apply density + default M:F** → separate male/female (and urinal) counts; round every “or part” up; document BA-agreed alternate sex ratios if any.
4. **Locate sex-separated toilet rooms** on every relevant floor/zone; for malls overweight B–2/F female capacity; for religious/funeral size for 1/0.5 m² density.
5. **Confirm flushing water strategy** — waterworks / well / other; salt-compatible tanks **above highest WC**; overflows to conspicuous points; tank access for cleaning.
6. **Size wet ducts** — soil ≥80 mm, waste ≥32 mm, AS rules, vents ≥80 mm terminating ≥1 m above roof, cleaning eyes at bends, duct access to all connexions.
7. **Separate rainwater** — ≥700 mm² pipe / 10 m² roof; ≥65 mm stacks; no link to soil/waste/vent; footpath discharge via cast-iron conductor; eaves gutters ≥1/100.
8. **Draw foul & surface outfalls** — 30 m foul (from lot boundary) / 30 m surface (from building); reserve disconnecting MH on owner land; under-building drains = straight CI + relieving structure.
9. **If no foul sewer** — allocate septic (≥18 m from wells; 2.3–41 m³; L = 3–4 W; depth 1.2–1.8 m) or cesspool (≥20 m wells, ≥15 m buildings; 135 L/p/day × 1 month) + emptying route.
10. **Gathering ground?** — watertight pressure drainage, hatch boxes ≤30 m, ≤1 MH per line.
11. **Kitchen / industrial / car park** — grease / petrol / cooling / neutralising plant space (reg. 90).
12. **If latrines** — open-air door/vent geometry, 0.2 m² flyscreened opening, floor +1.5 m finishes, ≤50 L receptacles.
13. **Programme** — drainage test before backfill; BA-only public connexion & disconnecting trap; no private works on unleased GLA.
14. **Cross-link Cap 123A** — show UFA and sanitary fitment schedules on plans (reg. 8(1)(k)); drainage plans with sizes, falls, traps, vents, MHs, and outfalls.
