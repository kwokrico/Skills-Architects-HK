# Cap 123B — Building (Construction) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 1 February 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. B

> ⛔ **Status: REPEALED.** The Cap 123B consolidated PDF is a **repeal stub only** (“已廢除 / Repealed”). There are **no operative Cap 123B design clauses**.  
> **Operative successor:** Cap **123Q** Building (Construction) Regulation (commenced **1.2.2021**, consolidated 24.6.2021) + BD Codes of Practice accepted under **PNAP APP-53**.  
> This summary elaborates every Cap 123B topic architects still need at SD — now expressed as **Cap 123Q** rules (the substance that replaced Cap 123B via L.N. 5 & L.N. 8 of 2020).

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123B formerly prescribed design and construction of **buildings, streets, building works and street works**. From **1 February 2021** it is wholly repealed and replaced by Cap **123Q**, a largely **performance-based** regulation with **12 Parts + Schedule** covering materials, dead/imposed/wind loads, structural methodology, GI/foundations/site formation/retaining walls/bulk excavation, external walls/cladding/curtain walls, moisture protection, fire-resisting construction principles, protective barriers, lifts/escalators building interface, and miscellaneous works (ground treatment, wells, chimneys/flues, fireplaces, vermin, person-sized ducts).

Enforcement is via Cap 123 ss.14 / 16 (BA may refuse plan approval or consent) — Cap 123B/123Q themselves carry no offence/penalty clauses.

### 3. Loads — definitions architects must brief correctly (Cap 123Q ss. 4–7)

| Load | Meaning | Hard rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Dead** (s. 4) | Permanent weight: walls, floors, roofs, finishes, **permanent partitions**, other permanent construction | Unit weights from reliable data — include finishes & fixed partitions in early massing |
| **Imposed** (s. 5) | Everything except dead & wind — **including forces from adjacent ground** | Greatest load likely from intended use; heavier equipment / displays override Table 1 (s. 7(3)) |
| **Wind** (s. 6) | Pressure **or suction**, any direction | Based on building response; wind magnitude return period **≥ 50 years** |

**How to apply imposed load (s. 7):** Take whichever produces the **greater adverse effect** — Schedule Table 1 **UDL** **or** concentrated / line load.

#### 3.1 Floating partitions (positions not on plan) (s. 7(2))
Treat partition weight as additional UDL imposed load:
- ≥ **1/3** of partition weight per metre length, spread per m²; **and**
- for **office** floors: **not less than 1 kPa**

**SD takeaway:** Fix partition layouts on GP, **or** budget ≥1 kPa office partition allowance + capacity for fit-out churn. Floating partitions **cannot** take multi-storey load reduction (s. 8(5)(g)).

### 4. Minimum imposed loads — Schedule Table 1 (ex-Cap 123B reg. 17 tables)

Use the **greater** of distributed (kPa) or concentrated (kN). Concentrated load normally on any **50 mm** square (vehicle areas: **200 mm** square).

#### Class 1 — Domestic / residential sleep (**2 kPa / 2 kN**)
Domestic; dormitories; private hotel/motel/guesthouse sitting rooms, bedrooms, toilets; hospital/nursing home/RCHE wards, bedrooms, toilets; domestic bathrooms (Jacuzzi separate), pantries, kitchens.

#### Class 2 — Institutional / office / light work
| Use | Dist. kPa | Conc. kN |
|---|---|---|
| Medical consulting / treatment; OT / X-ray | 2.5 | 3 |
| Labs; light workrooms (no central power machines / storage); general offices; light E&M rooms; meter rooms (not storage); office/non-industrial pantries | 3 | 4.5 |
| Banking halls; kitchens & laundries **not** domestic | 4 | 4.5 |
| Projection rooms | 5 | 4.5 |

#### Class 3 — Education / assembly / leisure
| Use | Dist. kPa | Conc. kN |
|---|---|---|
| Childcare / kindergarten | 2.5 | 3 |
| Classrooms, lecture / tutorial / computer / reading (no book storage); internet centres; dance practice; non-assembly leisure; billiard / bowling; massage / sauna / bath house (**pools/fountains separate**) | 3 | 4.5 |
| Fixed-seat assembly / worship; restaurants, bars, canteens, F&B (not domestic); cafes, mahjong, amusement centres; columbaria (not niches) | 4 | 4.5 |
| Art galleries / museums; grandstands; public halls; theatres / cinemas; concert halls; conference / waiting | 5 | 4.5 |
| Assembly **without** fixed seating; dance / disco; footbridges; footpaths / terraces / plazas; garden open areas (incl. short-grass turf); karaoke; gymnasia; **refuge floors**; ice rinks (**ice weight separate**), ball courts, golf ranges | 5 | 4.5 |
| Stages & TV studios used as stages | **7.5** | **9** |

> Fixed seating counts only if removal and reuse of the space for other purposes are **unlikely**.

#### Class 4 — Retail (**5 kPa / 4.5 kN**)
Department stores, supermarkets, markets, shops for display and sale.

#### Class 5 — Storage / plant / industrial (storey height drives load)
| Use | Dist. load | Conc. |
|---|---|---|
| Library rooms with book storage (not stack rooms); offices for storage / normal filing | 5 kPa | 4.5 kN |
| Bookstore / library **stack rooms** | **3.5 kPa per m storage height**, min **10 kPa** | ≥ 9 kN |
| Cold storage | **5 kPa per m**, min **15 kPa** | ≥ 9 kN |
| Printing plant paper storage | **8 kPa per m** | ≥ 9 kN |
| Battery / UPS rooms | **10 kPa per m** | ≥ 9 kN |
| Refuse / general / warehouse storage (else) | **2.5 kPa per m** | ≥ 9 kN |
| Plant / boiler / fan / motor rooms & the like | **7.5 kPa** | 9 kN |
| Factories / workshops — light / medium / heavy / printing | **5 / 7.5 / 10 / 12.5 kPa** | 9 kN |

> **Storage height** = floor to ceiling / soffit / roof / other physical obstruction that limits stack height. Clear storey height on schematic plans **directly drives** imposed load.

#### Class 6 — Vehicles
| Use | Dist. | Conc. |
|---|---|---|
| Vehicles ≤ **3 000 kg** gross | 3 kPa | **20 kN** on **200 mm** square |
| Vehicles > 3 000 kg | By engineering principles | By engineering principles |

#### Class 7 — Roofs & canopies
| Roof type | Dist. kPa | Conc. kN |
|---|---|---|
| Inaccessible, slope ≤ 5° | 2 | 1.5 |
| Inaccessible, >5° to ≤20° | 0.75 | 1.5 |
| Inaccessible, >20° to <40° | Interpolate 0.75 → 0 | 1.5 |
| Inaccessible, ≥40° | 0 | 1.5 |
| Other roofs (not Classes 1–6 uses), slope ≤20° | 2 | 1.5 |
| Other roofs, >20° to <40° | Interpolate 2 → 0 | 1.5 |
| Other roofs, ≥40° | 0 | 1.5 |
| Canopies | **0.75** | **1.5** |

#### Class 8 — Projections & circulation
| Element | Dist. | Conc. / line |
|---|---|---|
| **Utility platforms** | Same as accessed floor, **≥ 4 kPa** | **2 kN/m** along outer edge |
| **Balconies** | Same as accessed floor, **≥ 3 kPa** | **2 kN/m** along outer edge |
| Stairs, landings, corridors | Same as accessed floor, **≥ 3 and ≤ 5 kPa** | 4.5 kN |
| A/C hoods / platforms / projecting window hoods | — | **1.5 kN/m** outer edge |
| Maintenance catwalks | — | **1 kN** at 1 m centres |

**SD takeaway:** Label every floor zone with intended use **and** Table 1 class on early plans — load jumps (office 3 → filing 5 → stack/cold by height; refuge 5; plant 7.5; stage 7.5) change structure before DD.

### 5. Load reduction (Cap 123Q s. 8 + Schedule Table 2)

#### Allowed — column / pier / wall / foundation carrying multi-floor distributed imposed load

| No. of floors (incl. roof) qualifying | Classes 1–4 & 7 | Factories/workshops Class 5 ≥ 7.5 kPa |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0% | 0% |
| 2 | 5% | 10% |
| 3 | 10% | 20% |
| 4 | 15% | **25% max** |
| 5–8 | 20–35% | 25% max |
| >8 | **40% max** | 25% max |

#### Beam reduction (s. 8(2)–(3))
Single-span beam supporting **≥ 45 m²** floor at a level: **5% per complete 45 m²**, max **20%** for that floor — beam design only.

#### Never reduce (s. 8(5))
- Equipment / machinery / display loads under s. 7(3)
- Factories / workshops designed for **< 7.5 kPa**
- Floors used by **vehicles**
- Office **storage / filing**
- **Dynamic** effects
- **Storage** floors
- Partition loads where positions are **not** shown on plan

**Factory floor (s. 8(4)):** If designed ≥ 7.5 kPa, after reduction total must not fall below all floors at **7.5 kPa without Table 2 reduction**.

### 6. Protective barrier loads & vehicle barriers (Cap 123Q s. 9 + Schedule Table 3)

#### Person barriers (Table 3) — apply separately; also check wind if applicable (take greater)
| Category | Line load (kN/m) | Infill UDL (kPa) | Infill point (kN) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Congregation **not** expected | 0.75 | 1 | 0.5 |
| May congregate, overcrowding **not** expected | 1.5 | 1.5 | 1.5 |
| Susceptible to **overcrowding** | **3** | 1.5 | 1.5 |

Line load acts at **1.1 m** above floor **or** top of barrier — **whichever is lower**.

#### Vehicle barriers (s. 9(3))
Min design impact force: **[0.5 M v² / (δc + δb)] kN**  
- *M* = heaviest allowed vehicle gross mass (kg)  
- *v* = velocity normal to barrier (m/s)  
- *δc*, *δb* = vehicle & barrier deformation (mm)  
Force uniform over **1.5 m** length, horizontally at **bumper height**.

### 7. Dynamic effects & overload control (Cap 123Q ss. 10–12)

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Industrial floors (s. 10) — if not derived from building-specific plant data | Slabs & beams **+2.5 kPa** vertical; frames & foundations additional **horizontal = 10%** of that 2.5 kPa on adverse whole-number of floors (≥ 0.2 × total dynamic floors); need not act with wind |
| Designed load notices (s. 11) | Industrial buildings & warehouses — durable notices at each staircase per storey (or suitable place); split by floor zone if loads differ; **exclude** dynamic effects from posted figure |
| No overloading (s. 12) | Beyond proper bearing capacity except for testing |

### 8. Structure performance — design methodology gate (Cap 123Q ss. 13–16)

| Section | Hard rule |
|---|---|
| s. 13 Methodology | Conform to laws of mechanics + recognized engineering principles & practices |
| s. 14 Strength & serviceability | Sustain & transmit dead + imposed + wind to ground; adequate FoS; no adverse crack/deflection/damage to self **or neighbours**; do not reduce neighbours’ FoS |
| s. 15 Stability | Adequate **margin of safety against instability** |
| s. 16 Construction methods | Appropriate methods + precautions; temporary **or** permanent state within dimensional tolerance; no neighbour damage / FoS loss |

**SD takeaway:** Neighbour vibration / settlement / façade impact is a Cap 123Q duty from schematic — not only a construction courtesy. Cap 123A geo / ELS packs must demonstrate Cap 123Q s.14 / s.21 compliance.

| Rule | SD implication |
|---|---|
| s. 17 GI must give adequate geotech + other relevant data to recognized standards | Do not freeze massing / basement depth without GI scope |
| s. 18 Foundation must take ground conditions, **installation method**, and **group effects**; GI **before** construction; ground must safely take all loads with FoS | Pile / footing strategy is a SD decision; protect adjacent buildings/services |

| s. 20 Proof tests on **representative foundation units** (load / core drill cast-in-place / other suitable) | Programme time + cost for pile proofs |

### 10. Site formation, retaining walls & Mid-Levels bulk excavation (Cap 123Q ss. 21–25)

#### Site formation (s. 21)
Adequate margin of safety for works **and remainder of site** during and after construction; no damage / FoS loss to buildings, land, streets, services.

#### Minor retaining wall exemption (s. 22) — **all three** must apply
1. Upper–lower ground difference **≤ 1.5 m**; **and**
2. Upper ground average inclination **≤ 15°**; **and**
3. No foundation / structure surcharge on the wall  

Only then is the wall a **minor retaining wall** (s. 24 drainage extras do **not** apply).

#### Retaining wall design (s. 23)
Safely support earth/fill + other loads under most onerous construction & service conditions; GI-based (s. 17); FoS against **sliding, overturning, bearing failure, and deep failure beneath**.

#### Non-minor retaining wall extras (s. 24)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Filter against soil | Pass water; restrain particle migration |
| Drainage to relieve water pressure | Performance maintainable for **service life** |
| Backfill | Compactable to stable fill |
| Upper **and** lower levels | Channels of suitable size **or** paving → surface water drain at adequate gradient |

#### Mid-Levels bulk excavation (s. 25) — Scheduled Area No. 1
**Bulk excavation** = any excavation **except** GI, utility trenches, drains, sewers, pile installation.  
Must be limited to minimize **cumulative adverse effect** on Mid-Levels hillside stability from multiple sites.

**SD takeaway:** Flag Mid-Levels / Scheduled Area 1 early — basement bulk cut is a hillside cumulative control (also Cap 123A: foundation must accompany site formation plan on Area 1).

### 11. External wall, cladding, curtain wall (ex-Cap 123B envelope Parts → Cap 123Q ss. 26–31)

| Term (s. 26) | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Cladding** | Facing / architectural decoration additional to structural elements |
| **Curtain wall** | Non-load-bearing enclosure fixed to load-bearing structure |
| **Non-combustible** | Passes a **recognized non-combustibility test** |
| **Load-bearing** (this Part) | Bears load other than self-weight or wind on its own surface |

#### External wall (s. 27)
- Materials: **permanent, impervious, non-combustible**
- **Adequate access** to outer surface (and projections) for maintenance / repair — **new Cap 123Q duty** replacing Cap 123B silence on M&R access

#### Cladding (s. 28)
| Must | Detail |
|---|---|
| Non-combustible; suitable thickness / strength / durability | Spec early |
| Fixed & supported to maintain long-term stability & integrity | Sequence matters |
| Flexible joints H + V for differential movement | Movement joint set on elevation strategy |
| Suitable **metal dowels & fixings** permanently into structure; corrosion-protected | No decorative adhesive-only logic |
| If weather-exposed: access for maintenance | Gondola / BMU / cradles / ladders planned in SD |

#### Curtain wall (ss. 29–31)
| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Loads | Sustain & transmit dead + imposed + wind to load-bearing structure without damaging deflection |
| Water | Collect & discharge seepage / condensation |
| Materials | **Non-combustible only**; electrolytic separation / surface treatment; corrosion-protected anchors |
| Support fixing | Only by **cast-in anchorage in structural concrete** **or** **weld/bolt to structural steel** |
| Fixing effect | Must not impair host member integrity / performance |
| Access | Outer surface maintenance / repair access required (s. 31(3)) |

**SD takeaway:** CW supports must land on **real** structure — not blockwork / finishes. BMU / cradle / reachable AC strategy is **statutory** for EW, cladding, CW, and roofs.

### 13. Fire resisting construction — principle mandate (Cap 123Q s. 35)

Cap 123Q states performance duties only; detailed FRR / compartmentation live in the **FS Code** and related PNAPs. On fire, the building must:
1. Inhibit spread **within** the building and to **nearby** buildings  
2. Resist fire & smoke spread **between buildings** and **between different uses** inside  
3. Maintain stability long enough for evacuation, rescue / firefighting, and to avoid consequential damage to neighbours  
4. Resist fire spread **over the roof** to other buildings, having regard to location  

**SD takeaway:** Separation, roof disposition, and use-mixing are fire geometry from day one — Cap 123Q makes neighbour and use-separation a structural/envelope duty, not only an FSI checklist item.

### 14. Protective barriers — where compulsory (ex-Cap 123B barrier regs → Cap 123Q ss. 36–38)

> Cap 123Q is **performance-based** on barrier geometry (no Cap 123B-style dimensional schedule in the statute). When/where barriers are required, and what loads they take (Table 3), are statutory. Detailed opening sizes / climbability practice continue under **PNAP APP-110**, Loading Code, and External Maintenance CoP.

#### Exempt (s. 36) — Division does **not** apply
- Assembly hall **stage**
- Vehicle parking bay for **loading / unloading** of goods
- **Inaccessible** roof or area (maintenance/repair access only; not for human occupation)
- Spaces (other than accessible roof) in **1-family domestic** premises

**Inaccessible** (s. 36(2)) = not intended for human occupation **and** only accessible to personnel for M&R.

#### Must provide (s. 37)
1. At edge of balcony, verandah, floor, roof, staircase, landing or projection — to control persons, objects, **and vehicles**; **and**
2. Wherever adjacent level difference **> 600 mm** — barrier at the **higher** level (whether or not within a building)

#### Design (s. 38)
- Prevent falling / rolling / sliding / slipping **through** gaps; **and**
- Prevent climbing **over**

**SD takeaway:** The **600 mm** level-difference rule is the schematic hard number for steps, terraces, planter edges, and sunken courts — including outdoor areas. Size guardrails for Table 3 congregation / overcrowding category.

### 15. Lifts & escalators — building interface (ex-Cap 123B reg. 9A → Cap 123Q ss. 39–41)

Division 2 applies where a lift / escalator is (to be) installed, **except**:
- Amusement rides (Cap 449)
- Belt / bucket / scoop / roller conveyors
- Furnace / machine-feeding hoists
- Goods lifts ≤ **3.5 m** travel that **do not pass through any floor**
- Car lifts ≤ **3.5 m** travel that do not pass through any floor
- Construction-site personnel / materials lifts
- Wharf / pier ramps
- Stage / orchestra lifts
- Stairlifts along a flight of stairs
- Disability lifting platforms with travel ≤ **2 m**

| Duty | Requirement |
|---|---|
| s. 40 Building design | Adequate structural strength, **space**, protection, **access**, **ventilation** for safe operation / inspection / maintenance; **restricted space** (shaft + machine spaces) inaccessible except inspection / maintenance / repair / rescue |
| Retrofit lifts | Same rules when added after completion |
| s. 41 Notices | Durable notices: danger of entering restricted space / interfering with operation; and **do not use lift in fire** at every lift entrance |

**Restricted space** = lift shaft + associated equipment/machinery space (or escalator machinery space) per Cap 618 definitions.

**SD takeaway:** Machine rooms / MRL pits / shaft ventilation and rescue access are Cap 123Q building fabric — coordinate with Cap 618 lift contractor early so structural cores are not redrawn post-GP.

### 16. Miscellaneous hooks that bite early (Cap 123Q ss. 42–47)

| Topic | Critical constraint |
|---|---|
| **Ground treatment** (s. 42) | Prove method & materials to BA; BA may require ground tests; protect neighbours if treatment may affect them |
| **Wells** (s. 43) — historic Cap 123B reg. 85 territory | BA permission to sink / reopen; not near septic / cesspool / sewage sump / contaminated ground; prevent surface sullage entry; lining; filter if particle risk; prevent unauthorized entry; no stability damage |
| **Chimney / flue** (s. 44) — if internal dia/breadth/width **> 200 mm** **or** height **> 3 m** | Position/shield against ignition & outer-surface burn risk; flue termination so products of combustion **do not** enter windows, openings, FA inlets, MV inlets/exhausts of this or other buildings |
| **Fireplace** (s. 45) | Hearth / recess prevent fire to this or other buildings |
| **Vermin** (s. 46) | Construction must not provide vermin habitation |
| **Person-sized ducts** (s. 47) | Access opening + structure to bear a person’s weight (also absorbed Cap 123J duct-access rule) |

### 19. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing occupancy mix, envelope, levels, or basement cut:

1. **Confirm statute** — Cap 123B is dead; all construction-performance citations = Cap **123Q**.
2. **Map every floor zone to Table 1 class** — filing vs general office, kitchen vs domestic, refuge (5 kPa), plant (7.5), storage-by-height, stages (7.5).
3. **Lock partition strategy** — shown layouts or office ≥1 kPa floating-partition surcharge; floating partitions cannot take multi-storey reduction.
4. **Flag no-reduction uses** — vehicles, storage, filing, dynamic industrial, equipment displays.
5. **Utility platforms ≥ 4 kPa** and balconies **≥ 3 kPa** + **2 kN/m** edge line — do not under-design domestic projections.
6. **GI before foundation narrative** — schedule RGE if slopes, retention, deep excavation, or Scheduled Area 1.
7. **Retaining walls > minor threshold** get full drainage / filter / dual-level channels; FoS against four failure modes.
8. **Envelope materials non-combustible** for EW / cladding / CW; CW supports only cast-in concrete or weld/bolt to structural steel.
9. **Maintenance access** for EW, cladding, CW, roof — BMU / cradle / ladder / AC platform strategy is statutory.
10. **Level difference > 600 mm** → protective barrier; size load category (Table 3) for assembly / plaza density.
11. **Fire geometry** — inhibit internal / neighbour / use-to-use / roof spread; stability for escape & firefighting (s. 35) — feed FS Code next.
12. **Lift / escalator cores** — shaft restricted space, ventilation, access, fire warning notices (ss. 40–41); exclude devices under s. 39.
13. **Materials** — APP-53 list or BD AIP for alternatives before DD.
14. **Admin package** — Cap 123A still governs plan / consent / certs; geo / foundation / ELS must show Cap **123Q** compliance.
