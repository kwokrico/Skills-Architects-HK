# Cap 123G — Building (Private Streets and Access Roads) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 9 September 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. G

> Scope note: Cap 123G is the **geometric and construction rulebook for private streets, cul-de-sacs, access roads, pedestrian ways, footpaths, and (construction of) service lanes** — not public road standards (Highways / TD), not plot ratio / site coverage (Cap 123F), and not sanitary-fitment drainage capacity (Cap 123I, except foul/surface definitions for manhole cover shape). Use this before freezing site layout, vehicular approach, basement ramps tying into private roads, estate road hierarchy, and any claim that a private street will count as a Cap 123F “specified street.”

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123G sets **minimum widths, footpath presence rules, junction geometry, gradients, horizontal/vertical curves, curve-widening, turning space, surfacing build-ups, kerbs, camber/crossfall, manhole grades/shapes, channel/drain sizing rainfall intensities, and BA powers over public-street interface works and selected waivers**. It applies to **private** streets / cul-de-sacs / access roads / pedestrian ways / service lanes formed in connection with building works under Cap 123 — not to Government-maintained public streets (except where your scheme forces works *into* a public street, which BA must execute under reg. 26).

Area type (residential / industrial / mixed) and major vs minor street classification are **determined by the Building Authority** (reg. 5(2)(a)) — do not assume OZP residential zoning alone locks Cap 123G Table I.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Statutory definitions you must lock before drawing (reg. 2)

These definitions drive every width table. Mis-labelling a link as “access road” when BA will treat it as a private street (or vice versa) changes total reservation by metres.

| Term (EN / 中) | Statutory meaning | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Carriageway / 車路** | That part of a private street, cul-de-sac or access road used or intended for **vehicular** traffic | Width, gradient, curve, camber, Grade A covers all apply here |
| **Footpath / 行人路** | That part of a private street, cul-de-sac or access road used or intended **only** by pedestrians | Must exist per reg. 4; widths from Tables I/II or reg. 6 — not optional landscaping |
| **Cul-de-sac / 盡頭路** | A **private street** closed at one end | Still a species of private street → both-side footpaths; special cul-de-sac minima until 120 m trap fires |
| **Pedestrian way / 行人徑** | A **private street** used or intended **only** by pedestrians | ≥3.5 m + vehicle barrier (reg. 7); not the same as a footpath strip beside a carriageway |
| **Public street / 公眾街道** | Street vested in **and** maintained by the Government | Cap 123G geometry does not redesign it; crossover works → reg. 26 |
| **Residential area / 住宅區** | Area developed / to be developed **primarily** with buildings used wholly or mainly for **habitation** | Table I + residential cul-de-sac minima |
| **Industrial area / 工業區** | Area developed / to be developed **primarily** with buildings used wholly or mainly for **industrial undertakings, offices, or other commercial uses** | Table II (same as mixed) |
| **Area of mixed usage / 混合用途區** | Area with habitation buildings **and** industrial/office/commercial buildings — and **not** a residential area or an industrial area | Table II; BA decides the label |

**BA sole determinations (reg. 5(2)(a)):**
1. Whether the area is residential / industrial / mixed  
2. Whether a street is **major** or **minor**

**SD kill switches:**
- “Mostly residential podium with retail” can still be argued as **mixed** → Table II major street = **10.5 + 3.7 + 3.7 = 17.9 m** total min corridor, not Table I’s 14.7 m
- Calling a through-route an “access road” to dodge Table I/II does not work if layout reads as an estate street
- Pedestrian way ≠ service lane ≠ footpath — different widths and construction sections

### 2. Connectivity — no orphan roads (reg. 3)

Every **new** private street, cul-de-sac, and access road must be accessible from:
- an **existing street**; **or**
- **another new street**

**SD takeaway:** Phased estates must show a continuous street chain from day-one occupation. A cul-de-sac that only connects later via “future road” fails unless that future road is also a committed new street in the same approval narrative.

---

### 3. Footpath presence — which sides (reg. 4)

| Road type | Footpaths required | Why it moves the massing |
|---|---|---|
| Private street | **Each side** (both) | Building setbacks / landscape edges cannot eat one side |
| Cul-de-sac | **Each side** (both) | Same — even at hammerhead arms |
| Access road | **At least one side** | You may put buildings hard to the non-footpath side *only if* this really is an access road |

**SD traps:**
- One-side footpath layouts are **illegal** on private streets / cul-de-sacs — common mistake on narrow lots
- “Shared surface / woonerf” without a defined footpath strip still needs a Cap 123G footpath of the statutory minimum width unless BA has accepted another regime (this Cap does not provide a shared-surface exemption)
- Footpath width is measured as the **footpath part** — do not count verge, planting strip, or cycle track as footpath unless BA accepts it as the pedestrian strip

### 4. Width hard numbers + total corridor maths (regs. 5–7)

Always reserve **carriageway + footpath(s)** as one corridor. Kerbstones sit at the interface; channels sit against kerbs — allow extra for channel width (≥300 mm each side under reg. 25) in detailed sections.

#### 4.1 Private streets — residential (Table I)

| Street type | Carriageway | Each footpath | **Min total (CW + 2 FP)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Major** | **7.3 m** | **3.7 m** | **14.7 m** |
| **Minor** | **5.5 m** | **2.75 m** | **11.0 m** |

#### 4.2 Private streets — industrial or mixed usage (Table II)

| Street type | Carriageway | Each footpath | **Min total (CW + 2 FP)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Major** | **10.5 m** | **3.7 m** | **17.9 m** |
| **Minor** | **7.3 m** | **2.75 m** | **12.8 m** |

> If major/minor is unclear at concept, design to **major** widths for that area type — BA alone decides (reg. 5(2)(a)(ii)).

#### 4.3 Cul-de-sacs (reg. 5(1)(a)(ii) / (b)(ii)) — before the 120 m trap

| Area type | Carriageway | Each footpath | **Min total (CW + 2 FP)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Residential | **5.0 m** | **2.75 m** | **10.5 m** |
| Industrial / mixed | **7.3 m** | **2.75 m** | **12.8 m** |

#### 4.4 Cul-de-sac → private street upgrade (reg. 5(2)(b)) — critical length trap

If centre-line carriageway length from the junction with a street that is a **thoroughfare** exceeds **120 m**, the cul-de-sac is **deemed a private street**.

**Effects when the trap fires:**
- Cul-de-sac special minima **no longer apply**
- Full Table I or II private-street widths apply (major/minor as BA determines)
- You still need turning space at the closed end (reg. 15) because it remains closed at one end functionally — but width jumps

| Example | Length | Residential minor street effect |
|---|---|---|
| Short cul-de-sac | ≤ 120 m | CW 5.0 + FP 2.75×2 = **10.5 m** |
| Long cul-de-sac deemed private street (assume minor) | > 120 m | CW 5.5 + FP 2.75×2 = **11.0 m** (and possibly major = **14.7 m**) |

**SD takeaway:** Measure along **carriageway centre line** from the thoroughfare junction — not along lot boundary, not crow-flies. Keep dead-ends ≤ 120 m or budget private-street widths from the start.

#### 4.5 Access roads (reg. 6)

| Case | Carriageway | Footpath (min one side) | **Min total (CW + 1 FP)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Default** (reg. 6(1)) | **5.0 m** | **1.6 m** | **6.6 m** |
| **Reduced** (reg. 6(2) — all gates) | **2.75 m** | **1.5 m** | **4.25 m** (+ passing bays) |

**Reduced-width access road — ALL three conditions mandatory (reg. 6(2)):**

| Gate | Rule | How to fail it at SD |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Unit count** | Access to **≤ 12 separate buildings** **or** **≤ 24 flats** (flats may be in same or different buildings) | 13 houses, or 25 flats, or a school + flats over the limit |
| **(b) Floor area** | Aggregate floor area of **all** those buildings/flats **≤ 3 500 m²** | Easy fail on 24 flats of ~150 m² each |
| **(c) Passing spaces** | Spaces enabling vehicles to pass, spaced so distances along the access road **do not exceed 60 m** | Long single-track without lay-bys; measure along the road, not as crow-flies |

**Worked gate checks:**
- 10 townhouses × 200 m² floors = 2 000 m² → gates (a)+(b) OK if ≤12 buildings; still need passing bays ≤60 m
- 20 flats × 180 m² = 3 600 m² → **fails (b)** even though ≤24 flats → must use default **5.0 / 1.6 m**
- 24 flats × 140 m² = 3 360 m² → (a)+(b) OK; lay out passing bays so no stretch > 60 m without a pass

**SD takeaway:** Reduced access roads are for small low-rise clusters only. Estate spines and multi-block sites almost always need default 5.0 m CW (+ at least one 1.6 m FP). Passing bays consume land — draw them before packing GFA.

#### 4.6 Pedestrian ways (reg. 7)

| Rule | Value / requirement |
|---|---|
| Min width | **3.5 m** |
| Vehicle control | Must be **so protected as to prevent vehicles entering** |
| Legal nature | It is a **private street** used only by pedestrians — not a leftover residual strip |

**SD takeaway:** Bollards / planters / steps / chicanes / raised thresholds must be designed as part of the pedestrian-way package. 3.5 m clear must remain after protection devices (do not put bollards inside the 3.5 m if they steal clear width — coordinate with BA practice / BFA).

#### 4.7 Quick corridor picker (SD cheat sheet)

| What you are drawing | Likely min clear width to reserve* |
|---|---|
| Resid. private street — minor | **11.0 m** |
| Resid. private street — major | **14.7 m** |
| Ind./mixed private street — minor | **12.8 m** |
| Ind./mixed private street — major | **17.9 m** |
| Resid. cul-de-sac ≤120 m | **10.5 m** |
| Ind./mixed cul-de-sac ≤120 m | **12.8 m** |
| Access road — default | **6.6 m** (+ turning if dead-end) |
| Access road — reduced (all gates) | **4.25 m** + passing bays ≤60 m |
| Pedestrian way | **3.5 m** + vehicle barrier |

\*Excludes channel overhang beyond footpath/carriageway edges, planting, EVA over-width, BFA, HyD/TD public-road standards, and firefighting appliance widths under Cap 123F / FS Code — those can **increase** the reservation.

### 5. Junction geometry (regs. 8–10) — site-planning killers

#### 5.1 Kerb radius (reg. 8)

At every junction of a private street, cul-de-sac or access road with **any** street, kerb-line radius shall be:

| Footpath condition | Min kerb radius |
|---|---|
| Meeting footpaths same width | ≥ that footpath width |
| Meeting footpaths different widths | ≥ width of the **wider** footpath |

**Examples:**
- New resid. minor private street (FP 2.75 m) joining existing street with 3.7 m footpath → kerb R ≥ **3.7 m**
- Access road FP 1.6 m joining street FP 2.75 m → kerb R ≥ **2.75 m**

**SD takeaway:** Corner truncation / splay eats buildable land and sightlines — model kerb arcs on the block plan early. Larger footpaths force larger corner radii.

#### 5.2 Right-angle junctions + straight-run “landing” lengths (reg. 9)

Junctions must be made **at right angles**, and the carriageway centre-line must continue at that angle for:

| Junction type | Min straight continuation from place of entry into junction |
|---|---|
| New private street or cul-de-sac × **existing** street | **30 m** |
| Two or more **new** private streets meeting | **30 m** on **each** street |
| Access road × street | **7.5 m** |

**What fails:**
- Skewed driveway mouth to follow lot boundary
- Immediate horizontal curve starting inside the 30 m / 7.5 m landing
- Y-junctions / acute entries sold as “urban design”

**Waiver path:** reg. 27 (impracticable due to ground or other sufficient reason) — discretionary; BA may order alternative street works.

#### 5.3 No undulation on major street (reg. 10)

Where a **minor** street carriageway meets a **major** street carriageway, grade the minor into the major so as to **avoid undulation on the major street**.

**SD takeaway:** The major street profile wins. Do not let a steep minor approach create a hump or dip across the major road — push level change into the minor’s approach (which already has its own 1:30 landing under reg. 11).

### 6. Gradients (reg. 11) — approach landings are statutory

| Location | Max gradient | ≈ % | Absolute ban |
|---|---|---|---|
| Private street / cul-de-sac / access road generally | **1 in 6** | ~**16.7%** | Steeper than 1:6 anywhere on these roads |
| First **30 m** of private street / cul-de-sac from junction with any street | **1 in 30** | ~**3.3%** | Steep start off junction |
| First **7.5 m** of access road from junction with any street | **1 in 30** | ~**3.3%** | Same for access roads, shorter landing |

**Interaction with reg. 9:**
- Private street: you typically need **30 m straight at right angles** *and* that same zone (or overlapping zone from junction) limited to **1:30**
- Access road: **7.5 m** straight + **7.5 m** at 1:30 — usually the same landing length serves both

**SD takeaway for steep sites:**
1. Reserve a near-flat approach pad at every street/access junction  
2. Put the 1:6 max ramp after the landing  
3. Check basement / podium ramp interfaces that discharge onto private roads — Cap 123G applies to the private street/access road portion; vehicle-ramp design elsewhere still needs Cap 123Q / FS / BFA coordination  
4. If 1:30 landing is geotechnically impossible → early reg. 27 waiver discussion (not a free pass)

### 7. Horizontal curves (reg. 12) and curve widening (reg. 14 + Table III)

#### 7.1 Default min radii (to centre line of carriageway)

| Road type | Min horizontal radius |
|---|---|
| Private street / cul-de-sac | **30 m** |
| Access road | **9 m** |

**SD takeaway:** A 30 m centre-line radius on a private street consumes a large plan footprint (plus outer-edge widening if BA later permits a smaller R). Prefer access-road geometry only where the link truly qualifies as an access road.

#### 7.2 When BA permits R < 30 m on private street / cul-de-sac (regs. 14 & 27)

If BA waives reg. 12 via reg. 27 and permits a smaller horizontal curve, the carriageway must be **gradually widened on the outer edge** from the tangent points to the middle of the curve, with mid-curve widening **not less than Table III**:

| Carriageway width | Permitted centre-line R | Min mid-curve widening |
|---|---|---|
| **≤ 6 m** | < 18 m | **1.2 m** |
| ≤ 6 m | 18 m–24 m inclusive | **1.0 m** |
| ≤ 6 m | > 24 m | **0.6 m** |
| **> 6 m** | < 18 m | **1.0 m** |
| > 6 m | 18 m–24 m inclusive | **0.6 m** |
| > 6 m | > 24 m | **0.3 m** |

**SD takeaway:** Sub-standard curves are not free — budget outer widening into the lot / slope / building setback. Widening is on the **outer** edge only; do not steal the inner footpath to “balance” the plan.

---

### 8. Vertical curves / stopping sight (reg. 13)

Any vertical curve in a private street, cul-de-sac or access road must satisfy:

> From a point **1 m** above any part of the carriageway, a clear view of the top of an object **1 m** high at all distances along the carriageway up to **40 m**.

**SD translation:**
- Crest curves over ridges / podium edges / bridge approaches cannot be too sharp
- Sag curves under structures / canopies need the same sight check
- “Any part of the carriageway” means you check along the road — not only at the apex
- Coordinate with landscaping / signage / gates that could block the 1 m–to–1 m cone within 40 m

**Waiver:** reg. 27 may waive reg. 13 if impracticable — expect compensatory works (mirrors are not written into Cap 123G as an automatic substitute).

---

### 9. Turning space for dead ends (reg. 15)

| Situation | Where turning space is required |
|---|---|
| Every cul-de-sac | At the **closed end** — adequate space to enable vehicles to turn |
| Access road that is **not a thoroughfare** | At the end **furthest from** its junction with a street |

**SD notes:**
- Cap 123G says “adequate” — no fixed hammerhead dimensions in this Cap; size for the design vehicles you claim (refuse, fire, removal, goods) and cross-check Cap 123F EVA / FS Code fire appliance geometry where those apply
- “Reverse out onto the public street” is **not** a compliant substitute for a dead-end turning space
- Cul-de-sacs that become deemed private streets (>120 m) still need the closed-end turn
- Show turning geometry on Cap 123A block plans / street plans early so GFA packing does not erase it

### 10. Construction build-ups architects must brief (Part III — regs. 16–22, 25)

These become Cap 123A street-works / road-detail specifications. Brief them at SD so levels, falls, and structural slabs over basements work.

#### 10.1 Carriageway surfacing (reg. 16) — pick one path

| Option | Build-up (minima) |
|---|---|
| **(a) Concrete** | ≥ **150 mm** concrete on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore |
| **(b) Bitumen macadam** | ≥ **75 mm** bitumen macadam on ≥ **200 mm** rolled hardcore + ≥ **25 mm** fine bitumen macadam topping |
| **(c) Other** | Material **approved** by BA |

**Waiver (reg. 28):** If adequate bearing-capacity tests of the carriageway foundation ground are submitted and BA is satisfied, BA may waive reg. 16 and order surfacing materials based on those test results.

**SD takeaway:** Soft fill / podium landscaping fill under estate roads needs early geotech input if you hope to thin the pavement via reg. 28. Default to (a) or (b) thicknesses in level budgets (especially over basement roofs — structural slab ≠ Cap 123G pavement unless coordinated).

#### 10.2 Pedestrian ways & service lanes (reg. 17)

| Option | Build-up (minima) |
|---|---|
| **(a) Concrete** | ≥ **100 mm** concrete on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore |
| **(b) Bitumen macadam** | ≥ **50 mm** bitumen macadam on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore + ≥ **25 mm** fine bitumen macadam |
| **(c) Other** | BA-approved |

**Scope note:** Part III heading expressly includes **service lanes**. Cap 123G does **not** set planning *widths* for service lanes (that is Cap 123F reg. 28 territory) — but once a service lane exists, its **surfacing** must meet reg. 17.

#### 10.3 Footpaths (reg. 18)

| Option | Build-up (minima) |
|---|---|
| **(a) Concrete + granolithic** | ≥ **50 mm** concrete covered with ≥ **12.5 mm** granolithic paving |
| **(b) Other** | BA-approved |

#### 10.4 Kerbstones — size & material (reg. 19)

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Size | **150 mm** wide × **300 mm** high × length **≥ 750 mm** |
| Private street / cul-de-sac kerbs | **Granite** only |
| Access road kerbs | Granite **or** concrete |

**SD takeaway:** Specifying concrete kerbs on a private street fails reg. 19(2). Length ≥750 mm affects joint layout on curves.

#### 10.5 Kerb height above channel (reg. 20)

Top of every kerb: **not less than 75 mm** and **not more than 175 mm** above the level of the adjacent channel.

**SD takeaway:** Coordinates with BFA / dropped kerbs at crossings — dropped crossings are a detailing exercise; the general kerb/channel relationship must still sit in the 75–175 mm band where kerbs exist.

#### 10.6 Camber & crossfall (reg. 21)

| Surface | Required fall | Exception |
|---|---|---|
| Carriageway | Camber **1 in 40** | Bend that is **super-elevated** |
| Footpath | Crossfall toward kerb **1 in 48** | — |

**SD takeaway:** Floor levels of buildings / shopfront thresholds adjacent to footpaths must respect the footpath crossfall and kerb upstand — do not set FFL assuming a dead-flat pavement.

#### 10.7 Manhole covers & gratings — BS grade (reg. 22)

| Location | Required grade |
|---|---|
| Carriageway of private street / cul-de-sac / access road | **Grade A** (current relevant British Standard Specification) |
| Footpath / pedestrian way / service lane | **Grade B** (same BS reference) |

**SD takeaway:** Do not put Grade B covers in the carriageway to save cost — vehicle load path requires Grade A.

#### 10.8 Channels (reg. 25)

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Construction | Concrete, ≥ **150 mm** thick and ≥ **300 mm** wide |
| Cross-section fall | **1 in 30** toward the kerb |
| Longitudinal fall | ≥ **1 in 100** |
| Softening if 1:100 impracticable | BA may permit ≥ **1 in 250** longitudinal |

Applies to channels in private streets, cul-de-sacs, access roads, and pedestrian ways (reg. 25(1)).

**SD takeaway:** Near-flat sites need designed longitudinal fall or BA consent to 1:250 — do not assume zero-fall “feature paving” channels.

### 11. Drainage of private streets (regs. 23–24) — Cap 123I interface

#### 11.1 Manhole cover shape = foul vs storm discriminator (reg. 23)

| Drain / sewer purpose | Cover shape on plan |
|---|---|
| **Foul water** (as defined in Cap 123I reg. 2) | **Rectangular** |
| **Surface water** (as defined in Cap 123I reg. 2) | **Rounded** |

Applies to manholes in private streets, cul-de-sacs, access roads, pedestrian ways, and service lanes.

**SD takeaway:** Site plans and drainage plans must show cover shapes consistently — surveyors and contractors use shape as a field cue; wrong shape is a Cap 123G fail even if pipes are correct.

#### 11.2 Mandatory drainage system (reg. 24(1)–(2))

Every private street, cul-de-sac, access road, pedestrian way, and service lane must have:
- **channels**, and  
- **drains and sewers**  

for carriage of **rain-water and surface water**, sized and graded to carry off all rain falling on and surface water draining to that street/lane.

#### 11.3 Design rainfall intensity floors (reg. 24(3))

| Time of Concentration | Design intensity **not less than** |
|---|---|
| **< 30 minutes** | **150 mm/h** |
| **≥ 30 minutes** | **100 mm/h** |

**SD takeaway:** Short urban catchments (typical estate roads) usually sit in the **< 30 min** band → design to **150 mm/h** unless drainage engineer proves otherwise. This Cap sets the **floor**; DSD / lease / BA may demand more for flood-prone sites.

### 12. Public-street interface & BA waivers (Part IV — regs. 26–28)

#### 12.1 Works in public streets — BA executes, owner pays (reg. 26)

Where either:
- forming / laying out any private street, cul-de-sac, access road or pedestrian way; **or**
- providing any means of access to a building fronting or abutting a **public street**  

necessitates work in or alteration of a **public street**, that work **shall be carried out by the Building Authority**, who may recover cost from the owner of the private street / cul-de-sac / access road / pedestrian way / building.

If work is done otherwise than by BA (contravention):
- BA may remove / alter / demolish it  
- BA may carry out other work it considers necessary  
- Cost recoverable as if done under reg. 26(1)

**SD / programme takeaways:**
- Do not put contractor DIY kerb drops / crossover construction into the public street on the tender programme without BA execution route
- Budget BA cost recovery and lead time for run-ins, sightline trimming, drainage ties into public channels
- Coordinate with HyD / TD as needed — Cap 123G still routes the *BO* execution duty through BA

#### 12.2 Waiver matrix (regs. 27–28)

| Regulation | Can BA waive? | Trigger | BA may also… |
|---|---|---|---|
| **reg. 9** (right-angle / straight landing) | Yes (reg. 27) | Impracticable owing to nature of ground or other sufficient reason | Order alternative street works |
| **reg. 11** (gradients) | Yes (reg. 27) | Same | Order alternative street works |
| **reg. 12** (horizontal curves) | Yes (reg. 27) | Same | Order alternative works; if R < 30 m on private street/cul-de-sac → **reg. 14 Table III widening mandatory** |
| **reg. 13** (vertical curve / sight) | Yes (reg. 27) | Same | Order alternative street works |
| **reg. 16** (carriageway surfacing) | Yes (reg. 28) | Adequate bearing-capacity tests submitted & accepted | Prescribe surfacing materials based on test results |
| Widths (regs. 5–7), footpaths (reg. 4), turning (reg. 15), drainage intensity (reg. 24), etc. | **No general waiver in Cap 123G** | — | Do not invent “BA will waive width” without another statutory route |

**SD takeaway:** Geometry waivers are **discretionary and compensatory**. Steep / tight urban sites need early in-principle discussion — not end-of-DD surprise letters.

### 13. Cross-links architects constantly confuse

| Topic | Cap 123G role | Companion control |
|---|---|---|
| Private street geometry / construction | **Primary** | Cap 123A street-works plan submissions |
| Whether a private street counts as Cap 123F **specified street** (≥4.5 m) for site class A/B/C | Width here helps you *meet* ≥4.5 m, but **specified street** status is Cap **123F** reg. 18A | Cap 123F |
| Site area for PR/SC | Street / service lane land **excluded** from site area under Cap 123F | Cap 123F reg. 23(2) |
| Service lane **width / location** | Cap 123G only sets **surfacing** (reg. 17) | Cap 123F reg. 28 |
| EVA / fire appliance access | Cap 123G does not replace firefighting access widths | Cap 123F + CoP Means of Access + FS Code |
| BFA on footpaths / crossings | Cap 123G kerb/crossfall still apply | Cap 123F reg. 72 + Design Manual: Barrier Free Access |
| Foul vs surface water definitions | Used for manhole cover shape (reg. 23) | Cap **123I** reg. 2 |
| Public road design standards | Cap 123G is **not** HyD/TD standard | HyD / TD manuals for public streets |
| Structural capacity of pavement over basement | Cap 123G thicknesses are minima for street construction | Cap **123Q** + RSE design |

### 14. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing site layout, road hierarchy, and building footprints:

1. **Name every link** — private street vs cul-de-sac vs access road vs pedestrian way vs service lane; prove connectivity to existing/new street (regs. 2–3).  
2. **Get BA area type + major/minor likely view** — residential vs industrial/mixed locks Table I vs II (reg. 5(2)(a)).  
3. **Reserve total corridors** — use §4.7 cheat sheet; both-side footpaths for streets/cul-de-sacs; ≥1 side for access roads (regs. 4–6).  
4. **Check 120 m cul-de-sac trap** — centre-line from thoroughfare junction; if >120 m, apply private-street tables (reg. 5(2)(b)).  
5. **Gate reduced access roads** — ≤12 buildings or ≤24 flats; ≤3 500 m² aggregate floors; passing spaces ≤60 m spacing (reg. 6(2)).  
6. **Protect pedestrian ways** — ≥3.5 m clear + prevent vehicle entry (reg. 7).  
7. **Draw junction landings** — right angle; 30 m (street/cul-de-sac) or 7.5 m (access road) straight; kerb R ≥ wider footpath; no major-street undulation (regs. 8–10).  
8. **Profile the road** — max 1:6 overall; 1:30 for first 30 m / 7.5 m from junction; H-curves ≥30 m / ≥9 m; 1 m / 1 m / 40 m vertical sight (regs. 11–13).  
9. **If seeking sub-30 m private-street curve** — budget outer-edge Table III widening + reg. 27 waiver path (reg. 14).  
10. **Reserve turning heads** for all cul-de-sacs and non-through access roads (reg. 15).  
11. **Brief construction depths & falls** — pavement options, granite vs concrete kerbs, 75–175 mm kerb upstand, 1:40 camber, 1:48 footpath crossfall, channel 150×300 / 1:30 / 1:100 (regs. 16–22, 25).  
12. **Drain strategy** — channels + drains/sewers; foul covers rectangular, surface rounded; size to ≥150 or ≥100 mm/h by Time of Concentration (regs. 23–24).  
13. **Public-street interface** — any work in public street via BA only; budget cost recovery & programme (reg. 26).  
14. **Cross-check Cap 123F** — specified street / site class, site-area exclusion of streets/lanes, service-lane planning widths, EVA / BFA — Cap 123G alone is not enough.
