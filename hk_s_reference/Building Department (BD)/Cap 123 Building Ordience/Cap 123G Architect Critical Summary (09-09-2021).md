# Cap 123G — Building (Private Streets and Access Roads) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 9 September 2021 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. G

> Scope note: Cap 123G is the **geometric and construction rulebook for private streets, cul-de-sacs, access roads, pedestrian ways, footpaths, and service lanes** — not public road standards (Highways / TD), not plot ratio / site coverage (Cap 123F), and not drainage fitment capacity (Cap 123I, except manhole shape cross-refs). Use this before freezing site layout, vehicular approach, basement ramps tying into private roads, and estate road hierarchy.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123G sets **minimum widths, footpath rules, junction geometry, gradients, horizontal/vertical curves, turning space, surfacing build-ups, kerbs, camber/crossfall, channel/drain sizing criteria, and BA powers over public-street interface works** for private streets, cul-de-sacs, access roads, pedestrian ways, and (for construction) service lanes. Area type (residential / industrial / mixed) and major vs minor street classification are **determined by the Building Authority** — do not assume OZP residential zoning alone fixes Cap 123G widths.

---

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Classify the road type before drawing (regs. 2–3) — SD hold point

Lock nomenclature early; widths and junction rules differ by type:

| Term | Meaning | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Private street** | Estate / internal street (not a public street) | Full width tables + footpath **both sides** |
| **Cul-de-sac** | Private street **closed at one end** | Special min widths; **turning space** at closed end; >120 m treated as private street |
| **Access road** | Vehicular approach to buildings (not full private street) | Narrower defaults; footpath **≥1 side**; easier junction straight-run (7.5 m) |
| **Pedestrian way** | Private street for **pedestrians only** | ≥3.5 m + **vehicle barrier** |
| **Footpath** | Ped-only strip within private street / cul-de-sac / access road | Widths from Tables I/II or access-road rules |
| **Carriageway** | Vehicular part of the above | Width + gradient + curve controls apply here |
| **Public street** | Vested in & maintained by Government | You do **not** build works in it yourself (reg. 26) |
| **Residential / industrial / mixed usage area** | Habitation-primary vs industry/office/commercial-primary vs mixed | **BA decides** which applies (reg. 5(2)(a)) |

**Connectivity (reg. 3):** Every new private street, cul-de-sac, and access road must connect to an **existing street or another new street** — no orphan loops without a street access.

**Cul-de-sac length trap (reg. 5(2)(b)):** If centre-line carriageway length from junction with a thoroughfare street exceeds **120 m**, the cul-de-sac is **deemed a private street** → residential/industrial private-street width tables apply instead of cul-de-sac minima.

### 2. Footpath presence rules (reg. 4) — massing / setback drivers

| Road type | Footpaths required |
|---|---|
| Private street | **Both sides** |
| Cul-de-sac | **Both sides** |
| Access road | **At least one side** |

**SD takeaway:** One-side footpath is only lawful on access roads — not on private streets/cul-de-sacs. Reserve both street edges early if you label the link a private street.

---

### 3. Width hard numbers (regs. 5–7) — freeze carriageway + footpath corridors

#### 3.1 Private streets — residential areas (Table I)

| Street type | Carriageway min | Each footpath min |
|---|---|---|
| **Major** | **7.3 m** | **3.7 m** |
| **Minor** | **5.5 m** | **2.75 m** |

#### 3.2 Private streets — industrial or mixed usage areas (Table II)

| Street type | Carriageway min | Each footpath min |
|---|---|---|
| **Major** | **10.5 m** | **3.7 m** |
| **Minor** | **7.3 m** | **2.75 m** |

> Major vs minor is a **BA determination** (reg. 5(2)(a)(ii)) — treat Table I/II major widths as the safe envelope if hierarchy is unclear at concept.

#### 3.3 Cul-de-sacs (reg. 5(1))

| Area type | Carriageway min | Each footpath min |
|---|---|---|
| Residential | **5 m** | **2.75 m** |
| Industrial / mixed | **7.3 m** | **2.75 m** |

#### 3.4 Access roads (reg. 6)

| Case | Carriageway min | Footpath min |
|---|---|---|
| **Default** | **5 m** | **1.6 m** |
| **Reduced** (all of (a)+(b)+(c) must be true) | **2.75 m** | **1.5 m** |

**Reduced-width access road is allowed only when ALL of these apply (reg. 6(2)):**
1. Serves **≤ 12 separate buildings** or **≤ 24 flats**; **and**
2. Aggregate floor area of all those buildings/flats **≤ 3 500 m²**; **and**
3. Vehicle **passing spaces** are provided so that distances along the access road between them **do not exceed 60 m**

**SD takeaway:** Dense residential clusters or GFA > 3 500 m² kill the 2.75 m reduced option — default to 5 m carriageway + 1.6 m footpath unless all three gates are proven.

#### 3.5 Pedestrian ways (reg. 7)

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Min width | **3.5 m** |
| Vehicle control | Must be **protected to prevent vehicles entering** |

### 4. Junction geometry (regs. 8–10) — site planning killers

#### 4.1 Kerb radius (reg. 8)
At every junction of a private street / cul-de-sac / access road with any street, kerb-line radius ≥:
- width of the footpath, if both meeting footpaths are equal; **or**
- width of the **wider** footpath, if they differ

#### 4.2 Right-angle junction + straight-run lengths (reg. 9)
Junctions must be **at right angles**, and the carriageway line must continue at that angle for:

| Junction type | Min straight continuation from junction entry |
|---|---|
| New private street or cul-de-sac × existing street | **30 m** |
| Two or more new private streets | **30 m** each |
| Access road × street | **7.5 m** |

**SD takeaway:** Skewed driveway mouths and immediate horizontal curves off the public street fail reg. 9 unless BA waives under reg. 27.

#### 4.3 Major / minor grade-in (reg. 10)
Where a **minor** street meets a **major** street, grade the minor into the major so there is **no undulation on the major street**.

---

### 5. Gradients & curves (regs. 11–14) — ramp / road profile hold points

#### 5.1 Gradients (reg. 11)

| Location | Max gradient |
|---|---|
| General private street / cul-de-sac / access road | **1 in 6** |
| First **30 m** of private street / cul-de-sac from junction with any street | **1 in 30** |
| First **7.5 m** of access road from junction with any street | **1 in 30** |

**SD takeaway:** Steep sites cannot put 1:6 (or worse) immediately off the junction — build a flat/approach “landing” zone into the road reservation from day one.

#### 5.2 Horizontal curves (reg. 12)

| Road type | Min radius (to centre line of carriageway) |
|---|---|
| Private street / cul-de-sac | **30 m** |
| Access road | **9 m** |

#### 5.3 Vertical curves / sight (reg. 13)
From a point **1 m** above any part of the carriageway, must have clear view of the top of an object **1 m** high at all distances along the carriageway up to **40 m**.

#### 5.4 Curve widening when BA permits R < 30 m (reg. 14 + Table III)
If BA waives the 30 m private-street/cul-de-sac radius (via reg. 27), gradually widen the carriageway on the **outer edge** from tangent points to mid-curve by at least:

| Carriageway width | Permitted centre-line R | Min mid-curve widening |
|---|---|---|
| **≤ 6 m** | < 18 m | **1.2 m** |
| ≤ 6 m | 18–24 m incl. | **1.0 m** |
| ≤ 6 m | > 24 m | **0.6 m** |
| **> 6 m** | < 18 m | **1.0 m** |
| > 6 m | 18–24 m incl. | **0.6 m** |
| > 6 m | > 24 m | **0.3 m** |

---

### 6. Turning space (reg. 15) — dead-end layouts

| Situation | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Closed end of every cul-de-sac | **Adequate space to enable vehicles to turn** |
| Access road that is **not a thoroughfare** | Same, at the end **furthest from** the street junction |

**SD takeaway:** Hammerheads / turning circles consume land — reserve them before GFA packing; “reverse out to public street” is not compliant end strategy for cul-de-sacs / non-through access roads.

### 7. Construction build-ups architects must brief (regs. 16–22, 25)

Not decorative — these become Cap 123A street-works / road detail specifications.

#### 7.1 Carriageway surfacing (reg. 16) — pick one approved path
| Option | Build-up |
|---|---|
| Concrete | ≥ **150 mm** concrete on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore |
| Bitumen macadam | ≥ **75 mm** bitumen macadam on ≥ **200 mm** rolled hardcore + ≥ **25 mm** fine bitumen macadam topping |
| Other | **BA-approved** material |

BA may waive reg. 16 if bearing-capacity tests are submitted and satisfactory, then may prescribe alternative surfacing based on those results (reg. 28).

#### 7.2 Pedestrian ways & service lanes (reg. 17)
| Option | Build-up |
|---|---|
| Concrete | ≥ **100 mm** on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore |
| Bitumen macadam | ≥ **50 mm** on ≥ **75 mm** rolled hardcore + ≥ **25 mm** fine bitumen macadam |
| Other | BA-approved |

#### 7.3 Footpaths (reg. 18)
| Option | Build-up |
|---|---|
| Concrete + granolithic | ≥ **50 mm** concrete + ≥ **12.5 mm** granolithic paving |
| Other | BA-approved |

#### 7.4 Kerbs (regs. 19–20)
| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Kerbstone size | **150 mm** W × **300 mm** H × length ≥ **750 mm** |
| Material — private street / cul-de-sac | **Granite** |
| Material — access road | Granite **or** concrete |
| Kerb height above adjacent channel | **75–175 mm** |

#### 7.5 Camber / crossfall (reg. 21)
| Surface | Fall |
|---|---|
| Carriageway camber (unless bend is super-elevated) | **1 in 40** |
| Footpath crossfall toward kerb | **1 in 48** |

#### 7.6 Manhole covers / gratings (reg. 22)
| Location | Grade (per current relevant BS) |
|---|---|
| Carriageway | **Grade A** |
| Footpath / pedestrian way / service lane | **Grade B** |

#### 7.7 Channels (reg. 25)
| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Concrete channel size | ≥ **150 mm** thick × ≥ **300 mm** wide |
| Cross-section fall toward kerb | **1 in 30** |
| Longitudinal fall | ≥ **1 in 100** (BA may permit ≥ **1 in 250** if 1:100 impracticable) |

### 8. Drainage of private streets (regs. 23–24) — early coordination with Cap 123I

#### 8.1 Manhole cover shape = foul vs storm discriminator (reg. 23)
| Drain purpose | Cover shape on plan |
|---|---|
| Foul water (per Cap 123I definition) | **Rectangular** |
| Surface water (per Cap 123I definition) | **Rounded** |

#### 8.2 Mandatory drainage + rainfall intensity for sizing (reg. 24)
Every private street, cul-de-sac, access road, pedestrian way, and service lane needs channels, drains, and sewers sized for rainwater + surface water.

| Time of Concentration | Design rainfall intensity (not less than) |
|---|---|
| **< 30 minutes** | **150 mm/h** |
| **≥ 30 minutes** | **100 mm/h** |

**SD takeaway:** Estate road length, catchment, and outfall capacity belong in concept drainage strategy — Cap 123G hardcodes intensity floors that affect channel depth and outfall sizing.

### 9. Public-street works & BA waivers (regs. 26–28) — programme / risk

| Rule | Practical effect |
|---|---|
| Work **in** / alteration of a **public street** needed for private street / access / means of access (reg. 26) | Must be carried out by the **Building Authority**; cost recoverable from owner — **do not DIY** crossover / kerb works in public streets |
| Unauthorised public-street works | BA may remove / alter / reinstate and recover cost |
| Waiver of regs. **9, 11, 12, 13** (reg. 27) | Only if BA satisfied compliance is impracticable (ground nature or other sufficient reason); BA may order **alternative street works** instead |
| Waiver of reg. **16** surfacing (reg. 28) | Needs adequate bearing-capacity tests submitted & accepted; BA may prescribe materials from those results |

**SD takeaway:** Steep / tight urban sites that cannot hit 30 m curve, 1:30 junction grade, or right-angle entry need an **early BA in-principle strategy** — waivers are discretionary and may come with compensatory works (incl. Table III widening for sub-30 m curves).

### 10. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing site layout, road hierarchy, and building footprints:

1. **Name every link** — private street vs cul-de-sac vs access road vs pedestrian way; confirm connectivity to an existing/new street (reg. 3).
2. **Get BA area type + major/minor likely view** — residential vs industrial/mixed locks Table I vs II (reg. 5).
3. **Apply width corridors** — carriageway + both-side (or one-side for access roads) footpaths; check 120 m cul-de-sac → private-street upgrade (regs. 4–5).
4. **Gate reduced access roads** — ≤12 buildings / ≤24 flats, ≤3 500 m² aggregate floors, passing bays ≤60 m spacing (reg. 6(2)).
5. **Protect pedestrian ways** — ≥3.5 m + no vehicles (reg. 7).
6. **Draw junction landings** — right angle; 30 m street / 7.5 m access-road straight; kerb R ≥ wider footpath; no major-street undulation (regs. 8–10).
7. **Profile the road** — max 1:6 overall; 1:30 for first 30 m (street/cul-de-sac) or 7.5 m (access road); H-curves ≥30 m / ≥9 m; 1 m/1 m/40 m sight rule (regs. 11–13).
8. **Reserve turning heads** for all dead ends (reg. 15).
9. **Brief construction depths** — carriageway / footpath / channel / kerb geometry into AP street-works package (regs. 16–22, 25).
10. **Drain strategy** — foul covers rectangular, surface rounded; size to 150 or 100 mm/h by Time of Concentration (regs. 23–24); any public-street interface works via BA only (reg. 26).
