# Cap 123P — Building (Inspection and Repair) Regulation
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 31 March 2022 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. P

> Scope note: Cap 123P is the **MBIS / MWIS inspection-scope, repair-standard, RI / QP document, detailed-investigation, and handover rulebook** under Cap 123 ss.30B–30E. It is **not** plan approval (Cap 123A), plot-ratio / projection geometry under Cap 123F, construction quality (Cap 123Q), minor-works classification (Cap 123N), or the notice / age triggers themselves (those live in Cap 123 Part 2A). Use this before schematic design on any **existing building** A&A, façade / CW works, common-parts upgrade, projection / appendage design, signboard interface, or window replacement — wherever a prescribed inspection / repair may already apply, or will govern maintainability of what you design.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123P defines what counts as a **prescribed inspection** (ss.3–5) and **prescribed repair** (ss.6–8), what building elements must be examined (common parts, external walls, prescribed projections, signboards, windows), and to which construction standards inspection and repair are measured. It also locks Registered Inspector (RI) / Qualified Person (QP) appointment notices, report and repair-proposal contents, detailed-investigation BA endorsement, independence certificates, copy-delivery clocks, representative / nominee continuity, and contractor site-document duties that run alongside Cap 123’s Mandatory Building Inspection Scheme (MBIS) and Mandatory Window Inspection Scheme (MWIS).

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. How Cap 123P sits under Cap 123 (do not confuse the layers)

| Layer | Instrument | What it decides | Cap 123P role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trigger / notice | Cap 123 **s.30B** (MBIS) | BA may order prescribed inspection / repair of **common parts, external walls, prescribed projections, signboards** on buildings aged **≥ 30 years** (age from OP or other BA evidence) | Cap 123P fills the **scope, standard, and document** definitions once a notice / duty applies |
| Trigger / notice | Cap 123 **s.30C** (MWIS) | Window inspection / repair obligations | Cap 123P fills window **scope, QP docs, dual-QP rules** |
| Who must act | Cap 123 **s.30D** (RI) / **s.30E** (QP) | Appointment limbs: inspect `(1)(a)` vs supervise repair `(1)(b)`; RI personal duties; QP duties | Cap 123P operationalises **what** they submit, **when**, and **to whom** |
| This Cap | Cap **123P** | Prescribed meaning of inspection / repair; Schedules; projections list; BA packs; detailed investigation; RI representative; copy flow | Design / programme constraint book for existing-stock schemes |

**SD takeaway:** Cap 123P alone never “starts” MBIS/MWIS — confirm Cap 123 ss.30B–30E status (active notice, impending cycle, or voluntary early survey) before treating this Cap as dormant or as a free upgrade mandate.

### 2. When Cap 123P bites at schematic design

| Project situation | Cap 123P bites? | SD implication |
|---|---|---|
| Existing building ≥ ~30 years / already under s.30B notice | **Yes (MBIS)** | Schedules 1–2 + s.9 projections + signboards become **condition-survey / repair-budget** drivers before freezing massing or façade package |
| Window inspection / replacement / MWIS notice | **Yes (MWIS)** | Separate **QP** track (s.4(1)(e), s.14) — do not bury windows inside “common parts” or “external wall” limbs (s.4(2)–(3)) |
| A&A to lobby, stairs, FRR, common drains, guarding, skylights | **Yes** | Sch. 1 items — defects found → prescribed repair to **rendered safe** (ss.7–8) |
| Façade / cladding / CW / fins / louvres / EW appendages | **Yes** | Sch. 2 + **finish** (tiling, rendering, cladding, false ceiling) in scope |
| Balconies, verandahs, planter boxes, drying racks, canopies, BS supports / pipes on EW | **Yes** | Prescribed **projections** (s.9) under Cap 123 s.30B(5) |
| Signboard on / fixed to building | **Yes** | Display surfaces, **fixings**, supporting structures (s.4(1)(d)) |
| Parallel Cap 123A A&A / Cap 123N MW while MBIS repair runs | **Yes — dual track** | Prescribed repair ≠ automatic current-code upgrade; extras need their own legal path + possibly s.22 revised proposal |
| New-build only on cleared site | Generally **no** for this Cap | Still design for **future inspectability** (access, demountable CW / cladding, exposed critical structure) once the asset ages into MBIS/MWIS |

|---|---|---|
| **Authorized signatory (AS)** — RGBC | Person appointed by the registered general building contractor under Cap 123 **s.8B(2)(d)** | Used for (i) QP representative when QP is RGBC (s.10); (ii) RI independence certificates vs contractor AS (ss.12(3), 13(2)(c)) |

### 4. What counts as a prescribed inspection (ss.3–5)

#### 4.1 Legal gateway (s.3)
An examination or assessment of a building is a **prescribed inspection** only if it satisfies **both**:
- **Scope** — s.4; **and**
- **Standard / purpose** — s.5.

Incomplete walkovers that skip applicable Schedule items are **not** prescribed inspections under the Ordinance’s definition.

#### 4.2 Five inspection limbs — full coverage rule (s.4(1))

An examination/assessment must cover **all applicable** of the following (including **finish** to each item where stated):

| Limb | Must cover | Explicit exclusions | Architect note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a) Common parts** | Components of all applicable items in **Schedules 1 and 2** (+ finish to each) | **Windows in common parts** are **not** in this limb (s.4(2)) | Sch. 2 items that sit in common parts (e.g. internal-face EW structure in common areas) still get Sch. 2 treatment under (a) |
| **(b) External wall** | Components of all applicable **Schedule 2** items (+ finish) | **Windows on that EW** excluded (s.4(3)) | Windows always go via limb (e) / QP |
| **(c) Projection** | Components of any projection prescribed in **s.9** (+ finish) | — | Classify appendages against s.9 list first (see §6) |
| **(d) Signboard** | **Display surfaces, fixings, and supporting structures** | — | Fixings / structure often control façade interface, waterproofing, and load path into the EW |
| **(e) Window** | Components of the window | — | Own QP regime; never “include under façade package” for statutory purposes |

#### 4.3 Finish definition (s.4(4)) — design liability hook
**Finish** includes any **tiling, rendering, cladding, or false ceiling**.

| Spec choice | Cap 123P consequence |
|---|---|
| Stone / tile / mosaic on EW or common parts | Inspectable finish — delamination / staining / hollow spots become MBIS findings |
| Render / textured coating | Same |
| Cladding / rainscreen / aluminium panel | Same — plus often Sch. 2 CW / appendage interface |
| False ceiling in common parts (lobby, corridor, plant corridor) | Inspectable — do not bury Sch. 1 structure / drains / FRR behind sealed uninspectable ceilings without access strategy |

#### 4.4 Purpose of prescribed inspection (s.5(1)) — three mandatory outcomes
By reference to the s.5(2) standards, the inspection must:
1. Ascertain whether the building is **safe**, **liable to become dangerous**, or **has been rendered dangerous**;
2. Identify any **defect or deficiency**; and
3. Make a **proposal for any prescribed repair**.

**SD takeaway:** An RI “condition survey” that stops at photos without defect identification + repair proposal (when defects exist) does not satisfy Cap 123P.

#### 4.5 Benchmark standards (s.5(2)) — use the **latest** applicable (s.5(3))

| Standard source | What it means | SD use |
|---|---|---|
| **(a)** Works as shown on **BA-approved plans** according to which the building was completed | Original approved construction | Pull GA / structural / drainage / FRR approved set as baseline |
| **(b)** Works as shown on plans submitted under **Cap 123N** (Minor Works) | Later legal MW alters the benchmark | Search MW records before assuming “original only” |
| **(c)** Improvement works completed under **Cap 502** or **Cap 572** | Fire safety improvement works raise the comparison bar | Confirm FS direction / completion status on commercial / composite / domestic stock |
| **(d)** Standard of design & construction **prevalent at time of construction** | Era practice when plans are incomplete | Gap-filler — **not** today’s Cap 123F / FS Code 2011 |

**Conflict rule (s.5(3)):** If more than one of (a)–(d) applies → reference the **latest** applicable standard.

**SD takeaway:** Prescribed inspection measures against **historical latest legal fabric**, not automatic upgrade to current codes. If the scheme wants current MOE / FRR / BFA, that is a **parallel A&A / Cap 502·572 / Cap 123A** path — cost and programme separately.

### 5. What counts as a prescribed repair (ss.6–8)

#### 5.1 Legal gateway (s.6)
A repair or testing of a building is a **prescribed repair** only if it satisfies **both** s.7 (scope) and s.8 (standard).

#### 5.2 Scope (s.7) — repair only what the inspection flagged
Repair / testing must cover, as the case requires, **all items identified as defective or having deficiencies under s.5(1)(b)**.

| Implication | Practice |
|---|---|
| Not a blank cheque for whole-building upgrade | Scope is **defect-led** from the inspection report |
| Missed items in inspection → missed items in repair | Survey completeness at SD / pre-tender is critical |
| “While we’re up there” extras | Outside prescribed-repair scope unless added via **s.22 / s.23 revised proposal** and/or separate Cap 123A / Cap 123N path |

#### 5.3 Repair vs testing standards (s.8)

| Action | Required effect |
|---|---|
| **Repair** (s.8(1)) | Building / element must be **rendered safe** after the repair |
| **Testing** (s.8(2)) | (a) Verify any finding made in a prescribed inspection; **or** (b) prove the building is safe / has been rendered safe |
| Benchmark (s.8(3)–(4)) | Same s.5(2) standards; if multiple apply → **latest** |

**SD takeaway:** “Rendered safe” ≠ “brought to 2024 FS Code / DMBA / Cap 123F”. Do not sell clients a current-code façade upgrade under a prescribed-repair budget line alone.

### 6. Prescribed projections (s.9) — hard list for Cap 123 s.30B(5)

For Cap 123 **s.30B(5)**, each of the following **is prescribed as a projection**:

| s.9 | Projection type | Typical SD examples | Classification traps |
|---|---|---|---|
| (1)(a) | **Balcony** | Domestic / clubhouse balconies | Floor-loaded outdoor space — not a “window canopy” |
| (1)(b) | **Verandah** | Covered outdoor rooms | Same — structure + finish in projection limb |
| (1)(c) | **Planter box** | EW / railing planters | Soil load, waterproofing, fixings — inspectable |
| (1)(d) | **Drying rack** | Laundry racks on EW | Often illegal / oversized additions — still projection class if present |
| (1)(e) | **Window canopy** | Only if s.9(2) test passed | See three-part test below |
| (1)(f) | **Supporting structure for a building service installation** | AC platforms, cable-tray supports, BS plant brackets on EW | **Excludes drainage system** supports (drains sit under Sch. 1 item 3 instead) |
| (1)(g) | **Pipe or duct associated with (f)** | Condensate, refrigerant, ventilation ducts on EW | Associated with the BS installation in (f) |

#### 6.1 Window canopy three-part test (s.9(2)) — all must be true
A structure projecting from an external wall is a “window canopy” under s.9(1)(e) only if it is:
1. **Located above an opening**; **and**
2. **Not carrying any floor load**; **and**
3. **Either cantilevered or supported by brackets**.

| If the “canopy”… | Likely Cap 123P home |
|---|---|
| Carries floor load / is walkable | **Balcony / verandah** (s.9(1)(a)–(b)) or Sch. 2 structure — not window canopy |
| Not above an opening (e.g. decorative EW shelf) | May fall outside s.9(1)(e) but still Sch. 2 **appendage / architectural projection** |
| Is a continuous podium canopy / street canopy | Often Cap 123F / Cap 123A projection path — classify vs s.9 carefully |

**SD takeaway:** Tag every façade appendage on a schedule: `s.9 projection type` vs `Sch. 2 appendage` vs `Sch. 1 fixture` vs `window (QP)`. Wrong tag → wrong RI/QP path and wrong repair proposal.

### 7. Schedule 1 — Common parts inspection items (other than external walls)

Referenced by ss.4 & 17. These are the **common-parts audit list** for MBIS.

| # | Statutory item | What RI must be able to examine | Schematic / A&A design constraints |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Building structures** including transfer structures, cantilevered structures, and **exposed pile caps** | Transfer plates/beams, cantilevers in common areas, exposed pile caps | Do not permanently bury transfer / cantilever / pile-cap zones behind sealed finishes without **inspection access**. A&A that loads or cuts transfer zones needs RSE + may trigger detailed investigation (s.17) |
| **2(a)** | **Means of escape** | Stairs, exit routes, doors, widths, travel paths in common parts | Lobby / stair A&A that narrows MOE or adds storage into escape routes creates s.5 defect findings against latest applicable standard |
| **2(b)** | **Means of access for firefighting and rescue** | Fireman’s lift lobbies, EVA-linked access, rescue stair interfaces in common parts | Do not casually relocate fireman’s lift / lobby geometry in A&A without FR consultant + BA path |
| **2(c)** | **Fire resisting construction** | FR walls, floors, doors, enclosures, protected lobbies | FRR strip-out for “open lobby” looks is a classic MBIS / Cap 502·572 conflict — budget FR reinstatement or approved upgrade |
| **3** | **Common drain pipes** (aboveground & underground) + associated fixings | Stacks, common branches, underground common drains, brackets/fixings | Risers / ducts / false floors need **access panels**; underground common drains need survey before basement A&A |
| **4** | **Fixtures and installations** including metal gates, fence walls, protective barriers, parapets, balustrades, skylights | Guarding, courtyard/roof gates, fence walls, skylights | Guarding height/fixity and skylight weather/structure are inspectable — not “soft FF&E” |

**Finish overlay:** Every item above includes its **finish** where applicable (s.4(1)(a) + s.4(4)).

### 8. Schedule 2 — External wall inspection items

Referenced by ss.4 & 17. Applies under:
- **s.4(1)(a)** when the item is part of **common parts**; and
- **s.4(1)(b)** for the **external wall** limb.

| # | Statutory item | What RI must be able to examine | Schematic / A&A design constraints |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Building structures** including transfer structures and cantilevered structures | EW transfers, cantilevered bays / slabs / features | Bay windows, EW transfers, structural fins tied into frame — design **access + repair sequence** (gondola / BMU / terrace) |
| **2** | **Non-structural items** including fins, grilles and metal louvers | Architectural screens, acoustic/visual fins, metal louvres | Spec demountable fixings; corrosion / fatigue / loose panels are classic findings |
| **3** | **Curtain walls** | CW systems, anchors, panels, seals (as components of the CW) | CW package = core MBIS EW item — design replaceable units, accessible anchors, water-testability |
| **4** | **Appendages, architectural projections, and fixtures and installations** (including metal gates, protective barriers, parapets and balustrades) | Ornament, edge protection, EW gates, parapets/balustrades | Overlaps conceptually with s.9 projections — classify each element; do not leave “decorative” EW features outside the survey schedule |

**Windows on EW / in common parts:** excluded from Sch. 1 / Sch. 2 limbs (s.4(2)–(3)) → **QP / s.14** track.

**SD takeaway:** Anything you put on the EW as architecture is likely an inspection target. Budget BMU / gondola / reachable terraces and demountable detailing for future prescribed repair.

### 9. Team map — RI vs QP vs contractor vs representative

| Role | Cap 123 hook | Cap 123P operational duties |
|---|---|---|
| **RI — inspection** | s.30D(1)(a) | s.11 appointment notice; s.12 inspection pack; s.17–21 detailed investigation; s.30(1) copy delivery; may file s.12(3) independence cert if different repair RI |
| **RI — repair supervision** | s.30D(1)(b) | s.11 notice; ensure repair per proposal (s.22); revised proposal ≤7 days; s.13 completion pack + independence; may appoint **representative** (s.24); nominee under s.30D(10) (s.28); stop-work if ceases (s.29) |
| **QP — inspection** | s.30E(1)(a) | Window inspection certificates / reports (s.14); copy delivery (s.30(3)/(5)) |
| **QP — repair supervision** | s.30E(1)(b) | Ensure repair per proposal when not also inspect QP (s.23); s.14(5) cert; stop-work if ceases (s.29(2)) |
| **QP representative** | Cap 123 s.30E(4) | If QP is RGBC or RMWC → **authorized signatory** is the prescribed representative (s.10) |
| **Registered contractor** | Cap 123 | Executes prescribed repair; must keep inspection report / proposal (and revised proposal) **on site** and produce to BA (s.32) |

#### 9.1 Appointment notification (s.11) — first programme clock
| Who | Deadline | Form | Who must sign |
|---|---|---|---|
| RI under s.30D(1)(a) or (b) | **Within 7 days** after appointment date | Specified form to BA | RI **and** the person for whom the inspection/repair is carried out |
| QP under s.30E(1)(a) or (b) | **Within 7 days** after appointment date | Specified form to BA | QP **and** that person |

**SD takeaway:** Client / IO / owner signature on the appointment notice is mandatory — lock appointing authority early in aged buildings with fragmented ownership.

#### 9.2 Independence — RI must not be inside the contractor (ss.12(3), 13(2)(c))
Where prescribed repair is required:
- If a **different** RI is appointed under s.30D(1)(b), the **inspecting** RI (s.30D(1)(a)) must, within **14 days after repair completion**, certify to BA that they are **not** a partner, director, or AS of the registered contractor doing the repair (s.12(3)).
- The **repair-supervising** RI must always file the same independence certificate with the s.13 completion pack (s.13(2)(c)).

**SD takeaway:** Do not propose one commercial entity as both inspecting RI interest and repairing contractor for building (non-window) prescribed repair without checking partner / director / AS conflicts.

### 10. Document packages — building track (ss.12–13)

Windows are excluded from ss.12–13 (see §11).

#### 10.1 Inspection complete — RI under s.30D(1)(a) — **within 7 days** (s.12(2))

Submit to BA:

**A. Inspection report must include:**
1. Statement of **methods** adopted for the prescribed inspection;
2. Report on **findings**, including any inspection record and **test result**;
3. **Assessment** on the findings;
4. *(If applicable)* **proposal for prescribed repair** required to render the building safe;
5. *(If applicable)* report of any **building works** the RI must notify BA of under Cap 123 **s.30D(5)(b)**.

**B. Certificate (specified form) — RI certifies:**
1. Prescribed inspection carried out in accordance with the Ordinance;
2. Opinion that either:
   - (A) building is **safe** and **no prescribed repair** is required; **or**
   - (B) building has been **rendered dangerous** or is **liable to become dangerous**, and prescribed repair is required to render it safe; **and**
3. Inspection report prepared in accordance with the Ordinance.

#### 10.2 Extra independence cert when different repair RI (s.12(3))
If repair is required **and** a different RI is appointed under s.30D(1)(b): inspecting RI submits independence certificate **within 14 days after completion of the prescribed repair**.

#### 10.3 Repair complete — RI under s.30D(1)(b) — **within 14 days** (s.13(2))

Submit to BA:

**A. Completion report must include:**
1. Report on the prescribed repair, including:
   - record of repair works carried out;
   - report or certificate of **materials used**;
   - statement of **methods of testing** adopted;
   - record of **test results**;
2. *(If revisions were made to the s.12 repair proposal)* an account of **all revisions**.

**B. Certificate — RI certifies:**
1. Repair carried out in accordance with the Ordinance;
2. Repair carried out in accordance with the **s.12(2)(a)(iv) proposal** or the **s.22(2) revised proposal**;
3. In RI’s opinion, building has been **rendered safe**;
4. Completion report prepared in accordance with the Ordinance.

**C. Independence certificate** — RI is not partner / director / AS of the registered contractor.

### 11. Document packages — window track (s.14) — three scenarios

#### 11.1 Same QP inspects only; no repair needed (s.14(1))
| Clock | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| **Within 14 days** after inspection completion | Specified-form certificate: QP **or representative** carried out prescribed inspection per Ordinance; window is **safe**; no prescribed repair required |

#### 11.2 Same QP does inspection **and** repair (appointed under both s.30E(1)(a) and (b)) (s.14(2))
| Clock | Deliverable |
|---|---|
| **Within 14 days** after repair completion | Certificate covering: inspection done (by QP or representative); window was dangerous / liable to become dangerous and repair required; repair done per Ordinance; window **rendered safe** |

#### 11.3 Different QPs for inspect vs repair (s.14(3)–(5))

**Inspecting QP (s.30E(1)(a)) — within 7 days after inspection:**
1. **Inspection report** with: findings (records/tests); assessment; *(if applicable)* repair proposal to render window safe;
2. **Certificate**: inspection done (QP or representative); window dangerous / liable; repair required.

**Repair-supervising QP (s.30E(1)(b)) — within 14 days after repair:**
Certificate: repair per Ordinance; repair per s.14(4)(a)(iii) proposal or **s.23(2)** revised proposal; window rendered safe.

**SD takeaway:** Dual-QP window jobs force a **written repair proposal** from the inspect QP — single-QP combined jobs roll inspection + repair into one post-repair certificate (s.14(2)) without a separate s.14(4) report to BA, but copy-delivery to the client still applies (s.30(5)).

### 12. Detailed investigation (ss.15–21) — when walkover is not enough

#### 12.1 Application (s.15)
This Division **does not apply** to prescribed inspection of a **window**. Window defects stay on the QP track.

#### 12.2 Trigger (s.17)
During building prescribed inspection, if the RI under s.30D(1)(a):
- identifies any **serious defect** constituting **structural instability** or **serious health hazard**; **or**
- cannot ascertain the **extent or cause** of a defect,

then, for making a prescribed-repair proposal, a detailed examination/assessment may be conducted on any:
- Schedule 1 item;
- Schedule 2 item;
- projection; or
- signboard,

to ascertain seriousness, extent, or cause.

#### 12.3 BA endorsement path (ss.18–20) — programme critical path

| Step | Rule | Clock / effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1. RI decides detailed investigation needed | Must notify BA in writing of intention **and** submit proposal for endorsement (s.18(1)) | Before start |
| 2. Proposal contents (s.18(2)) | (a) purpose; (b) proposed scope, method, particulars; (c) justification for method; (d) summary of all defects subject to investigation + annotated photos + marked-up plans | Incomplete packs invite refusal |
| 3. BA decision (s.19) | Endorse in whole / in part, or refuse (with reasons) | BA must notify RI **within 28 days** of receiving notice + proposal |
| 4. Default start rule (s.20(1)) | **Must not commence** before BA endorsement; must follow endorsed proposal / endorsed parts | Hold point |
| 5. Client override (s.20(2)) | May proceed **without** endorsement only if: (a) client duly informed of BA refusal; **and** (b) client decides nevertheless to proceed | Document client instruction — risk sits with client/RI pathway |
| 6. Specialist (s.21) | RI may engage & supervise a specialist; this is an exemption for Cap 123 **s.30D(3)(a)** | Budget specialist (e.g. materials lab, structural investigator) under RI supervision |

**SD takeaway:** Serious structure / health findings or unknown defect causation add **BA endorsement + up to 28 days** before the repair scheme (and any A&A that depends on that fabric) is reliable. Do not freeze structural A&A on “assumed sound transfer plate” without survey allowance.

### 13. Repair must follow the proposal — revise within 7 days (ss.22–23)

| Who | Must ensure repair follows | When circumstances change |
|---|---|---|
| RI supervising building repair (s.30D(1)(b)) | Proposal in **s.12(2)(a)(iv)** | Submit **revised proposal to BA within 7 days** after the matter is revealed / circumstance arises (s.22(2)) |
| QP supervising window repair **only** (s.30E(1)(b) but **not** also (a)) | Proposal in **s.14(4)(a)(iii)** | Same — revised proposal within **7 days** (s.23(2)) |

**Gaps / traps:**
- s.23 applies only to a QP appointed under (1)(b) **but not** (1)(a). Combined inspect+repair QP under s.14(2) does not use the s.23 revised-proposal limb in the same way — scope changes still need disciplined documentation and Ordinance compliance.
- Scaffold-side “extras” (new canopies, illegal structure removal beyond proposal, aesthetic upgrades) silently morphing the prescribed-repair scope without s.22 / s.23 revision + proper Cap 123A / Cap 123N path = compliance risk.

### 14. RI representative for repair supervision (ss.24–25)

Only the **repair-supervising RI** (s.30D(1)(b)) may appoint a representative to perform supervision duties on the RI’s behalf (s.24(1)).

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Pre-notice | Notify BA in writing of intended appointment **not less than 7 days before** making the appointment (s.24(2)) |
| Contents | Particulars, **qualifications**, and **experience** of the representative (s.24(4)) |
| Change (other than the appointment itself) | Notify BA within **7 days** of the change (s.24(3)) |
| Personal responsibility | Despite representative, RI remains **personally responsible** for supervision of the prescribed repair (s.24(5)) |
| BA rejection (s.25) | BA may reject if quals/experience not sufficient or suitable; must notify RI with reasons and specify rejection date; appointment **ceases** from that date |

**SD takeaway:** Representatives are allowed for site attendance bandwidth — they are **not** a liability shield. BA can kill the appointment mid-programme; keep a backup candidate with documented experience on similar façade / MBIS repair.

### 15. Change of RI / QP — stop-work rules (ss.26–29)

Applies once an s.11 appointment notification has been made (s.26).

| Event | Duty | Effect on works |
|---|---|---|
| RI or QP becomes unwilling / unable to act (s.27) | Notify BA in writing within **7 days** after cessation | See stop-work below for repair roles |
| Repair RI nominates temporary RI under Cap 123 **s.30D(10)** (s.28) | Notify BA in specified form within **7 days** of nomination, stating nomination + both parties’ confirmation; notify again within **7 days** if nomination ceases | Keeps repair supervision continuous |
| Repair RI ceases **and** no s.30D(10) nominee (s.29(1)) | — | **Prescribed repair must cease** until new RI under s.30D(1)(b) is appointed **and** s.11 notification made |
| Repair QP ceases (s.29(2)) | — | **Window prescribed repair must cease** until new QP under s.30E(1)(b) is appointed **and** s.11 notification made |

**SD takeaway:** For long façade / common-parts repair phases tied to A&A, continuous RI/QP cover is a **programme risk**. Plan s.30D(10) nominees / representatives before award.

### 16. Copy-delivery matrix (s.30) — full clocks

#### 16.1 Building — inspecting RI (s.30D(1)(a)) (s.30(1))
| Recipient | What | Clock |
|---|---|---|
| Person for whom inspection is carried out | Copy of each s.12(2) document submitted to BA | Within **7 days** after inspection completion |
| Repair RI (if different, under s.30D(1)(b)) | Same s.12(2) pack | Within **2 months** after s.12(2) submission date, **or** before repair commencement — **whichever earlier** |

#### 16.2 Building — repair RI (s.30D(1)(b)) (s.30(2))
| Recipient | What | Clock |
|---|---|---|
| Registered contractor | Copy of each s.12(2) document | **Before** repair commencement |
| Registered contractor | Copy of any s.22(2) revised proposal | **Same day** as BA submission |
| Person for whom repair is carried out | Copies of s.13(2) docs + any s.22(2) revised proposal | **Same day** as BA submission |

#### 16.3 Windows — inspecting QP (s.30E(1)(a)) (s.30(3))
| Scenario | Recipient | What | Clock |
|---|---|---|---|
| No repair | Client | s.14(1) certificate | **Same day** as BA submission |
| Repair + different repair QP | Repair QP | s.14(4) pack | Within **1 month** after s.14(4) submission, **or** before repair start — **whichever earlier** |
| Repair + different repair QP | Client | s.14(4) pack | **Same day** as BA submission |

#### 16.4 Windows — repair-only QP (s.30E(1)(b) but not (a)) (s.30(4))
| Recipient | What | Clock |
|---|---|---|
| Registered contractor *(only if QP is **not** also acting as that contractor)* | s.14(4)(a) inspection report pack | **Before** repair start |
| Registered contractor | Any s.23(2) revised proposal | **Same day** as BA submission |
| Client | s.14(5) cert + any s.23(2) revised proposal | **Same day** as BA submission |

#### 16.5 Windows — same QP for inspect + repair (s.30(5))
| Recipient | What | Clock |
|---|---|---|
| Client | s.14(2) document | **Same day** as BA submission |

**SD takeaway:** Repair must not start until the contractor holds the inspection pack (building track always; window track when QP ≠ contractor). Build this into tender prelims and commencement checklists.

### 21. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing existing-building A&A, façade, common-parts, projection, signboard, or window packages:

1. **Confirm Cap 123 ss.30B–30E status** — active notice, impending cycle, voluntary early MBIS/MWIS, or none.
2. **Map every fabric element to a limb** — common parts (Sch. 1 + applicable Sch. 2), external walls (Sch. 2), s.9 projections, signboards, windows (QP only).
3. **Classify every appendage** — balcony / verandah / planter / drying rack / window canopy (s.9(2) three-part test) / BS support & associated pipes-ducts vs Sch. 2 appendage / Sch. 1 fixture.
4. **Finish schedule risk** — tiling / rendering / cladding / false ceiling on EW or common parts = inspectable finish (s.4(4)); design access for inspection/repair.
5. **Access & demountability** — transfer / cantilever / exposed pile caps / CW / fins / louvres / guarding / signboard fixings must be inspectable and repairable without destroying the design.
6. **FRR / MOE / firefighting access** — Sch. 1 item 2; do not propose A&A that creates obvious deficiency against the **latest applicable** s.5(2) standard unless a parallel approved upgrade path is funded.
7. **Pull the standard baseline early** — approved plans, Cap 123N records, Cap 502 / Cap 572 FS improvement records; if conflict, **latest wins** (s.5(3) / s.8(4)).
8. **Separate repair vs upgrade budgets** — prescribed repair = **rendered safe** to historical latest standard; full current-code upgrade is separate A&A / FS improvement.
9. **Lock team & independence** — RI ≠ contractor partner/director/AS; QP AS rules if QP is RGBC/RMWC; appointment notices need owner/IO co-signature within 7 days.
10. **Programme the statutory clocks** — 7 / 14 / 28-day gates; dual-RI handover ≤2 months; dual-QP handover ≤1 month; contractor must hold inspection pack before repair start.
11. **Allow for detailed investigation** — structural instability / serious health hazard / unknown cause → BA endorsement path (up to 28 days) before locking structural A&A.
12. **Control mid-repair scope creep** — any change to the repair proposal → s.22 / s.23 revision within 7 days; scaffold extras need their own Cap 123A / Cap 123N path.
13. **Plan continuity** — RI representative (≥7 days pre-notice), s.30D(10) nominee, stop-work if repair RI/QP ceases without replacement.
14. **Site documentation** — contractor keeps s.12(2)(a) / s.14(4)(a) + revised proposals on site (s.32).
15. **Cross-link** — Cap 123 Part 2A (notices & core duties), Cap 123A (parallel building works), Cap 123N (MW that resets s.5 benchmark), Cap 123F (projection geometry if new projections), Cap 123Q (structural repair performance), Cap 502 / Cap 572 (FS improvement standards), FS Code / MOA / FRC CoPs as technical references for Sch. 1 item 2.
