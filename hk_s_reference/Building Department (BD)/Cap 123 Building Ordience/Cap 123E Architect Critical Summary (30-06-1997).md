# Cap 123E — Building (Lifts) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 30 June 1997 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. E

> ⛔ **Status: REPEALED.** The attached consolidated PDF is a **repeal stub only** (title + “已廢除 / Repealed”). There are **no operative design constraints** left in Cap 123E itself.  
> **Do not cite Cap 123E** on submissions. The historic rulebook (regs 1–54) still defines the *topics* an architect must resolve at SD — those duties now live in Cap **123Q** ss.39–41, BD **Code of Practice for Building Works for Lifts and Escalators 2011 (2020 Ed.)** via PNAP **APP-29**, Cap **618** / EMSD Design Code, FS Code, Cap **123F** / B(P)R 41B, and DMBA 2008.  
> Sibling stub: Cap **123D** Building (Escalators) Regulations — also repealed; escalators → same Cap 123Q + Lift CoP §5 + Cap 618 path.  
> Hard numbers below marked **(historic Cap 123E)** are the repealed text’s design DNA — use them as SD memory aids, then **verify** against current Lift CoP Table 1 / Cap 618 / FS Code before locking cores.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123E formerly prescribed Building Authority rules for **lifts installed in buildings** under Cap 123: exclusive liftway, enclosure & 2-hour FRP walls, shaft ventilation, pits, machine rooms, landing/car doors, structural support, safety gear, fireman’s lifts, passenger vs goods rated loads, service lifts, and disabled-person lifts. The 30 June 1997 consolidation shows the Cap **wholly repealed**. Lift *equipment* safety is now under Cap **618** (EMSD; from 17.12.2012, replacing Cap 327). *Building works* around lifts are performance-controlled under Cap **123Q** Part 11 Div 2, with detailed deemed-to-satisfy standards in the **2011 Lift Building Works CoP** (APP-29).

### 3. Scope & exemptions — what is / is not a “lift” for SD (ex-reg. 3 → Cap 123Q s.39)

Cap 123E (and now Cap 123Q Div 2) **did / do not apply** to, among others:

| Exempt type (historic / current) | SD implication |
|---|---|
| Crown / HA / military / foreign consular buildings (historic Crown exemptions) | Different ownership regimes — confirm with client |
| Goods / vehicle hoist: travel ≤ **3.5 m**, **no floor penetration** | Often outside Cap 123Q Div 2; still check Cap 618 / safety |
| Skip / furnace / machine-feed hoists; conveyors; amusement rides | Not passenger-lift package |
| Construction-site-only personnel / materials lifts | Temporary works, not permanent core |
| Stage / orchestra lifts; pier ramps | Specialist |
| Stairlift along stair flight; disability platform with level difference ≤ **2 m** (Cap 123Q) | Platform lift ≠ full passenger lift shaft |

**SD takeaway:** Normal passenger, fireman’s, goods, and service lifts in private buildings → full Cap 123Q + Lift CoP + Cap 618 package.

### 4. Liftway package (ex-regs 6–13) — core killers at massing

#### 4.1 Clearances & exclusive use (ex-regs 6–8)

| Historic rule | Current practice |
|---|---|
| Liftway, pit, enclosing walls, machine room must give **adequate clearance** for safe operation (reg. 6) | Cap 123Q s.40(1)(a) + Lift CoP Table 1 well / pit / headroom |
| **Nothing else** in the liftway except the lift + necessary associated equipment (reg. 7) | Lift CoP: well for lift only; **no other cables/devices; no fire sprinklers** |
| **Max 4 lifts** in one liftway (reg. 8) | Still treat as planning discipline; multi-lift wells need full-height partitions (Lift CoP §5.2 / historic reg. 17) |

#### 4.2 Enclosure & FRP (ex-reg. 9)

| Historic Cap 123E | Current |
|---|---|
| Completely enclosed except door & ventilation openings | Lift CoP: **totally enclosed** imperforate walls / floor / ceiling |
| Walls strong enough to support guides | Cap 123Q s.40 + APP-29 fixing details (guide brackets, machine, deflector sheave) |
| Enclosing walls FRP **≥ 2 hours** (reg. 9(2)(b)) | **FS Code Part C** sets current FRP for shafts — do not assume 2 h without checking occupancy / height / FS Code tables |

#### 4.3 Wall facing car entrance — smooth face (ex-reg. 10)

| Historic | Current Lift CoP (still SD-critical) |
|---|---|
| Inner face facing car entrance: smooth continuous surface, no projections/recesses so far as practicable | Continuous smooth hard vertical face |
| Unavoidable projection/recess: underside splayed **≥ 75°** from horizontal | Same 75° splay idea; projections >5 mm need lintel connection or splay ≥75° for ≥20 mm |
| With levelling device: smooth face over levelling zone + rundown distance | Coordinate with lift supplier levelling zone |
| — | Band below each landing sill (~350 mm): **no plaster-faced or glass** walls; extend ≥25 mm beyond car entrance each side |

#### 4.4 Shaft ventilation (ex-reg. 11) — roof / MR coordination

| Historic Cap 123E | Current Lift CoP |
|---|---|
| Vent at **top** of liftway to **open air** | Same principle; well must **not** ventilate other rooms |
| Location: wall above roof / below top floor of shaft, **or** in top floor of shaft with equal openings in MR walls/roof | Direct, ducted, or via machine/pulley room |
| Open area ≥ **0.15 m² per lift** in the liftway | ≥ **1%** of well horizontal cross-section; **never < 0.3 m²** net free area per well |
| — | Shared well 4 / 5 / 6 lifts → min vent **0.4 / 0.5 / 0.6 m²** |
| Historic “open air” definition: vertically uncovered; any horizontal dim ≥ **1.5 m**; if 4-sided, area ≥ **1 m² per 6 m** mean wall height | Still useful when arguing “open air” termination |

**SD takeaway:** Reserve shaft vent path at roof / MR early — do not borrow lift vents for toilets or corridors.

#### 4.5 Liftway not terminating on ground (ex-reg. 12)

| Historic | Current |
|---|---|
| Floor of lift (pit base) FRP ≥ **2 hours** if shaft does not terminate on/in ground | FS Code Part C + structural design of suspended pit |

#### 4.6 Habitable / accessible space under shaft (ex-reg. 13)

| Historic Cap 123E | Current Lift CoP |
|---|---|
| Prefer **not** to put accessible space under lift | Same preference |
| If used/likely used by persons below: pit floor for **greater of** (i) impact of car at full rated load **or** CW at rated / governor speed, **and** (ii) imposed load **5 kPa** | Pit base ≥ **5 kPa** imposed **and** either solid pier under CW buffer to solid ground **or** CW with safety gear |
| Plus: safety gear on car **and** CW, **or** solid pier under CW buffer from ground | Align with Cap 618 safety gear / buffer rules |

**SD takeaway:** Retail / carpark / plant under a tower core is a structural + safety-gear decision — flag before stacking programme under the shaft.

### 5. Lift pit (ex-regs 14–17) — basement / lowest landing

| Topic | Historic Cap 123E | Current Lift CoP / practice |
|---|---|---|
| Pit required | Yes, below lowest landing (reg. 14) | Yes for passenger/goods (Table 1 **Ph**); service lifts often **no pit** |
| Depth | Adequate clearance after buffers fully depressed | Table 1 by **load + speed** (e.g. ~1700–3650 mm; jumps at ≥2.0 m/s) |
| Waterproof | Waterproof construction | Impervious to water after equipment built-in; drain where needed |
| Illumination + stop switch (reg. 15) | Adequate artificial light + stop switch accessible from pit access | Cap 618 / works CoP + building provision for lighting circuit |
| Access if pit **> 1.6 m** below lowest landing (reg. 16) | Ladder **or** door | Ph **> 2 m** → access door mandatory; Ph **> 1.6 m and ≤ 2 m** → door if layout permits |
| Ladder rules (historic) | Permanently fixed, vertical, incombustible, accessible from lowest landing | Permanent access + handholds if no door; must not project into clear running space |
| Door rules (historic) | Min **1.4 m** H × **0.6 m** W; not into well; spring lock (open from inside without key); interlock so lift cannot run if open; FRP ≥ **1 hour**; bilingual DANGER notice ≥ **25 mm** | Same order of size (≥1.4 × 0.6 m); key lock; openable from inside; danger notice; APP-29 dual-key rules for many emergency doors |
| Multi-lift pit partition (reg. 17) | Rigid partition from pit floor ≥ **2.5 m** high across full depth; **full height** if car-roof edge to adjacent moving part < **0.3 m** | Full-height partition between lifts through full well height and depth |

**Fall-arrest (current):** ≥1 anchorage adjacent to lowest landing door, **1.5–1.8 m** above FFL — not required if compliant pit access door provided.

### 6. Counterweight, guides, structure (ex-regs 18–21, 24–25)

| Topic | Historic Cap 123E hard number | SD / RSE action now |
|---|---|---|
| CW screen (reg. 18) | Screen from **300 mm** above pit floor to ≥ **2.1 m** | Cap 618 / Design Code |
| CW location (reg. 19) | Must be in **same** liftway as its lift | No remote CW shaft freestyle |
| Guides (reg. 20) | Adequate length/rigidity; machined surfaces; deflection ≤ **6 mm** in normal conditions | RSE + lift supplier; APP-29 guide-rail bracket fixings |
| Guide brackets (reg. 21) | Spacing ≤ **3.5 m**; embedded in / bolted to shaft walls; **no** wood/fibre plugs | Structural embedment / chemical anchors per APP-29 |
| Equipment support (reg. 24) | Prevent looseness / displacement | APP-29: machine, pulley, guide fixings |
| Supporting beams/slabs (reg. 25) | Strength for **weight of all apparatus + 2× max resultant suspended load** with full rated load at rest | Cap 123Q Parts 3–4 + Loading Code + lift reactions; size MR / headroom slabs early |

### 7. Machine room & pulley penthouse (ex-regs 22–23) — roof / core profile

#### 7.1 Machine room (historic reg. 22 → Lift CoP §7)

| Item | Historic Cap 123E | Current Lift CoP |
|---|---|---|
| Required | Room for machinery for every lift | Conventional yes; **MRL** allowed only if **EMSD-approved** model (show on GBP) |
| Clear height | ≥ **2.1 m** (except service lift) | ≥ **2.1 m** to underside of structural roof beams in access/working areas |
| Lighting & ventilation | Adequate | Adequate; coordinate with shaft vent |
| Access / remove plant | Convenient access; plant removable | Access from **common areas only** (never through private flats); hoisting means so plant not manhandled on stairs |
| Floor strength | Support heaviest unit | Same + APP-29 |
| Floor FRP | ≥ **2 hours** | FS Code Part C |
| Walls/roof | Brick, concrete, or approved | Solid enclosure |
| Level change > **600 mm** | Guard rails + ladder | Rise > **0.6 m** → **stairs** (see below); ≤0.6 m → fixed ladder + top platform |
| Door | Self-closing; spring lock (open inside without key); FRP ≥ **½ hour**; bilingual DANGER notice ≥ **25 mm** | Door ≥ **600 mm** W × **1.8 m** H; **must not open inward**; danger notice; Cap 123Q s.41 restricted-space caution |
| Sprinklers | — | **Not normally** fitted in MR / pulley rooms |
| Allowed contents | Lift machinery | Lift equipment only (+ service-lift/escalator machines, room A/C, required FSI) |

#### 7.2 Machine-room access stairs (current Lift CoP 3.12.4)

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Width | ≥ **900 mm** |
| Tread / riser | Tread ≥ **225 mm** (anti-slip); riser ≤ **175 mm** |
| Flight | ≤ **16** steps without landing |
| Handrails | Both sides; outer edge barrier ≥ **1.1 m** |

#### 7.3 Pulley / overhead rooms (ex-reg. 23)

| Historic | Current |
|---|---|
| Clear height ≥ **1.2 m**; safe access; adequate artificial lighting | Pulley room clear height ≥ **1.5 m** under roof; access door height ≥ **1.4 m** |

#### 7.4 MRL & alternative MR clearances (current)

| Approach | Rule |
|---|---|
| MRL | Table 1 MR area / overall headroom **do not apply**; control panels accessible from **common areas** only |
| Alt. to Table 1 Ra footprint | Clearances: ≥ **0.7 m** depth in front of panels (≥0.5 m wide or full panel width); ≥ **0.5 × 0.6 m** at moving parts / manual emergency ops; access ways ≥ **0.5 m** (0.4 m if no moving/hot parts) — note on GBP |

**SD takeaway:** Fix conventional vs MRL **before** freezing roof plant height, top-storey headroom, and common-area cabinet niches.

### 8. Landing doors, car doors, car envelope (ex-regs 26–34, 38A)

#### 8.1 Landing doors (ex-reg. 26)

| Historic Cap 123E | Current |
|---|---|
| Full height & width of opening; FRP ≥ **1 hour** | FS Code Part C for landing door FRP |
| Interlock: open only in levelling zone; car cannot move unless door closed & locked | Cap 618 |
| Emergency opening provision, protected against accidental/malicious use | Cap 618 + APP-29 / APP-91 maintenance openings |
| Vision panel (if any): reject **100 mm** sphere; bottom ≥ **1 m** above landing; tempered/laminated/approved; flush on landing face if sliding | Cap 618 / Design Code |

#### 8.2 Car (ex-regs 27–34)

| Topic | Historic Cap 123E | Current |
|---|---|---|
| Rated load marking | Conspicuous; passenger lifts in **persons + mass**; person = **75 kg** | Cap 618 marking rules |
| Load unit | Must be a **car** | Same |
| Car doors | Imperforate; car cannot move unless closed; cannot open inside unless in levelling zone | Cap 618 |
| Collapsible / folding | Imperforate leaves; no jam risk; powered open ≤ **¼** total width | Cap 618 (largely obsolete for new passenger lifts) |
| Car vision panel | Same 100 mm / 1 m / tempered rules as landing | Cap 618 |
| Clear internal height | ≥ **2 m** (reg. 32) | Cap 618 / Design Code (still design around ≥2 m clear) |
| Lighting & ventilation | Adequate electric light + ventilation | Cap 618 + building power |
| Vent panel within **1.8 m** of car floor | Backplate required | Cap 618 |

#### 8.3 Fire-use notice (ex-reg. 38A → Cap 123Q s.41)

| Historic | Current |
|---|---|
| Inside car **and** outside near landing: bilingual “WHEN THERE IS A FIRE DO NOT USE THE LIFT”, characters ≥ **13 mm** | Cap 123Q s.41: permanent notice at **every lift entrance**; Lift CoP: bilingual at each landing (or every other if paired), characters ≥ **15 mm** |
| — | Cap 123Q s.41 also: restricted-space access doors — caution against entry / interference |

#### 8.4 Landing interface (current Lift CoP extras)

| Item | Rule |
|---|---|
| Water control | **Slight counter-slope** in front of each landing sill — no washing/drainage into well |
| Sill strength | Adequately support loads entering the car |

### 9. Safety, emergency & rescue (ex-regs 35–38B) — mostly Cap 618, but shaft architecture matters

| Topic | Historic Cap 123E | Architect / AP SD note |
|---|---|---|
| Safety gear (reg. 35) | On every car; governor-operated (access for maintenance/rescue); safety-rope OK if speed ≤ **0.5 m/s** and machinery prevents uncontrolled acceleration | Cap 618 Design Code — AP does not detail, but allow governor access |
| Overload device (reg. 35A) | ≥ **10%** over rated load → prevent movement + prevent power-door close (lifts from 3.5.1969) | Cap 618 |
| Emergency light (reg. 35B) | Auto-illuminate on power failure | Cap 618 + emergency power path |
| Emergency signal (reg. 36) | Bell/audible; switch in car; battery if electric | Cap 618 intercom / alarm |
| Emergency stop (reg. 37) | For open-work / collapsible-door cars — push/switch in car | Cap 618 |
| Emergency exit in car roof (reg. 38) | ≥ **350 × 500 mm** (or ≥ **400 mm** dia if non-rect.); openable **only from outside**; interlock | Cap 618 — affects car roof clearances / headroom |
| Emergency access to liftway (reg. 38B) | If liftway **> 11 m** high: rescue access ≤ **11 m** intervals; door ≥ **1.8 m** H × **0.5 m** W; not into well; spring lock; interlock; DANGER RESCUE DOOR notice ≥ **25 mm** | Lift CoP: if consecutive landing sills **> 11 m**, intermediate emergency doors so spacing ≤ **11 m**; door ≥ **1.8 × 0.5 m**; APP-29 dual-key rules |

**APP-29 emergency/inspection door lock upgrade (Aug 2015 / Sep 2019):** dual key-operated locks from outside (two keys simultaneously); openable from inside without key; warning figure ≥ **100 mm** at keyholes — except where inadvertent entry unlikely (e.g. landing doors as emergency doors, or sill ≥ **1 m** above adjoining floor → single designated key OK).

| Historic Cap 123E | Current |
|---|---|

### 11. Passenger vs goods vs service vs disabled (ex-Parts III–VI)

#### 11.1 Passenger lifts (ex-regs 39–42)

| Historic | Current |
|---|---|
| Car completely enclosed sides + top; no open-work in sides (except access/vent) | Cap 618 + Lift CoP |
| Doors full height & width of opening | Cap 618 |
| Min rated load vs net car floor area — **Table I** (e.g. 1.10 m² → 375 kg; 1.60 m² → 600 kg; 2.40 m² → 1000 kg; above 5 m² add formula) | Superseded by Lift CoP **Table 1** + Cap 618 car-area rules — do **not** submit Cap 123E Table I |

#### 11.2 Goods lifts (ex-regs 43–47)

| Historic | Current |
|---|---|
| Enclosed car; open-work panels allowed above **1.8 m** and in roof if openings reject **40 mm** sphere | Cap 618 |
| General freight: min rated load ≥ **330 kg/m²** of car floor | Cap 618 / BA approval for non-general freight |
| “General freight” only if: load normally evenly distributed; single piece / truck ≤ **¼** rated load; loading manual or non-powered truck only | Define loading regime at SD (pallet jack / forklift changes structure + door protection) |

#### 11.3 Service lifts (ex-reg. 53 + definition)

| Historic Cap 123E definition | Current Lift CoP §4 |
|---|---|
| Goods only; rated load ≤ **250 kg**; car floor ≤ **1 m²**; car height ≤ **1 200 mm** | Same thresholds |
| Many Part II / III rules disapplied | Dedicated §4: pit often **not** required; landing notice “SERVICE LIFT — PERSONS FORBIDDEN…” (≥25 mm); machinery room size by plant mass (≥150 kg → floor ≥1.5×1.5 m, height ≥1.2 m, etc.) |

#### 11.4 Lifts for disabled persons (ex-reg. 54 → DMBA)

| Historic Cap 123E (when B(P)R provided disabled lift) | Current DMBA 2008 |
|---|---|
| Essential controls **900 mm–1.2 m** above car floor | Align with DMBA control heights |
| Handrails at rear & sides to within **150 mm** of corners | DMBA handrail rules |
| — | Car min **1100 × 1400 mm**; door clear width ≥ **900 mm**; continuous accessible route entrance → lift → all floors (PNAP APP-152) |

### 12. Cap 123Q Part 11 Div 2 — the only remaining BD *statute* (ss.39–41)

#### 12.1 Application (s.39)
Applies to buildings with lift/escalator installed or to be installed, with the exemption list in §3 above (amusement, short goods/vehicle lifts, construction lifts, stairlifts, ≤2 m disability platforms, etc.).

#### 12.2 Performance (s.40)
Building must provide adequate **structural strength, space, protection, access and ventilation** for safe operation / inspection / maintenance, and keep **restricted space** (shaft + associated equipment) inaccessible except for inspection / maintenance / repair / rescue. Same duties for lifts **added after completion**.

#### 12.3 Notices (s.41)
Permanent, legible, durable notices at restricted-space access **and** every lift entrance (fire-use caution).

### 14. Lift CoP Table 1 — the numbers that actually size cores (replace Cap 123E Table I)

Fix **rated load + speed + conventional vs MRL** before locking floor-to-floor and roof. Illustrative extract (verify full Table 1):

| Rated load | Persons | Typical car Cw×Cd | Well Ww×Wd | Entrance Ew | Pit Ph (by speed) | Headroom Sh (by speed) | MR area Ra | Overall Uh (by speed) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **630 kg** | 8 | 1100×1400 | 1800×2100 | 800 | 1700–1800 | 4450–4850 | 15 m² | 7250–7650 |
| **800 kg** | 10 | 1400×1350 | 1900×2300 | 800 | 1700–2800 | 4450–5650 | ~15 | 7250–8450 |
| **1000 kg** | 13 | 1600×1400 | 2400×2300 | 1100 | 1800–3650 | 4300–6300 | 20 | 7200–10400 |
| **1250 kg** | 16 | 1950×1400 | 2600×2300 | 1100 | 1900–3650 | 4400–6300 | 22 | 7250–10400 |
| **1600 kg** | 21 | 2000×1750 | 2600×2600 | 1100 | 1900–3650 | 4800–6300 | 25 | 7800–10600 |

**Speed effect:** at ~**≥ 2.0 m/s**, pit/headroom jump sharply (Ph often ~**2800** mm); at **3.5 m/s**, Ph ~**3650** mm and Uh can exceed **10 m**. Deviation from Table 1 only with written confirmation from registered lift engineer / authorised person of registered lift contractor.

### 15. PNAP overlays that move schematics

#### 15.1 APP-29 — building works requirements
- Points AP/RSE to Lift CoP as deemed-to-satisfy Cap 123Q / ex-reg. 9A performance.
- Demand adequate **fixing details**: guide-rail brackets, lift machine, deflector sheave.
- Incomplete associated works (App. C lifts / App. D escalators) routinely block EMSD put-into-service.

#### 15.2 APP-89 — better lift service & GFA exemption

| Use | First “baseline” shaft % (accountable) | Extra exemptible (cap) |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic / hotel | **2.5%** of domestic/hotel GFA | Up to further **3.5%** (useful band ≈ 2.5–6%) |
| Office &lt; 10 000 m² office GFA | **5%** of office GFA | Up to further **3%** (≈ 5–8%) |
| Office ≥ 10 000 m² office GFA | **3.5%** of office GFA | Up to further **2.5%** (≈ 3.5–6%) |

Exclude shafts serving GFA-non-accountable storeys (e.g. refuge). Fireman’s + accessible lifts remain mandatory under other controls.

#### 15.3 APP-91 — maintenance / replacement
Typically max **two** doors per liftway open at once (hoarding overall max **four**) — design landings so future lift replacement does not gut FRP.

### 17. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing cores, basement, roof profile, and GFA:

1. **Confirm statute** — Cap 123E is dead; cite Cap **123Q** ss.39–41 + Lift CoP (APP-29) + Cap **618**.
2. **Lift schedule** — passenger / fireman’s / goods / service / accessible; load, speed, conventional vs **MRL**.
3. **Well package** — exclusive shaft; max multi-lift strategy; full-height partitions; FS Code FRP (historic 2 h was Cap 123E — verify FS Code).
4. **Table 1 envelope** — Ww×Wd, Ph, Sh, Uh, MR Ra (or MRL cabinets); watch ≥2.0 m/s jump.
5. **Under-shaft programme** — no accessible space below without 5 kPa + pier / CW safety gear.
6. **Pit access** — door / ladder / anchorage strategy if Ph > 1.6–2.0 m.
7. **Ventilation** — top-of-well vent ≥1% (min 0.3 m²); not shared with other rooms.
8. **Emergency doors** — landing sill spacing ≤ **11 m** or intermediate rescue doors; APP-29 lock rules.
9. **Machine room / MRL** — common-area access only; stair ≥900 if rise >0.6 m; hoisting hooks; no sprinklers normally.
10. **Landing interface** — counter-slope; fire notices; FRP landing doors.
11. **Fireman’s lift** — B(P)R 41B + FS Code Part D if building height/storeys trigger.
12. **BFA** — DMBA car ≥1100×1400, door ≥900; continuous route.
13. **GFA / SC** — Cap 123F accountability; APP-89 exemption math if oversizing service.
14. **Structure** — RSE owns guide reactions, pit, MR slabs, lifting beams (historic 2× suspended load DNA → current calc).
15. **OP path** — EMSD Form 5 + APP-29 associated works complete; Cap 123A OP does not replace Cap 618 permit.
