# Cap 123E — Building (Lifts) Regulations
**Architect critical summary for schematic design**  
Consolidated version: 30 June 1997 | Cap. 123 sub. leg. E

> ⛔ **Status: REPEALED.** The attached consolidated PDF is a **repeal stub only** (title + “已廢除 / Repealed”). There are **no operative design constraints** left in Cap 123E.  
> **Do not design or cite Cap 123E** on submissions. Split the former lift rulebook across **BD building fabric** (Cap **123Q** ss.39–41), **EMSD lift safety** (Cap **618** + Design Code), and **planning / fire / BFA** overlays (Cap 123F, FS Code, DMBA).  
> Sibling stub: Cap **123D** Building (Escalators) Regulations — also repealed; escalators follow the same Cap 123Q + Cap 618 path.

---

## Regulatory Overview

Cap 123E formerly prescribed Building Authority rules for **lifts installed in buildings** under the Buildings Ordinance (liftway enclosure, pits, machine rooms, landing doors, structural support, clearances, safety gear, etc.). The 30 June 1997 consolidation shows the Cap **wholly repealed**; lift *equipment* safety is now under the **Lifts and Escalators Ordinance (Cap. 618)** (EMSD; in force 17.12.2012, replacing Cap 327), while *building design around* lifts/escalators is performance-controlled under Cap **123Q** Part 11 Division 2.

## Critical main topics and subtopics

### 1. Immediate SD rule — do not design to Cap 123E

| Item | Action |
|---|---|
| Cap 123E regs (historic liftway / pit / machine-room / landing-door / safety-gear text) | **Obsolete** — treat as historical only |
| BD statute for building works around lifts | Cap **123Q** ss.**39–41** |
| Lift / escalator **equipment** design, install, examine, use & operate | Cap **618** + Lifts and Escalators (General) Regulation + EMSD **Code of Practice on the Design and Construction of Lifts and Escalators** (current Ed.; EN81-20/50 lineage) |
| OP gate | EMSD **Form 5** Permit to Use and Operate — not a Cap 123E certificate |
| Escalator twin | Cap **123D** also repealed → same Cap 123Q + Cap 618 path |

### 2. What Cap 123E used to own → where it lives now

Historic Cap 123E covered the **architectural / structural envelope of the lift**: exclusive liftway, enclosure & ventilation, pit depth / access / lighting / stop switch, machine room / pulley penthouse, supporting beams & slabs, landing doors, guides / counterweight screens, overload & emergency provisions. Map those topics at SD as follows:

| Former Cap 123E territory | Current control | Schematics implication |
|---|---|---|
| Liftway exclusive for lift + associated equipment | Cap **123Q** s.40 + Cap **618** Design Code | Core grid must deliver a dedicated shaft; no dual-use shaft for services / stores |
| Pit, overrun / headroom, machine room or MRL space | Cap **123Q** s.40(1)(a) + EMSD Design Code | Lock **pit depth, top overrun, MR / MRL** with lift consultant before freezing floor-to-floor and roof profile |
| Structural support / loads to beams & slabs | Cap **123Q** s.40 + Cap 123Q Parts 3–4 + Loading Code | RSE early for guide-rail reactions, MR slab, pit walls, seismic / impact where applicable |
| Landing doors / shaft openings / fire integrity | FS Code (FRP of shaft & lobby); PNAP **APP-91** (maintenance openings) | Shaft FRP and lobby strategy are fire-compartmentation design — not “MEP only” |
| Equipment safety gear, overload, car emergency devices | Cap **618** + EMSD Design Code | Specify via registered lift contractor / RE; AP does not “detail” Cap 123E safety gear |
| Safe access for inspection / maintenance / rescue; keep shaft off public circulation | Cap **123Q** s.40(1)(a)–(b) | Machine-room doors, pit access, rescue paths, and lobby locks are **architectural** |
| Warning “do not enter restricted space” / “do not use lift in fire” | Cap **123Q** s.41 | Plan conspicuous notice locations at restricted-space doors **and every lift entrance** |
| Escalators (former Cap 123D) | Cap **123Q** ss.39–41 + Cap **618** | Truss pit, inclination, clearances, MR / drive space — brief early if mall / transit atrium |

### 3. Cap 123Q Part 11 Div 2 — the only remaining BD *hard* lift statute (ss.39–41)

#### 3.1 When Cap 123Q Division 2 applies (s.39)
Applies to a building with a lift or escalator **installed or to be installed**, **except** (among others): amusement rides; conveyors; furnace/skip hoists; short goods / vehicle lifts (travel ≤ **3.5 m**, no floor penetration); construction-site only lifts; stage/orchestra lifts; stairlifts along a stair flight; disability lifting platforms with level difference ≤ **2 m**.

**SD takeaway:** Passenger, fireman’s, goods, and service lifts in normal buildings → Division 2 applies. Unusual short goods / platform devices may fall outside Cap 123Q Div 2 but can still trigger Cap 618 or other regimes — confirm with lift / EMSD consultant.

#### 3.2 Design & construction performance (s.40) — freeze these at massing
The building must be designed and constructed so as to:

| Duty | What to reserve in SD |
|---|---|
| **(a)** Adequate **structural strength, space, protection, access and ventilation** for safe **operation, inspection and maintenance** | Shaft clear sizes; MR / MRL volume + HVAC path; pit; headroom; rescue / maintenance access routes; structural capacity for guides & equipment |
| **(b)** **Restricted space** inaccessible except for inspection / maintenance / repair / rescue | Restrict public access to shaft + associated equipment space (Cap 123Q defines *restricted space* via Cap 618 “associated equipment or machinery”) |
| Addition after OP | Same s.40 duties apply to **lift/escalator added after completion** — retrofit is not a free pass |

#### 3.3 Mandatory notices (s.41)
| Location | Notice content |
|---|---|
| Conspicuous position on every door / access to **restricted space** | Caution: danger of entering restricted space; danger of interfering with operation |
| Conspicuous position at **every lift entrance** | Caution against using a lift when there is a fire |
| Form | **Permanent**, legible, durable materials |

### 4. Split BD vs EMSD — do not mix the gates

| Domain | Authority | Primary instrument | Architect action at SD |
|---|---|---|---|
| Building fabric around lift (shaft, pit, structure, access, ventilation, notices) | **BD** (AP / RSE) | Cap **123Q** ss.39–41; Cap 123A plan package | Show shaft, pit, overrun, MR/MRL, structural support intent on GBP / structural plans |
| Lift / escalator **as plant** (design, construction, installation, examination, use & operate) | **EMSD** | Cap **618** + General Regulation + Design Code | Appoint registered lift contractor pathway; programme Form 5 before OP |
| Fireman’s lift, FRP shaft, lobby, MOE interface | **BD + FSD** | FS Code 2011; Cap 123F MOE | Core location, lobby depth, pressurisation / smoke control coordination |
| Accessible lift dimensions | **BD** | **DMBA 2008** (+ PNAP APP-152) | Min car **1100 × 1400 mm**; door clear width **≥ 900 mm**; route entrance → lift → all floors |
| Lift-shaft GFA / SC strategy | **BD** | Cap **123F** regs 20 & 23(3)(a); PNAP **APP-89** | Extra shafts beyond baseline % may gain GFA/SC exemption — model early for domestic / office |

### 5. PNAP / Code triggers that actually move schematics

#### 5.1 APP-89 — better lift service & GFA exemption (domestic / office)
Cap 123 / Cap 123E do **not** prescribe minimum lift *service* quantity (except fireman’s + accessible lifts). APP-89 is voluntary quality + density trade:

| Use | Shaft area baseline (rough rule — check current APP-89) | Extra exemptible (cap) |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic / hotel (shafts serving those floors) | First **2.5%** of domestic/hotel GFA | Further exemption up to **3.5%** of that GFA (i.e. useful band ≈ 2.5–6%) |
| Office &lt; 10 000 m² office GFA | First **5%** of office GFA | Further up to **3%** (band ≈ 5–8%) |
| Office ≥ 10 000 m² office GFA | First **3.5%** of office GFA | Further up to **2.5%** (band ≈ 3.5–6%) |

Exclude shafts serving GFA-non-accountable storeys (e.g. refuge). Fireman’s and accessible lifts still mandatory under other controls.

#### 5.2 APP-91 — maintenance / replacement of lift installations
Shaft openings for maintenance: typically **max two doors per liftway open at once** (hoarding overall max four) — design permanent landing strategy so future lift replacement does not gut FRP.

#### 5.3 EMSD Design Code — spatial envelope (brief M&E at concept)
Freeze with lift supplier / consultant before DD:
- Pit depth, car & counterweight clearances, guide layout
- Headroom / overrun above top landing (traction vs MRL)
- Machine room vs machine-room-less footprint and ventilation / cooling
- Rated load, speed, bank size ↔ waiting-time brief (link APP-89 if chasing GFA exemption)
- Power supply, emergency supply, rescue communications

### 6. Schematic-design go / no-go gate (architect checklist)

Before freezing cores, roof profile, and GFA:

1. **Confirm statute** — Cap 123E is dead; cite Cap **123Q** ss.39–41 for building works + Cap **618**/EMSD for the lift itself.
2. **Core programme** — number of passenger / fireman’s / goods / accessible lifts; bank strategy for POP / peak wait (APP-89 if GFA chase).
3. **Shaft & restricted space** — exclusive liftway; no amenity bleed; secure access only for inspection / maintenance / rescue (s.40).
4. **Vertical envelope** — pit + top overrun + MR/MRL height locked with lift consultant **before** fixing storey heights and roof plant.
5. **Structure** — RSE owns guide reactions, pit walls, MR slabs, lifting beams — Cap 123Q Parts 3–4 + s.40.
6. **Fire** — fireman’s lift (where required), FRP shaft, lobby depths, “do not use lift in fire” notice locations (s.41 + FS Code).
7. **BFA** — DMBA car ≥ 1100 × 1400 mm, door ≥ 900 mm clear; continuous accessible route.
8. **Ventilation & M&R access** — shaft / MR ventilation and permanent access paths per Cap 123Q s.40(1)(a); coordinate External Maintenance CoP where façades meet plant.
9. **GFA / SC** — Cap 123F accountability of shafts; APP-89 exemption math if oversizing service.
10. **OP path** — EMSD Form 5 on the critical path; Cap 123A completion package does not replace Cap 618 use permit.
