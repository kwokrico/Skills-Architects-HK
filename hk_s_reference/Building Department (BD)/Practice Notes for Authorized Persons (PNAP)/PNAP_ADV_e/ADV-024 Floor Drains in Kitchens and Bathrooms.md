Floor Drains in Kitchens and Bathrooms

  Water  seal  traps  are  required  to  be  provided  for  all  sanitary  fitments,
including  floor  drains  (if  provided),  before  they  are  connected  to  a  common  drainage
stack.    The water in the trap may dry up through evaporation unless water is replenished,
from time to time, by the occupants or through discharge of waste water.

2.
To  obviate  the  need  to  top  up  the  water  in  the  trap  of  floor  drains  in
kitchens and bathrooms, authorized persons (APs) may wish to consider diverting some
of  the  waste  water  from  a  waste  fitment  to  the  U-trap  of  floor  drains  in  their  designs.
APs  are  reminded  that their designs should  comply with the provisions of the Building
(Standards  of  Sanitary  Fitments,  Plumbing,  Drainage  Works  and  Latrines)  Regulations
and would ensure a smooth flow of the discharge.    Care should also be taken to prevent
back-flowing at the floor drain.

3.
Any  provision  requiring  modification  of  the  relevant  Building  (Standards
of  Sanitary  Fitments,  Plumbing,  Drainage  Works  and  Latrines)  Regulations  may  be
considered on the merits of individual case upon application.
