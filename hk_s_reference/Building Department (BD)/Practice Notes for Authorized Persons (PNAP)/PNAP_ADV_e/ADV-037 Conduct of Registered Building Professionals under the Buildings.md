Conduct of Registered Building Professionals1 under the Buildings Ordinance

Statutory Building Control System

The Buildings Ordinance (BO) aims to provide for the planning, design,
and construction of buildings and associated works by setting therein the building safety
and health standards.  Pursuant to the powers under the BO, the Buildings Department
(BD) is responsible for enforcing these standards, and building owners are required to
appoint and seek advice from registered building professionals if they wish to carry out
building works or street works.

2.
The  registered  building  professionals  have  been  conferred  statutory
duties under the BO to supervise the carrying out of the building works or street works,
notify  BD  of  any  contravention  of  regulations  and  comply  generally  with  the  BO.
While  BD  has  been  carrying  out  curtailed  and  audit  checks  on  submissions  and  audit
checks  on  completed  building  works  and  street  works,  it  is  the  responsibility  of  the
registered building professionals to ensure that the works fully comply with the BO, its
subsidiary legislation and any other relevant laws, codes of practice and practice notes.

The  BO  and  its  subsidiary  legislation  have  made  provisions  for  BD  to
3.
regulate  building  professionals  through  various  registration  systems,  prosecution  and
disciplinary  actions.    In  particular,  pursuant  to  section  7(1A)(b)  of  the  BO,  if  a
registered  building  professional  has  been  negligent  or  has  misconducted  himself  in  a
professional way, the Building Authority (BA) may bring such matters to the notice of a
disciplinary board appointed under section 5 of the BO.  Before referring such case of
negligence  or  misconduct  to  the  disciplinary  board,  BA  will  take  into  consideration
particulars of the case including the seriousness of the negligence or misconduct for the
purpose  of determining  whether the person  deserves  censure under  section  7(1)  of the
BO.

Examples of Negligence and Misconduct

1.  Compliance with the Buildings Ordinance (BO) and its subsidiary legislation

All  registered  building  professionals  should  take  due  diligence  to  ensure
compliance with the building laws.  Typical examples of non-compliances are:

(a)   Fails to perform the required supervision and the work diverges or
deviates  in  any  material  way  from  that  shown  in  the  plan
approved  by  the  Building  Authority  (BA)  or  a  plan  relating  to
minor works;

(b)   Knowingly  misrepresents  a  material  fact  in  any  document  given

to BA; and

(c)   Undertakes professional work but is not qualified to discharge.

2.  Integrity

3.  Co-operation with the Buildings Department (BD)

Proper administration of the BO relies on the full co-operation of the stakeholders
and  BD  expects  all  registered  building  professionals  to  assist  its  work  to  ensure
compliance  with  building  safety,  health  and  environment  standards.    Typical
examples of malpractice include:

4.  Compliance with other laws and regulations

Registered  building  professionals  should  comply  with  all  relevant  laws  and
regulations  when  providing  professional  services.    Typical  examples  of  non-
compliances include:

(a)    Repeatedly fails to remind the appointed contractor to observe site
safety under the Factories and Industrial Undertakings Ordinance
(Chapter 59); and

(b)   Uses BD’s logo in the name card, letterhead or publicity materials

which violates the intellectual property rights of BD.
