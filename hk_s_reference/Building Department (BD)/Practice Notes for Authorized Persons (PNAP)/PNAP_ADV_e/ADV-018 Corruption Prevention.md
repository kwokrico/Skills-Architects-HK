Corruption Prevention

 Recently,  there  have  been

  a  growing  number  of  corruption  cases  in  the
construction  industry  involving  works  in  both  the  private  and  public  sectors.    The
Buildings  Department  is  concerned  that  the  spread  of  corruption  in  the  construction
industry  might  lead  to  building  works  failing  to  comply  with  the  Buildings  Ordinance.
The purpose of this practice note is to draw the attention of Authorized Persons (AP) and
Registered  Structural  Engineers  (RSE)  to  the  need  to  combat  corruption  in  the
construction  industry  and  the  proper  way  to  handle  situations  if  they  or  their  staff  are
offered a bribe.

APs and RSEs are required under the Buildings Ordinance to give periodic
2.
supervision and make such inspections as may be necessary to building works on site to
ensure  that  they  are  constructed  in  accordance  with  the  provisions  of  the  Buildings
Ordinance and the approved building plans.  Very often, site staff are employed to assist
in these duties.  It is important for the APs and the RSEs to expressly prohibit their site
staff  from  soliciting  or  accepting  advantages  from  contractors  and  suppliers  or  offering
advantages  to  other  people  in  relation  to  their  work.    In  this  connection,  it  is  advisable
that  APs  and  RSEs  should  develop  company  Code  of  Conduct  and  promulgate  staff
instructions on these prohibitions.  When an AP, RSE or his staff is offered a bribe, he
should report immediately to the ICAC.  The telephone number for this purpose is :

   ICAC   :   2526 6366 (24 hours)

A bribe may not be always be money.  It can be a loan, a gift, a favour, an
3.
offer of employment or any other advantage.  The full definition of the term 弌dvantage”
is  contained  in  the  Prevention  of  Bribery  Ordinance,  Cap  201.    An  extract  of  the
definition is reproduced at Appendix I.

Advisory Services Group of ICAC

The  ICAC  has  a  dedicated  Advisory  Services  Group  which  provides  free
4.
and  confidential  corruption  prevention  advice  to  private  companies.    The  Group  also
assists  companies  in  their  development  of  company  Code  of  Conduct  and  staff
instructions.  APs and RSEs interested in promoting corruption prevention practices and
developing  related  staff  instructions  in  their  companies  are  invited  to  call  the  Group
hotline Tel No. 2526 6363.
