Colouring of Plans
Regulation 14(3) of the Building (Administration) Regulations stipulates that
one copy of every plan submitted for approval should be coloured in order to clearly
differentiate existing works from proposed new works and one part of any proposed new
works from other parts.
2. For consistency in the use of colour, the preferred colours as shown in
Appendix A should be adopted. For amendments to the approved works, the proposed
works should be coloured or otherwise identified from the approved works, with identical
colouring or other means, on all sets of plans submitted to the Buildings Department.
3. The use of these recommended colours and the differentiation of proposed
works from existing / approved works on all sets of amendment plans will expedite the
processing of plans and is therefore clearly in the interests of all parties.

Notes :
(a)  Ducts, light-wells and lift shafts should be left uncoloured.
(b)  For elevations and the larger plan areas, colouring may be in lighter washes of the
preferred colours in order to avoid a garish effect e.g. concreted, tiled or plastered
areas.
(c)  A light colour wash should be used to identify phased development.
(d)  A colour legend or key should be added to all sets of drawings.
