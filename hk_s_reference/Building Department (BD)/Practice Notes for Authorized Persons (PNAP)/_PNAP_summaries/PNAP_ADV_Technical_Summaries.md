## PNAP ADV-1: Asbestos
**Status/Context:** Health / environmental control advisory (ACM ban; EPD/LD abatement control on clearance, repair and demolition)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **General rule:** Avoid use of asbestos containing material (ACM); removal of ACM from existing buildings only by registered asbestos personnel.
* **Prohibition:** Factories and Industrial Undertakings (Asbestos) Regulation (Cap 59AD) prohibits asbestos/ACM in any industrial or manufacturing process including building operations. From **4 April 2014**, import, transhipment, use and supply of asbestos and ACM banned under Air Pollution Control Ordinance (APCO) Cap 311; ACM already in existing buildings not affected by the ban.
* **When ACM present/suspected — required control measures:** (a) appoint registered asbestos consultant early for asbestos investigation; (b) samples tested by registered asbestos laboratory where needed; (c) if ACM confirmed, submit to EPD an Asbestos Abatement Plan (planned removal) and/or Operation and Maintenance Plan (deferred treatment e.g. encapsulation/enclosure); (d) registered asbestos contractor under asbestos consultant supervision; (e) **28-day** advance notice to EPD before commencement; (f) worker protection per Labour Department (LD); (g) disposal of asbestos contaminated waste per EPD.
* **AP/RSE recommended sequencing:** Complete asbestos investigation and abatement before other building activities; exclude unauthorised personnel/scavengers until in-situ ACM inventoried, photographed, labelled and segregated; asbestos consultant certifies successful completion against investigation report and abatement plan, then immediately notify EPD.
* **Low-risk exemption:** For certain low-risk readily identifiable non-friable ACM, asbestos consultant and/or contractor may be exempted — Gazette Notice 25/1997 (GN 3021 and GN 3022, 6 June 1997); list on EPD website.
* **Reference CoPs (EPD):** Handling/Transportation/Disposal of Asbestos Waste; Preparation of Asbestos Investigation Report, Management Plan and Abatement Plan; Full/Mini Containment Method; Safe Handling of Low Risk ACM; Glove Bag Method. Also LD CoP Safety and Health at Work with Asbestos; BD CoP for Demolition of Buildings; ProPECC PN 2/97.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC/PR measurement impact. Design implication: new works must not specify/import/use ACM; existing ACM drives pre-demolition / UBW clearance programme time and method.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Primary submissions for abatement are to **EPD** (Abatement Plan / O&M Plan; 28-day notice); not a substitute BD plan submission.
* Registered asbestos consultant completion certification and immediate EPD notification after abatement.
* Registers of asbestos consultants, contractors, supervisors and laboratories held by EPD Asbestos Management and Control Section; LD Occupational Safety and Health Branch advises worker H&S.
* Similar practice note issued to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Specifying or allowing ACM after APCO ban / Cap 59AD prohibition.
* Starting demolition, repair or UBW clearance before asbestos investigation / abatement completion.
* Scavenger/unauthorised access before ACM inventoried, labelled and segregated.
* Missing 28-day EPD notice or disposing ACM waste outside EPD requirements.
* Using non-registered asbestos personnel except where Gazette exemption applies.

## PNAP ADV-3: Standardization of Floor Numbering
**Status/Context:** Design / emergency-response / OP–Rating & Valuation alignment (floor nomenclature good practice with BA review on GBP)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Except G/F and roof, assign floor numbers in a logical consecutive numerical series.
* **Above ground:** ascending order (e.g. G/F, 1/F, 2/F, 3/F…).
* **Below ground:** greater number = lower basement (e.g. G/F, Basement 1, Basement 2, Basement 3…).
* Consecutive order maintained **irrespective of function** — shop, car park, clubhouse, communal podium/sky garden, refuge floor, plant room, observation deck, etc. still take numbers in the building’s consecutive series.
* **Acceptable omissions only:** “4”, “13”, and numbers ending with “4”. Any other omissions **not** allowed.
* **Not accepted:** non-numerical names alone (OK if with normal number, e.g. “20/F Sky Garden”); alias / “also known as x/F”; illogical, non-conventional or non-consecutive numbers.
* **Appendix A nomenclature examples:** B4/F–B1/F; LG/F; UG/F; G/F; CL/F (cockloft); M/F (mezzanine); numbered floors with dual labels e.g. “7/F (or 7/F Mechanical Plant Floor)”, “20/F (or 20/F Sky Garden)”, “40/F (or 40/F Refuge Floor)”; R/F for roof. Notes confirm omission of 4, 13 and numbers ending in 4.
* **On-site display (Chinese and English):** conspicuous at main entrance lobby, lift lobbies, and walls of protected lobbies / landings of required staircases on each floor. Fireman’s lift floor-served notices per MoA CoP — FSD checks before OP.
* **Implementation cut-off (PN does not apply if):** Sales Brochure available to public and copy deposited with BD by recorded delivery **before 1 September 2010**; or ≥1 unit sold before that date; or OP application submitted before that date.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC measurement change. Floor labels for car park/podium/refuge/plant/sky garden must still occupy consecutive numbers — affects sales plans, DMC schedules, emergency addressing and Rating & Valuation floor designation, not PR/GFA counting rules.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* GBP (and other plans to BA): numbering of each floor must be clear and unequivocal. If BD or other departments find sequence illogical or confusing, BD advises amendment.
* OP floor numbers will conform to approved building plans. CRV adopts approved-plan floor numbers. BD uses approved plans and OP to answer floor-nomenclature enquiries; departure from approved descriptions **not normally entertained**.
* AP must bring this to clients and conveyancing solicitors’ attention.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Skipping floors other than 4/13/*4; marketing aliases without the approved numerical label → BA advice to amend; post-approval renaming requests refused.
* Plant/refuge/sky garden floors without consecutive numbers → emergency-service inefficiency and OP/FSD notice problems.
* Floor numbers on OP/plans vs sales brochure mismatch — client/solicitor exposure; AP duty to warn early.
* Missing bilingual conspicuous lobby/stair/lift displays — FSD may withhold fire-installation acceptance path to OP.

## PNAP ADV-4: Control of Environmental Nuisance from Construction Sites
**Status/Context:** Construction-stage environmental / public-safety advisory checklist (noise, air, drainage, health, roads, hoarding); cross-ref APP-23 for hoardings

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Appendix A checklist of practical prevention measures, legislative sanctions and responsible departments. Key controlled topics and selected metrics/sanctions from the Note:
* **Noise (NCO):** Percussive piling **7:00 a.m.–7:00 p.m.** on non–public-holiday days needs construction noise permit (CNP), generally prohibited other times. Restricted hours (7:00 p.m.–7:00 a.m. or any time on general holiday including Sunday): powered mechanical equipment for general construction and/or prescribed construction works in designated (predominantly built-up) areas also need CNP. Prescribed works include erection/dismantling formwork/scaffolding; loading/unloading rubble, boards, bars, wood, scaffolding; hammering. Max fine level 6 first conviction; **$200,000** subsequent.
* **Air / construction dust:** Cover/dampen stockpiles; hard-pave and water haul roads; cover loads; wash wheels; water site; erect boundary hoarding; specified-process licence for concrete batching if required. Notifying EPD beforehand for: site formation; reclamation; demolition; tunnel works (except part beyond **100 m** from exit to open air); building foundation; building superstructure; road construction. Without notice: max **$25,000** first / **$50,000** subsequent. Without dust reduction measures: **$50,000** first; **$100,000** + 3 months imprisonment subsequent. Specified process without licence: **$200,000** + 6 months + daily **$20,000**. Open burning (APCO Open Burning Reg): **$50,000** + **$500** per 15 minutes continuance (first conviction). Demolition without fans/catch platforms/dust screens: B(Demolition)R — max **$25,000** (level 4).
* **Drains/water:** Temporary drainage with de-silting before connection to main; sand/grease traps; perimeter channels/sand bags; pH/petrol interceptor as needed; wheel wash; seal sewer/drainage connections before demolition/foundation; no illicit surface-water connection into foul sewers. WPCO licence for construction discharges; WPCO polluting matter offence max **$200,000** + 6 months first offence. Unsealed demolition connections: B(Demolition)R **$25,000** (level 4). Foreign matter into public drainage: PHMSO max **$50,000** (level 5).
* **Health:** Mosquito stagnant water — drain/pump; if not possible spray diesel weekly; cover containers; level ground; remove tyres/cans; pest control. Fly/rodent/refuse controls: covered food/refuse; daily removal; proper latrines; seal holes. Chemical waste: register as chemical waste producer; packaging/labelling/storage; licensed collectors to licensed disposal — WDO / Chemical Waste (General) Reg, max **$200,000** + 6 months.
* **Roads/footpaths/hoardings:** Secure loads; wheel/body wash; Excavation Permit Processing Manual conditions; min footpath width; lighting/signing/guarding per HyD CoP; TD agreement on temporary traffic signs; hoarding width per approved hoarding plan (cross-ref **PNAP APP-23**); B(P)R for unsound/inadequate/hazardous hoardings.
* **EIAO:** If Schedule 2 Designated Project — obtain Environmental Permit before construction/operation and comply with all EP conditions.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No permanent GFA/SC impact. Temporary site layout implications: wheel-wash, de-silting, waste sorting areas, hoarding/covered walkway geometry, and buffer for noisy plant affect site logistics and may interface with hoarding plan approvals.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* BD-linked: demolition dust/debris fans-catch platforms; sealing drainage connections; footpath damage/repair interface; hoarding plan compliance under B(P)R (see APP-23).
* Separately: EPD CNP/WPCO licence/construction dust notification/chemical waste registration; HyD Excavation Permit; TD/HKPF for traffic management. Similar PN issued to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Works in restricted hours / percussive piling without CNP.
* Superstructure/foundation/demolition without EPD construction-dust notification or without dust measures.
* Demolition without sealed drains or without fans/catch platforms/dust screens.
* Temporary toilets/kitchens discharging to stormwater — WPCO offence.
* Unsound/narrow/obstructing hoarding vs approved plan — B(P)R / Summary Offences exposure.
* Schedule 2 DP commencing without EP.

## PNAP ADV-5: Tropical Hardwood Timber
**Status/Context:** Environmental / materials advisory (rainforest timber reduction; construction method alternatives)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* HK identified as major tropical timber importer; large construction-industry consumption often discarded as C&D waste to landfill.
* **Encourage alternatives** while BOO reviews requirements that encourage hardwood use:
  * **Hoardings:** Prefer steel (reusable); ASD and HD already require steel hoardings — industry urged to follow.
  * **Covered walkways:** Question necessity, especially where buildings set back from boundary (not essential precaution on every site).
  * **Formwork (largest timber consumer):** Consider profiled steel sheeting as permanent formwork/reinforcement; precast units; structural steel frames; steel formwork; proprietary steel props instead of timber props.
  * **Internal finishes:** Non-timber proprietary wall panels; alternatives for doors, frames, skirtings, architraves (especially if painted).
* **Reuse:** Contract specs so hardwood waste separated and reused rather than dumped.
* **Renewable sources:** Where no alternative, specify hardwood from renewable sources.
* Long-term: BOO consulting industry on construction-method changes.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC rules. Design choices (precast, steel framed systems, system formwork, omit non-essential covered walkway) affect site coverage of temporary structures and permanent detailing, not accountable GFA under B(P)R 23(3).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No dedicated BD certificate under this PNAP. Material/specification choices appear in contract documents; temporary works (hoarding/walkway) still under normal BA temporary structure / APP-23 pathways where applicable.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Defaulting to single-use tropical timber hoarding/formwork without exploring reusable/permanent alternatives (policy expectation, not a quantified BO offence in this Note).
* Providing covered walkway timber consumption where setback makes walkway unnecessary.

## PNAP ADV-6: Lightning Protection for Buildings
**Status/Context:** Design safety advisory (risk assessment–based LPS for new buildings)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Consider incorporating lightning protection systems in new buildings; need and selection of measures based on risk assessment per standards such as **BS EN 62305**, **AS/NZS 1768**, **IEC 62305** (these also cover design, construction and maintenance).
* **As a general rule, LPS required for buildings that are:**
  * (a) very tall or isolated;
  * (b) serving exhibition or assembly functions;
  * (c) used for caring or education (schools, hospitals, child care centres, residential care homes for elderly / persons with disabilities, etc.);
  * (d) for public utilities or public services (footnote: includes exposed support for electric lines — Electricity Supply Regulations Cap 406A reg 11 applies);
  * (e) in open areas such as outdoor swimming pools and sports fields.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC accountability stated. Roof LPS air terminals, down conductors and bonding may affect roof plant coordination and facade interfaces.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No specific BD form mandated in this PNAP. Structural fixings/supports for LPS should follow normal structural submission practice where they form building works; risk assessment and LPS detailing per cited national/international standards.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Omitting risk assessment for tall/isolated, assembly, care/education, public utility/service, or open swimming pool/sports-field buildings.
* Ignoring Cap 406A reg 11 for exposed electric-line supports.

## PNAP ADV-8: Registration of Slopes and Retaining Walls
**Status/Context:** Geotechnical catalogue update workflow (GEO Catalogue of Slopes / SIS; AP/RSE/RGE completion documentation)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* GEO maintains Catalogue of Slopes for sizable man-made slopes and retaining walls (“features”); each feature has a GEO registration number. Definitions (Appendix A): **F** fill ≥**15°** with min fill thickness **1 m**, no associated walls; **C** cut without associated walls; **R** retaining walls supporting slope/platform <**15°** (series of sub-registrable walls on overall slope >**45°** — combine heights); **CR** / **FR** cut/fill with associated walls; **DT** disturbed terrain (landslide scar repairs / composite cut-fill; natural gradient >**15°**; individual slopes below height criteria but total height registrable).
* **Registration criteria (Appendix B):** (a) cut slopes including associated walls, and retaining walls, **>3 m** high; (b) fill slopes including associated walls **>5 m** high; (c) fill incl. associated walls **<5 m** posing direct risk to life (Consequence-to-life Category **1 or 2** per PNAP 234); (d) DT features meeting total-height registration as above.
* **Non-registrable (examples):** seawalls/marine structures (water-facing); dams/water-retaining structures (but fill embankments in front of service reservoirs should be registered); basement/screen walls forming part of building with free-air side inside; tunnel/shaft linings (free-standing portal slopes/walls register); culverts/covered nullahs (free-standing open-nullah sidewalls meeting criteria do); bridge abutments (fill embankments/reinforced fill/slopes do); all temporary features under construction (abandoned quarry faces and completed controlled tips register as cut/fill). Walls designed and constructed **monolithic with a building** not registered — advise GEO if already in catalogue.
* Provide **1:1000** site plan with location plan, record sheet (Appendix C), photographs; soft copy basic data per Appendix D structure (downloadable SIS database from hkss.ced.gov.hk). Prefer digital photos.
* Maintenance responsibility: Lands D Slope Maintenance Responsibility Information Centre / website; lease (cutting-away clauses, green hatched black areas, etc.) for land outside lot.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Feature formation/modification is site-formation / geotech scope; registrable height and consequence-to-life category drive which permanent features must enter GEO catalogue and owner maintenance/DMC records.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Present Appendix C proforma (+ soft copy / photos) **with certificate of completion** of site formation and/or feature upgrading works.
* GEO returns copy of 1:1000 plan with registered numbers of newly formed/modified features.
* AP/RSE/geotech consultants advise development owners of registered numbers for maintenance records and to include feature numbers in **DMC**.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Completing site formation/feature upgrading without submitting slope record sheet/SIS soft copy to GEO.
* Failing to register low fill walls that are Consequence-to-life Cat 1/2 (<5 m fill criteria trap).
* Misclassifying non-registrable basement walls vs freestanding registrable walls; not notifying GEO of wrongly catalogued monolithic walls.
* Owners left without feature numbers → weak DMC/maintenance trail for MBIS-era slope responsibilities.

## PNAP ADV-9: Submission of Development Progress
**Status/Context:** Administrative statistical return (biannual project progress for Government forecasting)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Complete and return Appendix A to BA in **early March** and **early September** each year for every private building project in hand, including projects where client instructions received to prepare building proposals.
* Return fields include: address, lot no., anticipated OP date (temporary or full), progress narrative, domestic type / no. of self-contained units / saleable area per unit, non-domestic type / no. of units / total internal floor area.
* **Self-contained domestic unit:** independent dwelling with separate cooking facilities and bathroom (and/or lavatory).
* **Saleable area (for this return):** floor area exclusively allocated to the unit including balconies and verandahs but excluding common areas (stairs, lift shafts, pipe ducts, lobbies, communal toilets); measured from outside of exterior enclosing walls and middle of party walls; **exclude** bay windows, yards, gardens, terraces, flat roofs, carports and the like.
* **Non-domestic units:** for floors not yet partitioned, treat each floor as one unit (example given: 5 units/floor on 1–3/F + non-partitioned 4–10/F → **22** units).
* **Internal floor area:** all enclosed space measured to internal face of enclosing external and/or party walls.
* Submit to Director of Buildings [Attn: Chief Officer/Management] by fax **2523 9380 / 2840 0451** or by post.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC concession rules. Saleable / internal floor area definitions are for statistics only — must not be confused with B(P)R GFA measurement or lease SC/PR.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Biannual Appendix A progress return only; not a plan approval or OP document.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Omitting projects at proposal-instruction stage; miscounting non-partitioned floors; using GFA instead of saleable/IFA definitions → corrupt Government forecasts (reputational; note is cooperative urge).

## PNAP ADV-10: Lift Shaft Platforms
**Status/Context:** Construction safety / LD suspension-notice risk control for temporary lift-shaft platforms; TOP interface

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Lift shaft platforms = temporary wall-supported platforms inside lift shafts under construction (rain shelter; formwork platform; mid-shaft protection; debris-catching; crash deck). Governed by Construction Site (Safety) Regulations; general duties under OSHO Cap 509 and FIUO Cap 59.
* **LD may serve suspension notices (OSHO s10)** if any of the following not complied with:
  * (a) Platform properly designed by contractor-employed AP or RSE (“Contractor’s AP/RSE”) with full details/specs;
  * (b) Construction plans/method statements for erection, alteration, maintenance, dismantling prepared by engineer TCP **T4** or above (“Contractor’s Engineer”), checked and signed-off by Contractor’s AP/RSE;
  * (c) Construction under full-time immediate supervision of competent person TCP **T1** appointed in writing by contractor; upon completion checked by Contractor’s Engineer;
  * (d) Alterations designed by Contractor’s AP/RSE; method statement by Contractor’s Engineer, checked/signed-off by Contractor’s AP/RSE;
  * (e) Risk assessment by planning team (main + subcontractor managerial/supervisory reps) with ≥1 person TCP **T4** or equivalent before erect/maintain/dismantle; plus competent person inspection of stability;
  * (f) Maintain per construction plans; clear accumulated materials regularly;
  * (g) Dismantling under full-time supervision of T1 competent person in (c);
  * (h) No materials thrown/tipped/shot onto platforms unless designed for that purpose by Contractor’s AP/RSE;
  * (i) Suitable fall-arresting systems provided and used for erection/maintenance/dismantling.
* **On-site documents for LD inspection:** design plan; construction plan + method statement; record of dates and supervising competent person for erect/maintain/dismantle; risk assessment report.
* **TOP arrangements:** plan fire safety, worker and occupant safety thoroughly at design stage; all TOP conditions (boundary plan accepted/approved by BA), precautionary measures (e.g. fire separation of adequate fire rating between occupied and construction areas) and safety features must be fulfilled/maintained at all times including during lift shaft / platform works until OP issued. CIC Guidelines on Safety of Lift Shaft Works Vol. 1 paras 9 & 12 referenced.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No permanent GFA/SC impact. TOP boundary and fire separation between occupied and construction zones can constrain phased occupation sequencing and temporary layout.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Platform design/supervision is contractor-side (Contractor’s AP/RSE) under LD regime — distinct from project appointment AP/RSE unless so employed.
* TOP boundary plan acceptance/approval by BA; TOP conditions maintained until OP. Similar PN to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Undocumented / non–TCP-qualified design or supervision of platforms → LD suspension; fatality/injury history cited in Note.
* Dumping debris onto platforms not designed as debris decks.
* TOP occupied zones without maintained fire separation during lift-shaft works.
* Confusing Building Authority project AP with Contractor’s AP/RSE duties — both may exist; suspension risk sits primarily with contractor non-compliance but TOP safety obligations remain on the approved regime.

## PNAP ADV-11: Suspended Working Platforms
**Status/Context:** Construction/maintenance safety advisory under FIU (Suspended Working Platforms) Regulation; supports ADV-14 gondola design

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Definition: scaffold/working platform suspended by lifting gear, capable of being raised/lowered by lifting appliances, including appliances, gear, counterweights, ballast, outriggers, supports, and mechanical/electrical apparatus. Regulation applies to industrial undertakings using SWP for carrying persons (generally in force from **1 July 1995**; s17 from **1 January 1996**).
* **Construction:** sound, adequately supported, properly maintained; electrical parts/motors/cables protected against accidental/environmental damage.
* **Dimensions/edge protection:** sufficient length; width at least **440 mm**; guard-rails height **900–1150 mm**; toe boards ≥**200 mm**; space between toe board and lowest guard-rail ≤**700 mm**; sufficient safe access/egress.
* **Installation:** wire ropes only for raising/lowering/suspension; suspension points at adequate horizontal distance from building face to prevent contact; counterweights on outriggers ≥**3 times** weight necessary to balance load on projecting part when platform fully loaded; at each suspension point a safety rope with automatic safety device in addition to two suspension ropes; erection/dismantling/alteration only under competent person supervision.
* **Persons:** workers ≥**18** years; certificate after training recognised by Commissioner for Labour or provided by manufacturer/local agent; wear safety harness with lanyard to independent lifeline or anchorage with fittings while on platform.
* **Test/exam:** competent person inspect before use; competent examiner load test + thorough examination before use or after substantial repair, re-erection, adjustment, failure, collapse, or weather likely affecting stability; periodical tests thereafter; most recent certificate/report displayed prominently on platform; mark SWL and max persons; unique identification marking.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact by itself. Permanent roof outriggers/tracks/counterweight systems (see ADV-14) have lease height-restriction and structural approval implications.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Regulatory regime primarily Labour Department (FIU SWP Regulation; CoP and Guidance Notes on LD website). Structural supports forming building works still need BA approval pathway when permanent (ADV-14).
* Documents (c)–(f) cited: Guide to FIU SWP Reg; CoP Safe Use and Operation of SWP; Guidance Notes on Inspection/Thorough Examination/Testing; Safety Hints — via labour.gov.hk.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Platform <440 mm wide; inadequate guard-rail/toe-board geometry; counterweight <3× balance requirement; missing safety rope/automatic device.
* Untrained workers or no independent lifeline/harness.
* Use without current competent examiner certificates displayed.
* Fatal accidents from rope/mechanism failure cited — AP designing permanent gondola must design for ADV-11-compliant use (ADV-14 cross-ref).

## PNAP ADV-12: Display of Site Information
**Status/Context:** Public liaison / temporary works advisory (site contact display; B(P)R 66(4) development info vs advertising)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Display in prominent position on site for public contact: (a) address and/or lot number; (b) names of AP, RSE and registered contractor; (c) contact telephone number(s).
* **B(P)R 66(4):** advertisement not permitted on any hoarding or covered walkway; but no objection to display of basic development information such as proposed works, use, height, number of blocks, anticipated project completion date.
* Information displayed elsewhere than hoarding/covered walkway must be within lot boundaries and not interfere with site activities.

## PNAP ADV-14: Facilities for External Inspection and Maintenance of Buildings
**Status/Context:** Design-stage permanent access / fall-protection facilities for external façades; PlanD/LandsD height-restriction treatment of gondola enclosures; DMC common-area access

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Strongly advise design-stage incorporation of permanent safe access to external walls for drainage, window cleansing, re-painting, finishes overhaul and fabric repair.
* **Permanent suspended access / gondola (strongly recommended including residential):** at roof of new buildings; comply with ADV-11 for installation/use/maintenance. CIC identified gondola as desirable safety design feature.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Structural supports/fixings of gondola system require BA approval under BO / B(C)R pathway.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Residential towers without gondola provision / roof anchorage strategy → future façade workers rely on ad-hoc scaffolds with higher fall risk.

* Soil/waste stacks in private re-entrants without common access — maintenance deadlock and illegal entry disputes; contravene Note’s common-area advice.

## PNAP ADV-15: Fixing of Reinforcement for Concrete Works
**Status/Context:** Structural construction quality advisory (corrosion/spalling/collapse prevention; AP/RSE inspection hold points)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Do not fix bar/fabric reinforcement from a batch until batch testing completed (cross-ref former PNAP 122 / current reinforcement testing PN).
* Remove loose rust, excessive flaky rust or mill-scale by wire-brushing; **do NOT use** badly corroded, damaged or scaling steel.
* Sufficient spacers, chairs and supports to maintain reinforcement location per approved plans and specified minimum cover; spacers/chairs max spacing **1.5 m**; for bars **≤20 mm** diameter, reduce spacing to avoid sagging.
* Spacers of concrete or proprietary plastic/concrete type; as small as practicable; support reinforcement + construction loads without breaking/deforming/overturning; strength/durability ≥ surrounding concrete.
* Tie sufficient intersecting/lapping bars; ends of tying wire/devices/clips must **not encroach into cover**.
* Prevent contamination/displacement from traffic over reinforcement — use independently supported planks/ladders clear of completed reinforcement; protect against subsequent trades (e.g. conduits); for **cantilevered projecting structures**, critical that top reinforcement not deflect — extra chairs and ties.
* Laps/joints only at specified positions; connectors per manufacturer recommendations and equipment.
* Free-standing reinforcement secured/braced against wind; prefabricated cages adequately supported/braced before lifting.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Cover/spacer quality affects durability of projecting structures (balconies, canopies) that may carry GFA/SC implications under other APP notes if failed and rebuilt.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Registered contractor: continuous supervision so fixing matches approved plans and this Note; reinforcement remains correct through placement/compaction.
* Contractor informs AP/RSE to **inspect all reinforcement after fixing and well in advance of concreting**; for walls/columns, inspect **before complete formwork erection** for access.
* AP/RSE provide adequate quality supervision. Similar PN to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Concreting without AP/RSE inspection of fixed reinforcement; inspecting walls/columns only after shutters closed.
* Spacers >1.5 m or inadequate for ≤20 mm bars; tie wire ends in cover zone.
* Walking/wheelbarrows on cantilever top steel displacing reinforcement → known collapse/casualty contributor cited in Note.
* Using untested batch steel or badly corroded bars.

## PNAP ADV-17: Noise Annoyance Prevention — Design of Pump Room and Ventilation System
**Status/Context:** Building services acoustic design guidelines (NCO / TM compliance; spatial buffer planning)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Pump rooms — layout preference:** separate from residential flats using non-sensitive buffer (stairwells, switch rooms). Address air-borne and structure-borne paths.
* **Air-borne (pumps):** quieter pump/motor models; if total sound power level of installed pumps **>100 dB(A)**, consider insulating capacity higher than stated minima; walls and ceilings/floors adjoining noise-sensitive receivers (residential flats, schools) minimum **Sound Transmission Class (STC) 50**; wall openings (air grilles, fan outlets) locate away from sensitive areas or treat with acoustic louvre/silencer; doors facing sensitive areas minimum **STC 25**.
* **Structure-borne (pumps):** anti-vibration mounts sized to load + flexible joints (Appendix A); pipes/hangers on spring or rubber isolators; pipes through walls with rubber sleeve and gasket; pipe sizing to avoid excessive flow velocity noise (esp. through valves).
* **Ventilation — design target:** achieve noise level **5 dB(A) below** relevant Acceptable Noise Levels in para 2 of Technical Memorandum for Assessment of Noise from Places other than Domestic Premises, Public Places or Construction Sites (under Noise Control Ordinance).
* **All ventilation systems:** appropriate capacity and quieter (usually more energy-efficient) models; isolate rotating/vibrating parts with anti-vibration mounts, flexible connections, damping and sound-absorbing lagging (Appendix B).
* **Near sensitive receivers:** locate noise sources away; assess large fans / air-cooled chillers; incorporate acoustic panels, enclosures, silencers or louvres where facing sensitive receivers.
* Reference: EPD Practical Guide for Reduction of Industrial Noise / pamphlets; NCO and TMs for maximum allowable levels from pump rooms, ventilation and associated A/C systems.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* Buffer rooms (stair/switch) between pumps and flats; thicker STC 50 partitions; acoustic enclosures; plant setbacks — consume floor space and may enlarge plant rooms seeking B(P)R 23(3)(b) disregard (must still meet APP-2/APP-151 plant criteria if claiming non-accountable GFA).
* Rooftop chiller/louvre acoustic treatments can interact with height, screening and visual constraints under OZP/lease.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No dedicated BD acoustic certificate in this PNAP. Design should demonstrate awareness of NCO TM limits; EPD guidance documents referenced. Plant room disposition should be clear on GBP for BA/other department review where relevant.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Pumps hard-abutting flats without STC 50 envelope / AV mounts — NCO annoyance complaints and remedial A&A.
* Total pump SWL >100 dB(A) with only standard insulating capacity.
* Ventilation designed only to ANL, not to the Note’s **ANL − 5 dB(A)** good-practice target.
* Air-cooled chillers facing schools/residential without silencers/enclosures.

## PNAP ADV-21: Management Framework for Disposal of Dredged/Excavated Sediment
**Status/Context:** Advisory environmental / marine disposal framework under Dumping at Sea Ordinance Cap. 466 (DASO); re-issued August 2009 categorisation (formerly PNAP 252). Applies to private projects with marine disposal of dredged/excavated sediment where mud proposals not yet agreed by Marine Fill Committee (MFC). Revision April 2007.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Scope trigger:** Reclamation, marinas, deep basement excavation where excavated sediment quantity requires marine disposal. Re-testing required if time lapse between commencement of sampling and commencement of construction works > **3 years**, unless waived by DEP.
* **Rationale for dredging:** Allocation of disposal space not considered until need for sediment removal demonstrated to Secretary of MFC (preferably at EIA stage). Volumes of **Category L** sediment < **50,000 m³** exempted from this justification requirement.
* **Dredging without justification:** (a) emergency dredging for safety / averting environmental hazards; (b) maintenance/deepening of harbour fairways, berths, anchorages, navigation channels or approaches; (c) maintenance (not construction) of watercourses, rivers, stream courses, drainage channels or outfalls. Otherwise plan assuming mud kept in place; additional consolidation time not accepted as justification.
* **Classification (Appendix A — Chemical Exceedance Levels):** Category **L** (all contaminants ≤ LCEL); Category **M** (>LCEL and ≤UCEL for any contaminant); Category **H** (>UCEL for any contaminant). Ni and TBT: exceed UCEL if greater than the listed value (LCEL=UCEL).
* **Key CEL metrics (mg/kg dry wt. unless noted):** Cd 1.5/4; Cr 80/160; Cu 65/110; Hg 0.5/1; Ni 40/40; Pb 75/110; Ag 1/2; Zn 200/270; As 12/42; Low MW PAHs 550/3160 µg/kg; High MW PAHs 1700/9600 µg/kg; Total PCBs 23/180 µg/kg; TBT 0.15/0.15 µg TBT/L interstitial water.
* **Sampling grids (Tier II):** Low contamination — **200 × 200 m**, surface only; High — **100 × 100 m**, vertical profile; Very high (near outfalls/nullahs) — **50 × 50 m**, vertical profile. Vertical sub-sample tops: seabed, **0.9 m**, **1.9 m**, **2.9 m**, then every **3 m** to dredged bottom.
* **Sample sizes (guide):** Metals/metalloid **0.5 L**; Organic **0.5 L**; Biological response **6 L**. Storage **4°C**, dark, not frozen. Max holding: chemical **2 weeks**; biological **8 weeks**.
* **Tier III biological:** Category M — one set of three toxicity tests per composite (up to **5** continuous same-category samples): 10-day amphipod; 20-day polychaete; 48–96 h larvae. Category H with any contaminant > **10× LCEL** — dilution test (sample mixed with **9** portions reference sediment). Ammonia purge if overlying water ≥ **20 mg/L** (6 volume replacements/24 h, once repeatable). Fail if fail any one of three tests (survival/dry weight/normality criteria in Table 2: amphipod <**80%** of reference; polychaete <**90%**; larvae <**80%**; p ≤ **0.05**).
* **Programme:** Sampling/testing/SQR normally ~**8 months**. Biological screening SQR ≥ **3 months** before dredging contract tender (or ≥ **2 months** before maintenance works order). DEP reliability period starts on actual sampling commencement date; **not less than 3 years**.
* **Disposal types (Appendix C):** Type 1 open sea; Type 1 dedicated sites (Cat M pass); Type 2 confined marine (e.g. East Sha Chau mud pits); Type 3 special treatment/disposal (Cat H failing dilution bioassay). Vessel sampling may be required for **5%** of vessels.
* **Small-scale waiver:** DEP may waive sampling/testing for emergency dredging or maintenance dredging < **5,000 m³** in situ.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Disposal regime and programme constraints affect basement/reclamation construction methods and cost, not plot ratio measurement.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Agree rationale with MFC Secretary → consult DEP on existing data → submit sampling/chemical testing proposal to DEP (copy MFC) → Preliminary Sediment Quality Report (PSQR) → biological screening if Cat M/certain Cat H → formal Sediment Quality Report (SQR) ≥3 months before tender.
* Disposal space: MFC allocates (DEP delegated for <50,000 m³ Cat L). Include MFC/DEP disposal requirements and Appendix C Notes (1)–(6) in contract. Private projects charged per m³ in situ certified by AP/RSE/RGE.
* Fill/disposal details via MFC electronic form, copied to BD. DASO dumping permit applied for by contractor to DEP.
* HOKLAS (or equivalent) accredited labs for chemical tests; accredited labs for biological tests.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Programme failure if SQR reliability expires before contractor dumping permit — contract must state expiry and contractor pays for reliability extension.
* Keeping mud in place assumption ignored → MFC refusal. Incomplete option/risk assessments when claiming cost justification.
* Type 3 sediment: AP/RSE must identify/agree treatment/disposal with DEP (site-specific; not prescribed).
* Trailer suction hopper disposal at East Sha Chau requires DCE-approved down-a-pipe method.
* Close supervision of sampling critical — contaminant categorisation and disposal liability rest on data quality.

## PNAP ADV-23: Improvement of Visual Appearance and Landscape Treatment for Man-made Slopes and Retaining Walls
**Status/Context:** Advisory landscape / bio-engineering guidance for man-made slopes and retaining walls (formerly PNAP 270). BA strongly recommends following guidelines for new formation, upgrading and maintenance. Revision May 2004 (para 7 added).

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Shotcrete/chunam:** Last resort only. If used, avoid over the bottom **3 m** of a slope (most visible zone). Provide toe wall planters, planter boxes, tree pits, creeper holes, colour/pigments; tree rings of sufficient size for existing trees.
* **Consequence Category 3 soil cut slopes (PNAP 234):** Generally do not use shotcrete/chunam; prefer vegetated cover in upgrading/maintenance where safety allows. Stone pitching preferred over shotcrete/chunam where aesthetic alternative needed.
* **Rock (Grade I–III):** Generally no shotcrete/chunam where joints tight and average spacing > **60 mm**. May be needed for joint spacing < **60 mm**, open apertures, or rapidly weathering rock — confirm with suitably experienced GE/engineering geologist. Provide weepholes per Geotechnical Manual for Slopes Fig. 5.4.
* **Restraint of fractured rock:** Prefer wire mesh over shotcrete where possible. Masonry facing / surface finish for large buttresses/dentition; break monotony of large uniform finishes.
* **Trees:** Preserve healthy trees; structural supports if needed (not visually obtrusive); tree felling/transplant last resort. Unhealthy/decaying trees — experienced inspection; submit inspection report to BA for record **prior to** certificate for completion of site formation works.
* **Hydroseeding:** Provide biodegradable protective fabric after hydroseeding irrespective of slope gradient. Prefer low-maintenance native species; avoid high pruning/watering demand species.
* **References:** Geotechnical Manual for Slopes (2nd Ed.); GEO Publication No. 1/2000; GEO Report No. 116; PNAP 267 / LAO PNAP 8/2002 for tree felling.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC concession. Large slopes costly and hard to blend — reduce size by layout replanning or retaining structures (not too massive/visually obtrusive). Affects site formation geometry and usable site area indirectly.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Incorporate landscape measures in formation/upgrading/maintenance; specify maintenance requirements in slope maintenance manual.
* Vegetation inspection report to BA before site formation completion certificate (para 7).
* Obtain relevant Authority approval before any tree removal (lease / legislation).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Defaulting to shotcrete/chunam on Category 3 soil cuts or visible toe zone without exploring alternatives.
* Felling trees without Government prior approval where lease/legislation require it.
* Unhealthy trees left unchecked → fall hazard; failure to record inspection before formation completion certificate.

## PNAP ADV-27: Protection of Natural Streams/Rivers from Adverse Impacts Arising from Construction Works
**Status/Context:** Advisory ecological protection aligned with ETWB TC(W) No. 5/2005 (formerly PNAP 295). First issue May 2005. Similar note issued to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Natural stream/river definition:** Natural channel with natural water from upper terrains (perennial or intermittent wet season); natural bed (bedrock/boulders/cobbles/gravel/sand/silt/clay); largely natural banks with riparian vegetation. **Excludes** flood retention ponds, fishponds, engineered stormwater systems (drainage channels, nullahs, ditches — e.g. Tin Shui Wai / Yuen Long / Tuen Mun Nullahs, engineered Shing Mun River sections, concrete U-channels).
* **EIS:** Ecologically Important Streams/Rivers — AFCD updated list. Avoid EIS impacts especially.
* **Direct impacts:** Training, filling, culverting, narrowing, widening, damming, realignment, diversion, bank shotcreting.
* **Indirect impacts:** Site formation, landfilling, silt/polluted water discharge, debris dumping nearby, foundation dewatering, geotech/demolition near streams.
* **Engineering options ascending impact (App A):** (i) natural rock bank protection; (ii) selective obstruction/vegetation clearance; (iii) enlarge one bank; (iv) both banks; (v) deepening; (vi) realign new channel; (vii) new artificial non-vegetative smooth lining (concrete) — **last resort**.
* **EIS extras:** Soft robust bank techniques; geotextile-reinforced grassed lining, bio-engineering, stonewall, gabions; leave bottom intact where practicable; translocation/migratory pathways/compensation; place similar substrates after works; do not work full width if stream wide enough; fish ladder if dams on upper breeding reaches; consult AFCD for rare species; preserve pools.
* **Construction precautions (App B):** Temporary isolation (sandbags/silt curtains with lead edge at bottom); temporary river crossings on stilts; stockpile covered and away; wastewater hierarchy minimise → reuse/recycle → treatment; hydroseed disturbed banks; lateral support without unduly impeding flood flow; site supervisory staff stationed.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. May constrain site layout, foundation/dewatering and streamside landscaping.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Comply with applicable Cap. 28, 102, 131, 132BK, 208, 354, 358 as relevant. Incorporate mitigation into contract documents. Environmental review/study recommendations to be followed where required.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Concrete lining applied as default. Wet-season works in flowing streams → bank collapse / turbid water. Works across whole EIS width without refuge flow. Failure to consult AFCD before rare-species disturbance.

## PNAP ADV-31: Building External Finishes — Wet-fixed Tiles
**Status/Context:** Strongly advised design/construction QA guidelines for wet-fixed ceramic/mosaic tiles on cement-sand rendering (formerly PNAP 303). Revisions Oct 2020 / July 2021 (Appendix A amended). Similar note to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Tile joints:** Usually **8–10 mm**, in any case **not less than 3 mm**, for grout insertion.
* **Substrate / rendering:** Prefer spatter dash within **24 hours** after formwork strike; concrete dry/shrink **6 weeks** recommended before rendering. Intermediate coats: several days (or manufacturer) before next coat; final coat dry ≥ **2 weeks** before tiling. Each rendering coat thickness **8–16 mm**; total rendering ≤ **20 mm** (or manufacturer for proprietary). Undulating substrate → consider reinforcement/expanded metal lathing anchored to concrete.
* **Tiles:** Weight controlled. Size > **0.1 m²** → secure by mechanical means. Poor water-absorption tiles: special polymer-modified adhesive per manufacturer. Ceramic water absorption: ≤ **0.5%** up to first floor height; ≤ **3%** above first floor. Cement-slurry ceramic: soak ≥ **30 minutes**, drain before tiling; dampen rendering.
* **Reinforcement (if used):** Austenitic stainless steel wire preferably **2.5 mm** dia, mesh ~**50 × 50 mm**; stainless fixings. Galvanised reinforcement not recommended externally / moisture-exposed.
* **Grout:** Preferably type **CG2** (BS EN 13888:2009). Sealants must accommodate movement, resist water and UV.
* **M-sand:** May replace river sand per DEVB TC(W) No. 8/2018.
* **Pull-off tests:** ≥ **3 samples** of each tile type for every **10 typical floors** (or part) when fixed on rendering over concrete, direct on concrete, or slurry on rendering; locations selected by AP/RSE; not from tiles over waterproofing layers. HOKLAS-accredited lab; HOKLAS Endorsed Certificate.
* **MWCS:** Certain external rendering/tiles/roof finishes may use APP-147 simplified requirements (B(MW)R Sch.1).
* **Maintenance:** Regular inspection; recommend periodic debonding survey (hammer tap ± IR thermography/void detection).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Horizontal fins subdividing panels and mechanical keys affect facade detailing / projections.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* RGBC/RMWC method statements, QA/testing procedures and pull-off proposal/acceptance/non-compliance plan before tiling. AP/RSE audit checks on contractor supervision.
* Pre-cast off-site tiling encouraged for quality control. Trade-tested workers manufacturer-trained for proprietary systems.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oversized tiles (>0.1 m²) without mechanical fixing. Total rendering >20 mm / uneven thickness → debonding. Skip pull-off sampling or sample over waterproof layers. Spatter dash delayed >24 h without remedial bonding strategy. Tile fall from height liability.

## PNAP ADV-35: Greening in Buildings
**Status/Context:** Planning/design/installation considerations for building greening (first issue June 2017; revision Sep 2020 adding para 3 MWCS). Cross-refs APP-147 MWCS; APP-117 A&A structural; CoP Dead and Imposed Loads 2011; B(C)R 8 protective barriers.

## PNAP ADV-36: Modular Integrated Construction
**Status/Context:** Streamlined MiC facilitation under BO (first issue Dec 2017; last revision Apr 2022; **this revision July 2025** — para 4 and Apps A, B & C amended; Annexes I–III of App C added). Pre-acceptance IPA mechanism; pre-submission enquiry (ADM-19) determination within **45 days**.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics

Joints/gaps (esp. drainage/envelope) prone to seepage — design against ingress. QAS covers QC tests, calibration, production process, inspection/audit frequency (independent parties = HKCAS certification body or HKIAS inspection body).
* **Supervision frequencies (Table 1):** AP stream T3 weekly; RSE stream T3 weekly; RC stream T3 weekly + T1 continuous. MiC Supervision Plan ≥14 days before production. Log books at factory + site office copy.
* **Quality audit:** AP/RSE/AS first visit in person for first batch; then ≥ **monthly** audits (quarterly in-person; intervening months in-person **or** videotelephony ≥ **480p** colour on non-rewritable DVD-ROM). Audit reports to BD within **14 days**. Alternative: first factory visit then on-site audits after delivery — notify BA in writing **1 month** before production start; sample rate ≥ **1%** of each module type (App B Annex checklist: storey height, projections, prescribed windows/APP-116, BFA, drainage, FRC open-up dry walls, waterproofing, etc.; RSE rebar open-up 3×**100×300 mm**, covermeter 6×**450×450 mm**, NDT, tensile tests 1 specimen / **40 tonnes** same cast/thickness — results within **60 days** of delivery).
* **Pre-acceptance (App C):** IPA determination **45 days**; respond to comments within **30 days** or abandoned; BD further determination **30 days**. Not a prerequisite for GBP approval. Checklist: GBP/structural/drainage for MiC portions, MoE/FRC/BFA, structural connections permanent/temporary, QAS, installation sequence, waterproofing of joints, etc. Sample drawings Annexes I–III.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact

## PNAP ADV-37: Conduct of Registered Building Professionals under the Buildings Ordinance
**Status/Context:** Professional conduct / disciplinary framing (first issue May 2018). Covers AP, RSE, RGE, Registered Inspectors and Qualified Persons.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* RBPs have statutory duties to supervise works, notify BD of regulatory contraventions, and comply generally with BO. BD uses curtailed/audit checks — full compliance responsibility remains with RBP.
* BA may refer negligence/professional misconduct to disciplinary board under BO s.7(1A)(b) after considering seriousness for censure under s.7(1).
* **App A examples:** (1) Fail material supervision / material deviation from approved or MW plans; knowingly misrepresent facts to BA; undertake work unqualified. (2) Abet UBWs or disregard MWCS; falsify documents; advertise UBW skills; undeclared financial/personal interest. (3) Repeatedly withhold required info/reports; obstruct BD investigation; ignore BD written warnings. (4) Repeatedly fail to remind contractor of Cap. 59 site safety; misuse BD logo IP.
* Reports of irregularities: to Chief Officer/Technical Services, BD — treated in strictest confidence.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial impact. Conduct failures frequently arise from false GFA/concession certifications and unauthorised works.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Due diligence on all submissions/certificates. Whistleblow irregularities confidentially to BD Tech Services.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Disciplinary exposure for knowing misrepresentation, UBW facilitation, or chronic non-response to BD. Cap. 59 reminders repeatedly omitted. Using BD logo on stationery.

## PNAP ADV-38: Innovative Building Materials and Technologies
**Status/Context:** Pre-acceptance mechanism for innovative building materials and technologies (IBMT) — building, drainage and structural (excludes innovative geotechnical — approach GEO/CEDD). First issue April 2024.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Purpose:** Curtailed assessment of non-site-specific design/construction for BO safety, health and environmental standards to give industry confidence.
* **Applicant:** Supplier/manufacturer appoints AP, RSE and/or RGE (RGE if geotech matters, e.g. new foundation system) to evaluate BO compliance and submit PAA.
* **PAA contents:** (a) App A form signed by supplier/manufacturer + appointed professionals; (b) innovation brief (use, design principle, job ref, highlight innovative content — e.g. innovative cement replacement/aggregates; or no local/international standard); (c) building/drainage/structural plans justifying BO standards or equivalent/not-inferior performance; (d) technical specs + catalogue, tech docs, accredited lab tests/assessments, factory QAS with ISO 9001 or equivalent.
* **Not applicable:** Materials/products/systems/technologies already accepted in PNAPs (e.g. MiC) or codes of practice; or already in BD Central Data Bank.
* **Processing timelines:** Determination normally within **45 days** (IPA letter or comments). Respond to all comments within **30 days** or application may be abandoned. After response, BD determination within **30 days**. Incomplete response → refuse with outstanding list (new application required). Pre-application discussion encouraged.
* **IPA letter:** States requirements/limitations; reminds need for project approval & consent; states suitability for MWCS simplified requirements or not; published on BD website with brief description.
* **Validity:** Maximum **5 years**; renew via App B form with AP/RSE/RGE as appropriate to latest BO requirements.
* **Project adoption:** Pre-acceptance ≠ exemption from prior approval/consent. Plan submissions using pre-accepted IBMT should include s.42 MOD/exemption where appropriate. Pre-acceptance **not a prerequisite** for approving plans involving IBMT. Professionals must ensure project design complies with BO, approved plans, and IPA limitations.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No automatic GFA/SC concession. IBMT may enable designs that interact with other concession PNAPs only if those PNAPs separately allow; ADV-38 is a materials pre-acceptance track only.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Form IBMT 1 (App A): 2 signed coloured building/structural/drainage plan sets + 1 set supporting docs (innovation brief, specs, calcs if applicable, BO compliance evidence, lab reports, QAS, limitations list). Renewal Form App B before 5-year expiry.
* On project GBP: apply BO s.42 MOD/exemption as needed; observe IPA limitations/MWCS suitability.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Treating IPA as project approval/consent. Using expired (>5 years) pre-acceptance without renewal. Re-applying MiC or Central Data Bank items through IBMT track (not applicable). Ignoring IPA limitations or failing to seek s.42 MOD for project-specific use.
