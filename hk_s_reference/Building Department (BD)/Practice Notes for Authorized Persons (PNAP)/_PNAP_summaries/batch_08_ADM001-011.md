## PNAP ADM-1: Practice Notes in Force
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (PNAP categorisation and dissemination framework)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Three-part PNAP categorisation (from August 2009):** (a) **Part A – Administration** — serial numbers prefixed **ADM** (BD general administration; plan approval, consent, OP and related matters); (b) **Part B – Application of the BO and Regulations** — serial numbers prefixed **APP** (how BA applies/enforces BO and regulations, and other Ordinances relating to plan approval/building control); (c) **Part C – Advisory** — serial numbers prefixed **ADV** (advisory / good-practice notes).
* **Content continuity:** Extant PNAPs re-issued with new categorisation and serial numbers; contents remain unchanged at re-issue. Notes under one part may contain information related to other parts.
* **Audience / naming:** PNAPs also circulated to RGE (post Buildings (Amendment) Ordinance 2004); renamed *Practice Note for Authorized Persons, Registered Structural Engineers and Registered Geotechnical Engineers*; abbreviation remains **PNAP**.
* **Language / format:** Chinese version published with re-issue; all new and revised PNAPs bilingual thereafter.
* **Dissemination:** Hardcopy issue ceased; PNAPs disseminated via BD website (`www.bd.gov.hk`). Footnote indexes on extant PNAPs become redundant and are deleted when revised. Appendix A list of PNAPs in force will not be updated — use BD website for current list.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Framework document for PNAP series organisation only.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
None specific to plan submission. Practitioners must use current PNAPs on BD website rather than outdated hardcopy/Appendix A lists.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Relying on obsolete printed Appendix A list or hardcopy PNAPs — risk of missing revisions/new serial numbers.
* Cross-part content: an ADM note may contain APP-relevant technical matter and vice versa — do not assume category silos.

## PNAP ADM-2: Centralised Processing of Building Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (centralised referral, processing timelines, BA16 modifications, GFA-concession disclosure)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Purpose:** BA consults all interested government departments and collates comments within time limits; BD is the focal point for private development plan issues.
* **Electronic Submission Hub (ESH):** Preferred route — instant transmission to BD and relevant departments simultaneously. Paper: failure to submit requisite number of plan sets delays referral and may delay comment receipt/transmission to AP/RSE/RGE.
* **GBP checklist:** Complete Appendix A1 before general building plan submission; structural set quantities per PNAP ADM-8. FSI proposals and lease matters: Appendices B and C.
* **Tung Chung Cable Car Route Protection Area:** Plans for proposals within the area referred to Ngong Ping 360 Limited (and others per Appendix A/I) for compatibility with cable-car safety (Appendix I).
* **Comment treatment (ADM-19 fundamental issues):** Available department comments incorporated in approval/disapproval letters. Adverse comments within BO purview that are fundamental issues (PNAP ADM-19) = disapproval items. Other comments conveyed in separate paragraphs — **not** disapproval items. Late comments transmitted by separate letter or ESH notification.
* **Arbitration:** BA decides BO-governed matters; non-BO comments → approach departments direct; divergent departmental requirements → BD organises resolution meetings.
* **Pre-submission:** Discuss specific identified uncertainties with BD before formal submission. Complex sites: presentation (with 3-D model if appropriate) after submission, at request of / agreed by relevant Chief Professional Officer.
* **Certainty procedure:** (a) On **45th day** of new building-plan submission, AP/RSE/RGE may enquire whether fundamentally acceptable and request discussion of non-conformities before disapproval letter; (b) if fundamentally unacceptable and disapproval items notified within statutory **60 days**, new disapproval items will not normally be added on subsequent resubmission of the same proposal.
* **BA16 modifications:** Each exemption/modification on Form **BA16** with special circumstances and justification; guidance for frequent applications in Appendix E; conditions in Form **BD 106** (BO s42). Mandatory BD 106 conditions include: (a) incorporate relevant modification/exemption conditions in subsequent amendment plans before B(A)R 25 completion certificate (sample tables Appendix A3 of PNAP ADV-33); (b) submit checklist of valid Forms BD 106 with B(A)R 25 certificate (sample Appendix F).
* **Adjoining owners / other enactments:** BA approval does not confer title or waive lease (BO s14(2)). Works on adjoining lots/buildings: obtain owners’ consent before commencement; consult other authorities.
* **GFA concession transparency:** BD publishes GFA concession information on website after OP. Plans must clearly specify detailed breakdown of GFA concession areas (Appendix G). Summary of concessions and carpark numbers (Appendix H sample) with OP application; uploaded after OP.
* **Structural approval/consent results (from 2 July 2024):** Result letters for structural works approval/consent issued in **electronic form** even for paper submissions — hard copy no longer posted. Structural works = ELS, GI in scheduled areas, foundations and all structural works; **excludes** A&A and railway consultation structural submissions.
* **Standard paper GBP plan quantities (Appendix A Part 2):** **2** signed coloured sets for BA; **2** sets for D of FS (**3** if Fire Safety (Commercial Premises)/(Buildings)/(Industrial Buildings) Ordinances apply); **1** for DPO; Lands — **1** urban DLO (**2** if lease has Design, Disposition and Height clause), **2** in NT.
* **Abutting street check:** Appendix A asks whether site abuts a street **≥ 4.5 m** wide (among other site-control questions).
* **Appendix E hard metrics (frequent BA16 support):**
  * Filtration plant spacing justification if > **1.5 m** from plants/walls (communal pool / recreational plant rooms).
  * Cockloft void (single-staircase shops): min **1.5 m** clearance across front; one cockloft per shop; storage only; no sanitary provisions; not at staircase landing level; access only via G/F shop; openings with protective barriers.
  * Horizontal screen/covered walkway width **> 2 m** needs need justification.
  * Fresh air (Annex 2): ancillary offices ≤30% GFA of host premises — **5 ACH**; kitchens in licensed premises — **20 ACH**; toilets non-domestic — **10 ACH**; purpose office AC — **1.1 L/s/m²** or **10 L/s/person**; hotel bathrooms — **18 L/s/room** (toilet/hotel bathroom exhaust may be from adjacent air; no recirculation of exhaust).
  * Fresh air intake (Annex 3): ≥ **5 m** from contamination sources (exhausts, traffic, car parks, refuse, cooling towers, emergency generators, plumbing vents, kitchens, toilets); not below ground / close to cooling towers; face away from pollution; rain protection + screen.
  * Openable windows for curtain-wall non-domestic commercial: openable area **≥ 1%** floor area; evenly distributed; readily openable; units capable of natural ventilation if mechanical fails.
  * Staircase natural lighting omission: permanent artificial lighting min **30 lux** + emergency lighting horizontal illuminance at floor **≥ 2 lux** (FSI CoP / D of FS / BS5266 Part 1:1988).
  * Drain fall reduction (B(SSFPDWL)R 48): substantiate min velocity **750 mm/sec**.
* **Appendix G — APP-151 overall 10% GFA concession cap:** For new building plans submitted for approval on/after **1.4.2011**, Items **2.3, 5, 6, 11, 12, 14, 15, 17B–20, 22, 25, 26 and 29** subject to **10%** overall cap (PNAP APP-151). Item **17A** (trellis / horizontal screen–covered walkway with greenery to BA satisfaction per APP-42) **not** under 10% cap; Item **17B** is under cap. Carpark L/UL summary excludes PTT (Item 32).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
Directly governs **documentation and transparency** of GFA concessions (Appendices G/H), BA16 evidence packages for common GFA/SC/PR modifications (voids, plant, JPN features, MiC, carpark disregard, bonus dedication/surrender, etc.), and APP-151 **10% overall cap** itemisation. Does not itself create new concession quantum rules — those sit in APP-2/19/40/42/89/104/108/151/161 and JPNs — but incomplete Appendix G/H/BD 106 trail can block or reverse concessions at OP.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Complete Appendix A checklist (site special-control questions; documents; referral set counts); encourage ESH.
* **Forms:** BA4, BA5, BA6 (A&A stability), BA16 (+ justifications), BA17 (temporary building); fees per APP-55; Development Schedule; geotechnical assessment (2 sets) per APP-25 where required.
* **FSD (Appendix B):** Copy covering letter to FSD (paper). Identify FSD referral sets with bold note at lower right of every plan showing FSI under BO s16(1)(b)(ii) (paper only; ESH uses designated FSD folder). Collect endorsed plans / pay Cap. 95C Reg. 3 fee; paper plans uncollected after **3 months** from FSD notification deemed unwanted and disposed of. FSD comment headings: (a) Reasons for Refusal of Certificate under BO s16(1)(b); (b) Strongly Recommended Practice; (c) Optional Nature.
* **Lease (Appendix C):** Paper — GBP with Development Schedule + Coloured Building Plans + CAD (LAO PN 3/2018) or BIM files on DVD-ROM when computer used for floor areas; print AP name/phone on plans. ESH — upload GBP + CAD/BIM for direct DLO/BPU referral (ADM-17, APP-60). Amendments — LandsD streamlined reply slip. Lease enquiries/modifications **not** via ESH — go to DLO/BPU/Railway Development Section; lease modification should precede formal plan submission.
* **OP stage:** Appendix H GFA concession + carpark summary; valid BD 106 checklist (Appendix F) with B(A)R 25 certificate; amendment plans incorporating BD 106 conditions before completion certificate.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Insufficient paper plan sets → delayed referral and late departmental comments.
* Treating non-BO departmental comments as BA disapproval (or ignoring BO-fundamental items).
* BA16 without special-circumstances justification / without Appendix E supporting docs → modification delay or refusal.
* Missing BD 106 condition incorporation on amendment plans or missing valid BD 106 checklist at B(A)R 25 → OP/concession risk.
* Missing Appendix G breakdown on plans or Appendix H summary at OP → incorrect public GFA concession publication / BA pushback.
* Assuming BA approval waives lease or adjoining-owner consent (BO s14(2)).
* FSD paper plans not collected within 3 months.
* Lease modification after formal plan submission instead of preceding it.
* APP-151 capped items not tracked → breach of 10% overall GFA concession cap.
* False certification/declaration on checklists — criminal liability (Appendix A Matters to Note).

## PNAP ADM-3: Emergency Situations — Telephone Numbers for Use Outside Office Hours
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (out-of-hours emergency contact for sites under construction)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* BD staff on **24-hour** call outside office hours for emergencies (including sites with works in progress).
* AP/RSE/RGE duty: ensure no danger to the public from works they are responsible for; timely contact can avoid major thoroughfare clearance delays (e.g. collapsed scaffolding/hoarding) and avoid Government Contractor works at owner’s expense.
* Notify BD of telephone, fax and mobile numbers for emergencies; update changes via Appendix A letter to Registration Unit (Fax 2625 0437). Numbers used by BD staff **only** for emergencies outside office hours concerning sites for which the person is AP/RSE/RGE.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Submit/update emergency contact details using Appendix A form to Registration Unit, BD Headquarters, North Tower, West Kowloon Government Offices, 11 Hoi Ting Road, Yau Ma Tei, Kowloon.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Unreachable AP/RSE/RGE in emergency → public safety risk, site clearance delay, and potential cost of Government Contractor to owner.
* Failure to update changed numbers — BD cannot contact responsible professionals out of hours.

## PNAP ADM-4: Priority
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (priority processing eligibility for new-building submissions)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Priority status for processing **new building** submissions may be granted on application for:
  * (a)(i) Urgently required public utility and institutional buildings (e.g. schools, electricity sub-stations and similar where public interest shown and urgency demonstrated);
  * (a)(ii) Hong Kong Housing Society Projects;
  * (b) Projects on lands owned by Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks Corporation (formerly Hong Kong Industrial Estates Corporation);
  * (c) Projects submitted for/on behalf of Urban Renewal Authority by a wholly owned subsidiary or JV consortium in which URA is a partner;
  * (d) Projects likely to significantly contribute to Hong Kong’s economic growth — **positive support from relevant government departments required**.
* Other high public-interest projects outside the above: exceptional circumstances only, after consultation with concerned departments.
* Application for priority should accompany the **first submission** of plans to BD.
* A&A to existing buildings: **normally not** granted priority — exception: projects designed to combat pollution (air/noise/water) or eliminate/reduce discharge of existing polluting waste and noxious/toxic effluents.
* Priority kept under constant review; may be **withdrawn** if project does not proceed expeditiously.
* Priority applies **only** to BD matters — **no bearing on land transactions**.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Apply for priority with first plan submission (tick/indicate on ADM-2 Appendix A checklist as applicable). Provide evidence of urgency/public interest and departmental support for criterion (d).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Expecting priority for ordinary A&A or private commercial schemes without fitting criteria.
* Criterion (d) without departmental support → refusal.
* Slow project progress after grant → priority withdrawal.
* Assuming LandsD/lease transaction benefits from BD priority — expressly does not apply.

## PNAP ADM-6: Computer Programs for Use in Structural and Geotechnical Design
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (prior acceptance of structural/geotechnical design software)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* AP/RSE/RGE may use computer programs for calculations/designs, but must ensure programme is **acceptable to BA before** submitting plans for approval.
* **Prior acceptance applicant:** RSE for structural programs; RGE for geotechnical. Accepted programs assigned BD reference: prefix **‘S’** (structural) / **‘G’** (geotechnical) — quote in submissions.
* **Application (Appendix A) + user manual** must include: (a) mathematical modelling; (b) flow charts of analysis/design procedure; (c) Building Regulations, codes or other requirements complied with; (d) theory/algorithm and references; (e) limitations and applicability; (f) data input/output method; (g) interpretation of results; (h) verified examples covering every part of the program at representative input values. Additional test examples may be required. Structural: **one** copy of manual; geotechnical: **two** copies.
* **Submission use:** Provide computerised-calculation info in Part I structural calculations per PNAP ADM-8 para 9(b). RSE responsible for structural program execution; RGE for geotechnical. Complete Appendix B (structural) and/or C (geotechnical) statements in the submission.
* Programs without valid BD reference number: BA may require all para 3 documentation in the submission.
* **Validity:** Prior acceptance valid up to **three years** from last acceptance date; deemed expired three years after last acceptance. Renewal not earlier than **four months** before expiry. Program changes: submit amendment details + updated manual for acceptance **before** using revised program.
* Lists of accepted programs and limitations: New Buildings Divisions Receipt and Dispatch Counter (7/F, Cityplaza 3 / 14 Taikoo Wan Road) or BD website.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Prior-accept / renew via Appendix A with manuals/flow charts.
* With design submissions: Appendices B/C program statements + ADM-8 Part I computer documentation (name, version, developer, BD ref, validity; accepted scope/limitations; model assumptions; input graphics; critical output envelopes; interpretation).
* RSE/RGE confirmations of adequacy/suitability and (geotechnical) execution responsibility.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Using software without valid BD ‘S’/‘G’ reference → may force full re-documentation mid-submission or delay approval.
* Expired acceptance (>3 years) or unapproved revised version.
* Appendices B/C missing or incomplete Part I computer data per ADM-8.
* RSE/RGE remains responsible for design adequacy — prior acceptance of program is not a design liability transfer.

## PNAP ADM-7: Geotechnical Information Unit
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (access to GEO geotechnical archive / site research)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Geotechnical Information Unit (GIU) operated by Geotechnical Engineering Office; housed in Civil Engineering Library, First Lower Ground Floor, Civil Engineering Building, Homantin. Hours: **09:00–12:30** and **14:00–16:15**, Mondays–Fridays (except public holidays).
* Holdings: (a) ground investigation reports including geophysical surveys; (b) laboratory testing of soils/rocks; (c) card index of cut slopes, fill slopes and retaining walls; (d) landslip record cards; (e) piezometric monitoring data; (f) rain gauge records. Includes Mid-levels Study (1979–1981) geology/hydrology/soil-property data.
* Retrieval via GIU Cell Referencing System on **1:5 000** survey sheets (urban) and **1:20 000** sheets (remainder of Territory).
* Also: geotechnical/geological textbooks, journals, standards; published papers on HK geology/geotechnical engineering; computerized catalogue keyword search.
* Data may be examined in reading area but **not taken out**; photocopying at nominal charge.
* Catalogue of Slopes plans/data sheets/photographs available; general enquiries to librarian (tel. **2762 5147**). Catalogue information applications (forms from District Offices/GIU): preliminary information sent in **ten days**.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Supports desk study for geotechnical-sensitive design and APP-25/GI submissions.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No BD plan-submission procedure. Use GIU for pre-design research; Catalogue requests via application form with ~10-day preliminary reply.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Designing without checking GIU/Catalogue slope and GI history where site conditions warrant — inadequate geotechnical diligence for Mid-levels / scheduled areas / slope-affected sites.
* Attempting to remove GIU materials from library (prohibited).

## PNAP ADM-8: Structural Design Information
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (mandatory presentation standard for structural plans and calculations)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Structural plans:** Properly indexed; each drawing titled and numbered.
* **First drawing — general notes / material specification:** (a) design codes and standards; (b) concrete materials (cement, aggregates, PFA, GGBS); (c) reinforcement and other structural materials; (d) grade and characteristic strength; (e) covers, corrosion protection, fire resistance; (f) construction methods, workmanship, testing.
* Drawings: key plans, floor/layout plans, sections, elevations — layout, dimensions, levels, ID of all frames/members.
* **Imposed loads:** Notes on design imposed loads (including partitions, screeds, dynamic effects) on corresponding floor/layout plans; diagrammatic illustration where loads vary across the floor. Same design imposed loads also shown on **general building plans**.
* **A&A:** Show reinforcement/steelwork details of affected existing members extracted from approved plans of those members.
* **Foundation / geotechnical-content proposals** (RW, ELS, site formation): show site/environmental features — GI boreholes, adjacent buildings/structures and foundations, lands, streets, utilities, slopes, nullahs, retaining walls, etc.; precautionary/protective measures, monitoring and contingency plans. Reference: Code of Practice for Foundations 2017; PNAP APP-18, APP-54, APP-57, ADM-15.
* **Structural calculations — two parts:**
  * **Part I** (retained by BD): synopsis (foundation/structural system anatomy of stability; design methods/assumptions/codes/stress-deformation limits); computerised design info (program name/version/developer/BD ref/validity; accepted scope and limitations; model assumptions/parameters/boundary conditions; input framing graphics with nodes/elements/fixity; summary of critical moment/shear/displacement envelopes + force compatibility check; interpretation); lateral-load design (system, modelling, member locations, sectional properties, load summary and floor distribution, principal reactions, equilibrium check at foundation and major configuration-change levels); dead/imposed principal reactions at foundation and all floors; major transfer members; cantilevered canopies/balconies/corbels/major appendages; for geo-content works — geological conditions from SI/GI, parameters, founding stratum, allowable bearing, summary assessment of effects on nearby buildings/structures/slopes/RW/land/streets/services.
  * **Part II** (returned after processing): detailed element analysis/design printouts (typically BD pre-accepted programs).
* **Novel/sophisticated design:** Pre-submission enquiry/conference per PNAP ADM-19. Without prior enquiry, proposals examined in parallel — submit **one additional set** of plans and supporting documents for BD Technical Services Unit.
* **Appendix A checklist plan quantities (paper):** Plans — **2** signed sets for approval; Part I volumes as declared; Part II volumes and/or CD/DVD; GI report **2** sets or CD/DVD; adjacent/existing building appraisal **1** set or CD/DVD; Forms BA4, BA5, BA6, BA16 as applicable. Referral extras by trigger (examples): Scheduled Areas 1/2/4 — GEO **1** plan + **1** documents; Area 3 / railway protection — MTRCL + HyD Railway Division (**2** plan sets); Area 5 — GEO + DSD (**2** plans + **1** documents); Northshore Lantau Designated Area — GEO **1+1**; slopes/RW/deep excavation/disused tunnel — GEO **1+1**; culvert/nullah/stream — GEO+DSD+HyD (**3** plans); proposed railway — RDO/HyD **1**; CLK Airport — CAD **1**; structures in/over/under/upon street — LandsD+HyD (**2**); highway structures — HyD **1+1**; seawalls — Port Works CEDD **1+1**; reclamation/piers — MD + Port Works (**2+2**); public drainage — DSD **1**; water mains — WSD **1**; monuments/graded/archaeological — AMO **1**; novel structures — TSU BD **1+1**. Checklist non-exhaustive.
* **CD/DVD Part II / Appendix B documents (pre-full ESH):** Non-rewriteable CD-ROM ISO 9660 or DVD-ROM ISO/IEC 13346:1995; cover printed with BD ref, submission title, RSE/RGE name, disc number; RSE/RGE signs each cover. Files: PDF; each page ≤ **A3**; indexed; searchable text; images ≥ **300 dpi**. Once submitted, **not replaceable and not returned**; supplements via supplementary CD/DVD. Appendix B electronic-eligible list excludes hard-copy RSE/RGE signed compliance statements: materials/testing; QA docs; QA supervision/audit reports; RGE TCP T5/DSS reports; GI/lab reports; method statements; pre-construction surveys; adjacent building appraisals; monitoring readings; site/works management docs.
* ESH preferred for plans/forms/documents (PNAP ADM-17).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No direct GFA/SC measurement rules. Imposed-load annotation must align between structural and general building plans (affects occupancy/load assumptions feeding design and related planning compliance).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Organise plans/calcs as Parts I & II; include completed signed Appendix A checklist.
* Show imposed loads on both structural and GBPs.
* Use ADM-6 accepted programs with Part I computer documentation + Appendices B/C of ADM-6.
* Prefer ESH; otherwise CD/DVD format rules for Part II and Appendix B document types; keep signed compliance statements in hard copy.
* Novel methods: ADM-19 pre-enquiry or extra TSU set.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Missing first-drawing specs, load diagrams, or A&A existing reinforcement from approved plans → processing delay/disapproval.
* Incomplete Part I (especially lateral equilibrium checks, transfer members, cantilever appendages, geo assessment) while Part II dump is voluminous.
* Novel structure without ADM-19 enquiry and without extra TSU set.
* Inadequate referral sets for scheduled areas / railway / waterfront / monuments.
* CD/DVD not signed, wrong ISO format, >A3 pages, <300 dpi, or attempt to replace disc after submission.
* Imposed loads on structural plans not matched on GBPs.

## PNAP ADM-9: Colouring of Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (B(A)R 14(3) plan colouring conventions)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **B(A)R 14(3):** One copy of every plan submitted for approval must be coloured to differentiate existing works from proposed new works, and one part of proposed new works from other parts.
* **Preferred colours (Appendix A)** — RGB / AutoCAD index (plot screening **100** full intensity):
  * Hardcore/dry fill — Putty RGB(204,178,102) / AC **43**
  * Brick — Orange Red (255,63,0) / **20**
  * Concrete slab (lighter wash) — Witch Haze (223,255,127) / **61**
  * Concrete (plain/RC) — British Racing Green (0,76,38) / **118**
  * Solid concrete blocks — Electric Blue (127,223,255) / **141**
  * Hollow concrete blocks — Purple (191,127,255) / **191**
  * Lightweight partition (e.g. plasterboard) — Macaroni and Cheese (255,191,127) / **31**
  * Plaster/cement rendering — Wild Willow (204,204,102) / **53**
  * Impermeable/non-absorbent floor or wall — Neon Pink (255,127,223) / **221**
  * Glass — Electric Blue (127,255,255) / **131**
  * Timber — Muesli (153,133,76) / **45**
  * Metal work/steel — Heliotrope (223,127,255) / **201**
  * Stone finish — Dark Grey (173,173,173) / **253**
  * Sanitary fittings — Yellow (255,255,0) / **50**
  * Demolition works / deletion of approved works — Blue (0,63,255) / **160**
  * Underline for revision — Venetian Red (204,0,51) / **242**
* **Notes:** (a) Ducts, light-wells and lift shafts **left uncoloured**; (b) elevations / large plan areas — lighter washes of preferred colours; (c) light wash to identify **phased development**; (d) colour legend/key on **all** drawing sets.
* Amendments to approved works: colour or otherwise identify proposed from approved, with **identical** colouring/means on **all** sets submitted.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Colouring clarity affects BD reading of accountable vs non-accountable works on plans.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Provide one coloured copy per B(A)R 14(3); adopt Appendix A preferred colours; include colour legend; on amendment submissions colour-identify changes consistently across all sets.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Uncoloured approval copy contrary to B(A)R 14(3).
* Inconsistent colouring between amendment plan sets → processing delay.
* Colouring ducts/light-wells/lift shafts contrary to guidance (should remain uncoloured).
* Relying on colour alone for imaging (see ADM-10 — colours not identifiable on images; need hatching/annotations).

## PNAP ADM-10: Imaging Standards for Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (drawing standards for BD electronic archival imaging)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* BD Building Record Management System stores approved plans electronically; submitted plan quality must support clear electronic imaging.
* **"Plans" for this PNAP:** building, drainage, site formation, structural plans, and calculations referred to in ADM-8 paras 8–9.
* **Non-binding on approval:** Non-conformity with these guidelines is **not** a ground for disapproval; processing remains under BO requirements. Adoption requested for archive quality.
* **Plan sizes (outside edges):** A0 **1189×841** mm; A1 **841×594**; A2 **594×420**; A3 **420×297**; A4 **297×210**. Avoid larger than A0. Border normally inside dimensions.
* **Minimum unused borders:** A0/A1 — **20 mm**; A2/A3/A4 — **10 mm** (all four sides).
* **General:** centre mark each side of drawing frame; matt surface with good contrast; unique drawing number; revisions as (Rev.A), (Rev.B) etc.; clear title (location + works type); notes read from same direction (not including dimensions); numbered or graphic scale; legend of markings/symbols preferably on right; **colours not identifiable on images** — materials/construction also identifiable by hatching/annotations (e.g. rc, br, ms); lettering/numbering not covered by heavy hatch/dark shading/dark colour.
* **Official stamp space:** unused **90 mm (W) × 150 mm (H)** at lower right corner of every plan for BA approval stamps and certified-copy certification.
* **Plan Index with OP application:** lists all approved plans; itself imaged — use "A" sizes; **separate** Plan Index per category (building, structural, drainage, etc.). Columns: BD DWG NO. (BD use) | AP/RSE DWG NO. | DESCRIPTION | APPROVAL DATE. Mark latest as-completed approved plans with asterisk (*). Additional to record-plan system.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Adopt Appendix A drawing standards for all approval plans; leave 90×150 mm stamp space; submit category-specific Plan Index with OP application marking current as-built approved sheets with *.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oversize (>A0), insufficient borders, or missing stamp space — imaging/archive problems; may impede stamping though not per se disapproval grounds.
* Colour-only differentiation without hatch/annotation — unreadable archive images.
* Incomplete/incorrect Plan Index at OP — wrong as-built record of approved sheets.
* Text/numbers buried under dark colour/hatch.

## PNAP ADM-11: Change of Address
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (B(A)R 45 registered-address notification)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **B(A)R 45:** Every AP, RSE and RGE shall notify the Building Authority, in the specified form, of any change in the address at which he carries on business, within **14 days** thereof.
* Specified form: **Form BA 24** (cf PNAP APP-60).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Submit Form BA 24 online via BD Electronic Forms Submission System (`www.bd.gov.hk`), or send to Registration Unit, BD Headquarters, North Tower, West Kowloon Government Offices, 11 Hoi Ting Road, Yau Ma Tei, Kowloon.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Failure to notify within 14 days of business-address change — breach of B(A)R 45; BD correspondence may go to obsolete address (including consent/OP/emergency communications).
