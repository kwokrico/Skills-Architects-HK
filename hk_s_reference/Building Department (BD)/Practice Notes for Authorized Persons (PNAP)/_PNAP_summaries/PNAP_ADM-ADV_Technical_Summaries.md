## PNAP ADM-1: Practice Notes in Force
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (PNAP categorisation and dissemination framework)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Three-part PNAP categorisation (from August 2009):** (a) **Part A – Administration** — serial numbers prefixed **ADM** (BD general administration; plan approval, consent, OP and related matters); (b) **Part B – Application of the BO and Regulations** — serial numbers prefixed **APP** (how BA applies/enforces BO and regulations, and other Ordinances relating to plan approval/building control); (c) **Part C – Advisory** — serial numbers prefixed **ADV** (advisory / good-practice notes).
* **Content continuity:** Extant PNAPs re-issued with new categorisation and serial numbers; contents remain unchanged at re-issue. Notes under one part may contain information related to other parts.
* **Audience / naming:** PNAPs also circulated to RGE (post Buildings (Amendment) Ordinance 2004); renamed *Practice Note for Authorized Persons, Registered Structural Engineers and Registered Geotechnical Engineers*; abbreviation remains **PNAP**.
* **Language / format:** Chinese version published with re-issue; all new and revised PNAPs bilingual thereafter.
* **Dissemination:** Hardcopy issue ceased; PNAPs disseminated via BD website (`www.bd.gov.hk`). Footnote indexes on extant PNAPs become redundant and are deleted when revised. Appendix A list of PNAPs in force will not be updated — use BD website for current list.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Framework document for PNAP series organisation only.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
None specific to plan submission. Practitioners must use current PNAPs on BD website rather than outdated hardcopy/Appendix A lists.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Relying on obsolete printed Appendix A list or hardcopy PNAPs — risk of missing revisions/new serial numbers.
* Cross-part content: an ADM note may contain APP-relevant technical matter and vice versa — do not assume category silos.

## PNAP ADM-2: Centralised Processing of Building Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (centralised referral, processing timelines, BA16 modifications, GFA-concession disclosure)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Purpose:** BA consults all interested government departments and collates comments within time limits; BD is the focal point for private development plan issues.
* **Electronic Submission Hub (ESH):** Preferred route — instant transmission to BD and relevant departments simultaneously. Paper: failure to submit requisite number of plan sets delays referral and may delay comment receipt/transmission to AP/RSE/RGE.
* **GBP checklist:** Complete Appendix A1 before general building plan submission; structural set quantities per PNAP ADM-8. FSI proposals and lease matters: Appendices B and C.
* **Tung Chung Cable Car Route Protection Area:** Plans for proposals within the area referred to Ngong Ping 360 Limited (and others per Appendix A/I) for compatibility with cable-car safety (Appendix I).
* **Comment treatment (ADM-19 fundamental issues):** Available department comments incorporated in approval/disapproval letters. Adverse comments within BO purview that are fundamental issues (PNAP ADM-19) = disapproval items. Other comments conveyed in separate paragraphs — **not** disapproval items. Late comments transmitted by separate letter or ESH notification.
* **Arbitration:** BA decides BO-governed matters; non-BO comments → approach departments direct; divergent departmental requirements → BD organises resolution meetings.
* **Pre-submission:** Discuss specific identified uncertainties with BD before formal submission. Complex sites: presentation (with 3-D model if appropriate) after submission, at request of / agreed by relevant Chief Professional Officer.
* **Certainty procedure:** (a) On **45th day** of new building-plan submission, AP/RSE/RGE may enquire whether fundamentally acceptable and request discussion of non-conformities before disapproval letter; (b) if fundamentally unacceptable and disapproval items notified within statutory **60 days**, new disapproval items will not normally be added on subsequent resubmission of the same proposal.
* **BA16 modifications:** Each exemption/modification on Form **BA16** with special circumstances and justification; guidance for frequent applications in Appendix E; conditions in Form **BD 106** (BO s42). Mandatory BD 106 conditions include: (a) incorporate relevant modification/exemption conditions in subsequent amendment plans before B(A)R 25 completion certificate (sample tables Appendix A3 of PNAP ADV-33); (b) submit checklist of valid Forms BD 106 with B(A)R 25 certificate (sample Appendix F).
* **Adjoining owners / other enactments:** BA approval does not confer title or waive lease (BO s14(2)). Works on adjoining lots/buildings: obtain owners’ consent before commencement; consult other authorities.
* **GFA concession transparency:** BD publishes GFA concession information on website after OP. Plans must clearly specify detailed breakdown of GFA concession areas (Appendix G). Summary of concessions and carpark numbers (Appendix H sample) with OP application; uploaded after OP.
* **Structural approval/consent results (from 2 July 2024):** Result letters for structural works approval/consent issued in **electronic form** even for paper submissions — hard copy no longer posted. Structural works = ELS, GI in scheduled areas, foundations and all structural works; **excludes** A&A and railway consultation structural submissions.
* **Standard paper GBP plan quantities (Appendix A Part 2):** **2** signed coloured sets for BA; **2** sets for D of FS (**3** if Fire Safety (Commercial Premises)/(Buildings)/(Industrial Buildings) Ordinances apply); **1** for DPO; Lands — **1** urban DLO (**2** if lease has Design, Disposition and Height clause), **2** in NT.
* **Abutting street check:** Appendix A asks whether site abuts a street **≥ 4.5 m** wide (among other site-control questions).
* **Appendix E hard metrics (frequent BA16 support):**
  * Filtration plant spacing justification if > **1.5 m** from plants/walls (communal pool / recreational plant rooms).
  * Cockloft void (single-staircase shops): min **1.5 m** clearance across front; one cockloft per shop; storage only; no sanitary provisions; not at staircase landing level; access only via G/F shop; openings with protective barriers.
  * Horizontal screen/covered walkway width **> 2 m** needs need justification.
  * Fresh air (Annex 2): ancillary offices ≤30% GFA of host premises — **5 ACH**; kitchens in licensed premises — **20 ACH**; toilets non-domestic — **10 ACH**; purpose office AC — **1.1 L/s/m²** or **10 L/s/person**; hotel bathrooms — **18 L/s/room** (toilet/hotel bathroom exhaust may be from adjacent air; no recirculation of exhaust).
  * Fresh air intake (Annex 3): ≥ **5 m** from contamination sources (exhausts, traffic, car parks, refuse, cooling towers, emergency generators, plumbing vents, kitchens, toilets); not below ground / close to cooling towers; face away from pollution; rain protection + screen.
  * Openable windows for curtain-wall non-domestic commercial: openable area **≥ 1%** floor area; evenly distributed; readily openable; units capable of natural ventilation if mechanical fails.
  * Staircase natural lighting omission: permanent artificial lighting min **30 lux** + emergency lighting horizontal illuminance at floor **≥ 2 lux** (FSI CoP / D of FS / BS5266 Part 1:1988).
  * Drain fall reduction (B(SSFPDWL)R 48): substantiate min velocity **750 mm/sec**.
* **Appendix G — APP-151 overall 10% GFA concession cap:** For new building plans submitted for approval on/after **1.4.2011**, Items **2.3, 5, 6, 11, 12, 14, 15, 17B–20, 22, 25, 26 and 29** subject to **10%** overall cap (PNAP APP-151). Item **17A** (trellis / horizontal screen–covered walkway with greenery to BA satisfaction per APP-42) **not** under 10% cap; Item **17B** is under cap. Carpark L/UL summary excludes PTT (Item 32).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
Directly governs **documentation and transparency** of GFA concessions (Appendices G/H), BA16 evidence packages for common GFA/SC/PR modifications (voids, plant, JPN features, MiC, carpark disregard, bonus dedication/surrender, etc.), and APP-151 **10% overall cap** itemisation. Does not itself create new concession quantum rules — those sit in APP-2/19/40/42/89/104/108/151/161 and JPNs — but incomplete Appendix G/H/BD 106 trail can block or reverse concessions at OP.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Complete Appendix A checklist (site special-control questions; documents; referral set counts); encourage ESH.
* **Forms:** BA4, BA5, BA6 (A&A stability), BA16 (+ justifications), BA17 (temporary building); fees per APP-55; Development Schedule; geotechnical assessment (2 sets) per APP-25 where required.
* **FSD (Appendix B):** Copy covering letter to FSD (paper). Identify FSD referral sets with bold note at lower right of every plan showing FSI under BO s16(1)(b)(ii) (paper only; ESH uses designated FSD folder). Collect endorsed plans / pay Cap. 95C Reg. 3 fee; paper plans uncollected after **3 months** from FSD notification deemed unwanted and disposed of. FSD comment headings: (a) Reasons for Refusal of Certificate under BO s16(1)(b); (b) Strongly Recommended Practice; (c) Optional Nature.
* **Lease (Appendix C):** Paper — GBP with Development Schedule + Coloured Building Plans + CAD (LAO PN 3/2018) or BIM files on DVD-ROM when computer used for floor areas; print AP name/phone on plans. ESH — upload GBP + CAD/BIM for direct DLO/BPU referral (ADM-17, APP-60). Amendments — LandsD streamlined reply slip. Lease enquiries/modifications **not** via ESH — go to DLO/BPU/Railway Development Section; lease modification should precede formal plan submission.
* **OP stage:** Appendix H GFA concession + carpark summary; valid BD 106 checklist (Appendix F) with B(A)R 25 certificate; amendment plans incorporating BD 106 conditions before completion certificate.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Insufficient paper plan sets → delayed referral and late departmental comments.
* Treating non-BO departmental comments as BA disapproval (or ignoring BO-fundamental items).
* BA16 without special-circumstances justification / without Appendix E supporting docs → modification delay or refusal.
* Missing BD 106 condition incorporation on amendment plans or missing valid BD 106 checklist at B(A)R 25 → OP/concession risk.
* Missing Appendix G breakdown on plans or Appendix H summary at OP → incorrect public GFA concession publication / BA pushback.
* Assuming BA approval waives lease or adjoining-owner consent (BO s14(2)).
* FSD paper plans not collected within 3 months.
* Lease modification after formal plan submission instead of preceding it.
* APP-151 capped items not tracked → breach of 10% overall GFA concession cap.
* False certification/declaration on checklists — criminal liability (Appendix A Matters to Note).

## PNAP ADM-3: Emergency Situations — Telephone Numbers for Use Outside Office Hours
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (out-of-hours emergency contact for sites under construction)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* BD staff on **24-hour** call outside office hours for emergencies (including sites with works in progress).
* AP/RSE/RGE duty: ensure no danger to the public from works they are responsible for; timely contact can avoid major thoroughfare clearance delays (e.g. collapsed scaffolding/hoarding) and avoid Government Contractor works at owner’s expense.
* Notify BD of telephone, fax and mobile numbers for emergencies; update changes via Appendix A letter to Registration Unit (Fax 2625 0437). Numbers used by BD staff **only** for emergencies outside office hours concerning sites for which the person is AP/RSE/RGE.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Submit/update emergency contact details using Appendix A form to Registration Unit, BD Headquarters, North Tower, West Kowloon Government Offices, 11 Hoi Ting Road, Yau Ma Tei, Kowloon.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Unreachable AP/RSE/RGE in emergency → public safety risk, site clearance delay, and potential cost of Government Contractor to owner.
* Failure to update changed numbers — BD cannot contact responsible professionals out of hours.

## PNAP ADM-4: Priority
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (priority processing eligibility for new-building submissions)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Priority status for processing **new building** submissions may be granted on application for:
  * (a)(i) Urgently required public utility and institutional buildings (e.g. schools, electricity sub-stations and similar where public interest shown and urgency demonstrated);
  * (a)(ii) Hong Kong Housing Society Projects;
  * (b) Projects on lands owned by Hong Kong Science and Technology Parks Corporation (formerly Hong Kong Industrial Estates Corporation);
  * (c) Projects submitted for/on behalf of Urban Renewal Authority by a wholly owned subsidiary or JV consortium in which URA is a partner;
  * (d) Projects likely to significantly contribute to Hong Kong’s economic growth — **positive support from relevant government departments required**.
* Other high public-interest projects outside the above: exceptional circumstances only, after consultation with concerned departments.
* Application for priority should accompany the **first submission** of plans to BD.
* A&A to existing buildings: **normally not** granted priority — exception: projects designed to combat pollution (air/noise/water) or eliminate/reduce discharge of existing polluting waste and noxious/toxic effluents.
* Priority kept under constant review; may be **withdrawn** if project does not proceed expeditiously.
* Priority applies **only** to BD matters — **no bearing on land transactions**.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Apply for priority with first plan submission (tick/indicate on ADM-2 Appendix A checklist as applicable). Provide evidence of urgency/public interest and departmental support for criterion (d).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Expecting priority for ordinary A&A or private commercial schemes without fitting criteria.
* Criterion (d) without departmental support → refusal.
* Slow project progress after grant → priority withdrawal.
* Assuming LandsD/lease transaction benefits from BD priority — expressly does not apply.

**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (receipt points, counter hours, plan folding)

No spatial or GFA impact.

* Unfolded or wrongly folded plans impede time-stamping and can create receipt delay disputes.

## PNAP ADM-6: Computer Programs for Use in Structural and Geotechnical Design
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (prior acceptance of structural/geotechnical design software)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* AP/RSE/RGE may use computer programs for calculations/designs, but must ensure programme is **acceptable to BA before** submitting plans for approval.
* **Prior acceptance applicant:** RSE for structural programs; RGE for geotechnical. Accepted programs assigned BD reference: prefix **‘S’** (structural) / **‘G’** (geotechnical) — quote in submissions.
* **Application (Appendix A) + user manual** must include: (a) mathematical modelling; (b) flow charts of analysis/design procedure; (c) Building Regulations, codes or other requirements complied with; (d) theory/algorithm and references; (e) limitations and applicability; (f) data input/output method; (g) interpretation of results; (h) verified examples covering every part of the program at representative input values. Additional test examples may be required. Structural: **one** copy of manual; geotechnical: **two** copies.
* **Submission use:** Provide computerised-calculation info in Part I structural calculations per PNAP ADM-8 para 9(b). RSE responsible for structural program execution; RGE for geotechnical. Complete Appendix B (structural) and/or C (geotechnical) statements in the submission.
* Programs without valid BD reference number: BA may require all para 3 documentation in the submission.
* **Validity:** Prior acceptance valid up to **three years** from last acceptance date; deemed expired three years after last acceptance. Renewal not earlier than **four months** before expiry. Program changes: submit amendment details + updated manual for acceptance **before** using revised program.
* Lists of accepted programs and limitations: New Buildings Divisions Receipt and Dispatch Counter (7/F, Cityplaza 3 / 14 Taikoo Wan Road) or BD website.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Prior-accept / renew via Appendix A with manuals/flow charts.
* With design submissions: Appendices B/C program statements + ADM-8 Part I computer documentation (name, version, developer, BD ref, validity; accepted scope/limitations; model assumptions; input graphics; critical output envelopes; interpretation).
* RSE/RGE confirmations of adequacy/suitability and (geotechnical) execution responsibility.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Using software without valid BD ‘S’/‘G’ reference → may force full re-documentation mid-submission or delay approval.
* Expired acceptance (>3 years) or unapproved revised version.
* Appendices B/C missing or incomplete Part I computer data per ADM-8.
* RSE/RGE remains responsible for design adequacy — prior acceptance of program is not a design liability transfer.

## PNAP ADM-7: Geotechnical Information Unit
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (access to GEO geotechnical archive / site research)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Geotechnical Information Unit (GIU) operated by Geotechnical Engineering Office; housed in Civil Engineering Library, First Lower Ground Floor, Civil Engineering Building, Homantin. Hours: **09:00–12:30** and **14:00–16:15**, Mondays–Fridays (except public holidays).
* Holdings: (a) ground investigation reports including geophysical surveys; (b) laboratory testing of soils/rocks; (c) card index of cut slopes, fill slopes and retaining walls; (d) landslip record cards; (e) piezometric monitoring data; (f) rain gauge records. Includes Mid-levels Study (1979–1981) geology/hydrology/soil-property data.
* Retrieval via GIU Cell Referencing System on **1:5 000** survey sheets (urban) and **1:20 000** sheets (remainder of Territory).
* Also: geotechnical/geological textbooks, journals, standards; published papers on HK geology/geotechnical engineering; computerized catalogue keyword search.
* Data may be examined in reading area but **not taken out**; photocopying at nominal charge.
* Catalogue of Slopes plans/data sheets/photographs available; general enquiries to librarian (tel. **2762 5147**). Catalogue information applications (forms from District Offices/GIU): preliminary information sent in **ten days**.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Supports desk study for geotechnical-sensitive design and APP-25/GI submissions.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No BD plan-submission procedure. Use GIU for pre-design research; Catalogue requests via application form with ~10-day preliminary reply.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Designing without checking GIU/Catalogue slope and GI history where site conditions warrant — inadequate geotechnical diligence for Mid-levels / scheduled areas / slope-affected sites.
* Attempting to remove GIU materials from library (prohibited).

## PNAP ADM-8: Structural Design Information
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (mandatory presentation standard for structural plans and calculations)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Structural plans:** Properly indexed; each drawing titled and numbered.
* **First drawing — general notes / material specification:** (a) design codes and standards; (b) concrete materials (cement, aggregates, PFA, GGBS); (c) reinforcement and other structural materials; (d) grade and characteristic strength; (e) covers, corrosion protection, fire resistance; (f) construction methods, workmanship, testing.
* Drawings: key plans, floor/layout plans, sections, elevations — layout, dimensions, levels, ID of all frames/members.
* **Imposed loads:** Notes on design imposed loads (including partitions, screeds, dynamic effects) on corresponding floor/layout plans; diagrammatic illustration where loads vary across the floor. Same design imposed loads also shown on **general building plans**.
* **A&A:** Show reinforcement/steelwork details of affected existing members extracted from approved plans of those members.
* **Foundation / geotechnical-content proposals** (RW, ELS, site formation): show site/environmental features — GI boreholes, adjacent buildings/structures and foundations, lands, streets, utilities, slopes, nullahs, retaining walls, etc.; precautionary/protective measures, monitoring and contingency plans. Reference: Code of Practice for Foundations 2017; PNAP APP-18, APP-54, APP-57, ADM-15.
* **Structural calculations — two parts:**
  * **Part I** (retained by BD): synopsis (foundation/structural system anatomy of stability; design methods/assumptions/codes/stress-deformation limits); computerised design info (program name/version/developer/BD ref/validity; accepted scope and limitations; model assumptions/parameters/boundary conditions; input framing graphics with nodes/elements/fixity; summary of critical moment/shear/displacement envelopes + force compatibility check; interpretation); lateral-load design (system, modelling, member locations, sectional properties, load summary and floor distribution, principal reactions, equilibrium check at foundation and major configuration-change levels); dead/imposed principal reactions at foundation and all floors; major transfer members; cantilevered canopies/balconies/corbels/major appendages; for geo-content works — geological conditions from SI/GI, parameters, founding stratum, allowable bearing, summary assessment of effects on nearby buildings/structures/slopes/RW/land/streets/services.
  * **Part II** (returned after processing): detailed element analysis/design printouts (typically BD pre-accepted programs).
* **Novel/sophisticated design:** Pre-submission enquiry/conference per PNAP ADM-19. Without prior enquiry, proposals examined in parallel — submit **one additional set** of plans and supporting documents for BD Technical Services Unit.
* **Appendix A checklist plan quantities (paper):** Plans — **2** signed sets for approval; Part I volumes as declared; Part II volumes and/or CD/DVD; GI report **2** sets or CD/DVD; adjacent/existing building appraisal **1** set or CD/DVD; Forms BA4, BA5, BA6, BA16 as applicable. Referral extras by trigger (examples): Scheduled Areas 1/2/4 — GEO **1** plan + **1** documents; Area 3 / railway protection — MTRCL + HyD Railway Division (**2** plan sets); Area 5 — GEO + DSD (**2** plans + **1** documents); Northshore Lantau Designated Area — GEO **1+1**; slopes/RW/deep excavation/disused tunnel — GEO **1+1**; culvert/nullah/stream — GEO+DSD+HyD (**3** plans); proposed railway — RDO/HyD **1**; CLK Airport — CAD **1**; structures in/over/under/upon street — LandsD+HyD (**2**); highway structures — HyD **1+1**; seawalls — Port Works CEDD **1+1**; reclamation/piers — MD + Port Works (**2+2**); public drainage — DSD **1**; water mains — WSD **1**; monuments/graded/archaeological — AMO **1**; novel structures — TSU BD **1+1**. Checklist non-exhaustive.
* **CD/DVD Part II / Appendix B documents (pre-full ESH):** Non-rewriteable CD-ROM ISO 9660 or DVD-ROM ISO/IEC 13346:1995; cover printed with BD ref, submission title, RSE/RGE name, disc number; RSE/RGE signs each cover. Files: PDF; each page ≤ **A3**; indexed; searchable text; images ≥ **300 dpi**. Once submitted, **not replaceable and not returned**; supplements via supplementary CD/DVD. Appendix B electronic-eligible list excludes hard-copy RSE/RGE signed compliance statements: materials/testing; QA docs; QA supervision/audit reports; RGE TCP T5/DSS reports; GI/lab reports; method statements; pre-construction surveys; adjacent building appraisals; monitoring readings; site/works management docs.
* ESH preferred for plans/forms/documents (PNAP ADM-17).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No direct GFA/SC measurement rules. Imposed-load annotation must align between structural and general building plans (affects occupancy/load assumptions feeding design and related planning compliance).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Organise plans/calcs as Parts I & II; include completed signed Appendix A checklist.
* Show imposed loads on both structural and GBPs.
* Use ADM-6 accepted programs with Part I computer documentation + Appendices B/C of ADM-6.
* Prefer ESH; otherwise CD/DVD format rules for Part II and Appendix B document types; keep signed compliance statements in hard copy.
* Novel methods: ADM-19 pre-enquiry or extra TSU set.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Missing first-drawing specs, load diagrams, or A&A existing reinforcement from approved plans → processing delay/disapproval.
* Incomplete Part I (especially lateral equilibrium checks, transfer members, cantilever appendages, geo assessment) while Part II dump is voluminous.
* Novel structure without ADM-19 enquiry and without extra TSU set.
* Inadequate referral sets for scheduled areas / railway / waterfront / monuments.
* CD/DVD not signed, wrong ISO format, >A3 pages, <300 dpi, or attempt to replace disc after submission.
* Imposed loads on structural plans not matched on GBPs.

## PNAP ADM-9: Colouring of Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (B(A)R 14(3) plan colouring conventions)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **B(A)R 14(3):** One copy of every plan submitted for approval must be coloured to differentiate existing works from proposed new works, and one part of proposed new works from other parts.
* **Preferred colours (Appendix A)** — RGB / AutoCAD index (plot screening **100** full intensity):
  * Hardcore/dry fill — Putty RGB(204,178,102) / AC **43**
  * Brick — Orange Red (255,63,0) / **20**
  * Concrete slab (lighter wash) — Witch Haze (223,255,127) / **61**
  * Concrete (plain/RC) — British Racing Green (0,76,38) / **118**
  * Solid concrete blocks — Electric Blue (127,223,255) / **141**
  * Hollow concrete blocks — Purple (191,127,255) / **191**
  * Lightweight partition (e.g. plasterboard) — Macaroni and Cheese (255,191,127) / **31**
  * Plaster/cement rendering — Wild Willow (204,204,102) / **53**
  * Impermeable/non-absorbent floor or wall — Neon Pink (255,127,223) / **221**
  * Glass — Electric Blue (127,255,255) / **131**
  * Timber — Muesli (153,133,76) / **45**
  * Metal work/steel — Heliotrope (223,127,255) / **201**
  * Stone finish — Dark Grey (173,173,173) / **253**
  * Sanitary fittings — Yellow (255,255,0) / **50**
  * Demolition works / deletion of approved works — Blue (0,63,255) / **160**
  * Underline for revision — Venetian Red (204,0,51) / **242**
* **Notes:** (a) Ducts, light-wells and lift shafts **left uncoloured**; (b) elevations / large plan areas — lighter washes of preferred colours; (c) light wash to identify **phased development**; (d) colour legend/key on **all** drawing sets.
* Amendments to approved works: colour or otherwise identify proposed from approved, with **identical** colouring/means on **all** sets submitted.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Colouring clarity affects BD reading of accountable vs non-accountable works on plans.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Provide one coloured copy per B(A)R 14(3); adopt Appendix A preferred colours; include colour legend; on amendment submissions colour-identify changes consistently across all sets.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Uncoloured approval copy contrary to B(A)R 14(3).
* Inconsistent colouring between amendment plan sets → processing delay.
* Colouring ducts/light-wells/lift shafts contrary to guidance (should remain uncoloured).
* Relying on colour alone for imaging (see ADM-10 — colours not identifiable on images; need hatching/annotations).

## PNAP ADM-10: Imaging Standards for Plans
**Status/Context:** Administrative workflow (drawing standards for BD electronic archival imaging)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* BD Building Record Management System stores approved plans electronically; submitted plan quality must support clear electronic imaging.
* **"Plans" for this PNAP:** building, drainage, site formation, structural plans, and calculations referred to in ADM-8 paras 8–9.
* **Non-binding on approval:** Non-conformity with these guidelines is **not** a ground for disapproval; processing remains under BO requirements. Adoption requested for archive quality.
* **Plan sizes (outside edges):** A0 **1189×841** mm; A1 **841×594**; A2 **594×420**; A3 **420×297**; A4 **297×210**. Avoid larger than A0. Border normally inside dimensions.
* **Minimum unused borders:** A0/A1 — **20 mm**; A2/A3/A4 — **10 mm** (all four sides).
* **General:** centre mark each side of drawing frame; matt surface with good contrast; unique drawing number; revisions as (Rev.A), (Rev.B) etc.; clear title (location + works type); notes read from same direction (not including dimensions); numbered or graphic scale; legend of markings/symbols preferably on right; **colours not identifiable on images** — materials/construction also identifiable by hatching/annotations (e.g. rc, br, ms); lettering/numbering not covered by heavy hatch/dark shading/dark colour.
* **Official stamp space:** unused **90 mm (W) × 150 mm (H)** at lower right corner of every plan for BA approval stamps and certified-copy certification.
* **Plan Index with OP application:** lists all approved plans; itself imaged — use "A" sizes; **separate** Plan Index per category (building, structural, drainage, etc.). Columns: BD DWG NO. (BD use) | AP/RSE DWG NO. | DESCRIPTION | APPROVAL DATE. Mark latest as-completed approved plans with asterisk (*). Additional to record-plan system.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Adopt Appendix A drawing standards for all approval plans; leave 90×150 mm stamp space; submit category-specific Plan Index with OP application marking current as-built approved sheets with *.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oversize (>A0), insufficient borders, or missing stamp space — imaging/archive problems; may impede stamping though not per se disapproval grounds.
* Colour-only differentiation without hatch/annotation — unreadable archive images.
* Incomplete/incorrect Plan Index at OP — wrong as-built record of approved sheets.
* Text/numbers buried under dark colour/hatch.

## PNAP ADM-13: Monitoring for Site Safety and Quality
**Status/Context:** Administrative / site safety audit workflow (Site Monitoring Section programme; previously PNAP 186; this revision December 2010)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **SMS mission:** Site Monitoring Section (SMS) conducts systematic inspections to enhance safety and quality on construction and demolition sites, focusing on unsatisfactory aspects with public safety/quality interface.
* **Matters of concern (a–h):** (a) material divergence/deviation from approved plans; (b) defective materials; (c) absence of adequate precautionary measures; (d) lack of supervision by responsible persons; (e) departure from approved/agreed work sequence or procedures; (f) inadequate experience of workmen/plant operators for specially hazardous operations; (g) non-compliance with BA conditions or orders; (h) contravention of BO or subsidiary legislation.
* **Higher-frequency inspection triggers:** Demolition; deep excavations; percussion piling; steep slope formation; high retaining walls; works projecting over public thoroughfares; operation of heavy powered mechanical plant.
* **Warrant powers:** SMS officers carry warrant authorising: inspect any premises / enter any land; ascertain BO compliance; carry out tests, make openings, take reasonable samples for BO purposes.
* **AP/RSE/RGE co-operation duties:** Ready site access for SMS; issue approved plans, agreed sequences/procedures and BA conditions to registered contractors for keeping on site; for minor works — issue prescribed plans, works details and supervision plans to prescribed registered contractors on site; explain sequences/conditions/orders to contractors (with translations if needed); meet supervision requirements for building/demolition works; provide adequate precautionary measures (especially falling objects); notify BA ASAP of any contravention from works; rectify unsatisfactory aspects without delay.
* **Minor works (simplified requirements):** Selected cases under construction subject to audit site safety inspections (not full systematic approved-plan programme); matters of concern, remedies/sanctions, co-operation and interface generally apply as circumstances warrant.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial or GFA impact. Regulates site monitoring/audit interface, not SC/PR/GFA measurement.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* No new plan-submission procedure; SMS is an inspection/sanction interface parallel to New Buildings Division’s approval, consent, permits, licences, OP and performance-test functions.
* Contraventions outside BO ambit: SMS refers to appropriate government department.
* Similar practice note issued to registered contractors; AP should bring note to clients’ and RCs’ attention.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Material deviation from approved plans, missing precautionary measures, or inadequate supervision → SMS corrective advice; BO s23 / s24A orders, prosecution and disciplinary action considered case-by-case.
* Failure to keep approved plans / sequences / conditions on site, or to notify BA of contraventions promptly → elevated SMS enforcement risk.
* Hazardous-stage works without intensified supervision increase inspection frequency and sanction exposure.

## PNAP ADM-14: Minor Amendments to Plans and Specified Forms
**Status/Context:** Administrative plan-processing facilitation (previously PNAP 190; this revision June 2024 — ESH “Revised Submission” added)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Trigger:** Plans considered approvable under BO subject to minor amendments — AP/RSE/RGE may amend plans and relevant specified forms with BD’s prior agreement.
* **Four amendment routes:**
  * **(a) Amend in BD:** Usual interview with design team; AP/RSE/RGE may amend plans/forms in BD and authenticate by signature. For specialist/proprietary design (glazing, cladding, curtain wall, foundation, hoarding), AP/RSE/RGE or competent representative understanding the entire project encouraged to attend with specialist for interface resolution.
  * **(b) Substitute sheets/forms:** Hand in substitute sheets with all amendments clearly annotated and individually authenticated; first agree with case officer; substitute sheets to reach officer on agreed date — **not more than 2 working days** from approach to case officer. Specified forms may be replaced by substitute ones with prior case-officer agreement.
  * **(c) Take away part of submission:** Written request specifying plan/form reference or number to be removed; must contain undertaking to return amended plans/forms in **not more than 2 working days**; precise return date agreed beforehand; returned items clearly annotated and individually authenticated.
  * **(d) ESH Revised Submission:** Submit amended plans, forms and/or documents under ESH “Revised Submission” to supersede/supplement electronic submissions; amendments and deadline first agreed with case officer (as for paper).
* **Late return / non-submission:** For (b), (c), (d) — if not returned/submitted by agreed date, submission may be disapproved under BO **s16(1)(i)** and/or **s16(1)(c)** (prescribed plans / required specified forms not submitted).
* **Lease-implication amendments:** Paper — extra copy of plans with amendments annotated/coloured + Development Schedule indicating alterations/changes and extent of lease compliance for LandsD; examples include GFA exclusion of floor space, conversion of GFA-accountable floor space into hotel back-of-house. Electronic — amendments + Development Schedule via ESH for direct referral to relevant District Lands Officer.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
Does not redefine GFA/SC metrics, but lease-sensitive minor amendments expressly include **GFA exclusion** and **GFA-accountable → back-of-house conversion** — these require LandsD-bound annotated plans and Development Schedule; incomplete LandsD package can stall approval even if BO plan is otherwise minor-amendable.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Prior case-officer agreement mandatory for substitute / take-away / ESH revised paths; in-BD amendments authenticated by AP/RSE/RGE signature.
* 2-working-day return clock is critical; abuse or operational difficulties → BD may modify or cancel the facility.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Missing agreed-date return → s16(1)(i)/(c) disapproval risk.
* Lease-GFA amendments without coloured/annotated extra copy + Development Schedule (or ESH equivalent) → LandsD referral gap.
* Specialist works interviews without whole-project competent representative → unresolved interface queries and delay.

## PNAP ADM-15: Submission of Site Formation Proposals
**Status/Context:** Administrative split-submission workflow for site formation (previously PNAP 200; this revision February 2012 — para 7 on piling/monitoring added)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Split submission permitted:**
  * **Part A — Site formation details:** Layout and details of slope works and associated drainage; general layout and size of earth retaining structures (if any) per **B(A)R 8(1)(bb)**.
  * **Part B — Structural details:** Structural details of earth retaining structures.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC measurement rules. Site formation layout (slopes, ERS size/layout, drainage) constrains developable platform and temporary works envelope feeding later GBP density checks.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Enough sets of plans with essential information for consultation with government departments/authorities — cross-ref **PNAP ADM-2, ADM-8, APP-141**.
* Parallel processing of Parts A and B intended to expedite and delineate responsibilities.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Submitting Part B before soil-structure interaction can be prescribed → abortive/disapproval risk.
* Omitting APP-18 / APP-137 monitoring when piling is in the site formation package.
* Unclear Part A vs Part B marking or wrong AP/RGE vs RSE signing → responsibility and B(A)R 12 exposure.

## PNAP ADM-17: Submission of Plans and Documents in Electronic Format
**Status/Context:** Electronic Transactions Ordinance (Cap. 553) interface with BO submissions (previously PNAP 240; this revision May 2026 — RMWMS added; ESH Stages 1–3 complete)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Legal basis:** ETO s5 (electronic record usable for subsequent reference = writing); ETO s6 (digital signature with recognised certificate within validity = signature). Provisions operative since **7 April 2000**.
* **Designated BD systems:**
  * **ESH** https://esh.bd.gov.hk — plan submissions (24/7 except maintenance/downtime); digital certificate (personal) or **iAM Smart**.
  * **e-Counter** receipt@bd.gov.hk — total email ≤ **25 MB**.
  * **EFSS** — certain specified forms; total size ≤ **25 MB**; digital signatures.
  * **RMWMS** https://rmwms.bd.gov.hk — certain specified forms + supporting docs; launched **29 May 2026**; file size per form ≤ **25 MB**; Hongkong Post / Digi-Sign / iAM Smart.
* **ESH stages (Appendix A):** Stage 1 (**30 Jun 2022**) — above-ground structural plans without cross-dept referral (A&A excluded); Stage 2 (**31 Mar 2023**) — hoarding/walkway/gantry, demolition, drainage, ELS, GI, foundation, site formation, all structural (A&A excluded); Stage 3 (**30 Jun 2024**) — all plan types including GBP and A&A + related applications.
* **Volume:** Electronic = one set only; BA may still require additional hard copies under **B(A)R 11(2)**; BA may reply in hard copy.
* **ESH plan files (Appendix B):** PDF without security; **one drawing per file**; filename ≤ **50** alphanumeric; file ≤ **25 MB**; digital sign via ESH tool; paper size per ADM-10, landscape; unused stamp space **90 mm × 150 mm** lower right; native CAD/BIM PDF preferred, else scan ≥ **300 dpi**, 24-bit colour; no multiple layers/post-edited mark-up. Other documents: single-layer PDF, ≤ **500 MB**, stamp space **100 mm × 65 mm** front page. CAD/BIM supplementary ≤ **500 MB** each — see ADM-19 / ADV-34.
* **Electronic plan index (Appendix C):** Online; drawing title / number / revision each ≤ 50 characters; auto-compile if title block captions “Drawing Title:”, “Drawing Number:”, “Revision Number:” in machine-readable text, separate rows.
* **e-Counter formats:** Specified forms PDF with digital signature at specified locations; other records PDF (each page ≤ A3) or TIFF/JPEG at **200 dpi** with adjacent sides ≤ **3300 × 2340** pixels, 24-bit colour; each document digitally signed; total email ≤ 25 MB.
* **Statutory time:** Same as paper for ESH — starts **next day** after receipt day (even if public holiday / gale / black rainstorm). Example: Fri/Sat 23:59 → Sunday start; Sunday 00:00 → Monday start.
* **BA10 modification:** Digitally signed BA10 via ESH — BD favourably considers modification of **B(A)R 20(1)** to allow notification **not less than 1 day** before commencement (vs normal longer notice); may apply with consent. Condition: BA10 must be via ESH. Still remember **B(A)R 20(2)/(3)** — TCP appointment info + supervision plan **≥ 7 days** before commencement.
* **Paperless continuity:** After ESH first submission, subsequent amendments should continue via ESH.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No concession formulae. Electronic GFA/SC verification rules for CAD/BIM are under **ADM-19** Appendices E/F (cross-referenced for CAD/BIM supplementary via ESH).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Identity authenticated by digital certificate of Postmaster General or other recognised certification authority; failure to observe gazette format/manner/procedure **invalidates** submission.
* Using ESH = accept ESH as designated system for receiving BD electronic records (ETO s19(2)(a)(i)).
* Equipment for electronic records on site and production to BA under **B(A)R 36 & 40**.
* EFSS / RMWMS details also under **PNAP APP-60**.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Wrong format, oversized files, missing digital signatures, or multi-layer PDFs → invalid submission / clock not running as expected.
* Assuming ESH BA10 1-day modification also waives TCP/supervision-plan 7-day rule → B(A)R 20(2)/(3) breach.
* Mixing paper amendments after ESH start → tracking/processing failure; keep amendments on ESH.
* Inability to produce electronic records on site under regs 36/40.

## PNAP ADM-18: Site Auditing for Building Works
**Status/Context:** Strategic BO s22 site audit of foundation and superstructure (previously PNAP 254; this revision April 2018 — para 11 two-storey warehouse audits amended)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Strategy features:** Expand audits to detailed foundation and superstructure checking; performance-based frequency; remove predictability; separate submission-checking from auditing staff; rotate auditors; investigative focus on structural safety and integrity.
* **Performance-based frequency:** Initial visit rates site conditions, complexity, RC organisation, AP/RSE/RGE/RC supervisory attendance, documentation completeness, works quality → sets subsequent frequency; anomalies / substandard works → earlier next audit. Auditor discretion on % and components inspected.
* **Appendix II timing baseline (performance-based flowchart):** Initial auditing — foundation **1 month** after piling commencement; superstructure **3 months** after S/S commencement. Satisfactory performance → less frequent (e.g. foundation **2 months** / S/S **4 months**); unsatisfactory → more frequent (e.g. foundation **1 month** / S/S **2 months**). Continues until completion of all structural components.
* **Appendix I audit scope (selected):** Driven piles — mill certificates (proof yield); spun pile origin/dimensions; welding electrode grade; RSE/RGE/RSC presence per Site Supervision Plan; hammer final set/founding stratum; GI vs as-built consistency. Excavation piles — concrete/grout/steel grades; depth/diameter/bell-out; founding vs pre-drill. Spread footing — bearing stratum suitability; dimensions. Superstructure — hammer/tensile tests on RC; coring of in-situ concrete; transfer plate falsework acceptance + concreting sequence; prestress profile/grout vents; cantilever canopy CJ/cover; curtain/glass wall member size/grade and cast-in anchors.
* **Two-storey warehouse fast-track interface (para 11 / ADM-19):** Foundation bearing stratum inspection within **1 working day (8 working hours)** of notification. Scheduled quality + comprehensive S/S stage inspections combined into one joint inspection. Small warehouses with GFA **≤ 1,500 m²** — generally **one** unscheduled audit only unless irregularities warrant more.
* **Minor works:** Selected under-construction cases audited; Appendix I items (e.g. spread footing, RC, in-situ concrete, cantilever canopy) remain relevant.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC rules. Warehouse GFA ≤ 1,500 m² only affects BD’s reduced audit intensity for low-risk small warehouses under ADM-19 fast track.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* No advance appointment of audit date/time; completed or in-progress sites liable to audit; no fixed % of structural units pre-set.
* Anomaly investigation prioritises structural safety/integrity and frequent recurrence; extent/seriousness and inadvertence assessed.
* Parallel practice note to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Gaps in Site Supervision Plan attendance, founding records, mill certificates, or transfer-plate falsework acceptance → structural-integrity anomalies and intensified audits.
* Treating warehouse “one unscheduled audit” as immunity — irregularities trigger further audits.
* Interpreting rigorous audits as BD interfering with management — normal operations uninterrupted only if works are proper; substandard works attract scrutiny and sanctions.

## PNAP ADM-19: Building Approval Process
**Status/Context:** Core building approval, curtailed check, staged GBP, concept drawings, concurrent approval/consent and fast-track tracks (previously PNAP 272; this revision April 2025 — para 35 / Appendix H amended). Critical for design impact, GFA/SC documentation and BD submission strategy.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics

#### Plan hierarchy & self-regulation
* **GBP prevails:** GBP layout is basis for other plan types; AP/RSE/RGE must ensure other plans correspond to **latest approved GBP**. Approval of other plan types is **not** approval of building layout shown thereon.
* **Pre-OP/TOP / A&A completion:** All amendments approved and consented; latest amended GBP, structural, drainage and site formation plans (where applicable) must **tally** with each other.
* **Curtailed check:** GBP / superstructure / drainage — fundamental issues only; non-fundamental items not raised as disapproval; AP/RSE/RGE must ensure full BO/regulations compliance before commencement and rectify any contravention before completion certification. Random detailed audit of submissions; blatant/repeated contraventions → BA action.
* **Pre-submission enquiry:** Written enquiry → “letter of assurance” normally within **45 days**; pre-submission conferences with departments. Use early for new structural theory/materials/systems, sophisticated designs, unconventional methods.
* **Enquiry service:** Compliance/interpretation of regs and codes throughout development — answer normally within **4 weeks**.

#### GBP fundamental checks (Appendix A) — Density / Safety / Health / Allied
* **Density — Site parameters:** Claimed site area; site classification (**B(P)R 19(2)** determination?); required private street/service lane (deducted from site area, not built over).
* **Density — SC & PR:** GFA diagrams (accountable / non-accountable / exempted); SC diagrams (same); exempted & non-accountable areas; dedication/surrender for bonus; overall PR & SC checked against permissible (including granted bonus), mean street level, podium/building height.
* **Safety — MOA:** Fireman’s lifts / FRS number & disposition; initial access; EVA.
* **Safety — MOE:** Single-staircase requirements; MOE table (occupancy, exit doors/routes width); discharge value; G/F discharge; staircase inter-relationship; direct/travel distance; independent staircase for basement / kindergarten / child care; refuge floors; PPE premises.
* **Safety — FRC:** FRC table vs compartment volume/use; protection of adjoining buildings; staircase/lobby external walls; smoke vents; bridge/tunnel (fire shutter & by-pass lobby).
* **Health/environment:** Windows for habitable room/kitchen/office and disposition; open space required/provided and disposition.
* **Allied legislation major issues:** BFA (entrance, accessible routes, accessible toilet); fire certificate; OZP; vehicular run-in/out (TD/HyD); geotech assessment (**APP-25**); airport height; railway protection; Cap 370 s22 road works; Cap 123 s17A sewage tunnels; Cap 53 s6 monuments.

#### Superstructure / drainage / other fundaments
* **Superstructure (Appendix B/B1):** Master framing + detailed plans checked for stability/framing, FRR, loading, materials. Checklist covers structural system; wind modelling / GBP & foundation load compatibility; B(C)R 15 stability (max lateral deflection d/H); curtain wall anchorage; robustness (esp. precast); FRR vs GBP & FS Code 2011; floor loads B(C)R 17(1)–(2); Wind Code 2004 / impact B(C)R 17(3)–(4); dynamic B(C)R 17(5); Concrete 2013 / Steel 2011; concrete grade (flag if > C45); reinforcement CS2:2010; prestress/steel/aluminium/glass specs.
* **Drainage (Appendix C):** Disposal method acceptable to DSD/EPD; underground layout (no cross-connection SW/FW; reclaimed land — Foundations Code 2017 cl.2.4; keep drains out of slopes APP-76; terminal MH/invert/sizes to DSD); access (common drains/stacks in common areas APP-93; no water-borne pipes in structure APP-105); wells APP-4; gathering grounds WSD; fish culture zone ≤200 m EPD; railway protection (deep excavation / bored wells — MTRCL); condensate APP-112. Only a **percentage** of drainage randomly checked.
* **Demolition / site formation / ELS / foundation (Appendix D):** Codes/standards; geotech parameters; margins of safety; structural adequacy of major elements; effects on adjoining buildings/lands/services (settlement/groundwater); buildability/sequence; precautionary measures. Demolition also: hoarding/propping; appraisal & method; stability during/after; supports/protection.

#### Staged GBP (new developments; first GBP / major revision after 31 Mar 2023)
* Defer essential info per **ADV-33 Appendix A1** across three stages: Stage I first approval; Stage II before superstructure consent; Stage III before OP/TOP.
* **B(A)R 30(3)(c)** applies to amendment plans for deferred items unless major revision under **30(3)(b)**. BA may refuse consent under **BO s16(3)(a)** or OP/TOP under **s21(6)(a)** if all B(A)R 8(1) required plans/details not approved at material time.
* Stage I approval covers **Stage I items only**; BA requires under **B(A)R 10** a full Stage I+II GBP set for Stage II approval before S/S consent. Mark Stage II sets: “This set of plans is submitted for Stage II Curtailed Check”.
* Special early Stage II check on first GBP (case-by-case): simple buildings (single-storey plant room; ADM-19 para 36 two-storey warehouse); GIC / short Building Covenant; advanced projects near S/S consent.
* **Not applicable to A&A.**

#### Concept drawings (Appendix E) — design parameter summaries
* Recommended for pre-submission conference / enquiry / formal GBP: coloured key plans summarising in broad terms:
  * SC (accountable / non-accountable / exempted);
  * GFA (accountable / non-accountable / exempted domestic & non-domestic);
  * Fire safety strategy (MOE, MOA, FRC);
  * BFA (basic wheelchair access/facilities).
* Size A2/A3/A4; adopt sample colour schemes and MOE/MOA/FRC signs/indicators from BD website samples.

#### Electronic / BIM area calculation (paras 23–27; Appendix F)
* Computer GFA area calc → digital file verifiable by AutoCAD, MicroStation, Revit, ArchiCAD or other BA-accepted software; submit digital with paper GBP for centralised referral.
* AutoCAD/MicroStation: Appendix F guidelines; Revit/ArchiCAD: BIM GBP Guidelines + sample models (bd.gov.hk); see also **ADV-34**.
* If CAD requirements met and area layers clear: diagrammatic breakdowns of GFA, UFA, SC, PR, refuge floor, green feature areas **need not** be on plans — but **annotations and dimensions** of areas still required on plans.
* Soft: area-diagram layer floor plans; hard: layouts, area diagrams and calculations **without** breakdowns; soft ≡ hard or rejection; AP certifies each finalised DVD-ROM with permanent marker.
* Format: non-rewritable DVD-ROM; AutoCAD R14+ **.dwg** or MicroStation SE/J/V8+ **.dgn**; one hardcopy drawing per file; CSWP layer names e.g. ARC08240/44/46 (ND GFA), ARC08250/54/56 (Dom GFA), ARC08210–26 (SC), ARC08200/04 (OS), ARC08290/94 (UFA), ARC082_8 (dimensions auto-generated). Closed polylines; true dimensions auto-generated; areas/volumes to **3 decimal places** (m²/m³); true scale 1 unit = 1 mm or m.
* BIM completed-project model referred to LandsD/PlanD for 3D spatial/planning updates; submission taken as consent to extract walls/floors/stairs/lifts/routes, uses, heights, bulk, development parameters, etc.
* ADM-14 minor paper revisions that do not match digital file may still be approved if digital updated at next area-calculation amendment; at OP/TOP or A&A completion certificate submit consolidated digital file.

#### Minor amendments after first consent (paras 28–31; Appendix G)
* With **BO s42(1)** modification of **B(A)R 33(1)**, prior approval and consent not required for minor amendments (building, S/S incl. secondary structure once S/S permit granted, drainage, foundation, pile cap, site formation, ELS) **except** Appendix G exclusions. Document and deposit amendments in site office; obtain approval and consent for all before completion cert / OP/TOP. Works must comply with regs, department requirements and lease before execution.
* Apply for modification with/after first consent for that works type.
* **Not minor (must not defer) — examples:** Major/localised major revision (APP-55); material effect on fundamental issues; slope/RW/adjoining stability concerns; exemption/modification applications; OZP/TPB non-compliance; works outside lot. S/S: overall stability; fundamental issues; exemptions. Drainage: major building-revision-driven layout change; disposal system/public sewer connection change; >3 m excavation; outside lot. Foundation: design assumptions/method/materials/precautions/tolerances/monitoring/founding/capacity; railway Area 3 / APP-24 monitoring non-compliance; sewage tunnel Area 5 / APP-62 / DSD PN 2/2017 non-compliance; Area 1 bulk excavation overrun; H-piles on bedrock steeper than **25°**; piles over interacting slopes; boundary/sensitive clear-distance breach; Type 1↔2 coupler / new QA scheme needed; pile relocation **>5 m**; pile count increase **>5%** (non-LDBP) or **>15%** (LDBP) — round up. Pile cap / ELS / site formation: analogous assumption/monitoring/Area 1–3–5 limits; site formation level change **>1 m**; permanent ERS retained height change **>1 m**; localised ELS strut tweaks without stiffness reduction may remain minor.

#### Concurrent approval & consent / fast tracks
* **(A) A&A, drainage, secondary structural elements:** Concurrent if: no foundation (except spread footing excavation ≤ **4.5 m**) and no significant geotech content; no pre-start precautionary/safety works needing BA satisfaction first; all B(A)R 8 plans/docs; all consent supports (supervision/hoarding/APP-117 reports etc.). Curtain wall APP-37 compliance certificates/tests may wait until OP/TOP.
* **(B) Simple A&A fast track (30 days):** AP/RSE confirm no structural involvement and no fundamental-issue changes (or minor changes confirmed BO-compliant); no referrals except FSD; use Appendix H certification + BA8A.
* **(C) Two-storey warehouse (Appendix I):** Concurrent if warehouse for general storage, ≤2 storeys, no basement; Class A/B/C site with EVA per B(P)R 41D & FS Code; no compartment > **28,000 m³** or **10,500 m²**; other ADM-19 criteria. BD processes concurrent fast-track within **45 days**; foundation bearing stratum inspection within **1 working day** of notification. May go via EffO One Stop Centre or direct to BD with full plan suite + consent.
* **(D) Curtain wall / window wall / cladding repairs (Appendix J):** Prefer MWCS where scheduled (items 1.48, 1.60, 1.61, 2.8, 2.48, 3.6, 3.31). Else concurrent fast track **30 days** if structural system not altered; submit supervision plan; tempered glass heat-soak QSP + manufacturer QAS (APP-37); inferior thermal transmittance → provisional OTTV (APP-67) and where applicable APP-156 energy report; material upgrade → structural/non-combustibility/FR justifications. Like-for-like component replacement may waive structural analysis of replacement and parent check.
* **BA8A rules:** Covering letter notes concurrency; consent effective date = plan approval date (or deemed approval); consent not processed if plans not approved; if approval without concurrent consent, **B(A)R 32(3)** 28-day period runs from approval date.
* **Fire damper / AC plant frames / large metal ducts (paras 40–43):** MWCS items 1.46, 1.51, 2.42, 2.46 available. For projects with S/S consent / A&A consent after **31 Aug 2021**, include in plans before OP/TOP or A&A completion cert unless A&A plans state MWCS route. Streamlined: amended GBP for locations (ADV-33 App A4); concurrent 30-day structural approval/consent for frames/ducts. Alternatively put layouts + ADV-33 App B11 details in first GBP/S/S and use minor-amendment deferral for later tweaks.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* **Primary density gate:** GBP curtailed check explicitly verifies claimed site area, classification, street/lane deductions, GFA/SC diagrams (accountable/non-accountable/exempted), dedication/surrender bonus, and overall PR/SC vs permissible including bonus, street level and height.
* **Concept drawings** are the early vehicle to socialise SC/GFA splits and fire/BFA strategies with departments before locking massing.
* **CAD/BIM area layers** are the verification substrate for GFA, UFA, SC, PR, refuge and green-feature areas — soft/hard identity is an approval condition.
* **Staged GBP** does not relax density fundamentals at Stage I; deferred items cannot be assumed “approved” until their stage; missing Stage II/III packages block S/S consent or OP/TOP.
* **Warehouse / simple A&A / CW repair** tracks accelerate process but do not create GFA concessions; warehouse compartment volume/area caps are fire-compartment limits, not PR concessions.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Prepare with **ADV-33** checklists/sample tables/drawings; ADM-8 calculation presentation for structural Part I checklist.
* Concurrent tracks: BA8A + covering letter; warehouse via EffO or BD; Appendix H / I / J packages as applicable.
* Modification of B(A)R 33(1) for post-consent minor amendments; keep site documentation; clear all before OP/TOP.
* Secondary structural element definition includes curtain wall, window wall, cladding, space frame, precast, canopy, skylight, balustrade, external ceiling, grille/louvre, ventilation duct, maintenance platform, etc. (not overall stability).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Relying on foundation/drainage/structural approval as layout approval — **GBP layout prevails**; mismatch at OP/TOP is fatal.
* Assuming curtailed check = BD has checked everything — non-fundamentals remain AP/RSE/RGE duty; audit can trigger disciplinary action for blatant/repeated breaches.
* Executing “minor” amendments that fall within Appendix G (e.g. >5%/15% pile increase, >5 m pile move, >1 m formation change, outside lot, OZP conflict) without prior approval/consent.
* Stage I–only GBP treated as complete for S/S consent or OP — **s16(3)(a) / s21(6)(a)** refusal.
* Soft/hard CAD mismatch or non-CSWP area layers → rejection; OP without consolidated digital area file.
* Fast-track A&A certification (Appendix H) that falsely states no density/fire/health/allied change → false certification liability.
* Fire damper / large ducts omitted until late programme after Aug 2021 consent cut-off → OP delay.

## PNAP ADM-21: Site Parameters - Documentary Proof
**Status/Context:** Documentary proof of site area, boundaries and classification underpinning SC/PR (first issue January 2017). Critical for density parameters and superstructure consent hold-points.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Statutory frame:** Maximum permissible SC and PR depend on site area under applicant’s title or **realistic prospect of control**; boundaries and setting-out essential to support claimed area and site classification.
* **B(A)R 8(1)(h)(i):** Prescribed plans include diagrammatic plan with full dimensions and calculations showing height, SC, PR and disposition per **B(P)R 19, 20, 21**. ADV-33 Appendix A1 Item 3(a)(1.1) Part C: GBP approval requires substantiation by detailed **lease plan** or **Land Survey Plan (LSP)**.
* **AP duty:** Ensure accurate site parameters in proposal; update as necessary before certifying completed buildings comply with BO.
* **Common proofs (para 4):**
  * **(a)** DSO lease plan — lot areas, boundaries, setting-out coordinates;
  * **(b)** Authorized Land Surveyor LSP — same content;
  * **(c)** Lease documents — approximate areas/boundaries **without** setting-out coordinates;
  * **(d)** Deed Polls of subdivided lots — areas/boundaries ± coordinates;
  * **(e)** Approved GBP of existing buildings showing site area in SC/PR calculations.
* **Acceptance hierarchy:** **4(a) or 4(b)** acceptable for processing GBP. **4(c)–(e)** = **provisional reference only** to facilitate plan approval.
* **Superstructure consent gate:** Before consent to commence superstructure, **4(a) or 4(b)** must be submitted; BA imposes this via **B(A)R 10** conditions on GBP approval.
* **Discrepancies:** If 4(a)/4(b) disagree with other para-4 sources → BD generally follows **4(a) or 4(b)** for GBP processing; AP to note **BO s14(2)**. If both 4(a) and 4(b) exist and disagree → AP must provide rationale for the chosen set in GBP for BD acceptance **before** S/S consent. If post-approval 4(a)/4(b) show actual parameters differing from approved GBP affecting layout, SC or PR → submit **amended GBP** before S/S consent can be processed.
* **LSP decimal presentation (Cap. 473 Code of Practice):** Co-ordinates and dimensions to **0.001 m**; areas rounded (5 up / 4 down) to nearest **0.1 m²** if area **< 2000 m²**, and to nearest **1 m²** if area **≥ 2000 m²**. BD adopts this for GBP.
* **Two LSP sets:** One for BD central processing referral to DLO/DSO (with SMF-0043, survey report, survey record plan per LandsD PN 2/2013); opportunity to attach DLO encroachment-avoidance docs (LAO PN 4/2008).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* **Direct density control:** Claimed site area and classification drive permissible SC/PR under B(P)R 19–21; wrong area → wrong PR/SC envelopes, wrongful bonus base, and invalid GFA accounting.
* **Provisional proofs (lease docs / Deed Polls / old GBP)** can get GBP through but **do not** unlock S/S consent until DSO lease plan or ALS LSP lodged.
* Area rounding rules (0.1 m² vs 1 m² at 2000 m² threshold) affect calculated site area used in SC/PR arithmetic.
* Post-approval parameter shrinkage/growth that affects layout/SC/PR forces amended GBP — can wipe concessions predicated on former site area.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* With GBP: prefer 4(a)/4(b); if only 4(c)–(e), expect B(A)R 10 condition requiring 4(a)/4(b) before S/S consent.
* Two sets of LSP when relying on surveyor plan; prepare to Cap. 473 Code.
* Cross-check LandsD encroachment measures (LAO PN 4/2008).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Proceeding toward S/S consent on provisional site proofs only — consent blocked or BA condition breach.
* Ignoring discrepancy between lease plan and LSP, or between either and GBP site area — **BO s14(2)** / amended GBP required; building on inflated site area is title and density risk.
* Adopting approximate lease areas as final PR base without setting-out → classification and street/lane deduction errors (ADM-19 density checklist).
* Certifying completion while site parameters remain outdated vs as-surveyed title — BO compliance certification exposure.

## PNAP ADM-22: Withdrawal and Resubmission
**Status/Context:** Facilitating practice to reset statutory processing clocks (first issue September 2019)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **W&R meaning:** By letter to BA, AP/RSE/RGE simultaneously withdraw and resubmit an application (plans approval, OP, or consent) **without** physically taking back/resubmitting plans/documents — effect is to **re-commence the statutory processing period**.
* **Governing measures:** (a) W&R must be **in writing with reasons**; (b) W&R of the **same submission** may generally be entertained **not more than two times**.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial impact. Used tactically when densisty/fundamental queries cannot be cleared within statutory time — does not alter SC/PR rules.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Written W&R letter stating reasons; applies to plan approval, OP and consent applications within statutory time limits.
* Third+ W&R of same submission generally not entertained.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oral or undocumented W&R; W&R without reasons.
* Habitual W&R (≥3 on same submission) — facility refused; underlying deficiencies remain.
* W&R to buy time without curing fundamental/density defects → repeated refusal risk.

## PNAP ADM-23: Self-certification System for Plan Submission of Simple Structural Works
**Status/Context:** Fast-track self-certified structural plans (first issue June 2025). Categories A–E with ICE for C–E; processing times depend on ESH.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics

#### General eligibility (all A–E)
* Conventional materials only (RC, precast RC, structural glass/steel/stainless/aluminium, stone, and other CDB materials);
* Must **not** affect overall structural stability of parent building;
* No prestressed structure; no retaining structure.

#### Category scopes (Appendix A)
* **A (RSE only):** New development works of same nature/scale/complexity as a MW Schedule 1 item; or MW in existing building submitted for BA approval/consent instead of MWCS.
* **B (RSE only)** — not Cat A, not exempted/designated exempted — erection with anchors/supports of:
  * Metal/glass cladding, wind-loaded suspended false ceiling, exterior/suspended louvre/grille: highest point ≤ **100 m** AGL; panel least rectangle dimension ≤ **2 m** and area ≤ **6 m²**;
  * Vertical greening proprietary modular units: height/width ≤ **0.75 m**, projection ≤ **0.2 m**, + 100 m height rule;
  * Metal/glass canopy: panel limits + projection ≤ **2 m** from column/wall/beam;
  * Internal BSI suspension frames: imposed ≤ **2 kPa**, individual BSI ≤ **2000 kg**;
  * GRP tank/BSI frames on-grade or on slab: imposed ≤ **20 kPa**, individual ≤ **9000 kg**;
  * Internal maintenance platform frames: design imposed ≤ **2 kPa**.
* **C (RSE + ICE):** Stone cladding (new development, not Cat A): ≤100 m AGL; panel ≤2 m least dimension & ≤6 m².
* **D (RSE + ICE):** Single-storey refuse/plant room/covered walkway (span ≤ **8 m**, height ≤ **5 m**, no basement); or detached/semi-detached single-family house ≤ **3 storeys** (span ≤8 m, storey height ≤ **4.5 m**, no basement). May rest on podium / shallow foundation / pile cap on piles. Associated works (curtain wall, skylight, precast, external steel MP, metal stair, cat-ladder except DEW 28, etc.) if general criteria met.
* **E (RSE + ICE):** Shallow foundation (spread/pad/strip/raft etc.) supporting Cat D: allowable pressure ≤ **300 kPa** (or ≤ **150 kPa** if below GWT); not on soft clay/mud; founding ≤ **3 m** below existing GL; **not** in Schedule 5 Areas **1, 3, 5**; overall gradient within 10 m downhill ≤ **15°**; no slope >15° in that zone; no RW/terrace wall > **1.5 m** (or below 45° line from footing base) in that zone.

#### Certification & BD check
* RSE: BA5/BA6 (A&A) + Appendix B1 checklist + Appendix C certificate; plans/calcs per ADM-8; Appendix D standard statements on plans/calcs.
* Cat C–E: ICE (must be RSE on register), owner-appointed, no conflict of interest; Appendix E appointment + declaration on/before plan submission; ICE Appendix B2 checklist; Appendix C Part B + Appendix D ICE statement.
* BD curtailed check focuses on document completeness, registration status, and correctness of: compatibility with supporting structure; FRR; design loads; codes; materials/workmanship specs; testing criteria; corrosion resistance.
* **Processing times:**

| Submission | Via ESH | Other than ESH |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First / resubmission / major revision | **20** days | **40** days |
| Amendment incl. Type II works seeking fast-track consent | **10** days | **20** days |

* Once self-cert adopted, subsequent resubmissions/amendments should continue under self-cert; otherwise may be treated as normal **B(A)R 30** timeframe. Self-cert may still be introduced on later submissions even if first was normal.
* Concurrent approval + consent for A–E possible if corresponding building plans already approved; follow **ADM-19** and submit all consent supports.
* Random BD audit of fundamental issues; misrepresentation / misconduct → BO action.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC concession. Cat D small structures/houses and Cat E shallow foundations are process-accelerated only; parent building density still governed by approved GBP (ADM-19/21). Works must not affect overall structural stability — cannot be used to justify major structural system changes affecting massing loads.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Complete B1 (all cats) / B2 (C–E), Appendix C request (duplicate — owner & AP copies), plan statements Appendix D, ICE notice Appendix E for C–E.
* BA8A if concurrent consent sought.
* Footing FOS benchmarks on B1 (e.g. sliding/uplift ≥1.5; overturning ≥1.5 wind/GW, ≥2 other; anchorage FOS ≥3; optional FOS 1.1 if highest GW per Foundation Code 2.5.4).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Self-certifying works outside eligibility (prestress, retaining, Area 1/3/5 foundations, oversize panels, >100 m cladding, etc.) — false certification: criminal offence and/or disciplinary action (explicit on forms).
* ICE with conflict of interest or not owner-appointed.
* Dropping self-cert mid-stream → reversion to normal processing time.
* Concurrent consent without approved building plans or incomplete ADM-19 consent package.
* Misrepresentation discovered on audit → BO sanctions against RSE/ICE.

## PNAP ADV-1: Asbestos
**Status/Context:** Health / environmental control advisory (ACM ban; EPD/LD abatement control on clearance, repair and demolition)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **General rule:** Avoid use of asbestos containing material (ACM); removal of ACM from existing buildings only by registered asbestos personnel.
* **Prohibition:** Factories and Industrial Undertakings (Asbestos) Regulation (Cap 59AD) prohibits asbestos/ACM in any industrial or manufacturing process including building operations. From **4 April 2014**, import, transhipment, use and supply of asbestos and ACM banned under Air Pollution Control Ordinance (APCO) Cap 311; ACM already in existing buildings not affected by the ban.
* **When ACM present/suspected — required control measures:** (a) appoint registered asbestos consultant early for asbestos investigation; (b) samples tested by registered asbestos laboratory where needed; (c) if ACM confirmed, submit to EPD an Asbestos Abatement Plan (planned removal) and/or Operation and Maintenance Plan (deferred treatment e.g. encapsulation/enclosure); (d) registered asbestos contractor under asbestos consultant supervision; (e) **28-day** advance notice to EPD before commencement; (f) worker protection per Labour Department (LD); (g) disposal of asbestos contaminated waste per EPD.
* **AP/RSE recommended sequencing:** Complete asbestos investigation and abatement before other building activities; exclude unauthorised personnel/scavengers until in-situ ACM inventoried, photographed, labelled and segregated; asbestos consultant certifies successful completion against investigation report and abatement plan, then immediately notify EPD.
* **Low-risk exemption:** For certain low-risk readily identifiable non-friable ACM, asbestos consultant and/or contractor may be exempted — Gazette Notice 25/1997 (GN 3021 and GN 3022, 6 June 1997); list on EPD website.
* **Reference CoPs (EPD):** Handling/Transportation/Disposal of Asbestos Waste; Preparation of Asbestos Investigation Report, Management Plan and Abatement Plan; Full/Mini Containment Method; Safe Handling of Low Risk ACM; Glove Bag Method. Also LD CoP Safety and Health at Work with Asbestos; BD CoP for Demolition of Buildings; ProPECC PN 2/97.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC/PR measurement impact. Design implication: new works must not specify/import/use ACM; existing ACM drives pre-demolition / UBW clearance programme time and method.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Primary submissions for abatement are to **EPD** (Abatement Plan / O&M Plan; 28-day notice); not a substitute BD plan submission.
* Registered asbestos consultant completion certification and immediate EPD notification after abatement.
* Registers of asbestos consultants, contractors, supervisors and laboratories held by EPD Asbestos Management and Control Section; LD Occupational Safety and Health Branch advises worker H&S.
* Similar practice note issued to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Specifying or allowing ACM after APCO ban / Cap 59AD prohibition.
* Starting demolition, repair or UBW clearance before asbestos investigation / abatement completion.
* Scavenger/unauthorised access before ACM inventoried, labelled and segregated.
* Missing 28-day EPD notice or disposing ACM waste outside EPD requirements.
* Using non-registered asbestos personnel except where Gazette exemption applies.

## PNAP ADV-3: Standardization of Floor Numbering
**Status/Context:** Design / emergency-response / OP–Rating & Valuation alignment (floor nomenclature good practice with BA review on GBP)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Except G/F and roof, assign floor numbers in a logical consecutive numerical series.
* **Above ground:** ascending order (e.g. G/F, 1/F, 2/F, 3/F…).
* **Below ground:** greater number = lower basement (e.g. G/F, Basement 1, Basement 2, Basement 3…).
* Consecutive order maintained **irrespective of function** — shop, car park, clubhouse, communal podium/sky garden, refuge floor, plant room, observation deck, etc. still take numbers in the building’s consecutive series.
* **Acceptable omissions only:** “4”, “13”, and numbers ending with “4”. Any other omissions **not** allowed.
* **Not accepted:** non-numerical names alone (OK if with normal number, e.g. “20/F Sky Garden”); alias / “also known as x/F”; illogical, non-conventional or non-consecutive numbers.
* **Appendix A nomenclature examples:** B4/F–B1/F; LG/F; UG/F; G/F; CL/F (cockloft); M/F (mezzanine); numbered floors with dual labels e.g. “7/F (or 7/F Mechanical Plant Floor)”, “20/F (or 20/F Sky Garden)”, “40/F (or 40/F Refuge Floor)”; R/F for roof. Notes confirm omission of 4, 13 and numbers ending in 4.
* **On-site display (Chinese and English):** conspicuous at main entrance lobby, lift lobbies, and walls of protected lobbies / landings of required staircases on each floor. Fireman’s lift floor-served notices per MoA CoP — FSD checks before OP.
* **Implementation cut-off (PN does not apply if):** Sales Brochure available to public and copy deposited with BD by recorded delivery **before 1 September 2010**; or ≥1 unit sold before that date; or OP application submitted before that date.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC measurement change. Floor labels for car park/podium/refuge/plant/sky garden must still occupy consecutive numbers — affects sales plans, DMC schedules, emergency addressing and Rating & Valuation floor designation, not PR/GFA counting rules.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* GBP (and other plans to BA): numbering of each floor must be clear and unequivocal. If BD or other departments find sequence illogical or confusing, BD advises amendment.
* OP floor numbers will conform to approved building plans. CRV adopts approved-plan floor numbers. BD uses approved plans and OP to answer floor-nomenclature enquiries; departure from approved descriptions **not normally entertained**.
* AP must bring this to clients and conveyancing solicitors’ attention.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Skipping floors other than 4/13/*4; marketing aliases without the approved numerical label → BA advice to amend; post-approval renaming requests refused.
* Plant/refuge/sky garden floors without consecutive numbers → emergency-service inefficiency and OP/FSD notice problems.
* Floor numbers on OP/plans vs sales brochure mismatch — client/solicitor exposure; AP duty to warn early.
* Missing bilingual conspicuous lobby/stair/lift displays — FSD may withhold fire-installation acceptance path to OP.

## PNAP ADV-4: Control of Environmental Nuisance from Construction Sites
**Status/Context:** Construction-stage environmental / public-safety advisory checklist (noise, air, drainage, health, roads, hoarding); cross-ref APP-23 for hoardings

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Appendix A checklist of practical prevention measures, legislative sanctions and responsible departments. Key controlled topics and selected metrics/sanctions from the Note:
* **Noise (NCO):** Percussive piling **7:00 a.m.–7:00 p.m.** on non–public-holiday days needs construction noise permit (CNP), generally prohibited other times. Restricted hours (7:00 p.m.–7:00 a.m. or any time on general holiday including Sunday): powered mechanical equipment for general construction and/or prescribed construction works in designated (predominantly built-up) areas also need CNP. Prescribed works include erection/dismantling formwork/scaffolding; loading/unloading rubble, boards, bars, wood, scaffolding; hammering. Max fine level 6 first conviction; **$200,000** subsequent.
* **Air / construction dust:** Cover/dampen stockpiles; hard-pave and water haul roads; cover loads; wash wheels; water site; erect boundary hoarding; specified-process licence for concrete batching if required. Notifying EPD beforehand for: site formation; reclamation; demolition; tunnel works (except part beyond **100 m** from exit to open air); building foundation; building superstructure; road construction. Without notice: max **$25,000** first / **$50,000** subsequent. Without dust reduction measures: **$50,000** first; **$100,000** + 3 months imprisonment subsequent. Specified process without licence: **$200,000** + 6 months + daily **$20,000**. Open burning (APCO Open Burning Reg): **$50,000** + **$500** per 15 minutes continuance (first conviction). Demolition without fans/catch platforms/dust screens: B(Demolition)R — max **$25,000** (level 4).
* **Drains/water:** Temporary drainage with de-silting before connection to main; sand/grease traps; perimeter channels/sand bags; pH/petrol interceptor as needed; wheel wash; seal sewer/drainage connections before demolition/foundation; no illicit surface-water connection into foul sewers. WPCO licence for construction discharges; WPCO polluting matter offence max **$200,000** + 6 months first offence. Unsealed demolition connections: B(Demolition)R **$25,000** (level 4). Foreign matter into public drainage: PHMSO max **$50,000** (level 5).
* **Health:** Mosquito stagnant water — drain/pump; if not possible spray diesel weekly; cover containers; level ground; remove tyres/cans; pest control. Fly/rodent/refuse controls: covered food/refuse; daily removal; proper latrines; seal holes. Chemical waste: register as chemical waste producer; packaging/labelling/storage; licensed collectors to licensed disposal — WDO / Chemical Waste (General) Reg, max **$200,000** + 6 months.
* **Roads/footpaths/hoardings:** Secure loads; wheel/body wash; Excavation Permit Processing Manual conditions; min footpath width; lighting/signing/guarding per HyD CoP; TD agreement on temporary traffic signs; hoarding width per approved hoarding plan (cross-ref **PNAP APP-23**); B(P)R for unsound/inadequate/hazardous hoardings.
* **EIAO:** If Schedule 2 Designated Project — obtain Environmental Permit before construction/operation and comply with all EP conditions.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No permanent GFA/SC impact. Temporary site layout implications: wheel-wash, de-silting, waste sorting areas, hoarding/covered walkway geometry, and buffer for noisy plant affect site logistics and may interface with hoarding plan approvals.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* BD-linked: demolition dust/debris fans-catch platforms; sealing drainage connections; footpath damage/repair interface; hoarding plan compliance under B(P)R (see APP-23).
* Separately: EPD CNP/WPCO licence/construction dust notification/chemical waste registration; HyD Excavation Permit; TD/HKPF for traffic management. Similar PN issued to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Works in restricted hours / percussive piling without CNP.
* Superstructure/foundation/demolition without EPD construction-dust notification or without dust measures.
* Demolition without sealed drains or without fans/catch platforms/dust screens.
* Temporary toilets/kitchens discharging to stormwater — WPCO offence.
* Unsound/narrow/obstructing hoarding vs approved plan — B(P)R / Summary Offences exposure.
* Schedule 2 DP commencing without EP.

## PNAP ADV-8: Registration of Slopes and Retaining Walls
**Status/Context:** Geotechnical catalogue update workflow (GEO Catalogue of Slopes / SIS; AP/RSE/RGE completion documentation)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* GEO maintains Catalogue of Slopes for sizable man-made slopes and retaining walls (“features”); each feature has a GEO registration number. Definitions (Appendix A): **F** fill ≥**15°** with min fill thickness **1 m**, no associated walls; **C** cut without associated walls; **R** retaining walls supporting slope/platform <**15°** (series of sub-registrable walls on overall slope >**45°** — combine heights); **CR** / **FR** cut/fill with associated walls; **DT** disturbed terrain (landslide scar repairs / composite cut-fill; natural gradient >**15°**; individual slopes below height criteria but total height registrable).
* **Registration criteria (Appendix B):** (a) cut slopes including associated walls, and retaining walls, **>3 m** high; (b) fill slopes including associated walls **>5 m** high; (c) fill incl. associated walls **<5 m** posing direct risk to life (Consequence-to-life Category **1 or 2** per PNAP 234); (d) DT features meeting total-height registration as above.
* **Non-registrable (examples):** seawalls/marine structures (water-facing); dams/water-retaining structures (but fill embankments in front of service reservoirs should be registered); basement/screen walls forming part of building with free-air side inside; tunnel/shaft linings (free-standing portal slopes/walls register); culverts/covered nullahs (free-standing open-nullah sidewalls meeting criteria do); bridge abutments (fill embankments/reinforced fill/slopes do); all temporary features under construction (abandoned quarry faces and completed controlled tips register as cut/fill). Walls designed and constructed **monolithic with a building** not registered — advise GEO if already in catalogue.
* Provide **1:1000** site plan with location plan, record sheet (Appendix C), photographs; soft copy basic data per Appendix D structure (downloadable SIS database from hkss.ced.gov.hk). Prefer digital photos.
* Maintenance responsibility: Lands D Slope Maintenance Responsibility Information Centre / website; lease (cutting-away clauses, green hatched black areas, etc.) for land outside lot.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Feature formation/modification is site-formation / geotech scope; registrable height and consequence-to-life category drive which permanent features must enter GEO catalogue and owner maintenance/DMC records.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Present Appendix C proforma (+ soft copy / photos) **with certificate of completion** of site formation and/or feature upgrading works.
* GEO returns copy of 1:1000 plan with registered numbers of newly formed/modified features.
* AP/RSE/geotech consultants advise development owners of registered numbers for maintenance records and to include feature numbers in **DMC**.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Completing site formation/feature upgrading without submitting slope record sheet/SIS soft copy to GEO.
* Failing to register low fill walls that are Consequence-to-life Cat 1/2 (<5 m fill criteria trap).
* Misclassifying non-registrable basement walls vs freestanding registrable walls; not notifying GEO of wrongly catalogued monolithic walls.
* Owners left without feature numbers → weak DMC/maintenance trail for MBIS-era slope responsibilities.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Lift shaft platforms = temporary wall-supported platforms inside lift shafts under construction (rain shelter; formwork platform; mid-shaft protection; debris-catching; crash deck). Governed by Construction Site (Safety) Regulations; general duties under OSHO Cap 509 and FIUO Cap 59.

## PNAP ADV-11: Suspended Working Platforms
**Status/Context:** Construction/maintenance safety advisory under FIU (Suspended Working Platforms) Regulation; supports ADV-14 gondola design

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Definition: scaffold/working platform suspended by lifting gear, capable of being raised/lowered by lifting appliances, including appliances, gear, counterweights, ballast, outriggers, supports, and mechanical/electrical apparatus. Regulation applies to industrial undertakings using SWP for carrying persons (generally in force from **1 July 1995**; s17 from **1 January 1996**).
* **Construction:** sound, adequately supported, properly maintained; electrical parts/motors/cables protected against accidental/environmental damage.
* **Dimensions/edge protection:** sufficient length; width at least **440 mm**; guard-rails height **900–1150 mm**; toe boards ≥**200 mm**; space between toe board and lowest guard-rail ≤**700 mm**; sufficient safe access/egress.
* **Installation:** wire ropes only for raising/lowering/suspension; suspension points at adequate horizontal distance from building face to prevent contact; counterweights on outriggers ≥**3 times** weight necessary to balance load on projecting part when platform fully loaded; at each suspension point a safety rope with automatic safety device in addition to two suspension ropes; erection/dismantling/alteration only under competent person supervision.
* **Persons:** workers ≥**18** years; certificate after training recognised by Commissioner for Labour or provided by manufacturer/local agent; wear safety harness with lanyard to independent lifeline or anchorage with fittings while on platform.
* **Test/exam:** competent person inspect before use; competent examiner load test + thorough examination before use or after substantial repair, re-erection, adjustment, failure, collapse, or weather likely affecting stability; periodical tests thereafter; most recent certificate/report displayed prominently on platform; mark SWL and max persons; unique identification marking.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact by itself. Permanent roof outriggers/tracks/counterweight systems (see ADV-14) have lease height-restriction and structural approval implications.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Regulatory regime primarily Labour Department (FIU SWP Regulation; CoP and Guidance Notes on LD website). Structural supports forming building works still need BA approval pathway when permanent (ADV-14).
* Documents (c)–(f) cited: Guide to FIU SWP Reg; CoP Safe Use and Operation of SWP; Guidance Notes on Inspection/Thorough Examination/Testing; Safety Hints — via labour.gov.hk.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Platform <440 mm wide; inadequate guard-rail/toe-board geometry; counterweight <3× balance requirement; missing safety rope/automatic device.
* Untrained workers or no independent lifeline/harness.
* Use without current competent examiner certificates displayed.
* Fatal accidents from rope/mechanism failure cited — AP designing permanent gondola must design for ADV-11-compliant use (ADV-14 cross-ref).

## PNAP ADV-14: Facilities for External Inspection and Maintenance of Buildings
**Status/Context:** Design-stage permanent access / fall-protection facilities for external façades; PlanD/LandsD height-restriction treatment of gondola enclosures; DMC common-area access

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Strongly advise design-stage incorporation of permanent safe access to external walls for drainage, window cleansing, re-painting, finishes overhaul and fabric repair.
* **Permanent suspended access / gondola (strongly recommended including residential):** at roof of new buildings; comply with ADV-11 for installation/use/maintenance. CIC identified gondola as desirable safety design feature.
* **PlanD — gondola enclosures as rooftop utility structures:** may be exempted from height-restriction calculation if size is minimum for function; in general rooftop utility structure height should not exceed **15 m** beyond main roof **or 10%** of building height, **whichever is less**; also respect statutory town plan height restrictions and ridgeline view preservation from vantage points.

* **Cast-in anchors:** advise provision in new buildings for personal fall-protection attachment; follow CIC “Technical Guidelines On The Design, Installation & Maintenance Of Cast-in Anchors At External Walls Of New Buildings” (hkcic.org).
* **Lightwells/re-entrants:** special attention to access where drainage and service pipes placed; access to common maintenance facilities preferably via common areas or DMC-designated inspection/maintenance areas; **lowest level of re-entrants and lightwells** housing soil/waste pipes or stacks should be designated **common areas** with access (including cat-ladder where appropriate) for maintenance and refuse clearance.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* Gondola enclosure: potential exemption from **building height** calculation under PlanD/LandsD utility-structure practice if size is minimum / not excessive — **not** an automatic GFA disregard under B(P)R 23(3). Confirm lease and OZP notes project-specifically.
* Roof tracks, davits, counterweight rooms, cast-in anchors and re-entrant cat-ladder access can affect roof plant GFA, common-area designation and DMC; re-entrant/lightwell lowest levels as common areas have ownership/salesable vs common split implications (not GFA concession per se).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Structural supports/fixings of gondola system require BA approval under BO / B(C)R pathway.
* Cross-check PlanD OZP/height and LandsD lease height for rooftop utility exemption claims.
* Designate re-entrant/lightwell maintenance access in GBP and DMC common areas.
* Reference ADV-11 and CIC cast-in-anchor guidelines.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Residential towers without gondola provision / roof anchorage strategy → future façade workers rely on ad-hoc scaffolds with higher fall risk.
* Oversized gondola enclosure failing PlanD 15 m / 10% utility height test or lease “not excessive” test → height breach.
* Structural gondola supports installed without BA approval or without corrosion protection / façade sway restraint.
* Soil/waste stacks in private re-entrants without common access — maintenance deadlock and illegal entry disputes; contravene Note’s common-area advice.

## PNAP ADV-15: Fixing of Reinforcement for Concrete Works
**Status/Context:** Structural construction quality advisory (corrosion/spalling/collapse prevention; AP/RSE inspection hold points)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Do not fix bar/fabric reinforcement from a batch until batch testing completed (cross-ref former PNAP 122 / current reinforcement testing PN).
* Remove loose rust, excessive flaky rust or mill-scale by wire-brushing; **do NOT use** badly corroded, damaged or scaling steel.
* Sufficient spacers, chairs and supports to maintain reinforcement location per approved plans and specified minimum cover; spacers/chairs max spacing **1.5 m**; for bars **≤20 mm** diameter, reduce spacing to avoid sagging.
* Spacers of concrete or proprietary plastic/concrete type; as small as practicable; support reinforcement + construction loads without breaking/deforming/overturning; strength/durability ≥ surrounding concrete.
* Tie sufficient intersecting/lapping bars; ends of tying wire/devices/clips must **not encroach into cover**.
* Prevent contamination/displacement from traffic over reinforcement — use independently supported planks/ladders clear of completed reinforcement; protect against subsequent trades (e.g. conduits); for **cantilevered projecting structures**, critical that top reinforcement not deflect — extra chairs and ties.
* Laps/joints only at specified positions; connectors per manufacturer recommendations and equipment.
* Free-standing reinforcement secured/braced against wind; prefabricated cages adequately supported/braced before lifting.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Cover/spacer quality affects durability of projecting structures (balconies, canopies) that may carry GFA/SC implications under other APP notes if failed and rebuilt.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Registered contractor: continuous supervision so fixing matches approved plans and this Note; reinforcement remains correct through placement/compaction.
* Contractor informs AP/RSE to **inspect all reinforcement after fixing and well in advance of concreting**; for walls/columns, inspect **before complete formwork erection** for access.
* AP/RSE provide adequate quality supervision. Similar PN to registered contractors.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Concreting without AP/RSE inspection of fixed reinforcement; inspecting walls/columns only after shutters closed.
* Spacers >1.5 m or inadequate for ≤20 mm bars; tie wire ends in cover zone.
* Walking/wheelbarrows on cantilever top steel displacing reinforcement → known collapse/casualty contributor cited in Note.
* Using untested batch steel or badly corroded bars.

## PNAP ADV-17: Noise Annoyance Prevention — Design of Pump Room and Ventilation System
**Status/Context:** Building services acoustic design guidelines (NCO / TM compliance; spatial buffer planning)

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Pump rooms — layout preference:** separate from residential flats using non-sensitive buffer (stairwells, switch rooms). Address air-borne and structure-borne paths.
* **Air-borne (pumps):** quieter pump/motor models; if total sound power level of installed pumps **>100 dB(A)**, consider insulating capacity higher than stated minima; walls and ceilings/floors adjoining noise-sensitive receivers (residential flats, schools) minimum **Sound Transmission Class (STC) 50**; wall openings (air grilles, fan outlets) locate away from sensitive areas or treat with acoustic louvre/silencer; doors facing sensitive areas minimum **STC 25**.
* **Structure-borne (pumps):** anti-vibration mounts sized to load + flexible joints (Appendix A); pipes/hangers on spring or rubber isolators; pipes through walls with rubber sleeve and gasket; pipe sizing to avoid excessive flow velocity noise (esp. through valves).
* **Ventilation — design target:** achieve noise level **5 dB(A) below** relevant Acceptable Noise Levels in para 2 of Technical Memorandum for Assessment of Noise from Places other than Domestic Premises, Public Places or Construction Sites (under Noise Control Ordinance).
* **All ventilation systems:** appropriate capacity and quieter (usually more energy-efficient) models; isolate rotating/vibrating parts with anti-vibration mounts, flexible connections, damping and sound-absorbing lagging (Appendix B).
* **Near sensitive receivers:** locate noise sources away; assess large fans / air-cooled chillers; incorporate acoustic panels, enclosures, silencers or louvres where facing sensitive receivers.
* Reference: EPD Practical Guide for Reduction of Industrial Noise / pamphlets; NCO and TMs for maximum allowable levels from pump rooms, ventilation and associated A/C systems.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* Buffer rooms (stair/switch) between pumps and flats; thicker STC 50 partitions; acoustic enclosures; plant setbacks — consume floor space and may enlarge plant rooms seeking B(P)R 23(3)(b) disregard (must still meet APP-2/APP-151 plant criteria if claiming non-accountable GFA).
* Rooftop chiller/louvre acoustic treatments can interact with height, screening and visual constraints under OZP/lease.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
No dedicated BD acoustic certificate in this PNAP. Design should demonstrate awareness of NCO TM limits; EPD guidance documents referenced. Plant room disposition should be clear on GBP for BA/other department review where relevant.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Pumps hard-abutting flats without STC 50 envelope / AV mounts — NCO annoyance complaints and remedial A&A.
* Total pump SWL >100 dB(A) with only standard insulating capacity.
* Ventilation designed only to ANL, not to the Note’s **ANL − 5 dB(A)** good-practice target.
* Air-cooled chillers facing schools/residential without silencers/enclosures.

## PNAP ADV-21: Management Framework for Disposal of Dredged/Excavated Sediment
**Status/Context:** Advisory environmental / marine disposal framework under Dumping at Sea Ordinance Cap. 466 (DASO); re-issued August 2009 categorisation (formerly PNAP 252). Applies to private projects with marine disposal of dredged/excavated sediment where mud proposals not yet agreed by Marine Fill Committee (MFC). Revision April 2007.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Scope trigger:** Reclamation, marinas, deep basement excavation where excavated sediment quantity requires marine disposal. Re-testing required if time lapse between commencement of sampling and commencement of construction works > **3 years**, unless waived by DEP.
* **Rationale for dredging:** Allocation of disposal space not considered until need for sediment removal demonstrated to Secretary of MFC (preferably at EIA stage). Volumes of **Category L** sediment < **50,000 m³** exempted from this justification requirement.
* **Dredging without justification:** (a) emergency dredging for safety / averting environmental hazards; (b) maintenance/deepening of harbour fairways, berths, anchorages, navigation channels or approaches; (c) maintenance (not construction) of watercourses, rivers, stream courses, drainage channels or outfalls. Otherwise plan assuming mud kept in place; additional consolidation time not accepted as justification.
* **Classification (Appendix A — Chemical Exceedance Levels):** Category **L** (all contaminants ≤ LCEL); Category **M** (>LCEL and ≤UCEL for any contaminant); Category **H** (>UCEL for any contaminant). Ni and TBT: exceed UCEL if greater than the listed value (LCEL=UCEL).
* **Key CEL metrics (mg/kg dry wt. unless noted):** Cd 1.5/4; Cr 80/160; Cu 65/110; Hg 0.5/1; Ni 40/40; Pb 75/110; Ag 1/2; Zn 200/270; As 12/42; Low MW PAHs 550/3160 µg/kg; High MW PAHs 1700/9600 µg/kg; Total PCBs 23/180 µg/kg; TBT 0.15/0.15 µg TBT/L interstitial water.
* **Sampling grids (Tier II):** Low contamination — **200 × 200 m**, surface only; High — **100 × 100 m**, vertical profile; Very high (near outfalls/nullahs) — **50 × 50 m**, vertical profile. Vertical sub-sample tops: seabed, **0.9 m**, **1.9 m**, **2.9 m**, then every **3 m** to dredged bottom.
* **Sample sizes (guide):** Metals/metalloid **0.5 L**; Organic **0.5 L**; Biological response **6 L**. Storage **4°C**, dark, not frozen. Max holding: chemical **2 weeks**; biological **8 weeks**.
* **Tier III biological:** Category M — one set of three toxicity tests per composite (up to **5** continuous same-category samples): 10-day amphipod; 20-day polychaete; 48–96 h larvae. Category H with any contaminant > **10× LCEL** — dilution test (sample mixed with **9** portions reference sediment). Ammonia purge if overlying water ≥ **20 mg/L** (6 volume replacements/24 h, once repeatable). Fail if fail any one of three tests (survival/dry weight/normality criteria in Table 2: amphipod <**80%** of reference; polychaete <**90%**; larvae <**80%**; p ≤ **0.05**).
* **Programme:** Sampling/testing/SQR normally ~**8 months**. Biological screening SQR ≥ **3 months** before dredging contract tender (or ≥ **2 months** before maintenance works order). DEP reliability period starts on actual sampling commencement date; **not less than 3 years**.
* **Disposal types (Appendix C):** Type 1 open sea; Type 1 dedicated sites (Cat M pass); Type 2 confined marine (e.g. East Sha Chau mud pits); Type 3 special treatment/disposal (Cat H failing dilution bioassay). Vessel sampling may be required for **5%** of vessels.
* **Small-scale waiver:** DEP may waive sampling/testing for emergency dredging or maintenance dredging < **5,000 m³** in situ.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Disposal regime and programme constraints affect basement/reclamation construction methods and cost, not plot ratio measurement.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Agree rationale with MFC Secretary → consult DEP on existing data → submit sampling/chemical testing proposal to DEP (copy MFC) → Preliminary Sediment Quality Report (PSQR) → biological screening if Cat M/certain Cat H → formal Sediment Quality Report (SQR) ≥3 months before tender.
* Disposal space: MFC allocates (DEP delegated for <50,000 m³ Cat L). Include MFC/DEP disposal requirements and Appendix C Notes (1)–(6) in contract. Private projects charged per m³ in situ certified by AP/RSE/RGE.
* Fill/disposal details via MFC electronic form, copied to BD. DASO dumping permit applied for by contractor to DEP.
* HOKLAS (or equivalent) accredited labs for chemical tests; accredited labs for biological tests.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Programme failure if SQR reliability expires before contractor dumping permit — contract must state expiry and contractor pays for reliability extension.
* Keeping mud in place assumption ignored → MFC refusal. Incomplete option/risk assessments when claiming cost justification.
* Type 3 sediment: AP/RSE must identify/agree treatment/disposal with DEP (site-specific; not prescribed).
* Trailer suction hopper disposal at East Sha Chau requires DCE-approved down-a-pipe method.
* Close supervision of sampling critical — contaminant categorisation and disposal liability rest on data quality.

## PNAP ADV-23: Improvement of Visual Appearance and Landscape Treatment for Man-made Slopes and Retaining Walls
**Status/Context:** Advisory landscape / bio-engineering guidance for man-made slopes and retaining walls (formerly PNAP 270). BA strongly recommends following guidelines for new formation, upgrading and maintenance. Revision May 2004 (para 7 added).

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Shotcrete/chunam:** Last resort only. If used, avoid over the bottom **3 m** of a slope (most visible zone). Provide toe wall planters, planter boxes, tree pits, creeper holes, colour/pigments; tree rings of sufficient size for existing trees.
* **Consequence Category 3 soil cut slopes (PNAP 234):** Generally do not use shotcrete/chunam; prefer vegetated cover in upgrading/maintenance where safety allows. Stone pitching preferred over shotcrete/chunam where aesthetic alternative needed.
* **Rock (Grade I–III):** Generally no shotcrete/chunam where joints tight and average spacing > **60 mm**. May be needed for joint spacing < **60 mm**, open apertures, or rapidly weathering rock — confirm with suitably experienced GE/engineering geologist. Provide weepholes per Geotechnical Manual for Slopes Fig. 5.4.
* **Restraint of fractured rock:** Prefer wire mesh over shotcrete where possible. Masonry facing / surface finish for large buttresses/dentition; break monotony of large uniform finishes.
* **Trees:** Preserve healthy trees; structural supports if needed (not visually obtrusive); tree felling/transplant last resort. Unhealthy/decaying trees — experienced inspection; submit inspection report to BA for record **prior to** certificate for completion of site formation works.
* **Hydroseeding:** Provide biodegradable protective fabric after hydroseeding irrespective of slope gradient. Prefer low-maintenance native species; avoid high pruning/watering demand species.
* **References:** Geotechnical Manual for Slopes (2nd Ed.); GEO Publication No. 1/2000; GEO Report No. 116; PNAP 267 / LAO PNAP 8/2002 for tree felling.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC concession. Large slopes costly and hard to blend — reduce size by layout replanning or retaining structures (not too massive/visually obtrusive). Affects site formation geometry and usable site area indirectly.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Incorporate landscape measures in formation/upgrading/maintenance; specify maintenance requirements in slope maintenance manual.
* Vegetation inspection report to BA before site formation completion certificate (para 7).
* Obtain relevant Authority approval before any tree removal (lease / legislation).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Defaulting to shotcrete/chunam on Category 3 soil cuts or visible toe zone without exploring alternatives.
* Felling trees without Government prior approval where lease/legislation require it.
* Unhealthy trees left unchecked → fall hazard; failure to record inspection before formation completion certificate.

## PNAP ADV-27: Protection of Natural Streams/Rivers from Adverse Impacts Arising from Construction Works
**Status/Context:** Advisory ecological protection aligned with ETWB TC(W) No. 5/2005 (formerly PNAP 295). First issue May 2005. Similar note issued to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Natural stream/river definition:** Natural channel with natural water from upper terrains (perennial or intermittent wet season); natural bed (bedrock/boulders/cobbles/gravel/sand/silt/clay); largely natural banks with riparian vegetation. **Excludes** flood retention ponds, fishponds, engineered stormwater systems (drainage channels, nullahs, ditches — e.g. Tin Shui Wai / Yuen Long / Tuen Mun Nullahs, engineered Shing Mun River sections, concrete U-channels).
* **EIS:** Ecologically Important Streams/Rivers — AFCD updated list. Avoid EIS impacts especially.
* **Direct impacts:** Training, filling, culverting, narrowing, widening, damming, realignment, diversion, bank shotcreting.
* **Indirect impacts:** Site formation, landfilling, silt/polluted water discharge, debris dumping nearby, foundation dewatering, geotech/demolition near streams.
* **Engineering options ascending impact (App A):** (i) natural rock bank protection; (ii) selective obstruction/vegetation clearance; (iii) enlarge one bank; (iv) both banks; (v) deepening; (vi) realign new channel; (vii) new artificial non-vegetative smooth lining (concrete) — **last resort**.
* **EIS extras:** Soft robust bank techniques; geotextile-reinforced grassed lining, bio-engineering, stonewall, gabions; leave bottom intact where practicable; translocation/migratory pathways/compensation; place similar substrates after works; do not work full width if stream wide enough; fish ladder if dams on upper breeding reaches; consult AFCD for rare species; preserve pools.
* **Construction precautions (App B):** Temporary isolation (sandbags/silt curtains with lead edge at bottom); temporary river crossings on stilts; stockpile covered and away; wastewater hierarchy minimise → reuse/recycle → treatment; hydroseed disturbed banks; lateral support without unduly impeding flood flow; site supervisory staff stationed.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. May constrain site layout, foundation/dewatering and streamside landscaping.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Comply with applicable Cap. 28, 102, 131, 132BK, 208, 354, 358 as relevant. Incorporate mitigation into contract documents. Environmental review/study recommendations to be followed where required.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Concrete lining applied as default. Wet-season works in flowing streams → bank collapse / turbid water. Works across whole EIS width without refuge flow. Failure to consult AFCD before rare-species disturbance.

## PNAP ADV-31: Building External Finishes — Wet-fixed Tiles
**Status/Context:** Strongly advised design/construction QA guidelines for wet-fixed ceramic/mosaic tiles on cement-sand rendering (formerly PNAP 303). Revisions Oct 2020 / July 2021 (Appendix A amended). Similar note to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics

Intermediate coats: several days (or manufacturer) before next coat; final coat dry ≥ **2 weeks** before tiling. Each rendering coat thickness **8–16 mm**; total rendering ≤ **20 mm** (or manufacturer for proprietary). Undulating substrate → consider reinforcement/expanded metal lathing anchored to concrete.
* **Tiles:** Weight controlled. Size > **0.1 m²** → secure by mechanical means. Poor water-absorption tiles: special polymer-modified adhesive per manufacturer. Ceramic water absorption: ≤ **0.5%** up to first floor height; ≤ **3%** above first floor. Cement-slurry ceramic: soak ≥ **30 minutes**, drain before tiling; dampen rendering.
* **Reinforcement (if used):** Austenitic stainless steel wire preferably **2.5 mm** dia, mesh ~**50 × 50 mm**; stainless fixings. Galvanised reinforcement not recommended externally / moisture-exposed.
* **Grout:** Preferably type **CG2** (BS EN 13888:2009). Sealants must accommodate movement, resist water and UV.
* **M-sand:** May replace river sand per DEVB TC(W) No. 8/2018.
* **Pull-off tests:** ≥ **3 samples** of each tile type for every **10 typical floors** (or part) when fixed on rendering over concrete, direct on concrete, or slurry on rendering; locations selected by AP/RSE; not from tiles over waterproofing layers. HOKLAS-accredited lab; HOKLAS Endorsed Certificate.
* **MWCS:** Certain external rendering/tiles/roof finishes may use APP-147 simplified requirements (B(MW)R Sch.1).
* **Maintenance:** Regular inspection; recommend periodic debonding survey (hammer tap ± IR thermography/void detection).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Horizontal fins subdividing panels and mechanical keys affect facade detailing / projections.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* RGBC/RMWC method statements, QA/testing procedures and pull-off proposal/acceptance/non-compliance plan before tiling. AP/RSE audit checks on contractor supervision.
* Pre-cast off-site tiling encouraged for quality control. Trade-tested workers manufacturer-trained for proprietary systems.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oversized tiles (>0.1 m²) without mechanical fixing. Total rendering >20 mm / uneven thickness → debonding. Skip pull-off sampling or sample over waterproof layers. Spatter dash delayed >24 h without remedial bonding strategy. Tile fall from height liability.

## PNAP ADV-32: Provision of Babycare Rooms and Lactation Rooms in Commercial Buildings
**Status/Context:** Promotional guidelines prepared with FHB, DH, GPA, ArchSD, HD (formerly PNAP 305). Revision November 2018 (generally revamped). APs encouraged to recommend clients provide BB rooms and lactation rooms per these guidelines.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **BB room purpose:** Breastfeed/express milk, bottle-feed, change nappies; separate nappy-changing and baby-feeding areas with private breastfeeding areas. Recommend **≥1 BB room per building** (scale/need based).
* **Location:** Common areas; easily accessible (G/F level access or convenient lift if upper floors); **separated from toilets**; no foul-air transmission. Clear directional signs (Appendix A symbol). Accessible by both males and females; breastfeeding area signage “Breastfeeding Area for Woman only / 餵哺範圍，女士專用”.
* **BB room size:** Single user ≥ **5 m²** with wheelchair manoeuvring space. Multiple users ≥ **12 m²** with separate breastfeeding cubicle(s), one for wheelchair user. Sample layouts Figures 1–2 Appendix B.
* **Illumination/ventilation:** Per B(P)R **36** where sanitary fitments (e.g. sink) provided. Optimal ambient temperature (thermostat recommended). Family-safe finishes; non-slip floors; avoid sharp edges.
* **Doors:** Lockable breastfeeding cubicles / single-user BB rooms but openable from outside in emergency; optional “engaged” indicator.
* **Mandatory equipment:** Comfortable chairs/benches; nappy changing table(s) with raised edge + wipeable soft mat(s); sink(s) with lever tap(s) + soap dispenser(s); hand drying (paper towels); hinged-cover waste for nappies; small side table(s); electrical outlet(s) for pumps. Optional: alcohol disinfectant (sensor), clothing hooks, safety mirror, floor drain / leak sensor, emergency alarm. Prefer auto sensor tap/soap; nappy table perpendicular to wall with PwD height/knee space if space allows.
* **Lactation room:** For offices — recommend **≥1 per office building**. Common areas, authorised/employee access; separated from toilets. Size ≥ **7.5 m²**; accommodate ≥ **2 users** with privacy + wheelchair user; separate cubicles with doors. Sample layout Figure 3 Appendix B. Females-only signage; emergency-openable locks.
* **Lactation basic facilities:** Electric outlets; small side tables; comfortable chairs/sofas. Optional: refrigerator (need not be inside room), paper towels, covered waste, mirror, hooks, sink+soap if feasible, floor drain/leak sensor where sink provided. Expressed milk stored in refrigerator.
* **O&M:** Free of charge during building opening hours; notice of hours, target users, management contact; regular operational checks.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
**No GFA/SC concession stated in ADV-32.** Room sizes (5 / 12 / 7.5 m²) are design minima for amenity provision, not exemption metrics. Common-area BB/lactation rooms consume accountable GFA unless separately exempted under other instruments (not granted here).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Encouragement / guidelines only; show BB and lactation rooms on GBP layout with accessibility and sanitary provisions where sinks provided (B(P)R 36).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* BB room adjacent to toilets / foul air path. Undersized rooms or multi-user without wheelchair cubicle. Privacy door locks not emergency-openable from outside. Counting children’s/unisex issues in related ADV-28 differently from dedicated BB rooms.

## PNAP ADV-35: Greening in Buildings
**Status/Context:** Planning/design/installation considerations for building greening (first issue June 2017; revision Sep 2020 adding para 3 MWCS). Cross-refs APP-147 MWCS; APP-117 A&A structural; CoP Dead and Imposed Loads 2011; B(C)R 8 protective barriers.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Whether greening = building works:** Case-by-case totality of fixation, permanence, nature/intended use, size, associated works/facilities, ease of assembly/removal. Example: fixed planter boxes = building works; laying soil and planting vegetation therein ≠ building works under BO.
* **MWCS:** Certain greening installation works may use simplified requirements (B(MW)R Sch.1 / APP-147).
* **No new building works:** Plan submission not necessary, but AP/RSE must still assess load capacity, drainage, fire safety, change of use.
* **New works/strengthening:** Submit A&A proposal for approval.
* **Structural loads:** All combinations — drainage/substrate/growing medium/plant variety; use **fully saturated** weights; include irrigation system and construction loads. CoP 2011: soil, waterproofing, drainage system and plants = **dead loads**; plus imposed loads. Where people may congregate, adopt minimum imposed loads for corresponding usage. Extra caution: inaccessible roofs, long-span roof/podium, water ponds, cantilever slabs.
* **Drainage:** Effective heavy-rain discharge; surface fall **not shallower than 1:80** to structure and greenery; adapt system to greenery specification. Existing buildings may need additional/specific drainage. Sound waterproofing before greening; inspect/maintain; rectify leakage/blockage immediately.
* **MOE/refuge:** Greening must not diminish or obstruct refuge extent/safety.
* **Protective barriers & maintenance access:** Per B(C)R 8; provide safe access for greening maintenance.
* **Advice to owners:** Guidelines for safe use, regular inspection/maintenance of affected structures and drainage.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
**ADV-35 itself grants no GFA or SC concessions.** Greening amenities elsewhere (e.g. APP-42 / APP-152 / JPN walkways-trellis greenery justifying exclusion from overall GFA concession cap per ADV-33 checklist) are under those instruments — not created by ADV-35. Greening loads may constrain podium/roof usable area and structure design.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Determine building works status → MWCS or full A&A approval as applicable. Check approved records vs proposed greening loads (APP-117 for existing buildings).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Assuming planting = exempt from BO without fixation analysis. Saturated load underestimated on cantilever/long-span roofs → collapse/seepage. Greenery obstructing refuge. Drainage fall flatter than 1:80 → ponding/overload.

## PNAP ADV-36: Modular Integrated Construction
**Status/Context:** Streamlined MiC facilitation under BO (first issue Dec 2017; last revision Apr 2022; **this revision July 2025** — para 4 and Apps A, B & C amended; Annexes I–III of App C added). Pre-acceptance IPA mechanism; pre-submission enquiry (ADM-19) determination within **45 days**.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Definition:** Freestanding volumetric modules (finishes, fixtures, fittings, etc.) manufactured off-site, transported and assembled on site.
* **Design (App A):** Fire performance of MiC elements — non-code designs need equivalent performance ± fire engineering (FS Code). Joints/gaps (esp. drainage/envelope) prone to seepage — design against ingress. Structural CoPs for concrete, precast, steel apply; address (a) stability (wind/lateral + temporary assembly support); (b) robustness/integrity — tie modules and in-situ work H&V; prevent disproportionate collapse; (c) temporary stages (fabrication/lifting/transport/assembly) higher stresses; (d) cumulative movements (creep, shrinkage, differential, thermal) + fabrication/installation tolerances. Early design for access/recesses to inspect/repair structural connections, drains, services, joints.
* **Separate balcony/UP modules:** Should be **avoided** (long-term maintenance burden with in-situ joints). If proposed, such balcony/UP module area **will not be accepted as MiC floor area** under **Joint Practice Note No. 8** (BD/LandsD/PlanD) for **GFA and SC concessions**.
* **QA factory:** Valid Full Certificate under Building Technology Research Institute Modular Integrated Construction Manufacturer Accreditation Scheme (Accredited MiC Manufacturer), **or** ISO 9001 / equivalent. Imposed as BO s.17(1) item 6 condition on plan approval. QAS copy (or Accredited MiC Full Certificate) ≥ **14 days** before factory production start under B(A)R 10 (unless covered by unchanged IPA). QAS covers QC tests, calibration, production process, inspection/audit frequency (independent parties = HKCAS certification body or HKIAS inspection body).
* **Supervision frequencies (Table 1):** AP stream T3 weekly; RSE stream T3 weekly; RC stream T3 weekly + T1 continuous. MiC Supervision Plan ≥14 days before production. Log books at factory + site office copy.
* **Quality audit:** AP/RSE/AS first visit in person for first batch; then ≥ **monthly** audits (quarterly in-person; intervening months in-person **or** videotelephony ≥ **480p** colour on non-rewritable DVD-ROM). Audit reports to BD within **14 days**. Alternative: first factory visit then on-site audits after delivery — notify BA in writing **1 month** before production start; sample rate ≥ **1%** of each module type (App B Annex checklist: storey height, projections, prescribed windows/APP-116, BFA, drainage, FRC open-up dry walls, waterproofing, etc.; RSE rebar open-up 3×**100×300 mm**, covermeter 6×**450×450 mm**, NDT, tensile tests 1 specimen / **40 tonnes** same cast/thickness — results within **60 days** of delivery).
* **Pre-acceptance (App C):** IPA determination **45 days**; respond to comments within **30 days** or abandoned; BD further determination **30 days**. Not a prerequisite for GBP approval. Checklist: GBP/structural/drainage for MiC portions, MoE/FRC/BFA, structural connections permanent/temporary, QAS, installation sequence, waterproofing of joints, etc. Sample drawings Annexes I–III.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* **Within ADV-36 text:** GFA/SC concessions referenced via **JPN No. 8**; only MiC floor area counted — **separate balcony/UP modules excluded** from that MiC floor area.
* **Cross-links (other BD instruments, not quantitified in ADV-36):** ADV-33 App A2 item 27 documents **B(P)R 23(3)(a) MiC GFA exemption** with required plan note + module-type / MiC floor area diagram, citing **PNAP APP-161** (file not in this source batch). APP-151 lists “Buildings adopting MiC (JPN8)” as additional green-feature item (separate from the general APP-151 overall **10%** concession-cap schedule). **Do not invent % GFA bonuses from ADV-36 alone** — quantify from JPN-8 / APP-161 when those sources are consulted.
* APP-151 **10% overall cap** documentation regime for other concessions remains via ADV-33 / APP-151 (parallel compliance track).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Early MiC supplier engagement; ADM-19 pre-submission enquiry for unconventional prototypes (45 days). Optional IPA pre-acceptance (45/30/30 day cycle). Pre-acceptance ≠ GBP prerequisite.
* Plan approval conditions: s.17(1) item 6 (Accredited MiC / ISO + qualified supervision); B(A)R 10 (QAS/certificate 14 days pre-production; MiC Supervision Plan; audit report submissions). Follow APP-158 for QCST on FR constructions, drainage, structures.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Claiming JPN-8 MiC GFA/SC on separate balcony/UP modules. Production start without QAS/Accredited certificate on file. Missing first-batch factory visit or monthly audits / DVD evidence. On-site alternative without 1-month prior BA notice or <1% sampling. A&A after OP without approval (ADV-33 MiC note). Joint seepage / disproportionate collapse from inadequate tying.

## PNAP ADV-38: Innovative Building Materials and Technologies
**Status/Context:** Pre-acceptance mechanism for innovative building materials and technologies (IBMT) — building, drainage and structural (excludes innovative geotechnical — approach GEO/CEDD). First issue April 2024.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Purpose:** Curtailed assessment of non-site-specific design/construction for BO safety, health and environmental standards to give industry confidence.
* **Applicant:** Supplier/manufacturer appoints AP, RSE and/or RGE (RGE if geotech matters, e.g. new foundation system) to evaluate BO compliance and submit PAA.
* **PAA contents:** (a) App A form signed by supplier/manufacturer + appointed professionals; (b) innovation brief (use, design principle, job ref, highlight innovative content — e.g. innovative cement replacement/aggregates; or no local/international standard); (c) building/drainage/structural plans justifying BO standards or equivalent/not-inferior performance; (d) technical specs + catalogue, tech docs, accredited lab tests/assessments, factory QAS with ISO 9001 or equivalent.
* **Not applicable:** Materials/products/systems/technologies already accepted in PNAPs (e.g. MiC) or codes of practice; or already in BD Central Data Bank.
* **Processing timelines:** Determination normally within **45 days** (IPA letter or comments). Respond to all comments within **30 days** or application may be abandoned. After response, BD determination within **30 days**. Incomplete response → refuse with outstanding list (new application required). Pre-application discussion encouraged.
* **IPA letter:** States requirements/limitations; reminds need for project approval & consent; states suitability for MWCS simplified requirements or not; published on BD website with brief description.
* **Validity:** Maximum **5 years**; renew via App B form with AP/RSE/RGE as appropriate to latest BO requirements.
* **Project adoption:** Pre-acceptance ≠ exemption from prior approval/consent. Plan submissions using pre-accepted IBMT should include s.42 MOD/exemption where appropriate. Pre-acceptance **not a prerequisite** for approving plans involving IBMT. Professionals must ensure project design complies with BO, approved plans, and IPA limitations.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No automatic GFA/SC concession. IBMT may enable designs that interact with other concession PNAPs only if those PNAPs separately allow; ADV-38 is a materials pre-acceptance track only.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Form IBMT 1 (App A): 2 signed coloured building/structural/drainage plan sets + 1 set supporting docs (innovation brief, specs, calcs if applicable, BO compliance evidence, lab reports, QAS, limitations list). Renewal Form App B before 5-year expiry.
* On project GBP: apply BO s.42 MOD/exemption as needed; observe IPA limitations/MWCS suitability.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Treating IPA as project approval/consent. Using expired (>5 years) pre-acceptance without renewal. Re-applying MiC or Central Data Bank items through IBMT track (not applicable). Ignoring IPA limitations or failing to seek s.42 MOD for project-specific use.
