## PNAP ADV-21: Management Framework for Disposal of Dredged/Excavated Sediment
**Status/Context:** Advisory environmental / marine disposal framework under Dumping at Sea Ordinance Cap. 466 (DASO); re-issued August 2009 categorisation (formerly PNAP 252). Applies to private projects with marine disposal of dredged/excavated sediment where mud proposals not yet agreed by Marine Fill Committee (MFC). Revision April 2007.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Scope trigger:** Reclamation, marinas, deep basement excavation where excavated sediment quantity requires marine disposal. Re-testing required if time lapse between commencement of sampling and commencement of construction works > **3 years**, unless waived by DEP.
* **Rationale for dredging:** Allocation of disposal space not considered until need for sediment removal demonstrated to Secretary of MFC (preferably at EIA stage). Volumes of **Category L** sediment < **50,000 m³** exempted from this justification requirement.
* **Dredging without justification:** (a) emergency dredging for safety / averting environmental hazards; (b) maintenance/deepening of harbour fairways, berths, anchorages, navigation channels or approaches; (c) maintenance (not construction) of watercourses, rivers, stream courses, drainage channels or outfalls. Otherwise plan assuming mud kept in place; additional consolidation time not accepted as justification.
* **Classification (Appendix A — Chemical Exceedance Levels):** Category **L** (all contaminants ≤ LCEL); Category **M** (>LCEL and ≤UCEL for any contaminant); Category **H** (>UCEL for any contaminant). Ni and TBT: exceed UCEL if greater than the listed value (LCEL=UCEL).
* **Key CEL metrics (mg/kg dry wt. unless noted):** Cd 1.5/4; Cr 80/160; Cu 65/110; Hg 0.5/1; Ni 40/40; Pb 75/110; Ag 1/2; Zn 200/270; As 12/42; Low MW PAHs 550/3160 µg/kg; High MW PAHs 1700/9600 µg/kg; Total PCBs 23/180 µg/kg; TBT 0.15/0.15 µg TBT/L interstitial water.
* **Sampling grids (Tier II):** Low contamination — **200 × 200 m**, surface only; High — **100 × 100 m**, vertical profile; Very high (near outfalls/nullahs) — **50 × 50 m**, vertical profile. Vertical sub-sample tops: seabed, **0.9 m**, **1.9 m**, **2.9 m**, then every **3 m** to dredged bottom.
* **Sample sizes (guide):** Metals/metalloid **0.5 L**; Organic **0.5 L**; Biological response **6 L**. Storage **4°C**, dark, not frozen. Max holding: chemical **2 weeks**; biological **8 weeks**.
* **Tier III biological:** Category M — one set of three toxicity tests per composite (up to **5** continuous same-category samples): 10-day amphipod; 20-day polychaete; 48–96 h larvae. Category H with any contaminant > **10× LCEL** — dilution test (sample mixed with **9** portions reference sediment). Ammonia purge if overlying water ≥ **20 mg/L** (6 volume replacements/24 h, once repeatable). Fail if fail any one of three tests (survival/dry weight/normality criteria in Table 2: amphipod <**80%** of reference; polychaete <**90%**; larvae <**80%**; p ≤ **0.05**).
* **Programme:** Sampling/testing/SQR normally ~**8 months**. Biological screening SQR ≥ **3 months** before dredging contract tender (or ≥ **2 months** before maintenance works order). DEP reliability period starts on actual sampling commencement date; **not less than 3 years**.
* **Disposal types (Appendix C):** Type 1 open sea; Type 1 dedicated sites (Cat M pass); Type 2 confined marine (e.g. East Sha Chau mud pits); Type 3 special treatment/disposal (Cat H failing dilution bioassay). Vessel sampling may be required for **5%** of vessels.
* **Small-scale waiver:** DEP may waive sampling/testing for emergency dredging or maintenance dredging < **5,000 m³** in situ.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Disposal regime and programme constraints affect basement/reclamation construction methods and cost, not plot ratio measurement.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Agree rationale with MFC Secretary → consult DEP on existing data → submit sampling/chemical testing proposal to DEP (copy MFC) → Preliminary Sediment Quality Report (PSQR) → biological screening if Cat M/certain Cat H → formal Sediment Quality Report (SQR) ≥3 months before tender.
* Disposal space: MFC allocates (DEP delegated for <50,000 m³ Cat L). Include MFC/DEP disposal requirements and Appendix C Notes (1)–(6) in contract. Private projects charged per m³ in situ certified by AP/RSE/RGE.
* Fill/disposal details via MFC electronic form, copied to BD. DASO dumping permit applied for by contractor to DEP.
* HOKLAS (or equivalent) accredited labs for chemical tests; accredited labs for biological tests.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Programme failure if SQR reliability expires before contractor dumping permit — contract must state expiry and contractor pays for reliability extension.
* Keeping mud in place assumption ignored → MFC refusal. Incomplete option/risk assessments when claiming cost justification.
* Type 3 sediment: AP/RSE must identify/agree treatment/disposal with DEP (site-specific; not prescribed).
* Trailer suction hopper disposal at East Sha Chau requires DCE-approved down-a-pipe method.
* Close supervision of sampling critical — contaminant categorisation and disposal liability rest on data quality.

## PNAP ADV-23: Improvement of Visual Appearance and Landscape Treatment for Man-made Slopes and Retaining Walls
**Status/Context:** Advisory landscape / bio-engineering guidance for man-made slopes and retaining walls (formerly PNAP 270). BA strongly recommends following guidelines for new formation, upgrading and maintenance. Revision May 2004 (para 7 added).

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Shotcrete/chunam:** Last resort only. If used, avoid over the bottom **3 m** of a slope (most visible zone). Provide toe wall planters, planter boxes, tree pits, creeper holes, colour/pigments; tree rings of sufficient size for existing trees.
* **Consequence Category 3 soil cut slopes (PNAP 234):** Generally do not use shotcrete/chunam; prefer vegetated cover in upgrading/maintenance where safety allows. Stone pitching preferred over shotcrete/chunam where aesthetic alternative needed.
* **Rock (Grade I–III):** Generally no shotcrete/chunam where joints tight and average spacing > **60 mm**. May be needed for joint spacing < **60 mm**, open apertures, or rapidly weathering rock — confirm with suitably experienced GE/engineering geologist. Provide weepholes per Geotechnical Manual for Slopes Fig. 5.4.
* **Restraint of fractured rock:** Prefer wire mesh over shotcrete where possible. Masonry facing / surface finish for large buttresses/dentition; break monotony of large uniform finishes.
* **Trees:** Preserve healthy trees; structural supports if needed (not visually obtrusive); tree felling/transplant last resort. Unhealthy/decaying trees — experienced inspection; submit inspection report to BA for record **prior to** certificate for completion of site formation works.
* **Hydroseeding:** Provide biodegradable protective fabric after hydroseeding irrespective of slope gradient. Prefer low-maintenance native species; avoid high pruning/watering demand species.
* **References:** Geotechnical Manual for Slopes (2nd Ed.); GEO Publication No. 1/2000; GEO Report No. 116; PNAP 267 / LAO PNAP 8/2002 for tree felling.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC concession. Large slopes costly and hard to blend — reduce size by layout replanning or retaining structures (not too massive/visually obtrusive). Affects site formation geometry and usable site area indirectly.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Incorporate landscape measures in formation/upgrading/maintenance; specify maintenance requirements in slope maintenance manual.
* Vegetation inspection report to BA before site formation completion certificate (para 7).
* Obtain relevant Authority approval before any tree removal (lease / legislation).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Defaulting to shotcrete/chunam on Category 3 soil cuts or visible toe zone without exploring alternatives.
* Felling trees without Government prior approval where lease/legislation require it.
* Unhealthy trees left unchecked → fall hazard; failure to record inspection before formation completion certificate.

## PNAP ADV-24: Floor Drains in Kitchens and Bathrooms
**Status/Context:** Advisory sanitary drainage design note (formerly PNAP 277). First issue June 2003. Water seal traps required for all sanitary fitments including floor drains before connection to common drainage stack.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Water in floor-drain traps may evaporate if not replenished. APs may divert some waste water from a waste fitment to the U-trap of kitchen/bathroom floor drains to keep seals charged.
* Design must comply with Building (Standards of Sanitary Fitments, Plumbing, Drainage Works and Latrines) Regulations; ensure smooth discharge flow; prevent back-flowing at the floor drain.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Provisions requiring modification of the Sanitary Fitments Regulations may be considered on merits upon application.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Dried U-traps → sewer gas / hygiene hazard (SARS-era context). Back-flow at floor drain from poorly designed diversion branch.

## PNAP ADV-25: Extractor Fans in Bathrooms and Lavatories in Domestic Buildings
**Status/Context:** Advisory post-Amoy Gardens SARS ventilation guidance (formerly PNAP 285). First issue March 2004.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Where extractor fans provided, ensure plumbing seals intact and operate per design; consider air intake quality/quantity, airflow path and fan capacity.
* **WHO optimum bathroom ventilation:** **2 cfm/sq ft** (**10.2 L/s per m²**). Larger volume adds little comfort and risks building negative pressure.
* Provide air-relief opening (door undercut or louvred opening at door/wall) to minimise negative pressure. Avoid short-circuiting of ingress and exhaust airflow paths.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Affects bathroom door/wall detailing for relief openings.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Advisory recommendation; no dedicated statutory form in this PNAP.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Over-capacity extractors with sealed bathrooms → negative pressure, compromised trap seals, pathogen dispersal pathway (Amoy Gardens findings cited).

## PNAP ADV-26: Ventilation of Common Corridors and Lift Lobbies in Buildings
**Status/Context:** Advisory indoor environmental hygiene promotion post-SARS (formerly PNAP 287). First issue January 2004. Beyond prescribed B(P)R natural ventilation for habitation/office/kitchen rooms.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Promote ventilation to common corridors, lift lobbies and entrance lobbies at design stage. If natural ventilation not feasible, provide mechanical ventilation.
* Good practices for mechanical systems: (a) sufficient fresh air for anticipated population; (b) intake/exhaust designed to prevent contamination and short-circuiting; (c) system and ductwork conveniently accessible for maintenance.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. May affect lobby/corridor envelope openings and plant/duct space.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
Recommendations kept under review by BD; no mandatory checklist in this PNAP.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Contaminated fresh-air intakes or short-circuited MV systems undermine hygiene intent of corridor/lobby ventilation.

## PNAP ADV-27: Protection of Natural Streams/Rivers from Adverse Impacts Arising from Construction Works
**Status/Context:** Advisory ecological protection aligned with ETWB TC(W) No. 5/2005 (formerly PNAP 295). First issue May 2005. Similar note issued to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Natural stream/river definition:** Natural channel with natural water from upper terrains (perennial or intermittent wet season); natural bed (bedrock/boulders/cobbles/gravel/sand/silt/clay); largely natural banks with riparian vegetation. **Excludes** flood retention ponds, fishponds, engineered stormwater systems (drainage channels, nullahs, ditches — e.g. Tin Shui Wai / Yuen Long / Tuen Mun Nullahs, engineered Shing Mun River sections, concrete U-channels).
* **EIS:** Ecologically Important Streams/Rivers — AFCD updated list. Avoid EIS impacts especially.
* **Direct impacts:** Training, filling, culverting, narrowing, widening, damming, realignment, diversion, bank shotcreting.
* **Indirect impacts:** Site formation, landfilling, silt/polluted water discharge, debris dumping nearby, foundation dewatering, geotech/demolition near streams.
* **Engineering options ascending impact (App A):** (i) natural rock bank protection; (ii) selective obstruction/vegetation clearance; (iii) enlarge one bank; (iv) both banks; (v) deepening; (vi) realign new channel; (vii) new artificial non-vegetative smooth lining (concrete) — **last resort**.
* **EIS extras:** Soft robust bank techniques; geotextile-reinforced grassed lining, bio-engineering, stonewall, gabions; leave bottom intact where practicable; translocation/migratory pathways/compensation; place similar substrates after works; do not work full width if stream wide enough; fish ladder if dams on upper breeding reaches; consult AFCD for rare species; preserve pools.
* **Construction precautions (App B):** Temporary isolation (sandbags/silt curtains with lead edge at bottom); temporary river crossings on stilts; stockpile covered and away; wastewater hierarchy minimise → reuse/recycle → treatment; hydroseed disturbed banks; lateral support without unduly impeding flood flow; site supervisory staff stationed.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. May constrain site layout, foundation/dewatering and streamside landscaping.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Comply with applicable Cap. 28, 102, 131, 132BK, 208, 354, 358 as relevant. Incorporate mitigation into contract documents. Environmental review/study recommendations to be followed where required.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Concrete lining applied as default. Wet-season works in flowing streams → bank collapse / turbid water. Works across whole EIS width without refuge flow. Failure to consult AFCD before rare-species disturbance.

## PNAP ADV-28: Provision of Sanitary Fitments in Offices, Shopping Arcades, Department Stores, Places of Public Entertainment, Cinemas and Other Public Places
**Status/Context:** Strongly recommended voluntary upgraded standards pending Regulation amendments (formerly PNAP 297). Revision November 2012. BA prepared to grant modifications to vary male/female ratios and fitment standards in Cap. 123I (Sanitary Fitments regs) ss.5–7 where Appendix A adopted yields less than Regulations.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Male:Female population ratios:** Offices / sports stadiums — **1:1**; Shopping arcades, department stores, PPE, cinemas, religious institutions, funeral parlours — **1:1.5**.
* **Population assessment factors (usable floor area m²/person):** Shopping Basement–2/F: **3**; 3/F+: **4.5** (UFA includes arcade + public circulation). Assembly halls/auditoria/stadia without fixed seating: **0.5**; dance halls/disco: **0.75**; museums/exhibition/trademarts/display: **2**; religious/funeral worship areas: **0.5**. Fixed seating cinemas/PPE: seats + employees. Offices: Regulations s.5(5)(a).
* **Shopping/department stores — male WC:** 1–500: **1 per 125** (or part); >500: **4 + 1 per additional 250**. Urinals: **1 per 250** (or part). Female WC: 1–50: **1 per 25**; 51–125: **3**; 126–1800: **3 + 1 per additional 80** over 125; >1800: **24 + 1 per additional 90** over 1800. Basins (M/F): 1–500: **1 per 125**; >500: **4 + 1 per additional 250**.
* **PPE — male WC:** 1–400: **1 per 100**; >400: **4 + 1 per additional 250**. Urinals: **1 per 50**. Female WC: 1–250: **2 per 50**; >250: **10 + 1 per additional 40**. Basins M/F: **1 per 100**.
* **Cinemas — male WC:** 1–200: **1**; 201–500: **2**; 501–1000: **3**; >1000: **3 + 1 per additional 500**. Urinals: **1 per 100**. Female WC: 1–120: **1 per 40**; 121–250: **3 + 1 per add. 65** over 120; 251–420: **5 + 1 per add. 85** over 250; >420: **7 + 1 per add. 100** over 420. Basins: stepped 1/2/3 then +1 per 500 over 1000 (same M/F).
* **Sports stadiums:** Male WC **1 per 100**, urinals **1 per 100**; Female WC **1 per 50**; Basins M/F **1 per 100**.
* **Religious/funeral:** Male WC **1 per 150**, urinals **1 per 150**; Female WC **1 per 75**; Basins M/F **1 per 300**.
* **Offices:** Sanitary fitments per Regulations s.5(1), (3) & (4) (ratio modification under s.5(5)(c) as above).
* **Children’s fitments:** Design some urinals/basins/WCs for children; **do not** count children WCs toward prescribed ss.4–8 requirements.
* **Unisex toilets:** Encouraged for caregivers/opposite-sex child/parent cases; on plans state whether additional or counted to M/F with justification.
* **Minimum space (non-PwD public places) — WC cubicle:** Internal ≥ **700 × 1200 mm**; clear floor in front of WC ≥ **700 × 600 mm**; circulation outside cubicle ≥ **700 × 600 mm**.
* **Urinals:** Clear floor ≥ **500 × 500 mm**; centreline ≥ **250 mm** from side wall; centres ≥ **500 mm** apart; cubicle internal ≥ **1000 × 500 mm** + 500×500 clear; circulation ≥ **500 × 600 mm**. Trough: every **500 mm** = 1 urinal.
* **Lavatory basins:** Clear floor ≥ **600 × 600 mm**; centreline ≥ **400 mm** from side wall; centres ≥ **600 mm** apart; circulation ≥ **600 × 600 mm**. Clear floor spaces of different fitments must not overlap; circulation spaces may overlap. PwD spaces per DM:BFA 2008.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA concession. Larger WC cores and unisex/children provision affect net-to-gross and podium planning.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Apply BA16 modification to vary male/female ratio (ss.5(5)(c), 6(3)(b), 7(3)(b)) and/or fitment numbers where Appendix A yields less than Regulations.
* Indicate unisex toilets clearly on plans with counting justification.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Counting children’s WCs toward statutory adult prescription. Ambiguous unisex toilet counting (double-dipping or under-provision). Space below Appendix B minima in public toilets outside PwD scope.

## PNAP ADV-29: Construction Site Safety — Pay for Safety Scheme
**Status/Context:** Advisory procurement/safety culture note (formerly PNAP 298). Revision August 2017.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* BO duty: AP/RSE/RGE ensure building works under their supervision carried out safely; also Technical Memorandum for Supervision Plans and CoP for Site Supervision.
* **Pay for Safety Scheme (PFSS)** since 1996: employer pays contractor at pre-priced BQ/SOR Site Safety section rates upon satisfactory completion of safety-related items (incl. training), vetted/certified by employer’s representative.
* Reference links: DEVB Construction Site Safety Manual; Cap. 59AF Safety Management Regulation; REDA/HKCA Safety Partnering Programme; CIC PFSS Guidelines and Reference Materials Ch.7 (Mar 2017); LD/OSHC safety training courses.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* APs encouraged to recommend clients adopt PFSS in construction contracts. No BD statutory submission under this PNAP.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Safety costs not distinctly priced → resources undermined. PFSS does not displace TM/CoP supervision or Cap. 59 duties.

## PNAP ADV-30: Provision of Mechanical Ventilation under Building (Planning) Regulation 34
**Status/Context:** Advisory design engagement note for office mechanical ventilation under B(P)R 34(a) (formerly PNAP 301). First issue January 2007.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* Every office room needs adequate lighting and ventilation per B(P)R 30 & 31. Where B(P)R 31 cannot be complied with due to position/level/unsuitable surroundings, B(P)R 34(a) requires mechanical ventilation capable of supplying fresh air to all parts at **not less than 5 air changes per hour**.
* For large-scale/complex centralized plant + ducted systems serving multiple rooms/floors for B(P)R 34(a) compliance: advisable to engage **RPE (Building Services)** for design and installation advice.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact (services performance standard).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Engage RPE(BS) for complex central systems; demonstrate ≥5 ACH fresh air to all parts of deficient office rooms.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Claiming B(P)R 34 relief without proving 5 ACH to **all parts** of rooms. Complex ducted systems without BS engineering input.

## PNAP ADV-31: Building External Finishes — Wet-fixed Tiles
**Status/Context:** Strongly advised design/construction QA guidelines for wet-fixed ceramic/mosaic tiles on cement-sand rendering (formerly PNAP 303). Revisions Oct 2020 / July 2021 (Appendix A amended). Similar note to registered contractors.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Tile joints:** Usually **8–10 mm**, in any case **not less than 3 mm**, for grout insertion.
* **Substrate / rendering:** Prefer spatter dash within **24 hours** after formwork strike; concrete dry/shrink **6 weeks** recommended before rendering. Intermediate coats: several days (or manufacturer) before next coat; final coat dry ≥ **2 weeks** before tiling. Each rendering coat thickness **8–16 mm**; total rendering ≤ **20 mm** (or manufacturer for proprietary). Undulating substrate → consider reinforcement/expanded metal lathing anchored to concrete.
* **Tiles:** Weight controlled. Size > **0.1 m²** → secure by mechanical means. Poor water-absorption tiles: special polymer-modified adhesive per manufacturer. Ceramic water absorption: ≤ **0.5%** up to first floor height; ≤ **3%** above first floor. Cement-slurry ceramic: soak ≥ **30 minutes**, drain before tiling; dampen rendering.
* **Reinforcement (if used):** Austenitic stainless steel wire preferably **2.5 mm** dia, mesh ~**50 × 50 mm**; stainless fixings. Galvanised reinforcement not recommended externally / moisture-exposed.
* **Grout:** Preferably type **CG2** (BS EN 13888:2009). Sealants must accommodate movement, resist water and UV.
* **M-sand:** May replace river sand per DEVB TC(W) No. 8/2018.
* **Pull-off tests:** ≥ **3 samples** of each tile type for every **10 typical floors** (or part) when fixed on rendering over concrete, direct on concrete, or slurry on rendering; locations selected by AP/RSE; not from tiles over waterproofing layers. HOKLAS-accredited lab; HOKLAS Endorsed Certificate.
* **MWCS:** Certain external rendering/tiles/roof finishes may use APP-147 simplified requirements (B(MW)R Sch.1).
* **Maintenance:** Regular inspection; recommend periodic debonding survey (hammer tap ± IR thermography/void detection).

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA/SC impact. Horizontal fins subdividing panels and mechanical keys affect facade detailing / projections.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* RGBC/RMWC method statements, QA/testing procedures and pull-off proposal/acceptance/non-compliance plan before tiling. AP/RSE audit checks on contractor supervision.
* Pre-cast off-site tiling encouraged for quality control. Trade-tested workers manufacturer-trained for proprietary systems.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Oversized tiles (>0.1 m²) without mechanical fixing. Total rendering >20 mm / uneven thickness → debonding. Skip pull-off sampling or sample over waterproof layers. Spatter dash delayed >24 h without remedial bonding strategy. Tile fall from height liability.

## PNAP ADV-32: Provision of Babycare Rooms and Lactation Rooms in Commercial Buildings
**Status/Context:** Promotional guidelines prepared with FHB, DH, GPA, ArchSD, HD (formerly PNAP 305). Revision November 2018 (generally revamped). APs encouraged to recommend clients provide BB rooms and lactation rooms per these guidelines.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **BB room purpose:** Breastfeed/express milk, bottle-feed, change nappies; separate nappy-changing and baby-feeding areas with private breastfeeding areas. Recommend **≥1 BB room per building** (scale/need based).
* **Location:** Common areas; easily accessible (G/F level access or convenient lift if upper floors); **separated from toilets**; no foul-air transmission. Clear directional signs (Appendix A symbol). Accessible by both males and females; breastfeeding area signage “Breastfeeding Area for Woman only / 餵哺範圍，女士專用”.
* **BB room size:** Single user ≥ **5 m²** with wheelchair manoeuvring space. Multiple users ≥ **12 m²** with separate breastfeeding cubicle(s), one for wheelchair user. Sample layouts Figures 1–2 Appendix B.
* **Illumination/ventilation:** Per B(P)R **36** where sanitary fitments (e.g. sink) provided. Optimal ambient temperature (thermostat recommended). Family-safe finishes; non-slip floors; avoid sharp edges.
* **Doors:** Lockable breastfeeding cubicles / single-user BB rooms but openable from outside in emergency; optional “engaged” indicator.
* **Mandatory equipment:** Comfortable chairs/benches; nappy changing table(s) with raised edge + wipeable soft mat(s); sink(s) with lever tap(s) + soap dispenser(s); hand drying (paper towels); hinged-cover waste for nappies; small side table(s); electrical outlet(s) for pumps. Optional: alcohol disinfectant (sensor), clothing hooks, safety mirror, floor drain / leak sensor, emergency alarm. Prefer auto sensor tap/soap; nappy table perpendicular to wall with PwD height/knee space if space allows.
* **Lactation room:** For offices — recommend **≥1 per office building**. Common areas, authorised/employee access; separated from toilets. Size ≥ **7.5 m²**; accommodate ≥ **2 users** with privacy + wheelchair user; separate cubicles with doors. Sample layout Figure 3 Appendix B. Females-only signage; emergency-openable locks.
* **Lactation basic facilities:** Electric outlets; small side tables; comfortable chairs/sofas. Optional: refrigerator (need not be inside room), paper towels, covered waste, mirror, hooks, sink+soap if feasible, floor drain/leak sensor where sink provided. Expressed milk stored in refrigerator.
* **O&M:** Free of charge during building opening hours; notice of hours, target users, management contact; regular operational checks.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
**No GFA/SC concession stated in ADV-32.** Room sizes (5 / 12 / 7.5 m²) are design minima for amenity provision, not exemption metrics. Common-area BB/lactation rooms consume accountable GFA unless separately exempted under other instruments (not granted here).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Encouragement / guidelines only; show BB and lactation rooms on GBP layout with accessibility and sanitary provisions where sinks provided (B(P)R 36).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* BB room adjacent to toilets / foul air path. Undersized rooms or multi-user without wheelchair cubicle. Privacy door locks not emergency-openable from outside. Counting children’s/unisex issues in related ADV-28 differently from dedicated BB rooms.

## PNAP ADV-33: Essential Information in Plan Submissions
**Status/Context:** Core GBP/structural/drainage submission quality guidance (first issue Feb 2016; last revision Sep 2023; **this revision May 2024** — para 2(b), Apps B3 & B11 amended, App B12 added). Checklists **not** for submission to BD; for AP/RSE/RGE self-assurance. Cross-links ADM-19 curtailed check.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Document suite:** GBP — Apps A1 checklist, A2 typical MOD/exemption checklist, A3 sample MOD/exemption tables, A4 sample drawings. Structural — Apps B1–B12 (foundation, ELS, superstructure, curtain wall, glass balustrade, metal cladding, metal ceiling/grille/louvre, samples + embed details). Drainage — Apps C1–C3.
* **GBP staged information (App A1):** Stage **I** (first approval); Stage **II** (before superstructure consent); Stage **III** (before OP/TOP). Deferred II/III information: statement on first GBP that B(A)R 8(1) prescribed deferred items will be submitted/approved before superstructure consent or OP as appropriate.
* **Administration packs:** BA4/5/6/8A/16/17; 2 signed coloured sets to BA + FS sets + DLO sets (urban 1; NT 2; DDH lease 2); BD24 fee + cheque; ownership proof; BA16 supporting docs; priority/fast-track apps; geotech assessment (APP-25) where required.
* **GFA/SC documentation hard requirements:** Diagrams of accountable, disregarded/exempted areas, areas subject to **10% GFA cap**, and areas subject to prerequisites but **not** 10% cap — demarcated (B(P)R 23; **PNAP APP-151**). Overall PR & SC calculations; mean street level; mean height of roof over highest UFS; max **15 m** full-SC level on elev/section; summary of GFA concessions; APP-152 compliance details; EV charging facilities locations/dimensions; above-ground carpark treated as underground — details. Green balconies > **2 m²**: UFS figures for exemption calculation.
* **Key fire/access critical lengths flagged on plans:** Fireman’s lift/FRS passage critical when > **44 m** (open plan) / **59 m** (actual passage); G/F FS access point to fireman’s lift/FRS critical > **17 m**; staircase separation **6 m** if staircases < **7 m** apart; horizontal distance **48 m** when > **47 m**; travel distance critical > **47 m**; angle of exit doors critical if < **33°** (30° req); smoke outlets critical > **29 m**; FRC external wall/opening situations > **24%** fixed light; prescribed window surplus critical if < **10%** of required; PPE tier gradient ≤ **35°**, headroom ≥ **3 m**.
* **Typical MOD/exemption checklist metrics (App A2 samples):** Balcony/UP, wider corridors/lobbies, acoustic fins, non-structural prefabricated walls, voids, RRF, covered walkways/trellis (width >**2 m** needs justification; greenery detail for exclusion from overall GFA concession cap), lift shafts, cockloft clearance **1.5 m**, chimney plant spacing note ≤ **1.5 m**, filtration, pipe/air ducts, sky/podium gardens (JPN 1/2), **MiC GFA exemption** (item 27 — note on plans + diagram of module types and MiC floor area calculations; cites **PNAP APP-161**), openable windows ≥ **1% UFA** for curtain-wall commercial B(P)R 30(2)(a)(ii) reduction, ancillary office natural LV omission limited to ≤ **30%** GFA of host premises, etc. Many items require **APP-151** undertaking letters (DMC/SPA common-parts designation).
* **BIM colour coding:** GFA/SC/UFA/UFS/compartment diagrams with floor-plan underlay per Guideline for Using BIM in GBP Submission 2019 Table 1 colour system.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
Does **not** create new concessions, but is the primary gate for documenting APP-151 **overall 10% GFA concession cap**, prerequisite packages, APP-152, carpark EV disregard evidence, green balcony UFS, RRF/walkway/trellis cap interfaces, and **MiC floor area** for APP-161/JPN-related exemptions. Incomplete demarcation → concession refusal/deferral at Stage I/II.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Complete checklists internally; refer ADM-19 appendices for curtailed-check items. Land Survey Plan by authorised land surveyor (Cap. 473 CoP), duplicate, for site area/boundaries (Stage II typically).
* EV charging: RPE design info/certification (Stage timing per checklist). TD justifications for carpark layout / underground constraints as listed.
* Sample Tables (App A3) for granted/applied MODs; sample GBP details (App A4) for gas flue, UP/balcony/A/C, CW/prefabricated wall, VG, protective barriers, accessible WC, etc.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Missing 10% cap vs non-capped GFA demarcation on Stage I GBP. Deferred items without B(A)R 8(1) statement → invalid first approval path.
* Claiming APP-151 concessions without undertakings / DMC designation evidence. MiC GFA note and module/floor-area diagram omitted when claiming MiC exemption (item 27 / APP-161).
* Critical fire travel/FRS distances not measured at worst locations. Site classification street-abutting % / 4.5 m width incomplete for Class B/C sites.

## PNAP ADV-34: Building Information Modelling (BIM)
**Status/Context:** Encourages BIM for BO plan submissions; generally revamped May 2023 (first issue Sep 2016). Guidelines + samples on BD website; CIC standards at bim.cic.hk.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Identity rule:** BIM file information must be **identical** to prescribed plans. Minimise post-manual editing of drawings generated from BIM models.
* **Precedence:** BD processes approval based on paper or 2D PDF prescribed plans; BIM used to check contents. **On discrepancy, plans prevail.**
* **GBP:** Follow ADM-19 and “Guidelines for using BIM in General Building Plans Submission” + BD sample drawings/models.
* **Other statutory plans:** Follow “Guidelines for using BIM in Statutory Plan Submissions (other than GBP)” + CIC/BD joint samples/templates.
* **Optional real-time simulation:** Supplement with .wmv / .avi for Windows Media Player 11+ to illustrate phasing (demolition, foundation, ELS, site formation, substructure, superstructure, A&A).
* **Appendix A application examples:** GBP (irregular form, projections, site/street levels, area calcs, MoE/compartmentation, plan compatibility, A&A relationships); Drainage (complex systems, underground vs foundation/SF); Superstructure (materials/grades, FRR/cover, steel connections, transfer/load path, basement/geo features, façade/CW assembly, A&A temporary works); Foundation/ELS (pile levels, bearing stratum, geological relationships, top-down); Site formation; Demolition sequence/temporary measures.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No GFA concession. BIM strongly aids GFA/SC area demarcation and MoE documentation quality (supports ADV-33 / APP-151 packages).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Submit BIM with statutory plans where used; ensure model matches plans. Use CIC user guides/standards. BD continues to expand BIM facilitation over time.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Model–plan discrepancy (plans win, but processing delay / audit queries). Heavy post-editing of BIM-derived drawings defeating model consistency. Treating BIM alone as approved plans (not permitted).

## PNAP ADV-35: Greening in Buildings
**Status/Context:** Planning/design/installation considerations for building greening (first issue June 2017; revision Sep 2020 adding para 3 MWCS). Cross-refs APP-147 MWCS; APP-117 A&A structural; CoP Dead and Imposed Loads 2011; B(C)R 8 protective barriers.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Whether greening = building works:** Case-by-case totality of fixation, permanence, nature/intended use, size, associated works/facilities, ease of assembly/removal. Example: fixed planter boxes = building works; laying soil and planting vegetation therein ≠ building works under BO.
* **MWCS:** Certain greening installation works may use simplified requirements (B(MW)R Sch.1 / APP-147).
* **No new building works:** Plan submission not necessary, but AP/RSE must still assess load capacity, drainage, fire safety, change of use.
* **New works/strengthening:** Submit A&A proposal for approval.
* **Structural loads:** All combinations — drainage/substrate/growing medium/plant variety; use **fully saturated** weights; include irrigation system and construction loads. CoP 2011: soil, waterproofing, drainage system and plants = **dead loads**; plus imposed loads. Where people may congregate, adopt minimum imposed loads for corresponding usage. Extra caution: inaccessible roofs, long-span roof/podium, water ponds, cantilever slabs.
* **Drainage:** Effective heavy-rain discharge; surface fall **not shallower than 1:80** to structure and greenery; adapt system to greenery specification. Existing buildings may need additional/specific drainage. Sound waterproofing before greening; inspect/maintain; rectify leakage/blockage immediately.
* **MOE/refuge:** Greening must not diminish or obstruct refuge extent/safety.
* **Protective barriers & maintenance access:** Per B(C)R 8; provide safe access for greening maintenance.
* **Advice to owners:** Guidelines for safe use, regular inspection/maintenance of affected structures and drainage.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
**ADV-35 itself grants no GFA or SC concessions.** Greening amenities elsewhere (e.g. APP-42 / APP-152 / JPN walkways-trellis greenery justifying exclusion from overall GFA concession cap per ADV-33 checklist) are under those instruments — not created by ADV-35. Greening loads may constrain podium/roof usable area and structure design.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Determine building works status → MWCS or full A&A approval as applicable. Check approved records vs proposed greening loads (APP-117 for existing buildings).

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Assuming planting = exempt from BO without fixation analysis. Saturated load underestimated on cantilever/long-span roofs → collapse/seepage. Greenery obstructing refuge. Drainage fall flatter than 1:80 → ponding/overload.

## PNAP ADV-36: Modular Integrated Construction
**Status/Context:** Streamlined MiC facilitation under BO (first issue Dec 2017; last revision Apr 2022; **this revision July 2025** — para 4 and Apps A, B & C amended; Annexes I–III of App C added). Pre-acceptance IPA mechanism; pre-submission enquiry (ADM-19) determination within **45 days**.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Definition:** Freestanding volumetric modules (finishes, fixtures, fittings, etc.) manufactured off-site, transported and assembled on site.
* **Design (App A):** Fire performance of MiC elements — non-code designs need equivalent performance ± fire engineering (FS Code). Joints/gaps (esp. drainage/envelope) prone to seepage — design against ingress. Structural CoPs for concrete, precast, steel apply; address (a) stability (wind/lateral + temporary assembly support); (b) robustness/integrity — tie modules and in-situ work H&V; prevent disproportionate collapse; (c) temporary stages (fabrication/lifting/transport/assembly) higher stresses; (d) cumulative movements (creep, shrinkage, differential, thermal) + fabrication/installation tolerances. Early design for access/recesses to inspect/repair structural connections, drains, services, joints.
* **Separate balcony/UP modules:** Should be **avoided** (long-term maintenance burden with in-situ joints). If proposed, such balcony/UP module area **will not be accepted as MiC floor area** under **Joint Practice Note No. 8** (BD/LandsD/PlanD) for **GFA and SC concessions**.
* **QA factory:** Valid Full Certificate under Building Technology Research Institute Modular Integrated Construction Manufacturer Accreditation Scheme (Accredited MiC Manufacturer), **or** ISO 9001 / equivalent. Imposed as BO s.17(1) item 6 condition on plan approval. QAS copy (or Accredited MiC Full Certificate) ≥ **14 days** before factory production start under B(A)R 10 (unless covered by unchanged IPA). QAS covers QC tests, calibration, production process, inspection/audit frequency (independent parties = HKCAS certification body or HKIAS inspection body).
* **Supervision frequencies (Table 1):** AP stream T3 weekly; RSE stream T3 weekly; RC stream T3 weekly + T1 continuous. MiC Supervision Plan ≥14 days before production. Log books at factory + site office copy.
* **Quality audit:** AP/RSE/AS first visit in person for first batch; then ≥ **monthly** audits (quarterly in-person; intervening months in-person **or** videotelephony ≥ **480p** colour on non-rewritable DVD-ROM). Audit reports to BD within **14 days**. Alternative: first factory visit then on-site audits after delivery — notify BA in writing **1 month** before production start; sample rate ≥ **1%** of each module type (App B Annex checklist: storey height, projections, prescribed windows/APP-116, BFA, drainage, FRC open-up dry walls, waterproofing, etc.; RSE rebar open-up 3×**100×300 mm**, covermeter 6×**450×450 mm**, NDT, tensile tests 1 specimen / **40 tonnes** same cast/thickness — results within **60 days** of delivery).
* **Pre-acceptance (App C):** IPA determination **45 days**; respond to comments within **30 days** or abandoned; BD further determination **30 days**. Not a prerequisite for GBP approval. Checklist: GBP/structural/drainage for MiC portions, MoE/FRC/BFA, structural connections permanent/temporary, QAS, installation sequence, waterproofing of joints, etc. Sample drawings Annexes I–III.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
* **Within ADV-36 text:** GFA/SC concessions referenced via **JPN No. 8**; only MiC floor area counted — **separate balcony/UP modules excluded** from that MiC floor area.
* **Cross-links (other BD instruments, not quantitified in ADV-36):** ADV-33 App A2 item 27 documents **B(P)R 23(3)(a) MiC GFA exemption** with required plan note + module-type / MiC floor area diagram, citing **PNAP APP-161** (file not in this source batch). APP-151 lists “Buildings adopting MiC (JPN8)” as additional green-feature item (separate from the general APP-151 overall **10%** concession-cap schedule). **Do not invent % GFA bonuses from ADV-36 alone** — quantify from JPN-8 / APP-161 when those sources are consulted.
* APP-151 **10% overall cap** documentation regime for other concessions remains via ADV-33 / APP-151 (parallel compliance track).

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Early MiC supplier engagement; ADM-19 pre-submission enquiry for unconventional prototypes (45 days). Optional IPA pre-acceptance (45/30/30 day cycle). Pre-acceptance ≠ GBP prerequisite.
* Plan approval conditions: s.17(1) item 6 (Accredited MiC / ISO + qualified supervision); B(A)R 10 (QAS/certificate 14 days pre-production; MiC Supervision Plan; audit report submissions). Follow APP-158 for QCST on FR constructions, drainage, structures.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Claiming JPN-8 MiC GFA/SC on separate balcony/UP modules. Production start without QAS/Accredited certificate on file. Missing first-batch factory visit or monthly audits / DVD evidence. On-site alternative without 1-month prior BA notice or <1% sampling. A&A after OP without approval (ADV-33 MiC note). Joint seepage / disproportionate collapse from inadequate tying.

## PNAP ADV-37: Conduct of Registered Building Professionals under the Buildings Ordinance
**Status/Context:** Professional conduct / disciplinary framing (first issue May 2018). Covers AP, RSE, RGE, Registered Inspectors and Qualified Persons.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* RBPs have statutory duties to supervise works, notify BD of regulatory contraventions, and comply generally with BO. BD uses curtailed/audit checks — full compliance responsibility remains with RBP.
* BA may refer negligence/professional misconduct to disciplinary board under BO s.7(1A)(b) after considering seriousness for censure under s.7(1).
* **App A examples:** (1) Fail material supervision / material deviation from approved or MW plans; knowingly misrepresent facts to BA; undertake work unqualified. (2) Abet UBWs or disregard MWCS; falsify documents; advertise UBW skills; undeclared financial/personal interest. (3) Repeatedly withhold required info/reports; obstruct BD investigation; ignore BD written warnings. (4) Repeatedly fail to remind contractor of Cap. 59 site safety; misuse BD logo IP.
* Reports of irregularities: to Chief Officer/Technical Services, BD — treated in strictest confidence.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No spatial impact. Conduct failures frequently arise from false GFA/concession certifications and unauthorised works.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Due diligence on all submissions/certificates. Whistleblow irregularities confidentially to BD Tech Services.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Disciplinary exposure for knowing misrepresentation, UBW facilitation, or chronic non-response to BD. Cap. 59 reminders repeatedly omitted. Using BD logo on stationery.

## PNAP ADV-38: Innovative Building Materials and Technologies
**Status/Context:** Pre-acceptance mechanism for innovative building materials and technologies (IBMT) — building, drainage and structural (excludes innovative geotechnical — approach GEO/CEDD). First issue April 2024.

### 1. Core Technical Requirements & Metrics
* **Purpose:** Curtailed assessment of non-site-specific design/construction for BO safety, health and environmental standards to give industry confidence.
* **Applicant:** Supplier/manufacturer appoints AP, RSE and/or RGE (RGE if geotech matters, e.g. new foundation system) to evaluate BO compliance and submit PAA.
* **PAA contents:** (a) App A form signed by supplier/manufacturer + appointed professionals; (b) innovation brief (use, design principle, job ref, highlight innovative content — e.g. innovative cement replacement/aggregates; or no local/international standard); (c) building/drainage/structural plans justifying BO standards or equivalent/not-inferior performance; (d) technical specs + catalogue, tech docs, accredited lab tests/assessments, factory QAS with ISO 9001 or equivalent.
* **Not applicable:** Materials/products/systems/technologies already accepted in PNAPs (e.g. MiC) or codes of practice; or already in BD Central Data Bank.
* **Processing timelines:** Determination normally within **45 days** (IPA letter or comments). Respond to all comments within **30 days** or application may be abandoned. After response, BD determination within **30 days**. Incomplete response → refuse with outstanding list (new application required). Pre-application discussion encouraged.
* **IPA letter:** States requirements/limitations; reminds need for project approval & consent; states suitability for MWCS simplified requirements or not; published on BD website with brief description.
* **Validity:** Maximum **5 years**; renew via App B form with AP/RSE/RGE as appropriate to latest BO requirements.
* **Project adoption:** Pre-acceptance ≠ exemption from prior approval/consent. Plan submissions using pre-accepted IBMT should include s.42 MOD/exemption where appropriate. Pre-acceptance **not a prerequisite** for approving plans involving IBMT. Professionals must ensure project design complies with BO, approved plans, and IPA limitations.

### 2. GFA, Site Coverage, or Design Concession Impact
No automatic GFA/SC concession. IBMT may enable designs that interact with other concession PNAPs only if those PNAPs separately allow; ADV-38 is a materials pre-acceptance track only.

### 3. BD Submission & Certification Procedures
* Form IBMT 1 (App A): 2 signed coloured building/structural/drainage plan sets + 1 set supporting docs (innovation brief, specs, calcs if applicable, BO compliance evidence, lab reports, QAS, limitations list). Renewal Form App B before 5-year expiry.
* On project GBP: apply BO s.42 MOD/exemption as needed; observe IPA limitations/MWCS suitability.

### 4. Critical Red Flags & AP Liability
* Treating IPA as project approval/consent. Using expired (>5 years) pre-acceptance without renewal. Re-applying MiC or Central Data Bank items through IBMT track (not applicable). Ignoring IPA limitations or failing to seek s.42 MOD for project-specific use.
